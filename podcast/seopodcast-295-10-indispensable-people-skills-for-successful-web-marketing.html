#SEOPodcast 295 - 10 Indispensable People Skills for Successful Web Marketing

Episode43 min2015-10-22

Successful web marketing requires constant two-way communication between SEO agencies and clients, built on 10 people skills including listening, asking questions, accepting new ideas, and never setting campaigns to run without ongoing collaboration.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Agencies must listen first before acting, using structured calls to understand business goals, audience, and sales process.
  • Clients who dictate campaign details without letting the agency work often see declining results until they step back.
  • Both agency and client should admit mistakes quickly and put systems in place so the same errors do not repeat.
  • Never set it and forget it is the overriding principle for both agencies and clients in any web marketing campaign.
  • Monthly feedback loops catch critical issues like wrong traffic sources, bad keyword matches, and missed location updates.

Questions this episode answers

What people skills do SEO agencies and clients need for successful web marketing?

According to the episode, both sides need to listen without dictating, ask the right questions, accept outside ideas, let new information change opinions, avoid knee-jerk reactions, try different approaches, admit ignorance, hear hard truths, accept direction, and own up to mistakes. The overriding principle is never set it and forget it.

Why is client communication so important for an SEO campaign?

Without regular communication, agencies miss key changes like new business locations, wrong traffic sources, or bad keyword matches. The episode gives a real example where a phrase match campaign was generating clicks for movie projector rentals even though the client only rented digital projectors, only caught on a monthly call.

What should a client do if an SEO agency asks to increase the budget?

The episode says clients should not agree to a budget increase unless the agency proves why it is needed, explaining specifically what the extra spend covers, such as more pay-per-click spend, additional content, or increased social media posts, and showing the expected impact on results.

When a client's leads drop but traffic and rankings are improving, is it the SEO company's fault?

Not necessarily. The episode explains that declining leads alongside rising traffic and better rankings may point to issues the SEO company cannot control, such as a weakened sales process, a drop in star ratings from a bad review, or other business factors, rather than a failure in the marketing campaign.

Terms defined in this episode
Phrase Match Close Variants
A Google Ads matching behavior where a phrase match keyword can trigger ads for searches that are close variants of the phrase, sometimes pulling in unintended traffic as illustrated by a projector rental campaign receiving movie projector rental clicks.
Set It and Forget It
A failure mode where either the agency or the client stops actively communicating and monitoring a campaign after launch, described in the episode as the opposite of the collaboration needed for web marketing success.
