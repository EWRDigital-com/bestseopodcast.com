Key takeaways
  • Google's algorithm needs roughly two and a half months of consistent topical content to recognize and rank you for that authority.
  • Batching content, creating two months of posts in one or two days, removes daily pressure and keeps messaging consistent.
  • Narrowing your content to one core expertise area prevents dilution of SEO signals across too many verticals.
  • Getting past the three-to-five month discouragement hump, when engagement feels low, is critical to brand momentum.
  • Productizing a niche skill with a name, visuals, and a clear audience transforms a little-known service into a marketable offer.
Your brand should arrive before you do. People should know who you are before you actually step in the door.

Questions this episode answers

How long does it take to build topical authority for SEO?

According to this episode, Google's algorithm needs about two and a half months of consistently creating content within one topical area before it qualifies you for that authority. After that, you can start ranking for subtopics in that area, but you still need to reach the first page to see real results.

How do you create a lot of content without burning out?

Roz Miller-Choice recommends batching, spending one or two focused days creating content and then scheduling it out over time. This approach lets you prepare properly, stay consistent with your message, and step away from daily content pressure while still publishing regularly for months.

Why should small business owners build a personal brand instead of just a company brand?

In this episode, both guests agree that especially for small businesses, the owner is effectively the star of the business. Buyers are purchasing trust in that person. Positioning yourself as the go-to expert through consistent content means sales calls become less about persuasion and more about confirming fit and start date.

What should you do if you have expertise but are afraid to put yourself on camera or social media?

The episode advises getting out of your own way, recognizing that you judge yourself far harder than your audience does. Start ugly, improve over time, and remember that the value of what you share matters more than production quality. Roz Miller-Choice also offers done-for-you services for people who remain unwilling to be the face of their own brand.

Terms defined in this episode
Topical Authority
A state where Google recognizes a site or creator as the go-to source for a specific subject, earned by publishing consistent content in one area for roughly two and a half months, after which subtopic rankings improve.
Batching
Creating a large volume of content in one or two concentrated sessions and then scheduling it for release over weeks or months, removing the need to produce content daily.
Done For You Services
A service model where a team of creatives, including photographers, videographers, editors, writers, graphic artists, and producers, handles brand content production on behalf of an expert who does not want to manage it themselves.
Google EAT
A Google quality metric referenced in this episode as expertise, authority, and trust, used to evaluate how well an online presence maps to a person's real-world credibility and standing in their field.
