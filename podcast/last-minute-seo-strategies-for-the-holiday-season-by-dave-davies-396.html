Last-Minute SEO Strategies for the Holiday SeasonIt's the holiday season! Now is the time to zero in on just what you need to do to make your SEO work best for you during the holidays.

Questions this episode answers

What are the best last-minute SEO strategies for the holiday season?

The five strategies from the article by Dave Davies are: optimize Google My Business, update page titles and descriptions with target keywords, reverse keyword focus toward long-tail and local phrases, hijack top-ranking directories by claiming listings in them, and optimize on-page content for keywords already ranking near the top of page two or bottom of page one.

Why should you target long-tail keywords for quick holiday SEO wins?

Long-tail and local phrases like 'Echo Shoes Portland' have less competition, so a site can rank for them much faster than broad terms. The episode uses a Babe Ruth base-hit analogy: going for lower-hanging fruit gets you on base quickly and moves you toward bigger ranking gains over time.

How does Google My Business help with holiday SEO?

Google My Business verifies that a business is a legitimate entity, reinforces trust with Google, and enables the knowledge graph to appear in search results. Without a verified Google My Business listing, a business is unlikely to show a knowledge graph when users search for it.

What is the hijack strategy for holiday SEO?

If the top-ranking results for a target phrase are directory listings such as Yelp, you should claim and optimize your listing in those directories. Because directories dominate those phrases, getting into the top 10 of a directory is faster than outranking the directory itself.

if you do a search for the phrase that you want to place well for and the top three listings are some sort of directories, you probably should make sure you're in those directories.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization. The hosts describe it as beginning on the SERP itself, where the title and description are the first opportunity to earn a click.
Knowledge Graph
A Google feature that displays business information in a panel in search results, pulled partly from Google My Business and, for large brands, from Wikipedia.
Reverse Keyword Focus
A strategy of targeting longer, lower-competition phrases such as local or long-tail terms to achieve faster rankings rather than chasing high-competition broad keywords.
Enhanced CPC
A Google AdWords bidding option that automatically raises bids; the episode notes it was changed to bid up to the full daily budget rather than capping at 30 percent.
