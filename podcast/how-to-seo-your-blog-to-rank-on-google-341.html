Questions this episode answers

Why should I write blog posts directly in WordPress instead of in a separate document?

Writing directly in WordPress lets you use the Yoast plugin in real time. Yoast checks keyword density, word count, image alt tags, title tags, the slug, and readability, giving you a red, yellow, or green light so you know whether the page is optimized before you publish.

What is an LSI keyword and how do I find one for a blog post?

LSI stands for latent semantic indexing. It is a secondary keyword phrase closely related to your primary term, such as kids music lessons versus music lessons for kids. You find these variations using Google Keyword Planner. Using both in a post helps Google understand the content and can earn SEO value for both pages.

How do I get Google to index a new blog post faster?

Submit the new URL directly to Google using the submission tool, which requires only a paste of the URL and a not-a-robot confirmation. An even faster method mentioned in the episode is having someone plus one the post, or having someone click and read it, which signals Google to index the page more quickly.

What size should a blog featured image be for social sharing?

The episode recommends 1,200 pixels wide by 600 pixels high. At those dimensions, platforms like Facebook will automatically grab the image and display it as the card when the link is shared. If the image is the wrong size, Facebook may pick a random image from the site or display none at all.

LSI Keywords
Latent semantic indexing keywords, described in the episode as secondary keyword phrases that are closely related to your primary target term, such as a longer-tail variation, which Google recognizes as relevant to the main topic.
