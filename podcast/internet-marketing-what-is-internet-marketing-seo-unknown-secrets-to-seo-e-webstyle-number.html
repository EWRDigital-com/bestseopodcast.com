Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between white hat and black hat SEO?

White hat SEO means doing optimization the right way, following what Google wants. Black hat SEO uses techniques to game rankings and can get a website banned by Google or relegated to the bottom of search results. The hosts recommend always asking any SEO provider which approach they use.

Why should a local business like a plumber bother with SEO?

The yellow pages and print advertising are losing readership, and younger, tech-savvy consumers turn to search engines to find local services. SEO gets a local business onto the first page of Google so potential customers can find it when they search for relevant services in their area.

What does an SEO campaign actually involve?

According to the hosts at eWebStyle, SEO work includes keyword research, link building, meta tag optimization, and creating site maps. It also requires monthly monitoring because Google constantly adjusts its algorithm, which affects what changes need to be made to a website.

How does search engine marketing differ from search engine optimization?

Search engine marketing, or SEM, is a broader category made up of three things: pay per click sponsored ads, pay for inclusion in search results, and search engine optimization. SEO is one component of SEM, which is itself one component of overall internet marketing.

Terms defined in this episode
White Hat SEO
Doing search engine optimization the right way, following what Google considers legitimate, as opposed to black hat techniques that can get a site banned.
Black Hat SEO
Techniques used to manipulate search rankings in ways Google does not approve of. Sites caught using black hat methods can be banned or pushed to the bottom of search results.
SEM (Search Engine Marketing)
A category of internet marketing comprised of three things: pay per click, pay for inclusion, and search engine optimization.
Organic or Natural Search Placement
The non-sponsored search results on Google and other engines, which end users typically consider more relevant and trustworthy than paid ads.
