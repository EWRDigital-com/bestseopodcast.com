Content marketing mastery requires planning ahead, repackaging old content, optimizing for voice search, building quality links, and measuring success through email click-through rates, bounce rates, and scroll depth tracking.

Key takeaways
  • Plan content in advance using tools like Buffer or Trello to avoid last-minute scrambling.
  • Repackage existing content by breaking large articles into smaller posts or short videos for different platforms.
  • Optimize for voice search by using full questions as blog post titles and structuring content with H2s, H3s, and lists.
  • Links still account for about 40% of Google's ranking criteria, making internal link structure and quality inbound links essential.
  • Measure content success before publishing by defining how you will track email click-through rates, bounce rates, and scroll depth.

Questions this episode answers

How can I repackage old content to get more SEO value?

You can take older content and combine multiple posts into one large article, or break a long article into several smaller ones and turn those into short videos. The hosts note they do this for clients by recycling low-ranking content and republishing it to improve traction.

Is voice search worth optimizing for?

The hosts say 99% of voice searches are informational queries that are unlikely to drive sales. Still, optimizing is possible by using full questions as blog post titles, structuring content in featured snippets, and dividing pages into clear H2s, H3s, and numbered or bulleted lists.

What tools can track how visitors interact with content on a page?

The hosts recommend Hotjar, Crazy Egg, and Lucky Orange for tracking scroll depth, click patterns, and user behavior. They note Hotjar can slow a site down slightly. These tools help identify where visitors stop reading so content can be adjusted.

How should you evaluate link quality when doing outreach?

The hosts reference an Alexa tool and say sites that rank below 100,000 on Alexa are worth targeting for links. Sites ranking higher than 100,000 may not be worth the effort. They also warn that sites linking out indiscriminately dilute their SEO value.

Terms defined in this episode
Skyscraper technique
A content strategy, associated with Brian Dean of Backlinko, where multiple older blog posts are combined into one long, comprehensive piece of content.
Bounce rate
The percentage of visitors who leave a site without clicking further. The episode cites 70% as a target threshold, meaning more than 70% leaving signals a need to improve content.
Scroll depth
A metric tracked by tools like Hotjar and Crazy Egg that shows how far down a page visitors read before leaving, used to identify weak points in content.
SEO pie
A informal term used in the episode to describe the total set of criteria Google uses to rank pages, with links described as making up about 40% of that criteria.
