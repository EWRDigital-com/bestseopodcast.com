Why Traffic Is Down but Leads Are Up, and How to Read the Signals With Charlie Grinnell

Episode50 min2026-02-08Guest: Charlie Grinnell

Traffic going down while leads go up is a real trend, and brands need behavioral audience research, proper benchmarking against competitors, and smarter signal-reading to adapt to AI-driven search changes.

Key takeaways
  • Organic social should be a testing ground first, with ad spend following content that already performs, not the reverse.
  • Niche communities like Reddit signal broader market trends weeks or months before they hit mainstream platforms.
  • Tracking traffic as a primary KPI is now misleading because AI search creates an inverse correlation between traffic and leads.
  • Personas built on demographics miss the point since behaviors, like a 67-year-old using YouTube as a search engine, cross demographic lines.
  • Benchmarking against category growth is essential because internal metrics alone cannot reveal whether a brand is actually winning or losing.

Questions this episode answers

Why is website traffic dropping while leads are increasing?

According to this episode, AI-powered search is changing how people find and verify brands, meaning fewer people click through to sites but those who do are more intent-driven. The hosts describe this as an inverse correlation where traffic goes down and leads go up, making traffic a misleading primary KPI.

How should brands use Reddit and niche communities for marketing?

Charlie Grinnell advises brands to treat niche communities like Reddit as intelligence gathering, not as channels to join. These communities signal trends that hit mainstream platforms months later, so monitoring them helps brands plan content and activations ahead of competitors without risking tone-deaf self-promotion.

What is the right way to set up organic social media strategy?

Charlie Grinnell argues that organic social should come before paid ads, not after. Brands should publish as much content as possible, find what performs above baseline, and only then put ad spend behind it. Most brands currently do the opposite by shoveling ad creative onto social teams.

How does Wright Metric help brands with data and benchmarking?

Wright Metric aggregates over 30 different data tools into one platform, focusing on audience research, competitive benchmarking, and content insights. A team of analysts works with clients to clarify underlying business questions and then delivers research using proprietary tooling to enrich the aggregated data.

Terms defined in this episode
LLM visibility
How a brand or business appears and is described within large language model AI tools like ChatGPT, which users now consult to verify or research brands they discover elsewhere.
behavioral persona
Defining target audiences by their actions and behaviors rather than demographic attributes, since behaviors like preferring video search cross demographic lines.
competitive benchmarking
Measuring your marketing results against external category or competitor performance to determine whether growth reflects real gains or simply a rising tide.
inverse traffic-to-leads correlation
The emerging pattern where AI-driven search sends fewer visitors to websites but those visitors convert at higher rates, making raw traffic a misleading success metric.
