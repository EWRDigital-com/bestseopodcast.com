Questions this episode answers

Who are Google quality raters and what do they do?

Google quality raters are a team of anywhere from 10 to a hundred thousand people that Google has hired to determine how well Google search results solve a user's needs. Their feedback helps Google understand the impact of algorithmic changes, though the guidelines do not directly affect the algorithm or rankings.

What makes a page qualify for very high EEAT?

According to Google's search quality rater guidelines, a website or content creator who is the uniquely authoritative go-to source for a topic has very high EEAT. A wealth of firsthand experience or a very high level of expertise on the specific topic can also justify a very high EEAT assessment.

What are examples of pages that lack EEAT?

Google's guidelines cite a restaurant review written by someone who has never eaten at the restaurant, an article about skydiving written by someone with no expertise, a tax form hosted on a cooking website, and a shopping page with minimal customer service information as examples of pages lacking EEAT.

Consider the extent to which the content creator has the necessary firsthand or life experience for the topic. Many types of pages are trustworthy and achieve their purpose well when created by people with a wealth of personal experience.

Terms defined in this episode
EEAT
Google's expanded quality framework standing for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness, with Trust placed at the center as the most critical component.
Google Quality Raters
A team of anywhere from 10 to a hundred thousand people hired by Google to assess how well search results solve user needs, guided by a 172-page document of rating guidelines.
YMYL
Your Money, Your Life, a category of topics where highly inexpert pages should be considered untrustworthy and rated lowest by Google quality raters.
Search Quality Rater Guidelines
A 172-page Google document with three main sections covering page quality rating, understanding mobile user needs, and needs met rating, used to train quality raters.
