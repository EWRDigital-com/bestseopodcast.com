Questions this episode answers

How do I track LLM traffic like ChatGPT and Perplexity in GA4?

Brie Anderson recommends using source medium in GA4 rather than custom channel groupings, because new LLM sources can appear at any time and get excluded from a fixed channel list. She also notes that ChatGPT only added a medium and not a source, so manual review of source mediums and page referrer data is still required.

Is LLM traffic actually valuable compared to Google organic traffic?

Based on client data Brie reviewed, LLM traffic shows strong intent. Visitors from sources like Perplexity spend time on site, visit multiple pages, and often convert in the first session. Traffic from AI overviews or featured snippets tends to bounce quickly, with users just checking for the next sentence.

What is the bare minimum analytics setup a business should have in GA4?

Brie recommends tracking the actions that directly reflect how the business makes money. For lead-focused businesses that means form fills and phone calls. For e-commerce it means purchases with revenue, add to carts, and product page views. Google publishes a GA4 recommended events guide covering both scenarios.

Why do SEO agencies and clients end up misaligned on results?

Brie explains that clients often assume ranking for target keywords will automatically produce leads, but ranking and lead generation are separate outcomes. She recommends aligning on specific KPIs before work begins and confirming whether the goal is share of voice, branding, or actual lead generation, since each requires a different approach.

Terms defined in this episode
Source medium
A GA4 dimension that identifies both the source and the medium of a session, such as chatgpt.com or organic search, giving more granular attribution than channel groupings.
Attribution paths report
A report inside the advertising section of GA4 that shows the sequence of touch points leading to a conversion and assigns each touch point a percentage of credit using a cross-channel data-driven last non-direct click model.
Event-based tracking
The data structure GA4 uses, replacing the session-based structure of Universal Analytics, where individual user interactions are recorded as discrete events rather than grouped into sessions by default.
Tracking plan
A documented plan defining KPIs, events to set up, conversions to configure, and UTM conventions to use, created before a marketing campaign launches so results can be measured accurately.
