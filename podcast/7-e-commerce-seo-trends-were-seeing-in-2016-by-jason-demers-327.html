About this episode

This week Chris and Chuck talk about "7 e-commerce SEO trends we’re seeing in 2016" by Jason DeMers, an article from Search Engine Land. For those managing search engine optimization for e-commerce websites, contributor Jayson DeMers has some advice for what to focus on to stay ahead of the competition.

Questions this episode answers

What are the top e-commerce SEO trends for 2016?

Jason Demers identified seven trends: out-of-the-box SEO tools like Yoast, long-form content, social shareability, video content on product pages, mobile optimization, voice search and digital assistants, and local search strategy. The hosts note that most of these apply to any website, not just e-commerce.

How does voice search change keyword strategy for e-commerce?

Voice searches tend to be longer, conversational phrases rather than short typed keywords. Someone typing 'AC Repair Houston' on desktop might say 'I need an AC Repair guy' by voice. Long-form content helps capture these conversational queries within its natural sentence structures.

Should every e-commerce site focus primarily on mobile optimization?

Not necessarily. The hosts advise checking analytics first because desktop may still be the top traffic source depending on the industry. One financial services client doubled conversions simply by becoming mobile friendly, but other clients still see desktop as their primary traffic source.

Terms defined in this episode
Out-of-the-box SEO
Pre-built SEO solutions or plugins such as Yoast that help with on-site optimization steps like URLs and keyword density without requiring custom development.
Long-form content
Detailed web content that provides more depth, includes conversational phrases, and targets long-tail search queries, requiring research such as data, charts, and case studies to be effective.
Shikos
A term used by the hosts combining shares, likes, and follows across social media platforms.
Three pack
The condensed local search result display in Google that shows three local business listings, reduced from a previous seven-pack format, and now includes local ads.
