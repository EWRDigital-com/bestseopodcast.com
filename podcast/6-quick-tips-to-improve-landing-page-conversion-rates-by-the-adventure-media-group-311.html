6 Quick Tips to Improve Landing Page Conversion Rates by The AdVenture Media Group #311

Ep. 31130 min2016-02-23

Landing page conversion rates improve by applying six principles: clear concise copy, emotional triggers, audience affinity, reciprocity with free value, social proof, and following familiar design conventions.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Website visitors scan rather than read, so use bullet points and short sentences to convey your offer clearly.
  • Emotional triggers like limited availability create FOMO and motivate visitors to take action on your call to action.
  • Affinity means speaking the right language and using the right design for your specific target audience.
  • Giving away something free of genuine value makes recipients more likely to reciprocate with a larger purchase later.
  • Don't break design conventions; keep menus, cart functionality, and page layout where visitors expect to find them.

Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between a trigger and a call to action on a landing page?

A call to action is the specific thing you want visitors to do, such as buying a product or subscribing. A trigger is the emotional motivator that makes them take that action, like limited availability creating FOMO or a phrase like don't miss another tip encouraging newsletter signups.

How does social proof help landing page conversions?

Displaying Facebook likes, Twitter followers, reviews, and testimonials gives visitors evidence that others trust you. Tools like the Facebook page plugin can show visitors which of their own friends have liked your page, increasing the trust factor and likelihood of engagement or purchase.

What does affinity mean for a landing page?

Affinity means the editorial tone, language, design, and functionality of your page should match your target audience. If visitors feel out of place because the copy is too corporate or too casual for them, they are more likely to bounce and not convert.

Terms defined in this episode
Trigger
An emotional motivator on a landing page, separate from the call to action, that makes visitors want to take an action, such as limited availability creating a sense of urgency.
Affinity
The degree to which a landing page's tone, language, design, and functionality match the expectations and identity of its target audience, making visitors feel they are in the right place.
Social Proof
Evidence such as Facebook likes, follower counts, reviews, and testimonials displayed on a page to show that other people trust and engage with a brand, increasing conversion likelihood.
Reciprocity
The principle that giving away something of genuine free value motivates recipients to return the favor, often with a disproportionately larger action such as making a paid purchase.
