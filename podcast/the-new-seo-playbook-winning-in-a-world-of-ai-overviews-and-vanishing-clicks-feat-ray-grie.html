About this episode

Search is rapidly changing as traffic is being redistributed across platforms, with AI overviews and LLMs dramatically changing how users find information and interact with brands. • Despite traffic metrics declining, brand visibility may actually be increasing in places you can't easily measure • Media Mix Modeling (MMM) is emerging as a better way to evaluate marketing impact versus traditional attribution models…

Questions this episode answers

Why is website traffic dropping even when SEO rankings improve?

Ranking in the top positions now often means appearing inside AI overviews and People Also Ask features, which generate zero clicks. Users get answers directly on the search page, so impressions rise while site visits fall. The host noted 300 top-three positions at one client yet traffic still declined.

What is media mix modeling and why does it matter for SEO now?

Media mix modeling, or MMM, skips individual attribution and looks directly at spend versus revenue over time. Ray says enterprise teams are adopting it because traditional click and conversion tracking cannot capture zero-click or multi-platform brand touches that still contribute to revenue.

How can I measure topical authority and find content to prune?

Ray recommends using Screaming Frog's recent update, which visualizes topical clusters using embeddings and Euclidean distance scoring. Content that sits far from your core topic cluster may dilute site authority and should be pruned or merged to help Google focus its crawl budget on your strongest pages.

Terms defined in this episode
Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
A method where an LLM with a fixed training date is given live web search access so it can pull current information and produce more up-to-date answers than its foundational model alone could provide.
SERP Analytics
A broader approach than rank tracking that captures the visual shape of the full search results page, including shopping ads, hotel ads, and AI features, to measure how all elements affect user behavior and share of voice.
Embeddings
A technique that converts text into numbers so that mathematical operations, such as measuring Euclidean distance, can reveal how far apart topics are from each other on a site.
Media Mix Modeling (MMM)
A return-on-spend model that looks at total revenue relative to total spend across channels without trying to attribute individual clicks or conversions to specific touchpoints.
