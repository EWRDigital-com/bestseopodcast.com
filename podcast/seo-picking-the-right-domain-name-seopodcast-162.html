Key takeaways
  • Domain keywords must align with all on-page content or visitors will bounce for the wrong reasons.
  • Title tags and meta descriptions should use targeted keywords and a compelling call to action to drive clicks.
  • Product and service pages convert better when they include descriptions, pricing, bullet-pointed features, and FAB: Feature, Advantage, Benefit.
  • Adding media like YouTube videos and hover zoom images enhances the user experience and signals quality to Google.
  • Reviews give visitors an emotional push to purchase and can cost a sale if they are negative or missing.

Questions this episode answers

Why does my domain name need to match my page content?

If your domain targets a specific phrase like online jobs without investment but your page content covers unrelated SEO tips, visitors will bounce for the wrong reason. The calls to action and blog posts must directly relate to what users expect to find based on the domain name they typed in.

What should a good meta description do for SEO?

A meta description should describe the product or service on that specific page and include a call to action. It is the first opportunity searchers see what you offer before clicking, so it must be both keyword relevant and compelling enough to encourage the click-through.

What is FAB and why does it matter for content pages?

FAB stands for Feature, Advantage, Benefit. Including FAB in bullet points on a product or service page gives visitors the key information they need to make a purchase decision. Bullet points are indexed differently by Google and are more likely to be read than paragraphs of text.

How important are customer reviews on product pages?

Reviews give visitors an emotional push to purchase and can directly cost a sale if they are poor. The hosts recommend featuring reviews on the same page as product images, videos, and descriptions, and ideally pulling in a live feed from Facebook or Google Plus so reviews can be shared.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization. It begins at the SERP, the Search Engine Result page, which is the first opportunity to tell people what you offer before they click.
FAB
Feature, Advantage, Benefit. A sales framework the hosts recommend including in bullet points on product and service pages to drive conversions.
Bounce
When a visitor leaves a page without converting. The hosts distinguish a good bounce, where the visitor called or filled out a form, from a bad bounce caused by mismatched content or a confusing checkout.
