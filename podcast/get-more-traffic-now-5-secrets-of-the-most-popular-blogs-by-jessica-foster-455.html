Get More Traffic Now: 5 Secrets of the Most Popular Blogs by Jessica Foster #455

Ep. 45529 min2019-06-06

Popular blogs like Business Insider, Huffington Post, TMZ, The Verge, and Lifehacker grow traffic by being first to publish, writing with purpose, using tipsters, earning referral links, and generating community content.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Publishing trending content first, before competitors, earns Google credit and drives significant traffic gains.
  • Every piece of content should have a clear purpose, audience pain point, and defined user action goal.
  • Product reviews attract referral traffic from major brands, as The Verge demonstrates with Nike and other shoe sites.
  • Community-generated and crowd-sourced content, like Lifehacker uses, is one of the best ways to grow a platform.
  • Targeting keywords with no real search volume wastes budget; data should drive every content and SEO decision.
Before you create content, ask yourself these questions. Have I asked my audience directly what they're interested in reading? Do I know their pain points, fears, needs, and desires?

Questions this episode answers

What is Business Insider's secret to getting massive blog traffic?

Business Insider, which generates more than 30 million website visitors per month, focuses on timeliness, getting to a story first. CEO Henry Blodgett identified being first to publish as a key secret. The hosts suggest using Twitter as a real-time news source and then posting to Facebook to capture that advantage.

How did Huffington Post grow its blog traffic?

Huffington Post, with an estimated 16.7 million visitors per month, grew by publishing with a purpose and filling a niche with unbiased news stories. The episode advises creators to ask whether they know their audience's pain points, have data to support content decisions, and know what actions they want readers to take.

How can a blog get referral traffic from big brand websites?

The Verge, with an estimated 11.6 million visitors per month, attracted referral traffic from sites like Nike, Sneaker News, and Foot Locker by creating content such as product reviews and comparisons. The hosts recommend writing reviews of products from brands you want referral traffic from.

What content strategy does Lifehacker use to grow traffic?

Lifehacker, with an estimated 3.5 million monthly visitors, relies on community-generated content and crowd sourcing. The episode advises starting with your audience to build a topic list, using SEO tools to find keywords, and inviting guest contributors to write articles for your site.

Terms defined in this episode
AIDA model
A content framework the hosts use to build out three pieces of content per keyword theme in each category, which they say triggers strong results in the Google algorithm.
Evergreen content
Content that remains relevant over time, contrasted in the episode with fresh, trending content that captures timely search interest.
Community generated content
Content created by users and contributors rather than the site owner, cited as Lifehacker's primary growth strategy and described as one of the best ways to grow a platform.
Referral traffic
Visitors who arrive at a site through links on other websites, illustrated by The Verge receiving traffic from Nike, Sneaker News, Foot Locker, and Finish Line.
