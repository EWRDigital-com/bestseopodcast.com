Questions this episode answers

Should I manage my own PPC campaign or hire an agency?

The hosts say that even when a business owner improves their own PPC campaign, the time cost, around 30 minutes a day or roughly a full work day per week, outweighs the savings. That time could be spent on higher-value activities, so hiring an expert or agency is recommended.

Why is SEO more valuable than pay-per-click advertising?

Both Google and Bing admit that 75% of clicks happen in the organic section of search results, not in paid ads. The hosts argue that if you spend $1,000 on pay-per-click, you should actually be spending $3,000 on SEO because it has that much more value.

How do you get more Google Places reviews without faking them?

The hosts recommend changing internal business processes to drive existing customers to leave reviews. An example given is adding a link to your Google Places page in the checkout thank-you message of an e-commerce store, asking customers to review their experience or the product.

What does an SEO campaign from eWebStyle include?

Their campaigns cover on-page basics like titles, meta information, link sculpting, and content generation, plus off-page link building, directory submissions, Google Places and Bing local page management, and regular reporting followed by consultation to address what the numbers actually mean for the business.

In their words

what looks good on black and white may not necessarily be what's happening at the office. I could see you're getting a thousand clicks and you could say, no, my purchases haven't gone up.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Six-Pack
The local search results showing map pins labeled A through G that appear above natural listings on a Google search results page, managed as part of an SEO campaign.
Organic Search Results
The center section of a search results page, excluding paid sponsored ads at the top and right, where 75% of clicks happen according to the hosts.
On-Page SEO
Optimization work done directly on a website including titles, meta information, link sculpting, navigation, page count, and content generation.
Off-Page SEO
Link building activities done outside the website such as directory submissions, guest blog posts, and articles that build links to improve search rankings and support local Places pages.
