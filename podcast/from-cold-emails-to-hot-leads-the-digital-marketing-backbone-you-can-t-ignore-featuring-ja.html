From Cold Emails to Hot Leads: The Digital Marketing Backbone You Can't Ignore featuring Jay Schwedelson

Episode40 min2025-06-27

Email marketing remains the backbone of digital lead generation, with consistency, proper DNS setup, and authentic voice driving results far better than design or one-time campaigns.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key moments
Key takeaways
  • Fix DNS records first or email deliverability will fail and campaigns will be wasted.
  • Send emails two to four times a month as a baseline, never less than once a month or your list goes cold.
  • Email design does not matter. Recipients remember hearing from you, not your colors or pictures.
  • Build the back-end nurture sequence before worrying about traffic or front-end pages.
  • A lead magnet must have perceived value of a couple hundred dollars or more, otherwise nobody opts in.

About this episode

Email marketing still converts better than most digital channels, yet many businesses overlook this powerful tool because of misconceptions about its effectiveness or complexity. • Email is definitely not dead, it's been generating millions in revenue for over a decade • DNS records (SPF, DKIM, DMARC) are now essential for good deliverability • Different platforms serve different needs, MailChimp for beginners,…

Questions this episode answers

Is email marketing still effective in 2024?

Yes. Jason Wright says he has generated millions of dollars online with email marketing and has used it as the foundation of his business for 10 years. The claim that email is dead is described as an excuse people use to avoid doing the work.

How often should I send marketing emails?

Somewhere between two to four times a month is recommended as a good range. Once a month means people forget you, and more than once a week is considered too much. Starting at twice a month for six months before increasing frequency is advised.

What email marketing platform should I start with?

MailChimp is recommended as the starting tool because it is easy to use and fast to set up in a day. ActiveCampaign is described as the king for automation and CRM with a cleaner interface. GoHighLevel is powerful but has a big learning curve and is very overwhelming for new users.

How do I evaluate or hire a trustworthy email marketing expert?

Get them on a video call, ask questions, and watch for overselling. Jason Wright advises looking for someone with real experience, not someone living at home with no real-world exposure. Anyone who guarantees specific results in a fixed timeframe is a red flag.

Terms defined in this episode
DNS records for email
Technical domain settings required for email deliverability. Jason Wright says DNS records used to be the exception but are now the rule, and skipping them means emails will not reach inboxes.
Lead magnet
An offer used to collect a name and email address. Wright says it should have a perceived value of a couple hundred dollars or more, and recommends short authentic videos over generic ebooks.
Email sequencing
The timing and order of emails sent to new subscribers. Wright compares it to a new relationship where you communicate more often at first and taper off as the relationship matures.
7-11-4 formula
A Google zero moment of truth study finding that prospects need seven hours of content, eleven exposures to a message, and contact across four different channels before they trust and buy.
