How Authentic Storytelling Builds Brand Trust with Owen Garitty

Episode49 min2025-07-21Guest: Owen Garitty

Authentic brand storytelling builds trust and drives sales by focusing on customer experience across every touchpoint, using a hero, hub, and hygiene content framework distributed through multiple channels.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key moments
Key takeaways
  • Map storytelling across every brand touchpoint, from first contact through post-sale support, not just one channel.
  • Shift from describing features like 'excellent customer service' to showing the actual customer experience and emotional impact.
  • Use three content tiers: high-production hero content, product-focused hub content, and behind-the-scenes hygiene content.
  • Distribute one video into white papers, blog posts, social posts, infographics, trade show signage, and search campaigns.
  • Use first-party data to validate what advertising platforms report, since platforms have incentive to show the rosiest picture.

About this episode

Authentic storytelling in marketing is much more than a trendy buzzword; it's a comprehensive strategy that should permeate every customer interaction from first touch to final purchase and beyond. • Effective storytelling means presenting your brand's story as an evolving journey that spans multiple touchpoints • The customer journey increasingly happens off your website, making platform-diverse content essential •…

Questions this episode answers

What is the hero, hub, hygiene content framework for brand storytelling?

Hero content is high-production, brand-defining material. Hub content is one step below and focuses on specific products. Hygiene content is top-of-mind, behind-the-scenes material like phone-shot reels. Owen Garitty recommends planning a story arc across all three tiers and all touchpoints.

How do you distribute a single brand video to maximize its impact?

Owen Garitty recommends turning one video into a white paper on LinkedIn, blog posts, a social media strategy, trade show signage, an infographic, and a layered search sub-campaign. Refreshing these assets every couple of months extends the investment cycle further.

Why do brands fail at storytelling even when they know they need to improve it?

According to Owen Garitty, brands are too close to their own story, make assumptions about customers that are no longer accurate especially as decision makers age down, and lack confidence in their own message. Bringing in an objective outside partner helps overcome these barriers.

How should brands approach cause-based marketing without alienating their audience?

Owen Garitty advises framing it as problem-based marketing, choosing subjects that are not objectionable to the core customer base. His example is the Lionheart project for Safariland, which focused on preventing veteran suicide, a subject meaningful and safe to their entire constituent audience.

Terms defined in this episode
Hero, Hub, Hygiene content framework
A three-tier content model: hero is high-production brand content, hub is product-focused content one step below, and hygiene is frequent behind-the-scenes or top-of-mind awareness content.
Top of mind awareness survey
A research method Owen Garitty uses to track year-over-year changes in brand awareness and brand favorability scores as campaigns run in market.
Challenger brand mentality
Treating your brand as a risk-taker that tests new directions, measures analytics on the back end, and pivots quickly away from what does not connect rather than playing it safe.
First-party data validation
Cross-checking advertising platform performance reports against your own website visits and click-to-purchase data, because platforms have incentive to report the most optimistic results.
