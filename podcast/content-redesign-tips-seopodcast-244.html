Content Redesign Tips - #SEOpodcast 244

Episode36 min2014-08-29

When redesigning a site, audit existing content first by asking five questions: what content exists, what drives revenue, whether CTAs work, what is missing, and whether content supports the site's goal.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Audit all existing content, including articles, videos, FAQs, PDFs, and blog posts, before starting any site redesign.
  • Identify low-traffic, high-value pages during a redesign and improve them so they can rank and convert.
  • Review internal site search logs to find what users seek but cannot find, then create that missing content.
  • A/B test CTA color and placement on a redesigned site because a new layout may require different presentation to perform.
  • Remove or rework thin, irrelevant, or outdated content to avoid Panda algorithm penalties during a redesign.

Questions this episode answers

What content questions should I ask before a site redesign?

The episode covers five questions drawn from a Search Engine Watch article by Lisa Barone: what content currently exists, what content drives revenue, whether your calls to action are working, what information is missing or needs reworking, and whether the content supports the site's overall goal.

How do I find out what content is missing from my site?

Check your internal site search logs and focus especially on searches that return no results. Those zero-result queries show what users expect to find on your site but cannot, signaling topics where you need to produce new content or pages.

Is a high bounce rate always bad for SEO?

Not always. If your call to action is to get users to call by phone and a visitor calls immediately after landing and then leaves, Google Analytics records that as a bounce even though the user converted. Analyzing bounce rate in context of your specific calls to action is essential.

How can I improve conversions on high-traffic pages that are not converting well?

The hosts cite a client example where adding a pop-up call to action that appeared about a page down during scrolling improved conversion on high-traffic diagnosis pages. Placing strong, visible calls to action where users actually are in the page can lift conversion even when users primarily want information.

Terms defined in this episode
Panda algorithm
A Google algorithm update referenced in the episode that targets thin, irrelevant, or low-value content, making it important to remove or improve such content during a site redesign.
301 redirect
A permanent redirect used to point one domain or page to another, discussed in the episode as a way to consolidate an inherited domain with 28,000 links into an existing site.
bounce rate
In Google Analytics, a bounce is recorded when a user visits one page and leaves without a second interaction, even if they completed a desired action like making a phone call.
internal site search logs
Records of what users type into a site's own search function, useful for identifying content gaps, especially queries that return no results.
