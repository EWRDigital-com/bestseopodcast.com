Questions this episode answers

How much traffic goes through the Google Business Profile versus the regular website?

According to the host, roughly 20 to 33 percent of all local traffic flows through the GMB. The exact share shifts with changes like Google Guaranteed Leads, and it varies by industry, with urgent-need industries like legal services relying on maps and paid ads more heavily than organic SEO.

What makes a location page effective for local SEO?

A well-optimized location page should include the business name, address, and phone number in the body of the page, link to the GMB, carry unique copy tailored to that area, use proper headers, include schema markup, and be reachable through normal site navigation to avoid being flagged as a doorway page.

What is a doorway page and why is it a problem?

A doorway page is a page that cannot be reached through normal site navigation, acting as an orphan or hidden page, or one that redirects visitors elsewhere without capturing information. Google can issue a manual penalty for doorway pages, which can seriously damage local search visibility.

Why should you respond to negative reviews professionally?

The host explains that people want to see how a business handles problems, not whether they are perfect. Responding without emotion, as if speaking to a journalist or on the front page of a newspaper, shows potential customers you are trustworthy and willing to resolve issues.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number, the core citation elements Google uses to verify a business identity and distinguish it from other businesses with similar names.
Doorway page
A page that cannot be navigated to from the homepage, acts as an orphan or hidden page, or redirects users without capturing information, which can result in a manual penalty from Google.
Structured data / Schema markup
Code drawn from schema.org that creates signals telling Google what a page is about. Using approved or less common schema types can give a site a ranking advantage by checking signals competitors may not use.
Crawl budget
The amount of crawling Google allocates to a site. Using consistent header hierarchy and adding new content encourages Google to return and index more pages, growing the crawl budget over time.
