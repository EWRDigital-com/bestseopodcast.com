Key takeaways
  • Match form and CTA text to the visitor's original search query to maintain continuity and boost conversions.
  • Limit form fields based on your sales process. Fewer fields drive more submissions but may reduce lead quality.
  • Make phone numbers clickable links so mobile users, over 65% of searchers, can tap to call directly.
  • Display review badges from Yelp, Angie's List, or Google Plus Local to support purchasing decisions.
  • Use the thank you page to upsell, gather info, request social follows, or redirect visitors deeper into your site.

Questions this episode answers

What is SEVO in SEO?

SEVO stands for Search Engine Visitor Optimization. According to this episode, it focuses on optimizing a website to convert visitors who arrive from search engines, addressing elements like forms, CTAs, phone numbers, photos, reviews, unique selling propositions, and the thank you page.

How many form fields should a conversion form have?

It depends on your sales process. If your process is phone-heavy, a short form with just name, email, and phone is sufficient. If you need to qualify leads before calling, add more fields, but be aware that more fields will deter people from filling the form out.

Why should phone numbers on a website be clickable links?

Because over 65% of searches happen on mobile devices. Making a phone number a link lets mobile visitors tap to call immediately without copying and pasting the number into a separate phone app, reducing friction and increasing the chance of conversion.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization. The practice of optimizing a website to convert visitors who arrive from search engines, covering forms, CTAs, phone numbers, photos, social proof, and thank you pages.
Solo-Mo
Social, Local, and Mobile. A content strategy framework where published content is designed to support social sharing, local geographic targeting, and mobile device viewing.
Continuity
The alignment of messaging from a search query through the ad click to the landing page and conversion form, so visitors feel they are in the right place at every step.
