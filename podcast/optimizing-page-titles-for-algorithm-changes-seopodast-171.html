Optimizing Page Titles for Algorithm Changes - #seopodast 171

Episode32 min2012-12-11

Optimizing page titles for algorithm changes requires avoiding keyword stuffing, duplicate titles, and company branding in place of target phrases, while keeping all titles within 60 characters and unique per page.

Key takeaways
  • Avoid three page title mistakes: not having them, branding company name instead of target phrase, and keyword stuffing.
  • The rule of 60 means Google shows only the first 60 characters of a page title in search results.
  • Alt text and page titles remain valid exact match keyword opportunities even as Google targets exact match in anchor text and domains.
  • Set up conversion tracking and goals before running paid search campaigns, not after spending budget.
With all of the algorithm changes and things Google has been doing, page title is still top of the list in regards to things you have to have correct.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common page title mistakes to avoid for SEO?

According to this episode, the three main page title mistakes are: not having a title at all, branding the company name instead of the target phrase, and keyword stuffing. Duplicate page titles across multiple pages are also a problem to avoid.

What is the rule of 60 for page titles?

The rule of 60 means Google only shows the first 60 characters of a page title on the search engine results page before cutting off to the meta description. You should include your target phrase and an offer within those first 60 characters.

How do you set up Google Authorship author tags?

Set up a Google Plus page with a clear profile photo, add your website to the contributor section of your about page, then on your site link the author byline text to your Google Plus profile. On WordPress, the Yoast plugin handles this automatically.

How much has Google not provided keyword data grown?

The hosts note that the original estimate was that not provided would affect only 3 to 5 percent of searches, but an article they read the morning of the episode showed it had reached 39 percent across the board, with their own observations suggesting it could be closer to 50 percent.

Terms defined in this episode
Rule of 60
Google displays only the first 60 characters of a page title on the search results page, so the target phrase and offer should appear within those first 60 characters.
CIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
The hosts' term for optimizing the meta description as a first opportunity to convince a searcher to click through to your site after seeing it in search results.
301 vs 302 redirect
A 301 is a permanent redirect that passes link juice; a 302 is a temporary redirect that does not, so using the wrong one can cause loss of link equity.
Relative author tag
A markup method linking a piece of content to its author's Google Plus profile, causing the author's photo to appear next to the result on the search engine results page.
