Key takeaways
  • Respond to every review, every star rating, every time, to show customers you care.
  • Social media signals account for only about 7.24% of Google ranking factors, so budget accordingly.
  • For B2B businesses, prioritize LinkedIn; for B2C businesses, start with Facebook.
  • Follow the 80-20 rule on social: 80% value and community engagement, 20% selling.
  • Assign social media management only to people who understand brand voice and platform guidelines to avoid costly mistakes.

Questions this episode answers

How much impact does social media have on Google search rankings?

According to a Moz pie chart discussed in the episode, social media factors account for only about 7.24% of Google ranking factors. The hosts warn that if businesses are spending 35 to 45% of their ad budget on social, they may not be getting proportional SEO value from it.

How should small businesses allocate time on social media?

The hosts cite a statistic that 43% of small businesses spend more than six hours a week on social media. They caution that time may not be effectively spent unless businesses first identify the right platform for their target market and commit to genuine community engagement.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
A share, like, and follow combined, used by the hosts as a shorthand for audience engagement actions across social platforms.
Guideline non-compliance
Violating a social media platform's rules, such as Facebook forbidding the use of personal timelines for business promotions, which can result in account bans.
Social media listening
Monitoring social platforms for customer messages and mentions so a business can respond promptly, treated in this episode as a required component of modern customer service.
80-20 rule (social media)
A content balance where 80% of posts bring value or engage the community and only 20% are promotional or sales-oriented.
