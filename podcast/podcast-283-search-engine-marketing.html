Podcast 283 - Search Engine Marketing

Episode31 min2015-08-26

Search engine marketing combines SEO and PPC to target customers at every stage of a complex sales funnel, and CMOs should use SEM for testing, remarketing, branding, and tying online campaigns to offline efforts.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • SEM is the combination of SEO and PPC, used together to actively target keywords and run paid campaigns.
  • The sales funnel varies by device, so mobile and desktop users may have different search intent and conversion paths.
  • Remarketing can be used at every funnel stage, from branding new audiences to re-engaging past buyers with related products.
  • NAP consistency, meaning identical name, address, and phone across all directory listings, directly affects local search rankings.
SEM is a highly controllable and highly customizable channel.

Questions this episode answers

How can remarketing be used beyond following users with ads for products they already viewed?

The episode explains you can use remarketing for branding with display ads showing your logo and color scheme, for promoting new services to existing audiences, and for showing past buyers ads about related products, covering nearly every stage of the sales funnel.

Terms defined in this episode
SEM (Search Engine Marketing)
The combination of SEO and PPC, covering any active effort to target keywords and run paid campaigns through search engines.
Attribution
The process of crediting a sale or conversion to the correct channel or touchpoint, such as a PPC ad, social click, or direct visit, in a multi-step user journey.
Remarketing
Showing targeted ads to users who have previously visited your site, used to nudge purchases, build brand awareness, or promote related products at multiple funnel stages.
NAP Consistency
Keeping a business name, address, and phone number identical across all online directories and citation sources to support accurate local search indexing.
