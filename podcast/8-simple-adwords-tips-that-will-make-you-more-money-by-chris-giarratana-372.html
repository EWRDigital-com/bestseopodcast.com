Questions this episode answers

What are negative keywords in AdWords and why should I use them?

Negative keywords tell Google what your product or service is not, preventing ads from showing on searches that do not match customer intent. For example, a pool repair company should negative out terms like cleaning and maintenance so it does not pay for clicks it cannot convert.

When should I change keyword match types in a PPC campaign?

Start with broad match to collect data quickly, then review after two to three weeks. Use what you learned about negative keywords and high-performing terms to shift to phrase match, tightening the budget while improving relevance. The article suggests reviewing match types every 30 days.

What are ad extensions and which ones should I use in AdWords?

Ad extensions are links or features placed below an ad, such as phone numbers, directions, or menus. Use only the ones relevant to your business. If phone calls are not part of your sales process, skip the call extension and choose one that fits your actual conversion path.

How do I decide whether to run mobile-optimized campaigns in AdWords?

Check Google Analytics under Audience, then Mobile, then Overview. Look at how much traffic comes from mobile, how many pages mobile users view, and how many conversions come from mobile. If mobile traffic and conversions are very low, save the resources. If mobile is your top traffic source, a mobile-optimized campaign is essential.

Terms defined in this episode
Negative keywords
Keywords added to an AdWords campaign to prevent ads from showing on searches that do not match what a business offers, stopping wasted spend on irrelevant clicks.
Keyword match types
Modifiers in AdWords, including broad match, broad match modified, phrase match, and exact match, that control how closely a user's search query must match a keyword before an ad is shown.
Geo-targeting bid adjustment
A setting in AdWords that lets advertisers raise or lower bids for specific geographic locations, allowing higher bids in areas where close rates or customer concentrations are stronger.
