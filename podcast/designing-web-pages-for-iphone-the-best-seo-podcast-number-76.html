Questions this episode answers

What is the most used mobile browser and why should I care for SEO?

Safari Mobile holds 65% of all mobile browsing share according to this episode. Because of that dominance, the hosts recommend optimizing your site specifically for Safari so it displays correctly for the majority of mobile visitors, most of whom are likely on iPhones.

How do mobile search results pages differ from desktop results?

On mobile, there is no right-hand column of ads. The top results shown before scrolling are all sponsored pay-per-click ads, shaded just like on desktop Google. This means a searcher may only see paid ads until they scroll down, which can quickly consume advertiser budgets.

What is re-marketing and how does it work for small businesses?

Re-marketing tracks visitors to your website and then shows them your banner ads across other major sites they visit later, such as news or sports sites. The hosts explain it as a way for a potential client to keep seeing your brand throughout their day after visiting your site once.

Do I need to build a full mobile site to show up properly on an iPhone?

Not necessarily. The hosts describe a short line of code, credited to a contact named Darren, that tells a Safari browser how to properly display your existing site on an iPhone. It is not a separate mobile site but a display optimization for Apple products including iPad, iPod, and iPhone.

Terms defined in this episode
iAd
An Apple advertising platform that lets app developers include advertising code in their apps, allowing ads to appear to users who download or use those apps.
Re-marketing
An ad technique where a user who visits your website is then shown your banner ads on other websites they browse later in the day, across major news, sports, and blog sites.
Mobile SERP
The search engine results page as it appears on a mobile device, which has no right-hand ad column and shows only a small number of sponsored ads above the organic results before the user scrolls.
