Questions this episode answers

Does Google Plus One improve SEO rankings?

According to this episode, Google Plus One has no overall SEO effect. It does affect personalized search results for logged-in users, meaning Google may surface pages that a user's connections have plus-oned, but this benefit applies only to that individual user and not to general rankings.

What is a bounce rate and does time on site affect it?

A bounce is when a visitor lands on a page and leaves from that same page without clicking elsewhere, regardless of how long they stay. Time on site is measured separately, from the first click to the last click on the site. Spending 30 minutes on one page still counts as a bounce.

How can I prevent content scrapers from outranking my original content?

When you publish fresh content, submit your site for reindexing through Webmaster Tools so Google can identify you as the originator. If a scraper gets indexed before you, Google may mistakenly credit them as the original source, which the Panda update was designed to prevent but does not fully stop.

Why should I use Microsoft Ad Center for PPC instead of only Google AdWords?

Ad Center costs less per click than AdWords and a click from Bing is still a highly targeted potential client. The episode recommends maxing out the Ad Center budget first, then managing AdWords. You can build campaigns in AdWords, export them, and import them directly into Ad Center using the same criteria.

Terms defined in this episode
Search Engine Visitor Optimization (CVO)
A term coined by eWebStyle referring to the art of converting site visits into conversions and paying clients, using CTAs, USPs, good graphics, and minimizing clicks to the desired action.
Bounce
A session where a visitor lands on a page and leaves from that same page, counted as a bounce regardless of how much time was spent on that page.
First Link Priority
An SEO concept related to how search engines treat the first link on a page, discussed in the context of WordPress SEO best practices.
301 Redirect
A permanent redirect used when URLs change during a site redesign to preserve existing rankings by pointing old indexed URLs to the correct new URLs.
