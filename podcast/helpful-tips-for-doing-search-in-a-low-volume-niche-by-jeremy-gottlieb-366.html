Helpful Tips for Doing Search in a Low-Volume Niche by Jeremy Gottlieb #366

Ep. 36630 min2017-03-281,259 plays

For low-volume niche SEO, combine strong technical SEO foundations, targeted PPC on money keywords with RLSA remarketing, and conversion rate optimization tuned to a longer B2B sales cycle.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Technical SEO foundations like clean link profiles, internal linking, and NAP consistency matter even when search volume is low.
  • Bid PPC budget on money keywords and use RLSA remarketing to stay visible when organic search volume is thin.
  • Low-volume niches, especially B2B, usually have long sales cycles, so design your funnel around education before conversion.
  • Use softer lead captures like free white papers or newsletter signups rather than immediate contact forms in low-volume markets.
  • Test and measure conversion elements every 30 page views and adjust button color, text, or placement based on data.
when an organization cannot bank on large enough search volume in its particular niche to provide a necessary runway for growth, it needs to think creatively about how to best harmonize organic and paid search channels

Questions this episode answers

What SEO strategies work when your niche has very little search volume?

The episode recommends three approaches: build a solid technical SEO foundation with clean link profiles and strong internal linking, run PPC ads targeting money keywords with RLSA remarketing, and optimize for conversions by designing a funnel that educates visitors over a longer sales cycle before asking for a transaction.

Should you run paid search ads in a low-volume niche?

Yes. The episode says PPC complements SEO in low-volume situations by placing your budget on money keywords and using RLSA to reach people who already visited your site. It also lets you control ad messaging even when you already rank organically.

What is RLSA and why does it matter for low-volume niches?

RLSA stands for remarketing list for search ads. In a low-volume niche, the episode recommends using it so that anyone who has already visited your site can be retargeted, keeping your brand in front of people who may not search again but are still relevant prospects.

How should you handle conversion rate optimization in a low-search-volume B2B market?

The episode advises accepting that a one-visit conversion is unlikely, then building a funnel with softer lead captures like free white papers or newsletters. It also recommends testing and measuring every 30 page views and adjusting elements such as button color, ad text, or image placement based on results.

Terms defined in this episode
RLSA
Remarketing list for search ads, a paid search feature that targets users who have previously visited your website when they later perform searches.
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
The practice of improving a website so that visitors take a desired action, including testing elements like button color, placement, and ad text and measuring results.
Transactional query
A search query that signals the user has a high likelihood of completing a transaction, making it a priority keyword target in low-volume niches.
