How to Recover from 5 Common Google Penalties by Aaron Aglus #352

Ep. 35241 min2016-12-20

Google issues five main manual action penalties for unnatural links, user-generated spam, hacked sites, cloaking or sneaky redirects, and thin content, each requiring specific cleanup steps found in Search Console.

Key takeaways
  • Check Google Search Console immediately if you suspect a manual action penalty, as all penalty details appear there.
  • For unnatural links, contact site owners to request removal first, then submit a disavow file to Google if removal fails.
  • User-generated spam in blog comments must be moderated or deleted, then the site resubmitted to Google to lift the penalty.
  • Thin or duplicate content can trigger a manual penalty, so investing in original quality content is the safest long-term strategy.

Questions this episode answers

How do you recover from an unnatural links penalty from Google?

You must first contact the owners of sites hosting the bad links and request removal. If they do not remove the links, you then send Google a disavow file documenting your attempts. The hosts describe this as a long, tedious, and time-consuming process.

How can I prevent or fix a hacked site Google penalty?

Keep WordPress plugins and the WordPress version updated. Use a server-level login barrier before the WordPress login to block brute force attacks. If hacked, clean the site and watch for spam hacks, malware hacks, and phishing hacks, then alert Google once resolved.

What should I do if my site gets a thin content manual penalty?

Stop publishing duplicate, spun, or scraped content. Invest in original content that is relevant to your industry, includes media like images and video, and provides real value. The hosts say they have never received this penalty because they prioritize content quality.

Terms defined in this episode
Manual Action Penalty
A penalty applied by a human Google reviewer, not an algorithm, when a site is found to have issues like unnatural links, spam, or cloaking. Details appear in Google Search Console.
Disavow File
A file submitted to Google listing links you want Google to ignore, used after you have already attempted to contact site owners to remove unnatural links pointing to your site.
Cloaking
Showing different content to Google than to regular site visitors, which is a violation of Google guidelines and can result in a manual action penalty.
Search Console
Google's tool, formerly called Webmaster Tools, where site owners can see manual action penalty messages, 404 errors, and other site health details.
