Questions this episode answers

What is Google's BERT update and how big is its impact?

BERT is described as the largest change to Google's search system since RankBrain, affecting one in ten queries. It is an additional signal layered on top of RankBrain to help Google better understand natural language, not a replacement for RankBrain.

Do I need to change my SEO strategy because of BERT?

According to the hosts, sites already doing SEO the right way and delivering genuine value to users largely benefited from BERT as poorly optimized competitors were penalized. The advice is to continue doing everything correctly rather than making reactive changes.

How should SEOs use LSI and word-association tools after BERT?

Tools like LSI graph can surface related word associations useful for broader content strategy, but the hosts caution that targeting the specific exact phrase still matters, especially on limited budgets, because Google does not always connect associated terms with enough weight to rank for the core keyword.

Terms defined in this episode
BERT
Google's natural language algorithm update described as the largest change to its search system since RankBrain, impacting one in ten queries and acting as an additional ranking signal.
RankBrain
Google's first artificial intelligence method for understanding queries, introduced almost five years before BERT, focused on matching queries to actual content on the page.
LSI Graph
A tool mentioned for identifying word associations and related terms around a target keyword to inform a broader content strategy.
Anchor Text
The clickable text of a link, described in the episode as important because the word targeted and the associated words surrounding it carry contextual ranking signals.
