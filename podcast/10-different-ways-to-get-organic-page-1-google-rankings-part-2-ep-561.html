Key takeaways
  • Structured data Schema is a recurring best practice across nearly every organic result type covered in the episode.
  • Fully populate and regularly monitor your Google Business Profile to expand your local knowledge panel and enable direct booking actions.
  • Review snippets with star ratings increase click-through rates and can be applied to service pages, though not the homepage.
  • For top stories carousel, use correct structured data markup and maintain proper image sizing: minimum 696 pixels wide for non-AMP pages.
  • Before targeting any result type, understand the opportunity, the competition, and the searcher intent for that specific content.

Questions this episode answers

What are the 10 ways to get organic page one Google rankings covered in this podcast series?

The episode covers plain text organic results, video rich results, carousel rich results, featured snippets, data table snippets, top stories carousel, local business knowledge panel, event rich results, review snippets, and logo in knowledge panel. Structured data Schema is recommended across nearly all of these result types.

How should you optimize for the local business knowledge panel on Google?

Populate your Google Business Profile as completely as possible, monitor your GBP dashboard regularly, respond to reviews and questions, keep all information current, and explore adding direct booking actions such as calendar links so searchers can complete actions without clicking through to your site.

How do review snippets help SEO and how do you get them?

When Google finds proper markup for reviews, it may display star ratings or review excerpts in search results. This increases click-through rates. Review snippets can be applied to service pages but not the homepage. Follow Google's schema instructions for simple reviews, aggregate ratings, or embedded review properties.

What image size does Google recommend for the top stories carousel?

For AMP pages, Google recommends images be a maximum of 1200 pixels wide. For non-AMP pages, Google's article structured data developer page recommends a minimum of 696 pixels wide. Correct structured data markup is also required to appear in the top stories section.

Terms defined in this episode
Data Table Snippet
A Google search result that reads data displayed in HTML tables and shows it as a snippet. Originally developed for news media, it requires proper structured data implementation rather than relying on Google to interpret raw HTML.
Top Stories Carousel
A Google search result displaying headline, source name, and an expanded image, open to blogs and media websites. Proper optimization can enable content to be accessed via Google Assistant voice search.
Aggregate Rating
A schema markup type that combines multiple reviews into a single score, such as 4.5 stars out of a hundred ratings, displayed in search results to increase click-through rates.
Google Business Profile (GBP)
Google's platform for local business listings, described in the episode as functioning like a separate search engine with its own algorithm, integrated with Google universal search, YouTube, and Google Analytics 4.
