Questions this episode answers

What are the biggest ranking factors for Google Business Profile?

According to Brad Wetherall, the top factors are the business name containing relevant keywords, the primary category, and reviews. Review frequency is more important than total volume, and keywords that appear in reviews can also influence what searches trigger your listing.

Can Google Business Profile reviews be recovered after they disappear?

Yes. When Google severs a review algorithmically, it stays tied to the user profile but stops displaying on the business profile. Providing Google support with the reviewer's name or the review text allows them to locate and reattach it to the listing.

Do citations and link building improve Google Business Profile rankings?

Citations and links do not strongly influence GBP rank directly, but they increase Google's data accuracy and trust in your business, which can streamline verification. With AI overviews, the context of what a citation says about your business matters more than having the citation itself.

What happens if a business buys fake Google reviews?

Google can remove all purchased reviews, then block the business profile from collecting any new reviews for 90 days or more. The risk of losing reviews and being locked out of collecting them outweighs any short-term benefit from buying reviews.

Terms defined in this episode
Evolution of Trust (EOT)
An internal Google initiative that built an algorithm to rate how much trust a user has earned through positive edits, photo additions, and reviews, determining whether risky profile changes like address or name updates are accepted or trigger reverification.
MUM (Multi-list Unified Model)
An internal Google system that allows Google to understand and interpret photos and videos, not just text, enabling it to infer business attributes such as a full bar or karaoke from image content alone.
Search Generative Experience (SGE)
The internal name Google used while piloting what became the AI overview, a feature that provides direct answers rather than links to sources.
Brand Mentions
References to a business name in authoritative third-party sources online. Brad Wetherall describes these as becoming the new backlinks as AI allows Google to draw connections without needing an actual hyperlink.
