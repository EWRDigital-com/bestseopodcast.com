Questions this episode answers

What load time should my mobile site target to reduce bounce rates?

The episode cites a target of under five seconds, preferably three seconds. It also states that if load time goes from one second to seven seconds, bounce rates increase 113 percent, making speed the single most repeated theme of the checklist.

Do most purchases still happen on mobile or desktop in 2018?

According to the hosts, more conversions still happen on desktop, particularly for products or services with a longer sales cycle. A client example given is Manning Pool Service, which gets more mobile traffic but more desktop conversions. Purchases requiring more research or commitment still trend toward desktop.

How should PPC ad copy be adjusted for mobile users?

The episode recommends adding phrases like purchase from your smartphone, order from your mobile, or easy mobile leasing to remind users they are on a mobile device. It also advises using site links, call extensions, click-to-call ads, local extensions, and promoted pins, and only running ads when the business is open.

Why is Google My Business so important for local mobile marketing?

The hosts say claiming and optimizing a Google My Business listing should be done before even building a website. It is described as an easy, fast-impact step for brick and mortar businesses, and something surprisingly many long-established businesses have not yet done.

Terms defined in this episode
Micro-moments
Brief moments when a user reaches for their smartphone to find something or distract themselves. The episode states 96 percent of customers do this, and well-timed SMS or content in those moments can dramatically increase conversions.
Shiko
A term used by the hosts meaning a share, a like, and a follow on social media, counted as one unit for their weekly contest.
Accelerated Mobile Pages (AMP)
A framework for fast-loading mobile pages. The episode notes the founder of Search Engine Journal has seen many businesses invest and lose money trying to implement AMP pages, and questions its broad value.
Smart Bidding
Google's automated bidding tool mentioned in the checklist. The hosts describe it as letting Google bid for you, and suggest caution before handing over that control.
