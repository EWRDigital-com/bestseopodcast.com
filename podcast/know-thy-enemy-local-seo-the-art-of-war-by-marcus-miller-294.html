Know Thy Enemy: Local SEO & The Art of War by Marcus Miller #294

Ep. 29425 min2015-10-16

Local SEO success requires a defined strategy, targeted tactics using tools like Google My Business, battlefield awareness of the SERP, competitive analysis, measurement, and marketing that feels natural to the audience.

Key takeaways
  • Your local SEO strategy should complement your sales funnel and be understood by every team member.
  • Tactics require familiarity with tools like Google My Business, Bing Places, Yelp, and City Search before deploying them.
  • Know your battlefield: understand how users interact with each platform before running campaigns there.
  • Competitive analysis reveals what rivals do well and where gaps exist that you can fill.
  • Measure everything, A/B test landing pages, AdWords, keywords, colors, and buttons, then rinse and repeat what works.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five local SEO lessons from the Art of War article discussed in this episode?

The episode covers five points from Marcus Miller's Search Engine Land article: define your strategy, choose the right tactics and tools, understand your battlefield (the SERP or platform), know your competition's strengths and weaknesses, and measure results constantly to iterate and improve.

Why is knowing your battlefield important for local SEO?

Each platform has different user behavior. The hosts warn against asking people to subscribe on Twitter or pin you on Instagram. For local SEO, the battlefield is the search engine results page, and marketers must understand shifts like the move from a seven-pack to a three-pack.

How should a business approach competitive analysis in local SEO?

The hosts recommend analyzing five or six competitors to see how social they are, how their sites rank, who links to them, and what offers they make. If most competitors offer free shipping, you need to match or beat that offer to stay competitive.

Terms defined in this episode
Google My Business
A free Google tool for managing a local business listing, cited in this episode as a key tactic tool for local SEO alongside Bing Places, Yelp, and City Search.
Three-pack
The condensed local search results format Google shifted to from the seven-pack, showing only three local business listings on the search engine results page.
Hacked spam algorithm
A new Google algorithm change discussed in this episode that aggressively targets hacked content appearing in search results, estimated to impact 5% of queries.
Real-time Penguin
A forthcoming version of Google's Penguin algorithm that processes link removals and disavowals immediately rather than waiting for a scheduled refresh.
