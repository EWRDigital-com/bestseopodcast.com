Key takeaways
  • Google beacons use Bluetooth Low Energy to pinpoint a phone's location far more precisely than GPS or cell signal alone.
  • Businesses can configure a beacon to push links like review requests or promotional offers to phones in range.
  • Setting up a Google beacon takes only about two questions, making the process very quick.
  • B2B businesses with a physical office should primarily use the beacon to prompt visiting clients to leave a review.
  • Technology adoption lags, so businesses not yet using beacons are not being left behind just yet.

Questions this episode answers

What is a Google beacon and how does it work?

A Google beacon is a Bluetooth Low Energy device that transmits data to nearby phones, letting them determine a precise physical location. Google calls the underlying technology Eddystone. Once set up, the beacon can push links such as review requests or promotional offers to phones within range.

Can a Google beacon help with SEO or Google My Business rankings?

The hosts believe a beacon could help because it gives Google more accurate, verifiable foot-traffic data at a physical location. They note that no one was outright claiming a direct SEO ranking benefit, but more trustworthy location data could factor into Google's algorithm.

What are the best uses of a Google beacon for a small business?

For B2B businesses, the hosts recommend using a beacon primarily to prompt visitors to leave a review. Broader uses include pushing promotional offers, displaying product information when a customer stands near a specific item, and improving location conversion tracking in AdWords.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Beacon
A Bluetooth Low Energy device sent by Google that broadcasts data to nearby phones, enabling precise indoor location tracking and the ability to push links or offers to visitors at a physical business location.
Eddystone
Google's name for its beacon technology, referencing both an eddy current in a river and a lighthouse in the UK, reflecting the technology's function of identifying where you are.
BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy)
The wireless protocol used by Google beacons to transmit data to nearby phones while consuming very little power, allowing the device to last for years.
iBeacon
Apple's earlier beacon technology that works only on iPhones and has seen limited adoption outside Apple stores and major baseball stadiums.
