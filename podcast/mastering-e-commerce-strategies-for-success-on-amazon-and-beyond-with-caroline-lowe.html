Questions this episode answers

What fees do I need to account for when selling on Amazon?

Sellers face a 15% referral fee just for being on the platform, plus fulfillment fees that can reach around $5 per unit on a $30 product, plus ad spend of 20 to 25% in competitive categories like supplements. Combined with cost of goods, margins can disappear quickly on low-price items.

What conversion rate should I target on Amazon?

According to Caroline Lowe, 10% to 15% is the minimum target on Amazon for non-consumable products. A 2% to 3% rate is considered acceptable on a Shopify site. One beauty brand she worked with improved from 4% to 12% simply by redesigning product page images and copy.

What is Levanta and how does it help Amazon sellers?

Levanta is an affiliate platform built specifically for Amazon. It connects brands with influencers, and when an influencer drives a sale, the seller pays the affiliate roughly 10% while Amazon reduces its referral fee by 10%, making the cost roughly equal to paying a few hundred dollars monthly for the platform.

Should I focus on Amazon ads or organic optimization first?

Caroline Lowe recommends optimizing your organic product page before running ads. There are only two levers for more sales on Amazon: more sessions and higher conversion. Improving product images, copy, and backend SEO can lift both without additional ad spend.

Terms defined in this episode
FBA (Fulfilled by Amazon)
Amazon's fulfillment service where sellers send inventory to Amazon warehouses and Amazon handles shipping, with fees charged per unit based on size and weight.
Referral Fee
A 15% fee Amazon charges sellers for every sale made on its platform, regardless of whether the seller uses Amazon fulfillment or ships the product themselves.
DSP (Demand Side Platform)
Amazon's programmatic advertising tool that allows brands to run display ads on Amazon properties such as Twitch and Fire, as well as off-Amazon placements, retargeting shoppers who visited a product page.
Merchant Fulfilled
A fulfillment method where the seller ships products directly to customers rather than using Amazon warehouses, useful when Amazon receiving times are slow or stock runs low.
