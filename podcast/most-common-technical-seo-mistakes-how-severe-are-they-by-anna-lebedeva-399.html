Most Common Technical SEO Mistakes: How Severe Are They? by Anna Lebedeva #399

Ep. 39926 min2017-12-11

Anna Lebedeva's SEMrush study of 100,000 websites and 450,000,000 pages identifies broken internal links, 4XX errors, and misconfigured www domains as the most damaging and common technical SEO mistakes.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • SEMrush analyzed 100,000 websites and 450,000,000 pages to find the most common technical SEO errors.
  • 30% of websites have broken internal links, classified as harmful issues requiring immediate attention.
  • About 26.5% of sites had 4XX errors, with the 404 file not found error being most common.
  • 17% of sites had the www domain configured incorrectly, which can make a site invisible to users who type www.
  • Technical SEO has three layers: crawlability and site structure, on-page SEO, and technical SEO.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common technical SEO mistakes according to the SEMrush study?

The study of 100,000 websites and 450,000,000 pages found that 30% of sites have broken internal links, about 26.5% have 4XX errors with 404 being most common, and 17% have the www domain configured incorrectly. These are classified as harmful issues.

How severe are broken internal links for SEO?

Broken internal links are classified as harmful issues by the article. 30% of websites have them, and fixing basic crawlability errors like these is compared to showing up for 90% of a sale, forming a foundation before other SEO improvements can help.

Why does an incorrectly configured www domain matter for SEO and users?

If a user types www before a domain and the site fails to load, that visitor is lost. 17% of websites in the study had this misconfiguration. The hosts note that some site owners dismiss it, but any user who hits a broken www version may never return.

Terms defined in this episode
4XX errors
HTTP error codes indicating a client-side problem; the most common is the 404 file not found error, which signals a broken or missing page.
Crawlability
The ability of search engine bots to access and index a website's pages, forming the foundational layer of SEO alongside site structure.
www domain misconfiguration
A setup error where the www or non-www version of a domain fails to load, affecting 17% of websites in the SEMrush study.
