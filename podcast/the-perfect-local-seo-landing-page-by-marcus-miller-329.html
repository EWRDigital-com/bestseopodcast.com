The Perfect Local SEO Landing Page by Marcus Miller #329

Ep. 32929 min2016-07-14

A perfect local SEO landing page combines city-targeted on-page signals, schema-marked NAP, embedded maps, strong calls to action, social proof, and humanized team photos to both rank and convert local visitors.

Key takeaways
  • Include city in page titles and H1s and H2s for local ranking, but state inclusion is often unnecessary.
  • Mark up your name, address, and phone with schema so Google and directories can identify business details confidently.
  • Embed a Google Maps location on every contact page so mobile users can tap for directions.
  • Add substance to calls to action, such as a free quote or 10% off, to increase conversions.
  • Use real team photos and social proof like review widgets to humanize landing pages and lower visitor defenses.

About this episode

This week Chris and Chuck talk about an article by Marcus Miller: "The perfect local SEO landing page", posted over at Search Engine Land. In this article Marcus Miller shares tips, advice and an infographic on creating highly optimized, high-converting landing pages for local SEO.

Questions this episode answers

Should I include the state in a local SEO landing page title?

The hosts say city in the title is important but state is questionable and often unnecessary. They prefer a format like target term plus city, for example AC repair Houston. State can go in the footer or meta description, and is mainly useful when targeting statewide traffic or ambiguous city names.

What local SEO elements should a perfect local landing page include?

According to the article by Marcus Miller discussed on the episode, key elements include city and state in the title and headers, an HTML name address and phone marked up with schema, an embedded location map with driving directions, a clear call to action, social proof such as reviews, and team photos to humanize the page.

Why is schema markup important for a local business landing page?

Schema lets Google and all search engines clearly identify a business name, address, and phone. The hosts also note that directories which import data from sources like InfoUSA use schema markup to determine correct business details, so marking it up prevents errors across the web.

How should a call to action on a local landing page be written?

The hosts say a CTA needs substance beyond just the word call. Examples they give include call now for a free consultation, call now for a free quote, call now and receive 10% off, or fill out this form and get contacted back in five minutes. Vague CTAs do not motivate action.

Terms defined in this episode
Schema markup
Code added to HTML that lets Google and other search engines clearly identify structured information like a business name, address, and phone number, and is also used by directories to determine correct business details.
NAP
Name, address, and phone number of a business, which must be consistent across all platforms and ideally marked up with schema so it can be indexed and displayed correctly.
Social proof
Reviews, likes, shares, video testimonials, and other signals from real users that prove to visitors a business can do what it claims, used on landing pages to increase conversions.
CTA (Call to Action)
A directive on a landing page telling visitors what to do next, which should include specific substance such as a free quote or discount to encourage user response.
