Questions this episode answers

What is a Google crawl budget and why does it matter for enterprise ecommerce?

Google allocates a crawl budget based on a site's authority to find and process pages. For large ecommerce sites, only a portion of pages may be crawled, so architecture must prioritize the most relevant and valuable pages. Robots.txt files can block low-value pages from wasting that budget.

Should ecommerce product pages be optimized manually or automatically?

Core landing pages, category pages, and highly competitive pages should be optimized manually, including title tags, meta descriptions, and alt tags. Less competitive pages can use automated templates. Using manufacturer descriptions as-is creates duplicate content and provides no ranking advantage.

What free tools can help audit technical SEO on a large ecommerce site?

The episode recommends Screaming Frog, which has a free version covering up to 500 pages, and Google Chrome's Lighthouse tool, accessible by right-clicking a page and selecting Inspect then Lighthouse. Both help identify broken links, code issues, and performance problems.

How do internal redirects affect ecommerce site speed?

Each redirect is theorized to cause roughly a 30% drop in link juice passed to the original link, and a large number of internal redirects can severely slow page load times. One site mentioned in the episode dropped from 12 to 15 seconds load time down to 3 to 4 seconds after cleaning up redirects and removing excess plugins.

Terms defined in this episode
Information Architecture
The structure of a website that organizes content so both search engines and users can find it, turning website complexity into a comprehensible, navigable structure.
Crawl Budget
The number of pages Google will find and process on a site based on that site's authority, making it critical to ensure the most valuable pages are prioritized for crawling.
Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
The practice of improving how users interact with a website, including dwell time, calls to action, and navigation, to increase the rate at which visitors take desired actions.
Findability
The ability of both search bots and end users to locate content on a website; ordered, well-structured information ranks higher due to better availability and discoverability.
