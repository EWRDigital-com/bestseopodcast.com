How to Conduct a Complete Google Ads Audit By Corey Morris #379

Ep. 37944 min2017-06-28

A Google Ads audit has seven steps: review goals, account structure, campaign settings, ad groups, keywords, ads, and landing pages, ensuring every element works together to drive conversions and avoid wasted spend.

Key takeaways
  • Verify that conversion goals are correctly configured in both Analytics and AdWords before reviewing performance data.
  • Structure campaigns by location, service type, or audience segment so you can adjust budgets and bids at a granular level.
  • Each ad group should contain multiple ads rotated equally so you can identify which creative converts best.
  • Use negative keywords to stop ads from showing for irrelevant searches like wrong product categories or unrelated brands.
  • Landing pages must carry consistent wording and clear calls to action from the ad to maximize conversion rates.

Questions this episode answers

What are the seven steps of a complete Google Ads audit?

According to Corey Morris as discussed in this episode, the seven steps are: review your goals, review your account structure, review account and campaign settings, audit ad groups, audit keywords, audit the ads themselves, and evaluate the landing page experience and then create an action plan from your findings.

Why is account structure important in a Google Ads audit?

Good account structure gives you maximum control over bids, budgets, ads, and overall management. Organizing campaigns by location, service type, or target audience lets you quickly turn spend up or down in specific areas and spot which segments convert best.

How should you use negative keywords during a Google Ads audit?

Any word or phrase you do not want to trigger your ads should be added as a negative keyword. For example, a residential window repair company should add terms like car, windshield, and cell phone glass to avoid irrelevant clicks that waste budget.

What should you check on landing pages during a Google Ads audit?

Check for a strong headline and clear call to action, confirm that forms work, verify a dedicated call tracking number is in place, and ensure the page wording matches the ad copy. This continuity between ad and landing page supports higher conversion rates.

Terms defined in this episode
Negative keyword
A word or phrase added to a campaign to prevent ads from showing when that term appears in a search query, stopping irrelevant clicks and wasted budget.
Day parting
An older industry term for ad scheduling, meaning setting specific times of day or days of the week when your ads are allowed to run.
Ad rotation
A campaign setting that controls how often each ad in an ad group is shown, set to even rotation so all ads receive equal impressions for fair testing.
