Key takeaways
  • Moving from position three to position one in Google search results produces a 200% increase in traffic.
  • Google unindexed 76 out of 80 blog posts for one client because the posts were not helpful or useful.
  • Every piece of content should go through the same editorial process, with at least three sets of eyes before publishing.
  • Regularly updating evergreen content with new paragraphs causes Google to re-index it and can push it back to the top.
  • Guest blogging on trusted industry sites builds backlinks and lends your brand authority and credibility in readers' eyes.

About this episode

5 Tips To Improve Your Content Strategy, Sponsored by SE Ranking Content plays an important role in every marketing campaign. Here are some ways you can improve your content strategy and get better results.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five tips to improve an enterprise content strategy?

The five tips from the episode are: think like a publisher, make sure everything fits into your sales funnel, refine and document your editorial process, review and refresh or replace existing content, and use your network to build backlinks and relationships with industry sites and influencers.

How important is it to reach the number one position in Google versus just the first page?

According to the episode, reaching page one is just the beginning. Moving from position three to position one alone produces a 200% increase in traffic. The hosts stress that being in the top positions, not just on the first page, is where real traffic and ROI are generated.

How should you handle old or underperforming blog content?

The hosts recommend reviewing existing content and either refreshing it by adding new paragraphs to trigger re-indexing, combining low-traffic posts into a stronger single post, or replacing content that no longer serves a purpose. Evergreen content should keep being promoted across social media channels.

What is the difference between Google Search Console and Google Analytics?

As explained in the episode, Search Console tracks all traffic coming to your URL directly from Google, including any manual penalties, while Analytics fires a pixel and tracks what users do once they are on your site. Both tools together give a fuller picture of site performance.

Terms defined in this episode
SERP Analyzer
A tool in SE Ranking that lets you compare all 10 pages on the first search result page across factors like content, keyword density, page experience, backlinks, domain age, and internal and external linking.
Evergreen content
Content designed to have a long shelf life that continues to generate traffic, clicks, and interaction for months or even years after it is published.
Content Management System (CMS)
Technology platforms such as WordPress, Shopify, and Squarespace that allow you to manage content across channels without writing a single line of code.
Bowtie strategy
A linking approach where authoritative sites in your industry link to you and you link back to them, creating mutual association and cross-sharing of quality content.
