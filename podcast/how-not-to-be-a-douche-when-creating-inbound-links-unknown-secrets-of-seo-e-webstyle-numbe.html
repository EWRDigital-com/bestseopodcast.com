How not to be a douche when creating inbound links - Unknown Secrets of SEO E-Webstyle Number 60

Episode32 min2010-07-07

Avoid douchey link building by keeping keyword density between one and three percent, writing genuinely useful link-bait content, and sending personalized outreach emails instead of mass link exchange requests.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Keep keyword density between one and three percent to avoid stuffing penalties and user distrust.
  • Write link-bait content that others naturally want to reference, rather than relying solely on outreach emails.
  • Personalize link exchange emails by explaining specific value and relevance to the recipient's site.
  • Use internal links sparingly, one or two per paragraph, with proper anchor text.
  • Get links from credible, high-quality websites and avoid exchanging links with direct competitors.

Questions this episode answers

What is the recommended keyword density to avoid over-optimization?

Chris Burris and Paul Hanson say the SEO industry standard for keyword density is one to three percent. For a hundred-word page that means one to three uses of a given keyword. Going beyond that risks looking like stuffing to both search engine robots and real visitors.

Is it okay to use a business name instead of a real name when leaving blog comments for SEO?

The hosts disagree with the article's advice on this point. They argue that using a descriptive business name as your comment identity is not douchey because it provides relevant anchor text linking back to your site, and branding a business name can have legitimate value.

How should you approach link exchange outreach emails without looking spammy?

The hosts say you should personalize each email, explain why the link relationship makes sense for both parties, and show that you spent real time on the recipient's site. A phone call may even be worth it given how valuable links are. Mass carbon-copy emails are what make you look like a douche.

What is link baiting and why does it matter for link building?

Link baiting means creating content so useful or entertaining that other sites naturally want to link to it. The hosts cite their own discussion of Chris Spooner's article as an example, since they plan to link back to it, showing that quality content can earn links without any outreach at all.

Terms defined in this episode
CIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A phrase coined by eWebStyle meaning what you do with a visitor after they arrive from a search engine, focusing on user experience beyond just ranking.
Keyword Density
The percentage of times a target keyword appears relative to total words on a page. The hosts cite an industry standard of one to three percent.
Link Baiting
Creating high-quality or entertaining content specifically so that other websites are motivated to link to it organically, without direct outreach.
SERP (Search Engine Results Page)
The page of results returned by a search engine. The hosts note that CIVO begins at the SERP, where title tags and description tags are first seen by users.
