Top 12 Tips From Someone Who Has Been Working from Home for 12 Years by Ron Lieback #482

Ep. 48235 min2020-04-28

Working from home productively requires daily routines, avoiding multitasking, blocking time for specific projects, and treating email and social media as scheduled tasks rather than constant interruptions, based on 12 years of remote work experience.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Plan your week in advance and identify three big priority tasks each day or week to move work forward.
  • Multitasking causes lost productivity; finishing one project before switching is faster than hopping between tasks.
  • Check email at set times only, such as noon and 5pm, to protect deep work time.
  • Block specific time segments for calls, email, and breaks rather than letting the day run unstructured.
  • Treat social media like email: consume it at set times unless it is a direct part of your job.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most important tips for working from home productively?

According to the article discussed in this episode, the top tips include setting a daily routine by planning your week, avoiding multitasking, blocking time for specific projects, and limiting email to scheduled check-ins. The hosts also stress eating the frog first, meaning tackling the task you keep putting off before anything else.

How often should you check email when working from home?

The episode recommends checking email only twice on creative days, once around noon and once around 5pm, spending about 15 minutes each time. The hosts argue email is passive communication, and anyone with an urgent need will call, text, or use chat instead.

Does multitasking help or hurt remote work productivity?

The episode is clear that multitasking hurts productivity. Studies cited in the discussion show that switching between projects causes lost time that exceeds the brief transition itself. The hosts recommend working on one prioritized task at a time and letting others know you will follow up.

How should you handle social media distractions when working from home?

The episode suggests treating social media like email and consuming it only at set times unless your job requires it. One host notes he checks it in the morning and at night and otherwise only engages for business purposes, comparing healthy social media use to anything good in a healthy dose.

Terms defined in this episode
Eat the frog
A productivity concept discussed in the episode meaning you tackle the task you have been repeatedly avoiding or the most important task first, before moving on to smaller work.
Time blocking
Scheduling specific windows of time for distinct tasks such as email, calls, or creative work, as recommended in the 12 tips article, to prevent tasks from bleeding into each other.
Batch checking email
Reviewing and responding to email only at predetermined times, such as noon and 5pm, rather than monitoring it continuously throughout the workday.
