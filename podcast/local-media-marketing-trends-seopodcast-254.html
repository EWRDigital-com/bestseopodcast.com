Questions this episode answers

How can I tell if my website has been penalized by Google?

Check your Google Webmaster Tools messages for a manual penalty notification. If there are no messages but traffic has dropped, you may have an algorithmic penalty. Match the timing of your traffic decrease to known algorithm updates like Panda or Penguin to identify the likely cause.

What local media marketing trends should businesses prepare for in 2015?

The ten trends covered include growing mobile usage, mobile payments, connecting online and offline worlds, location-based targeting, wearable devices, social media influencing purchases, digital personal assistants for local search, cross-device integration, operational services like Eventbrite, and crowdsourcing platforms like Airbnb and Uber.

Why is conversational content important for local SEO?

Digital personal assistants and voice search users phrase queries the way they speak, such as asking for a specific restaurant near a highway. Writing content that mirrors natural spoken language makes a site more compatible with those voice search queries and improves the chance of appearing in results.

What is location-based ad targeting and how can businesses use it?

Location-based targeting lets advertisers show different ads based on a user's geographic area or demographic data. For example, a car dealership can show luxury vehicle ads in higher-income zip codes and budget vehicle ads in lower-income ones, using tools already available in Google AdWords, Facebook ads, and Bing AdCenter.

Terms defined in this episode
Algorithmic penalty
A drop in site traffic caused by a Google algorithm update, such as Panda or Penguin, rather than a manual action. It does not appear as a message in Webmaster Tools.
Manual penalty
A Google penalty applied by a human reviewer due to issues like thin content or bad links, with a notification visible inside Webmaster Tools messages.
Location-based targeting
An ad strategy that customizes what ads users see based on their geographic location, demographic data, or proximity to a business, available in Google AdWords, Facebook ads, and Bing AdCenter.
Conversational content
Website content written to match the natural spoken phrases people use in voice searches, such as asking for a specific type of restaurant near a landmark rather than typing short keyword strings.
