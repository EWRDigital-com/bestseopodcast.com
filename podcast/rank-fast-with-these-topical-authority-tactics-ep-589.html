Rank Fast with these Topical Authority Tactics Ep.589

Ep. 58936 min2023-12-076,356 plays

Topical authority lets sites rank faster than the standard 60-day re-ranking cycle by signaling semantic relevance through content clusters, internal linking, and regularly updated content.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Topical authority sites can get indexed and rank within hours of publishing, rather than waiting for the roughly 60-day full re-ranking cycle.
  • Updating old content on an already-authoritative site can produce fast ranking gains through the caffeine algorithm.
  • Avoid linking to or from bad neighborhoods, including sites that buy or trade links, because that link juice gets dampened.
  • Google is unindexing thin or low-quality pages aggressively, so building subtopics and keeping pages in the index through internal linking is critical.

Questions this episode answers

How long does it take to rank a site with topical authority?

According to this episode, Google re-ranks all data roughly every 60 days, but a site that is already a topical authority can get a new article indexed and ranked within hours of publishing. The host says he runs three-month campaigns to allow time for re-indexing and to see strong ranking movement.

What is a content cluster and why does it matter for SEO?

A content cluster is a group of articles built around a central topic and its subtopics, all connected through internal links. The episode explains that this semantic structure helps Google understand what a site is about, pushes link equity across pages, and keeps pages in the index, which is critical because Google is actively unindexing thin content.

Should you buy links to build topical authority?

The host strongly discourages buying links, explaining that paid link schemes create bad neighborhood associations. He says link juice from these sources gets dampened and can carry penalties, so the better approach is producing quality content and earning links through outreach after establishing authority.

Which SEO tools does the host recommend for measuring topical authority?

The host says tools like Moz, Ahrefs, SEMrush, SeRanking, and SpyFu are indicators rather than accurate measures because they use their own internal algorithms. He prefers Google Search Console for understanding what the search engine is actually doing, and recommends free options like Google-related searches and Google Keyword Planner.

Terms defined in this episode
Topical Authority
A site's demonstrated expertise within a specific subject area, built through content clusters and internal linking, which allows it to rank quickly for related queries.
Caffeine Update
A Google update designed to improve the speed and accuracy of search results; it allows topical authority sites to rank new or refreshed content very quickly between the roughly 60-day full re-ranking cycles.
Good Neighborhoods vs Bad Neighborhoods
A general SEO concept where sites linking to or from spammy or paid-link networks are in bad neighborhoods, which dampens link juice and can carry ranking penalties.
Hub Page
A resource-style page that links out to many related pages or sites; the host describes it as generating a special kind of linking power and encouraging link equity to flow back to the site.
