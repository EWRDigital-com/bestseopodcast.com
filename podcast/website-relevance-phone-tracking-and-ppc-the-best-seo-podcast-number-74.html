Questions this episode answers

How does landing page SEO affect PPC costs?

Google factors in the relevancy of the target website when pricing PPC bids. A well-optimized page that matches the keywords you are bidding on earns a better score, which can lower the price you pay per click compared to a page that is not optimized for those terms.

What is click-to-call tracking and why does it matter for PPC?

Click-to-call tracking links a specific PPC click to an actual inbound phone call. This tells you which ad positions and keywords are generating real calls, not just clicks, so you can decide whether paying more for a higher position is worth it based on call volume data.

Should a small business hire a professional to run PPC campaigns?

The hosts say yes. Professionals have already made the costly learning mistakes, can set up phone tracking, run A and B ad tests, and optimize bids across Google, Bing, and Yahoo. A business owner's time is better spent on their core work than managing campaign details.

Why should PPC ads and landing pages share the same keyword?

Continuity between the ad and the landing page keeps visitors from bouncing. If someone types in a keyword and clicks an ad, they expect to see that same keyword on the page they land on. Mismatched pages lose the visitor immediately.

Terms defined in this episode
Click-to-call ratio
A metric that measures how many people called a business after clicking a PPC ad, showing whether ad clicks are producing actual phone inquiries.
Landing page relevance
Google's measure of how closely a PPC target page matches the keywords being bid on, which can lower the cost per click for well-optimized pages.
A and B testing
Running two or more ad variations for the same keyword to determine which ad copy produces the highest click-through and conversion rates.
SEVO
Search engine visitor optimization, described in this episode as ensuring good calls to action for visitors who arrive from search or PPC traffic.
