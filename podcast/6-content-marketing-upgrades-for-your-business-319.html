6 Content Marketing Upgrades for Your Business #319

Ep. 31926 min2016-04-29

Six content marketing upgrades from Search Engine Journal include scheduling blog time like a client, building topical expertise, partnering with complementary brands, avoiding gated content for weak material, using paid promotions, and tracking engagement data to refine strategy.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Schedule dedicated time for research, drafting, editing, posting, and sharing content, just as you would for a client.
  • Avoid gating content unless it is genuinely exclusive and high quality, or you risk bounces and lost trust.
  • Partner with complementary businesses that share your client base without competing directly for the same work.
  • Use paid social promotions for new brands or content because organic reach on Twitter and Facebook is easily missed.
  • Track topics, formats, and channels by engagement metrics, then blend what works to find your content sweet spot.

Questions this episode answers

Why should I use paid promotions for content marketing?

Organic posts on Facebook and Twitter are easily missed, especially for new brands. Paid placements like boosted posts, Facebook ads, sponsored Instagram ads, and sponsored tweets guarantee visibility. Google drove 95% of US smartphone paid search clicks in the first quarter, showing how much paid placement matters.

How do I know which content is working?

Track which topics generated the most views, shares, or comments, which formats produced the longest page view time and lowest bounce rate, and which marketing channels generated the most leads. Then use that data to blend successful elements and craft content better suited to your audience.

Should a business blog cover one narrow topic or multiple areas of expertise?

It depends on your industry. A master plumber, for example, should be an expert in water heaters, drainage systems, faucets, and all related services, not just one. Do the research, get certified, and become an expert across all relevant areas because your clients expect it.

Terms defined in this episode
Gated content
Content that requires a visitor to submit information such as an email address or phone number, or complete a social action, before gaining access to it.
Paid promotions
Paid placements on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, or Twitter, such as boosted posts, sponsored ads, or sponsored tweets, used to guarantee content visibility.
Content curation
The ongoing process of selecting, creating, and managing high quality content, paired with tracking conversions to realize the full benefits of a content strategy.
