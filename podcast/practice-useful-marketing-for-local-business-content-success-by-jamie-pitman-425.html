About this episode

Practice useful marketing for local business content successAs a small business owner, you fight big brands and a ranking system that favors them. How can you compete? Contributor Jamie Pitman shares 3 tactics to take on the competition by using smart and useful content.

Questions this episode answers

Should local businesses have a blog for SEO?

According to Jamie Pitman of Bright Local, 68% of SEOs believe local businesses still need a blog in 2018, but the traditional blog may be better replaced by useful resources, how-to guides, and videos that earn links and engagement more effectively than posts about a new hire or new oven.

What content works best for local business SEO?

Jamie Pitman recommends three tactics: useful local resources such as event calendars and family activity lists that attract backlinks, how-to guides formatted with structured markup for featured snippets, and short informative videos that keep viewers on YouTube to improve watch time rankings.

How does YouTube watch time actually work for ranking?

According to the episode, watch time on YouTube is not the total time people spend watching your video. It is the average length of time a viewer stays on YouTube after watching, so creating playlists and series of videos keeps viewers on the platform longer and improves your rankings.

How can small local businesses compete with big brands on content?

Small businesses can move faster than large competitors. While a big brand is planning video production, a local business can film a useful video on a phone and publish it immediately. They can also add articles and videos to Google My Business listings, which many larger agencies managing local competitors are not yet doing.

Terms defined in this episode
Watch time
On YouTube, watch time is the average length of time a viewer stays on YouTube after watching your video, not just how long they watch the video itself, and it is a key ranking factor.
Featured snippet
A search result format that mostly answers questions directly on the results page. How-to guides formatted with structured markup improve eligibility for featured snippets and voice search.
Linkable asset
Content designed to attract backlinks, such as a calendar of local events or a list of family activities, which earns links even when not 100% relevant to a business niche.
Hyper-local SEO
An SEO approach focused on geo-specific content and community signals, such as links from local organizations, to establish relevance and credibility in a specific geographic area.
