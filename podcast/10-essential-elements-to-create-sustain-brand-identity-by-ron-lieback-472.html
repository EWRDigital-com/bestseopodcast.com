10 Essential Elements to Create & Sustain Brand Identity by Ron Lieback #472

Ep. 47232 min2020-01-27

Strong brand identity requires a clear target audience, a genuine unique selling proposition, and consistent messaging across every channel, as outlined in Ron Lieback's Search Engine Journal article on 10 must-have brand identity elements.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Know your target audience deeply, including their goals, frustrations, and decision-making processes, to speak their language.
  • A unique selling proposition must go beyond vague claims like great customer service and name a specific, provable differentiator.
  • Core values should reflect the culture already present in a company, not aspirational language invented for a wall poster.
  • Consistency across every channel, from Facebook to paid ads to office environment, is essential to avoid a dysfunctional brand experience.
  • Authenticity online matters because inconsistency between your digital presence and reality will drive potential customers away.
Jeff Bezos says that branding is what people say about you when you're not in the room.

Questions this episode answers

What is the first must-have element for developing a brand identity according to this episode?

The first element is finding and focusing on your target audience. This means digging deep into their passions, goals, frustrations, and decision-making processes so you can speak to them in their own language, whether for your brand or your customers' brands.

Why is customer service not a strong unique selling proposition on its own?

Because no competitor markets on mediocre customer service, so claiming great customer service is already expected. To make it a real USP you must niche it down with specifics, such as 24-hour on-call service or a US-based phone team, and then prove it with reviews or video testimonials.

How should a company define its core values?

According to the hosts, core values should be based on the culture that already exists inside the company. You identify what has held the team together, expand on what you like, and trim what you do not, rather than writing aspirational values that do not reflect actual behavior.

What does consistency across every channel mean for brand identity?

It means your messaging on Facebook, Instagram, TikTok, blogs, guest posts, website copy, PR, newsletters, videos, and paid advertising must all align. If your website looks polished but your physical office does not match, it creates a dysfunctional experience that pushes customers away.

Terms defined in this episode
Unique Selling Proposition (USP)
What makes your business different from competitors and gives customers a specific reason to choose you, requiring a concrete, provable differentiator rather than a broad claim like great customer service.
Blue Ocean Strategy
A book referenced in the episode as a resource for businesses struggling to define their USP by creating their own market rather than competing in an existing one.
Brand Identity
The combination of target audience focus, unique selling proposition, core values, and consistent cross-channel messaging that determines what people say about your business when you are not in the room.
Customer Avatar
A detailed target persona built by understanding what drives a specific customer segment, used to guide brand and marketing decisions.
