Questions this episode answers

What is B2G marketing and how does it work?

B2G stands for business to government marketing. It involves managing relationships with government officials, crafting targeted digital ad campaigns around local economic impact, and combining in-person lobbying with social media messaging to win contracts, pass legislation, or avoid harmful regulation.

Why did crypto marketing fail to win over government officials?

According to Sam Armes, crypto campaigns spent heavily on celebrity ads and flashy imagery that appealed to an audience they already had. They never produced polling data on jobs created per state or relatable ads featuring average Americans, so politicians did not see a constituency worth protecting.

How do you craft a B2G digital ad campaign for a new technology?

Armes says you first identify who is in power in your target state, then build ads around issues that audience cares about. In a blue state you might show how the technology helps immigrants; in a red state you might feature a pastor whose congregation benefited, matching the message to the political makeup of the market.

Terms defined in this episode
B2G marketing
Business to government marketing, which involves selling products, services, or ideas to government entities through relationship management, lobbying, and targeted digital campaigns.
Lobbyist
A marketer to government who manages relationships with officials, tracks legislation, and advocates for a client's interests; the term comes from people who literally sat in the lobbies of Congress waiting to speak with members.
Political ad qualification
The identity verification process required by platforms like Facebook before an account can run political advertisements; about three percent of Facebook accounts have qualified to run such ads.
