Questions this episode answers

What are the five most common mistakes that hurt conversion rates on a sales page?

Trevor Levine lists: relying on adjectives instead of action verbs, focusing on deliverables rather than results, using too many I and we statements instead of you and your, lacking word pictures that help readers visualize the outcome, and failing to describe the negative ripple effects of inaction or choosing the wrong solution.

How should a checkout page be optimized to reduce abandonment?

Do not link to the checkout page before fully agitating the problem and presenting the guarantee. On the checkout page itself, recap the risk reversal, show an image of what the buyer receives, and repeat shortened versions of the strongest testimonials with headshots to reassure buyers at the moment of purchase.

What is a tripwire offer and how does it work?

A tripwire pulls a valuable excerpt from a larger, more expensive course or program and offers it at a low price, such as seven dollars. Buyers who purchase the tripwire are then offered the full product, and the over-delivery on the low-priced item increases the likelihood they buy the higher-priced one.

How can a bold guarantee be structured without taking on unlimited financial risk?

Tie the bold guarantee to a conditional requirement the client must meet, such as submitting homework weekly or spending a minimum amount on ads. If the client does not meet the conditions, they do not qualify for the guarantee, protecting the seller while still making a compelling promise in ads and sales copy.

Terms defined in this episode
Video Sales Letter (VSL)
A sales presentation delivered entirely by video so the viewer cannot scroll to see the price before hearing the full pitch, used for higher-ticket products priced at one thousand dollars or more.
Decoy Pricing
A three-tier pricing structure where a middle option is made intentionally less attractive so buyers compare the top tier and perceive it as a bargain relative to the small price difference.
Tripwire Offer
A low-cost excerpt pulled from a larger program, sold cheaply to convert prospects into buyers and then lead them toward purchasing the full, higher-priced product.
Conditional Guarantee
A bold money-back or results guarantee tied to specific criteria the customer must fulfill, such as completing weekly homework, protecting the seller while maximizing the offer's appeal.
