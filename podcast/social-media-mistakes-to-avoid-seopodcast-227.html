Social Media Mistakes to Avoid - #SEOpodcast 227

Episode26 min2014-04-09

Businesses can improve social media results by tailoring content to each platform, posting at peak engagement times, prioritizing quality followers over quantity, and keeping tweets under 100 characters to allow room for retweets.

Key takeaways
  • Quality followers who engage and share matter more than large follower counts filled with people who ignore your content.
  • Keeping tweets under 100 characters leaves room for others to add a comment when retweeting, making your content more shareable.

Questions this episode answers

What is the biggest social media mistake businesses make according to this podcast?

Treating all social media platforms the same is cited as the top mistake. Each platform has a different audience and features. Twitter requires short, pointed content under 140 characters, Facebook suits longer posts and video, Google Plus attracts more technical users, and Instagram is best for images and reaching an 18 to 30 demographic.

When is the best time to post on Twitter and Facebook for maximum engagement?

According to the episode, Facebook engagement rates are 18% higher on Thursdays and Fridays. On Twitter, weekdays see 14% more engagement than weekends, with 5 p.m. having the highest amount of retweets. Posting only early in the morning is discouraged because many followers have not yet started their day.

Should businesses use automated direct messages on social media?

The hosts say automated messages are okay in some contexts, such as a brand offering a coupon or an artist sharing a free mixtape link, but generic spammy welcome messages are disliked and can lead to unfollows. Automated messages work better for products and artists than for service providers.

Why should you keep tweets under 100 characters even though the limit is 140?

Keeping tweets under 100 characters makes them easy to read and, more importantly, leaves room for someone to add their own comment when retweeting. If a tweet uses the full character limit, followers may skip retweeting it entirely because they cannot add their own message.

Terms defined in this episode
hashtag optimization
Creating website content optimized for the same hashtags you promote on social platforms, so that when people click those hashtags your site appears in results rather than competitors or unrelated accounts.
automated messages
Pre-set direct messages sent automatically when someone follows an account on social media, useful for brands offering coupons or artists sharing free downloads, but considered spammy when used as generic promotional greetings.
posting at inappropriate times
Publishing social media content at times when your target audience is unlikely to see it, such as early morning posts that miss peak engagement windows on Twitter and Facebook.
