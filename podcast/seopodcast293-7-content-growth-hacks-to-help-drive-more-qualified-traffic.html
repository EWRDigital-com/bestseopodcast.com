Questions this episode answers

How can I freshen up old content to improve SEO performance?

Remove dates from URL file paths so content does not look outdated, reuse and update annual event pages rather than creating new ones, update static pages like About Us and FAQ pages, and post consistently on a set schedule to keep content fresh and relevant.

What is Google AdWords Customer Match and how does it work?

Customer Match is a Google AdWords tool that lets you upload customer email addresses. When those customers are logged into Google, YouTube, or other Google properties, they will see your targeted ads. It functions as an advanced form of remarketing tied to email lists.

How should I handle recirculating older content?

Target content that already performed well, such as articles with high shares, high engagement, or many comments. Freshen the content first by adding new information, then recirculate it. You can also notify previous commenters by adding a new comment linking to any updated or spinoff post.

Terms defined in this episode
Customer Match
A Google AdWords tool that allows advertisers to upload customer email addresses and serve ads to those customers when they are logged into Google, YouTube, or other Google properties.
Referral traffic
Visitors who arrive at your site from another site rather than from direct search. According to the episode, referral traffic tends to have higher engagement, lower bounce rates, and higher conversion rates.
Content recirculation
The practice of putting previously published content back into your marketing channels, such as through boosted posts or promoted tweets, to extend its reach and drive additional traffic.
Meta description
A short text snippet that appears in search results beneath a page title. The episode recommends using successful paid ad text in meta descriptions to improve click-through and conversions.
