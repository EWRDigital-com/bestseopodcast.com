What is Geofencing: 4 Questions Every Business Owner Should Ask Before Using Geofencing Marketing #414

Ep. 41428 min2018-04-09

Geofencing is a location-based marketing tool that targets mobile users inside a virtual perimeter, and business owners should evaluate fit, targeting precision, privacy rules, and realistic reach before adopting it.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Geofencing works by triggering ads when a mobile device with location services and a data-sharing app enters a virtual perimeter.
  • Target where customers already are, such as a competitor's location, not your own parking lot.
  • Geofences should not be less than four minutes of travel time to the target location, and smaller is better.
  • About 50% of people in a geofenced area will actually end up on your targeted ad list.
  • Massachusetts prohibits geofencing near medical facilities, so check your state laws before launching a campaign.

Questions this episode answers

How effective is geofencing for reaching people in a targeted area?

The hosts estimate roughly 50% of people in a geofenced area will end up on the targeted list, because some users lack the right apps, have GPS turned off, or have no location services enabled. High-traffic locations work better than small, low-foot-traffic spots.

What is the best practice for setting the size of a geofence?

Smaller is better. A rule of thumb from the episode is that a geofence should not cover less than four minutes of travel time to the target location, whether by foot or car, and marketers should geofence competitor or related locations rather than their own premises.
