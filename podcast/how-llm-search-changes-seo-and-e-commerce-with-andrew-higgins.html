Questions this episode answers

How should e-commerce brands start optimizing for AI search?

Andrew Higgins recommends bite-sized steps that existing teams already understand: setting up a correct tracking pixel for ChatGPT, integrating a product feed, and organizing a product catalog with schema that LLMs can read. These are concrete starting points that do not require a full overhaul.

Is AI visibility a long-term standalone marketing category?

Higgins does not think a pure-play AI visibility platform will remain a distinct category. He expects it to be absorbed into broader marketing disciplines over six to twelve months. The data from current tools is still inconsistent, with twenty different tools often producing twenty different answers.

How is agentic commerce different from traditional e-commerce?

Agentic commerce involves agents working on behalf of shoppers, potentially completing entire purchase journeys inside a single surface like an LLM, including discovery, comparison, and transaction. Higgins sees agent-to-agent commerce as a much broader category than simply adding a buy button to a chatbot.

Terms defined in this episode
GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)
Optimizing brand content and data so it appears and ranks favorably inside large language model responses, as distinct from optimizing for traditional blue-link search results.
Agentic Commerce
A broad category where AI agents act on behalf of shoppers to discover, compare, and potentially complete purchases, including agent-to-agent transactions, without direct human involvement at each step.
RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)
A method large language models use to pull in external data at query time, referenced in the episode as one of the ways LLMs ingest and interpret brand information.
AI Slop
Low-quality, derivative AI-generated content that floods the internet without genuine usefulness, cited in the episode as the word of the year and a target of recent Google core updates.
