Google Authorship Tips - Internet Marketing Podcast Number 204

Episode25 min2013-10-11

Google authorship markup is for single, real-person authors on article pages, not product listings, multiple authors, or brand personas, and should always link to a Google Plus profile.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Authorship is for articles written by one real person, not product description pages, property listings, or e-commerce category pages.
  • Do not set up authorship on pages with duplicate or manufacturer-sourced product descriptions, since you are not the author.
  • Authorship does not support multiple authors or brand personas; all content links to a single Google Plus profile.
  • Use the rel equals author tag, not the publisher tag, or your avatar will not appear in search results.
  • For articles published in different languages, link all of them to the same Google Plus profile.
Be sure to have the correct tag on the inside.

Questions this episode answers

What pages should Google authorship NOT be set up on?

According to this episode, authorship should not be set up on product description pages, property listings, or e-commerce category pages. It also should not be used on pages where the content is duplicated from a manufacturer, since the person setting it up is not the actual author of that content.

What happens if you use the publisher tag instead of the author tag for Google authorship?

If you use the publisher link instead of the author tag, your avatar will not appear in search results. The hosts warn that Google's own instructions can reference the publisher link first, which causes confusion. You must use the correct rel equals author tag to get your photo to show.

Can a company or brand persona be set up as a Google authorship author?

No. At the time of this episode, Google authorship is only for real people, not brand personas or company names. The hosts note this as a limitation, expressing hope that Google will eventually allow a company to be listed as publisher alongside the individual author.

How should a site with multiple contributing authors handle Google authorship?

The hosts say authorship does not support multiple authors on a single page. Their workaround is to split transcribed podcast content into separate pieces, attributing each piece to one individual author, or to plan authorship credit arrangements before publishing a jointly written article.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Authorship
A markup setup that links a piece of content to its real-person author's Google Plus profile, causing the author's avatar to appear in search results next to the article.
rel=author vs rel=publisher
Two different HTML tags used in authorship markup. The author tag displays the individual's avatar in search results. The publisher tag references the company but does not show an icon in search results at the time of this episode.
SIVO
Search engine visitor optimization, described in the episode as interrelated with user experience and on-page optimization.
