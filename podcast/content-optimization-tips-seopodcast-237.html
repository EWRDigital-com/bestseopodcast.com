Content Optimization Tips - #SEOpodcast 237

Episode38 min2014-07-14

Content optimization requires keyword research, readability, trust, urgency, and page title focus, plus surveying your audience before creating content, according to nine industry experts covered in episode 237.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Use the QUART framework: Quality, Uniqueness, Authority, Relevance, and Trust when evaluating every piece of content.
  • Do keyword research before writing, then let good writers execute it so the content stays clear and engaging.
  • Keep page titles to about 55 characters based on current Google display limits to avoid truncation in search results.
  • Survey your audience before spending resources on content so you know what they want and how they want it delivered.
  • Create actionable content that encourages visitors to take a desired action rather than just read and leave.

Questions this episode answers

How many characters should a page title be for SEO in 2014?

According to Dr. Pete Myers, a marketing scientist at Moz cited in this episode, the safe bet for page title length is about 55 characters. Google increased font sizes in search results, which reduced how many characters are displayed, dropping the safe limit from 75 to 65 and then to 55.

Why is readability important for content optimization?

AJ Khan of Blind Five-Year-Old explains that people do not generally read, they scan. Using a strong font, interesting subheads, and short paragraphs makes content easier to absorb. The hosts also note that people will watch a five-minute video before reading two paragraphs, showing how presentation affects engagement.

What is the QUART framework for content?

QUART stands for Quality, Uniqueness, Authority, Relevance, and Trust. Forensic SEO consultant Alan Blyce introduced it as a checklist to evaluate every piece of content. The idea is to measure whether content will positively affect your business before you publish it.

Should you survey your audience before creating content?

Yes. Rand, described in the episode as the wizard of Mars, recommends surveying your audience before dedicating time and resources to a specific piece of content. The hosts add that even sending a few emails to select people in an industry to gather feedback can guide content creation effectively.

Terms defined in this episode
QUART
A framework introduced by Alan Blyce standing for Quality, Uniqueness, Authority, Relevance, and Trust, used to evaluate whether a piece of content will benefit your business before publishing.
Google Authorship
A system where content creators claim ownership of their work by setting up a Google+ profile, adding contributor links, and tagging posts with rel equals author so Google attributes the content to them.
Panda 4.0
A major Google algorithm update rolled out around May 20th that affected both indexing and ranking, reportedly impacting 7.5% of searches and causing significant ranking drops for sites like eBay.
Content dilution
A problem described by Dr. Pete Myers where trying to get every page and variation on a large site indexed and ranked spreads SEO efforts too thin, weakening overall rankings instead of concentrating authority on top pages.
