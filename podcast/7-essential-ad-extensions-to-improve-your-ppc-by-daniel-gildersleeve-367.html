7 Essential Ad Extensions to Improve Your PPC by Daniel Gildersleeve #367

Ep. 36729 min2017-04-05

Using all seven Google AdWords ad extensions relevant to your business goals increases click-through rates and conversions at no additional cost beyond the standard cost per click.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Site link extensions create deep links into your site and retain historical performance data when you swap ad copy.
  • Call extensions are critical for local service businesses, but only work well when a person answers quickly using a prepared script.
  • Message extensions work best when targeting younger audiences, especially millennials who prefer texting over calling.
  • Location extensions require a verified Google My Business profile and should not be used for national campaigns.
  • Price extensions let service providers showcase offerings and prices in a way shopping ads do not allow.
Remember, there are exceptions to every rule. Pay-per-click often performs completely different from one industry to another and from one audience to another.

Questions this episode answers

What are Google AdWords ad extensions and do they cost extra?

Ad extensions expand your ad with additional information such as call buttons, location details, site links, and pricing. There is no additional cost to using them, but you pay the same cost per click when users engage with them. Google only shows them when it predicts they will improve performance.

When should I use call extensions versus message extensions in PPC?

Use call extensions when your audience is likely to call, such as for emergency or local services, and ensure someone answers quickly with a script. Use message extensions when targeting younger or millennial audiences who prefer texting. Set schedules for both so they only run during hours when someone can respond.

What are price extensions and how are they different from shopping ads?

Price extensions show one to eight price cards below your text ad on desktop and mobile, each with a unique URL. Unlike shopping ads, they allow service providers to list services such as dog grooming or haircuts with prices. Promotional words like sale or free are not allowed and will disqualify your ad.

How do I set up location extensions in Google AdWords?

Location extensions must sync with a verified Google My Business profile. Claim, verify, and get your profile approved first, then link it to your AdWords account and enable location extensions on your campaign. If you add a second location, remove the first extension and add both locations together.

Terms defined in this episode
Site link extensions
Sub-ads below a main ad that include an additional title and two levels of description, creating deep links directly to specific pages inside a website.
Call-out extensions
Non-clickable text snippets added below an ad that highlight features or benefits such as free shipping, 24-7 customer service, or price matching.
Structured snippets
AdWords extensions that let advertisers attach a predefined header and a list of values, such as brands or destinations, to mark up ad content similar to schema markup on websites.
Price extensions
Boxes appearing below text ads on desktop and mobile that display one to eight price cards, each with a unique URL, allowing service providers to showcase offerings and prices.
