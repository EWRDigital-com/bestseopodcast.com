Customer Conversion Rate Tips - #SEOpodcast 266

Episode32 min2015-03-27

To improve customer conversion rates, businesses should optimize calls to action, use clear buttons, follow the F-rule layout, A/B test changes, add newsletter pop-ups, and keep forms short with concise labels.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Tweak call-to-action wording to include action words like subscribe, order, buy, or shop.
  • Use A/B testing but allow enough time and budget to gather meaningful data before making decisions.
  • Keep contact forms short, place labels inside fields, and pair them with a strong CTA.
  • Email is still the most visited place on the internet, so newsletter pop-ups add real marketing value.
  • Make your most important CTA more prominent than secondary ones based on which action closes more deals.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most important tweaks to increase conversion rates on a landing page?

The episode covers 12 tweaks including optimizing call-to-action wording with action words, using recognizable buttons, maintaining a natural content flow, following the F-rule layout, A/B testing, reducing distractions, using original graphics, adding newsletter pop-ups, giving something away, and keeping forms short.

What is the F rule for landing pages?

The F rule means visitors read across the top, then a bit further down, then scan the left side, forming an F shape. The episode recommends placing your most important elements and calls to action above the fold and within that F pattern to capture attention quickly.

How long should you run an A/B test before making decisions?

According to the episode, it depends on traffic volume and budget. High-traffic sites may get useful data in two days, but smaller businesses may need a couple of weeks and some ad spend to gather enough data to confidently identify what is and is not working.

Why should conversion rate optimization matter before running paid traffic campaigns?

The hosts warn that sending paid traffic to a site not designed to convert means visitors will not become customers, and the SEO or PPC effort will appear to have failed. They recommend optimizing pages to convert before investing in traffic generation.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
A term used by the hosts to describe on-site conversion optimization, meaning the things you do once traffic reaches your site to turn visitors into customers.
F rule
A landing page design principle where visitors naturally read across the top, then slightly lower across, then down the left side, forming an F shape, so key content and CTAs should fall within that pattern.
A/B testing
Testing two versions of a page element such as color, copy, or positioning against each other to measure which one produces better conversion results.
above the fold
The portion of a webpage visible without scrolling, where the most important calls to action and content should be placed to capture visitor attention immediately.
