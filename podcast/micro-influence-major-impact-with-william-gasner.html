Questions this episode answers

Why are micro influencers better than celebrity influencers for brand promotion?

Micro and nano influencers, those with fewer than 100,000 followers, tend to have higher engagement rates and more authentic connections with their audiences. Their content costs far less, generates more usable UGC, and the smaller account size means the TikTok and Instagram algorithms can push their content to new audiences if engagement is strong.

What is product seeding and how does it work?

Product seeding means sending free products to influencers in exchange for promotion rather than paying a flat fee. According to William Gasner, people willing to promote just for the product tend to be more authentic advocates. Brands can add affiliate commissions on top to incentivize ongoing promotion after the initial seeding.

How do LLMs and Google use social media posts for search rankings?

According to William Gasner, Google has started analyzing social posts to surface them in search results, and LLMs now analyze social posts to associate mentions with a brand and rank it within AI-generated answers. This makes maintaining a consistent social media presence increasingly important for visibility in both traditional and AI-powered search.

Terms defined in this episode
Nano or micro influencers
Smaller social media creators with fewer than 100,000 followers who typically generate higher engagement rates relative to their audience size compared to celebrity or mega influencers.
Product seeding
Sending free products to influencers in exchange for promotion, without a direct cash payment, to generate authentic user-generated content and brand awareness.
Whitelisting (Spark Ads on TikTok)
A tactic where a brand gets permission from an influencer to run paid ads directly through that influencer's social media profile, combining influencer trust with paid amplification.
UGC (user-generated content)
Content created by influencers or real customers showing a product in use, valued for authenticity and repurposable for ads, websites, and marketing materials.
