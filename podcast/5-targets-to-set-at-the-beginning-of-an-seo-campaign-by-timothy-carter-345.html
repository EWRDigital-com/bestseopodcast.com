5 Targets to Set at the Beginning of an SEO Campaign by Timothy Carter #345

Ep. 34538 min2016-11-02

To start an SEO campaign effectively, set five targets upfront: define your audience, identify a niche, choose keywords, estimate traffic opportunity, and set a conversion rate goal before executing any tactics.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Define your audience first, including how and where they search, such as mobile, voice, or Google versus Facebook.
  • Target a niche to surface long-tail phrases that rank faster, face less competition, and convert better than broad terms.
  • Keywords must now include synonyms and semantically related terms to align with Hummingbird-era search behavior.
  • Analyze competitor sites to gauge realistic traffic opportunity before committing to content and link-building volume.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five targets to set at the beginning of an SEO campaign?

According to the episode, the five targets are: audience, niche, keywords, traffic, and conversion rate. Each builds on the previous one, starting with understanding who your audience is and how they search, down to estimating the conversion rate you want to achieve.

Does conversion rate optimization affect SEO rankings?

The hosts argue yes, disagreeing with the article on this point. They say optimizing for conversions improves user engagement signals like bounce rate and time on site, which they believe Google interprets as a good user experience and rewards with higher rankings.

How did a medical billing business grow from 3 daily visitors to 1,800 using SEO podcast advice?

Guest Manny Oliveris shared that after six years of implementing tips from the podcast, including reducing bounce rate to 2.17 percent, improving page speed, cross-linking posts, and repurposing content on SlideShare, his site grew from roughly 90 monthly visits to about 40,000 monthly visits.

In their words

what you plan for at the beginning of a campaign can set the tone and the effectiveness for your entire execution. Set the wrong goals, aim for the wrong targets, and you can wind up wasting time and energy pursuing a result that doesn't align with your original vision.
