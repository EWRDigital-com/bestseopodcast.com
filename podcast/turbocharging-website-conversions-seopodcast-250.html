Key takeaways
  • Average website conversion rates hover between 2 and 10 percent depending on the industry.
  • One CTA per landing page removes decision clutter and raises the chance of conversion.
  • Site load time matters because people bounce at 3 seconds on desktop and 6 seconds on mobile.
  • Knowing the lifetime value of a customer is required before judging whether an ad campaign succeeded or failed.
  • Create separate AdWords campaigns for mobile ads because mobile users search and respond differently than desktop users.

Questions this episode answers

How can I improve website conversion rates?

The episode outlines five tactics: invest in professional design and use original photos, optimize site speed to avoid slow load times, limit landing pages to one clear CTA, build trust with security seals and testimonials, and test layout, button color, CTA wording, and images obsessively and regularly.

Why should I create separate AdWords campaigns for mobile ads?

Mobile users search differently and respond differently than desktop or tablet users. They are more likely in a purchasing decision and convert faster. Mobile-specific campaigns let you use appropriate language, such as action words like tap, which a combined campaign cannot accommodate.

How does knowing customer lifetime value affect ad campaign decisions?

Without knowing lifetime value you may judge a campaign as a failure based on a single purchase. The episode uses a yogurt shop example where a customer who spends $15 once but returns five times changes the math entirely, making a previously unattractive ad spend look worthwhile.

Terms defined in this episode
SIVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, covering both SEO of a site and understanding how visitors who arrive behave, including what changes can raise conversions.
CTA
Call to action, the specific action a site wants a visitor to complete, such as filling out a free consultation form or making a purchase.
UX
User experience, referenced in the context of site design choices like color palette and image quality that affect how visitors interact with a page.
Lifetime value of a customer
The total revenue a single customer generates across all repeat purchases, used to judge whether an ad campaign is actually profitable beyond the first transaction.
