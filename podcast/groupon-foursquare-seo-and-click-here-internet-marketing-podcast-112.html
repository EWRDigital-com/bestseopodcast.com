Groupon, Foursquare, SEO and "Click Here" - Internet Marketing Podcast 112

Episode32 min2011-08-18

Local business websites should display clear contact info, use keyword-rich anchor text instead of "click here," avoid flash-only builds, and keep content current to support both users and search engine indexing.

Key takeaways
  • Never use 'click here' as link text; embed a relevant keyword in the anchor text instead.
  • Keep phone numbers in plain HTML text, not images, so smartphones and search engines can read them.
  • Update your copyright date and About Us page regularly so Google does not index stale content.
  • Avoid flash-only websites; no flash-only site has been seen on Google's first page.
  • Do not let music play automatically on a website; statistical evidence shows it increases bounce rates.
What Google reads is it says click here and it says, hey, the information at the end of this link is about click here.

Questions this episode answers

Why should local business websites avoid vanity phone numbers?

Vanity numbers like 1-800-E-WEB-STYLE are hard to remember when mixed with digits, and smartphones cannot automatically detect and dial them from a webpage the way they can with a plain numeric phone number, making it harder for visitors to call you.

What is wrong with putting your phone number inside an image on your website?

If your phone number is in an image, search engines cannot index it and smartphones cannot click to call it. The hosts recommend placing phone numbers in plain HTML text, and if a vanity number is used as a graphic, put the real numeric number in HTML directly below it.

Is a Groupon deal a good idea for every small business?

According to the hosts, Groupon can work if you have enough capital to absorb an upfront loss for branding and foot traffic, but for low-margin businesses it can leave you losing money on every transaction. One example showed a deal that left the vendor with only 25% of an already halved price.

Why is it a problem to let your business website sit vacant or under construction?

Google indexes the vacant or under-construction page and can see that useful content has disappeared, which hurts your rankings. The hosts also warn that an expired domain or unpaid hosting can take the site down entirely, and without the site, any domain-based email goes down too.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword-rich anchor text
Link text that contains a relevant search keyword rather than generic phrases like 'click here,' so search engines understand what the linked page is about.
Vanity number
A phone number that uses letters to spell a word, such as 1-800-E-WEB-STYLE, which can be hard for users to dial and cannot be auto-detected by smartphones on websites.
Keyword density
The ratio of keyword occurrences to total text on a page; using filler words like 'click here' in links can dilute this ratio.
CTA (call to action)
A prompt on a webpage that guides a visitor toward a desired action, such as a button saying 'get a deal now' or 'download white paper now.'
