Questions this episode answers

Can you negotiate a lower price on a Yellow Pages print ad?

Yes. eWebStyle reported getting the same Yellow Pages ad at 50% off simply by telling the rep they were considering canceling. The host notes that in Paul Hanson's three-plus years as a Yellow Pages salesman, prices never dropped, making this a sign of how far the medium has declined.

Is DotNetNuke good for SEO?

According to the episode, DotNetNuke can support solid SEO as long as it lets you control title tags, meta tags, keyword tags, and content on a page-by-page basis. The hosts say any CMS that gives you that control is, in their words, an SEO-able system.

How did Twitter hurt the Bruno movie opening weekend?

The episode explains that Twitter let disappointed viewers spread negative opinions almost instantly after seeing the film. Instead of the projected $30 million opening weekend, Bruno earned around $20 million, with fast-spreading bad reviews on Twitter cited as the main cause.

What should a website have before you start driving SEO or paid traffic to it?

The hosts say a site needs clear calls to action, contact information including a phone number on every page, working purchase paths, and focused messaging. They warn that sending traffic to a site that cannot convert it is wasted money regardless of how much traffic arrives.

Terms defined in this episode
Content Management System (CMS)
A platform such as DotNetNuke or Joomla that lets users manage website content. The episode says a CMS is SEO-friendly when it allows page-by-page control of title tags, meta tags, and content.
Call to Action
An element on a webpage that prompts visitors to take a specific next step such as buying, calling, or submitting a form. The episode flags missing calls to action as a critical website flaw.
Break-even point
The number of sales needed to recoup an advertising spend. The episode calculated that a $450 per month ad budget with a $30 product requires roughly 17 new sales per month just to break even.
PPC (Pay-Per-Click)
A paid advertising model where advertisers pay per click. The episode discusses using PPC on multiple search engines beyond Google for branding and lead generation.
