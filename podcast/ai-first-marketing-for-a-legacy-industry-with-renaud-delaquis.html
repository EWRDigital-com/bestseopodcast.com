Questions this episode answers

How is Coastline Academy using AI in its business operations?

Coastline Academy uses AI for instructor scheduling and route optimization, scaling local marketing materials across 500 locations in nine states, analyzing employee personality assessments to identify top recruiter personas, and coordinating partnerships with competitor online driver's ed companies to generate leads for behind the wheel lessons.

Why is AI search not a major channel for a driving school?

Renaud Delaquis explains that driving school customers are primarily parents, not teenagers who use AI search tools. Demand is seasonal and habitual, with families often returning to the same school. He expects driving schools to benefit from AI search on a second or third wave, likely a few years out.

How does Coastline Academy measure the impact of local marketing efforts?

The company tracks utilization rate, aiming for 80 percent instructor schedule fill per area. It also monitors branded search queries, click through rates on paid brand campaigns, and compares student enrollment counts against census data for 10th graders to estimate local market share.

What did Coastline Academy learn from applying AI to recruiting?

After losing their HR director, the leadership team used AI to analyze personality assessments of existing employees. They found that retired educators were a top performing persona, generating higher average lesson counts per student and better aligning with the company mission of creating safer drivers.

Terms defined in this episode
Utilization rate
The percentage of instructor schedule slots filled in a given area, with Coastline Academy targeting 80 percent fill as the key metric for local marketing success.
LLM visibility
The ability of a business or brand to appear and be cited within large language model generated answers, discussed in this episode as an emerging but not yet mature channel for legacy service businesses.
Lift analysis
A method of measuring whether brand marketing is working by checking if branded search queries and click through rates increase in a market after local marketing activity.
AI first company
A business that builds AI into its core operations and workflows from the ground up, rather than adding AI tools onto existing processes, allowing it to scale tactics almost infinitely across markets.
