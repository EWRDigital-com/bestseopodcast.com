Questions this episode answers

Is WordPress good for enterprise-level websites?

Yes. WordPress is open source software that powers 39.5% of the world's websites, including Sony, Microsoft, and TechCrunch. It offers scalable infrastructure, a large developer community, strong security hardening, and significant cost savings compared to proprietary platforms like Magento or Adobe.

What are the best WordPress SEO plugins for enterprises?

The episode highlights All in One SEO with over 2 million users, Yoast SEO as the gold standard for meta and sitemap management, NitroPack for site speed with up to 69% speed score improvement, MonsterInsights for Google Analytics integration, and Ahrefs and SEMrush as broader SEO toolsets.

What on-page and off-page SEO best practices should enterprises follow in WordPress?

On-page: use a dedicated focus keyword per page, create valuable and authentic content, choose an SEO-friendly theme, and maintain XML sitemaps. Off-page: build backlinks with high-ranking sites through content partnerships, secure the site with SSL and HTTPS, and optimize Google My Business for local traffic.

Why is Google My Business important for enterprise SEO?

According to the episode, Google My Business functions as its own search engine and drives 30% of organic traffic. Enterprises with physical locations should actively upload photos, respond to reviews, answer questions, and use localized keywords to rank in the Google Maps three-pack.
