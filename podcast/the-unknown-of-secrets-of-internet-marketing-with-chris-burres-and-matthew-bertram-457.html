Questions this episode answers

When should a business do PPC instead of SEO?

According to this episode, PPC makes sense first when target keywords have very high difficulty scores in tools like SEMrush, in the 70s or 80s, or when there is no existing search demand for the product. Otherwise, SEO should come first as the core budget, with PPC added later.

What is the recommended order for digital marketing investment?

The hosts recommend a three-step method: first build a good website or high-converting landing page, then invest in SEO as the core ongoing budget, and then add PPC after that. They say doing them in tandem is fine if budget allows.

Is PPC worth it for low-cost products?

The hosts advise against PPC for products under roughly $500 unless there is strong recurring revenue. If a customer buys many times, the lifetime value can justify the ad spend even on a low-ticket item. Without recurring revenue, getting a positive return on PPC is very difficult.

What are the risks of relying only on PPC and not doing SEO?

PPC is compared to a drug injection: it generates leads while active, but the moment spend stops, all phone calls and leads stop too. SEO builds a lasting online asset. A client example in the episode showed their rankings held for over a year after the SEO campaign ended.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing
A PPC tactic where ads follow people who already visited your site. Platforms require a minimum audience size for anonymity and restrict it for sensitive categories like DWI attorneys.
Keyword Difficulty
A score in tools like SEMrush that indicates how hard it is to rank organically for a keyword. Scores in the 70s and 80s signal it will take a long time to rank through SEO alone.
Lifetime Value (LTV)
The total revenue a single customer is expected to generate over time, used to decide whether PPC spend is justified even for lower-priced products.
Geofencing
A targeting method that serves ads based on a specific IP address or geographic area, used in the episode to target oil and gas companies with tailored messages.
