Key takeaways
  • Post videos on YouTube for broader reach, but keep them on your own site if ad revenue depends on on-site views.
  • Optimizing a YouTube video for keywords you already rank for risks swapping your website listing for a YouTube listing.
  • Transcribe videos and podcasts so search engine spiders can read the text and index relevant keywords.
  • Keep videos short so more of your audience actually watches them through to the end.
  • Consistency in applying SEO fundamentals is the core secret to earning and maintaining search engine placement.

Questions this episode answers

Should I host my video on YouTube or on my own website for SEO?

In general the hosts recommend YouTube for reach, but if you make money from ads around the video on your site, host it locally. Also avoid optimizing a YouTube video for keywords where your own site already ranks well, because YouTube's authority could push your site out of that spot.

Should I transcribe my video or podcast for SEO?

Yes. Transcribing adds keyword-rich text that search engine spiders can read and index, since spiders cannot listen to audio or video. Posting the transcription on your blog or website drives traffic even if you cannot fit it in a YouTube description.

How do you optimize a video on YouTube?

On YouTube you are limited to the title and a short description, so place your target keywords in both. Reviews add more crawlable text. Keeping the video short also helps because more viewers will actually watch it, which can improve visibility.

Terms defined in this episode
Call to action
An element on a webpage that prompts visitors to take a desired step, such as buying a product. The hosts stress that driving traffic has little value if the site cannot convert visitors through effective calls to action.
Backlink
An inbound link from another website to your own. The episode discusses an unconfirmed claim that embedding a YouTube video on your site causes YouTube to automatically create a backlink to that site.
Transcription for SEO
Converting spoken audio or video content into text so search engine spiders can crawl and index the keywords, since spiders cannot listen to audio or watch video directly.
