Questions this episode answers

What is the first link priority rule in SEO?

The first link priority rule means Google treats the first link on a page pointing to another page as the most important one. If your menu navigation uses a generic anchor like recipe index, Google prioritizes that over a more keyword rich anchor like healthy recipes appearing later on the same page.

How can Flippa be used for link building?

Flippa is described as an eBay for websites. Sellers disclose their keywords, content marketing strategy, and link sources in their listings. SEOs can use those disclosed inbound links as a ready made list of link building contacts and opportunities relevant to a specific niche.

What is the upside down guest post technique?

The upside down guest post moves the author bio block from the bottom of a guest article to the top. This counters Google potentially devaluing links placed in the author bio area at the bottom, and puts the link in the abstract or introduction section of the article instead.

How do you use Google Search Console to find link building and traffic opportunities?

In Search Console, look for keywords that generate high impressions but few clicks. Those gaps signal ranking positions, often around position 10, where boosting the page through better meta descriptions, more content, multimedia, and backlinks can increase click through rate and drive more targeted traffic.

Terms defined in this episode
First Link Priority Rule
Google gives highest priority to the first anchor text it encounters on a page linking to another URL, so the first link seen, often in site navigation, carries the most weight for that destination page.
Skyscraper Technique
A link building strategy coined by Brian Dean where you create content superior to what already ranks, then reach out to sites linking to the original to earn links to your improved version.
Seed Keywords
A tool at seedkeywords.com that lets you create a search scenario and send it to friends or family to discover how real people would search for a topic, surfacing keyword ideas you might not consider.
Upside Down Guest Post
A guest posting variation where the author bio and link are placed at the top of the article rather than the bottom, to avoid Google devaluing links in the traditional bottom bio block area.
