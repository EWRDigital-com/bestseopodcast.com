Questions this episode answers

What is keyword prominence in SEO and why does it matter?

Keyword prominence refers to the ratio of your target keyword to total words in a given area like a title or heading. Tools like Web Position Gold suggest a prominence of around 60 to 68 percent in those areas, based on analysis of existing first-page websites, to help guide on-page optimization.

Can keyword stuffing get my website banned from Google?

Yes. Placing keywords in tiny or hidden text is considered keyword stuffing. Google has software to detect it, and a competitor can also report you. When caught, getting out of the supplemental index is difficult and can seriously damage a business that depends on search traffic.

How do I do competitive analysis for SEO?

Pull up the first-page competitors for your target keyword, then analyze their keyword frequency, word count, and keyword-to-word ratios in the title, heading, URL, and body text. Tools like Web Position Gold Page Critic and Advanced Web Ranking are mentioned as useful for this work.

Why should I not just copy what a competitor is doing on their website?

A competitor may be using black hat SEO techniques that could get your site banned. Copying their exact page text also triggers duplicate content issues and sends your site to the supplemental index. Study what they do, but apply it using proper, approved methods.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword Prominence
The ratio of a target keyword to total words in a specific page area such as the title or heading. Web Position Gold suggests around 60 to 68 percent prominence based on analysis of first-page websites.
Keyword Stuffing
Placing excessive keywords in tiny or hidden text on a page to manipulate rankings. Google can detect and ban sites for this, and competitors can report it.
Supplemental Index
A secondary Google index where pages with duplicate or low-quality content are placed, effectively removing them from normal search results.
Page Critic
A feature inside Web Position Gold that analyzes keyword frequency, word count, and keyword prominence ratios in the title, heading, URL, and body text of top-ranking pages.
