Easy Tips for a Great Call to Action - #SEOpodcast 257

Episode38 min2014-12-19

Effective CTAs use strong command verbs, emotional triggers, USPs, FOMO, device-aware language, creative phrasing, and numbers to drive clicks and conversions across paid and organic channels.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Start every CTA with a strong command verb like buy, shop, download, or subscribe to prime the audience mentally.
  • Combine a USP with your CTA, for example, request a consultation today and receive same-day service.
  • Use FOMO triggers like limited time only or first come, first served to push action-oriented audiences to convert.
  • Customize mobile CTAs with tap-to-call extensions and words like tap or swipe, since mobile users are in a higher position to convert.
Creating a nice USP slash CTA mashup is a great way to increase clicks.

Questions this episode answers

What makes a call to action effective in a PPC or content campaign?

An effective CTA uses a strong command verb, taps into the audience's emotional pain, includes a USP to differentiate from competitors, applies FOMO with limited-time language, and uses numbers or percentages. These principles apply whether traffic comes from paid ads, organic search, or social channels.

Should you keep running pay-per-click ads when you already rank number one organically?

Yes. Running PPC alongside a top organic ranking adds a second position on the first page, which increases credibility and causes the organic link to get more clicks. It also creates an opportunity to test different ads, CTAs, and phone numbers against the organic traffic coming to your site.

How should CTAs differ for mobile versus desktop users?

Mobile users are often looking for instant gratification and are in a higher position to convert. CTAs for mobile should be more aggressive, use call extensions so users see a direct call button, and include action words like tap or swipe. Phone numbers on mobile pages should be linked with phone number tags for tap-to-call.

What should you do if spammy external sites are linking to your page and sending bogus form submissions?

Go into Webmaster Tools and disavow those links. If the page is not valuable, consider changing the page name so the spammy links point to a dead page, or redirect the URL to preserve any link juice you want to keep. Also evaluate whether the traffic from those pages is generating any real business value.

Terms defined in this episode
FOMO
Fear of missing out. Used in CTAs through limited-time offers, expiration dates, or limited stock messages to drive action from people who are motivated by fear of loss.
USP
Unique selling proposition. Described in the episode as arguably one of the most important pieces of acquiring new leads, it gives prospects a reason why they should choose you over competitors.
Call Extension
A PPC ad feature for mobile devices that displays a call button directly in the ad, removing the need for users to manually dial a phone number.
Disavow
The process of telling Google via Webmaster Tools to ignore specific inbound links, used when spammy or irrelevant sites link to your pages and you want to avoid a penalty.
