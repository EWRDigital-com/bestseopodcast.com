#SEOpodcast 270 - Writing an Enjoyable Blog Post

Episode30 min2015-04-17

Writing an enjoyable blog post requires choosing audience-focused topics, crafting multiple headlines, hooking readers in the first three sentences, telling stories, spacing text well, using subheadings, and ending with a cliffhanger to keep readers coming back.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Focus blog content on your audience's pain points and problems, not what you personally like to write about.
  • Write 15 to 20 headline variations per post, then use a pay-per-click test to find which one gets the most clicks.
  • The first three sentences must captivate readers immediately or they will start scanning and likely bounce.
  • Break text into two to four sentence paragraphs, use header tags in order of importance, and add images for scannability.
  • End posts with a cliffhanger or invite readers to vote on the next topic to drive return visits and shares.

Questions this episode answers

How do you write a blog post introduction that keeps people reading?

According to this episode, the first three sentences must engage the reader immediately. The hosts suggest introducing your subject matter and the benefit of it right away. They also note that describing what happens when you do NOT have a product or solution can be more powerful than describing the benefits of having it.

How should you choose the best headline for a blog post?

The episode recommends writing 15 to 20 headline variations and then running a small pay-per-click campaign with all of them at the same time. Google will run the A-B test for you, and whichever title gets the most clicks is the one you should use for the post.

Why do stories work so well in blog content?

The hosts explain that stories create engagement and generate emotion, which is a top driver of purchasing decisions. They also point out that humans are hardwired to remember stories rather than facts, so presenting facts inside a story makes them more memorable and persuasive.

How do subheadings and text spacing affect blog readability?

The episode says well-spaced text with two to four sentence paragraphs looks far less intimidating to readers. Subheadings should be tagged correctly using H1, H2, and H4 header tags in order of importance so that scanners can quickly find what they are looking for and are encouraged to keep reading.

In their words

Don't think about what you like to read. Think about the problems your audience is having and how your blog content can help position your business as the solution.

Terms defined in this episode
Lede
Spelled L-E-D-E, it refers to the opening sentences of a post or article. The episode says those first three sentences are extremely important because they determine whether a reader stays or starts scanning.
Header tags
HTML tags such as H1, H2, and H4 used to mark subheadings in order of importance. The episode says H1 should be the main page title, H2 the first big subheading, and remaining subheadings tagged as H4s.
HTTPS as a ranking signal
Google officially confirmed in this episode that using a secure server is a ranking signal, though at the time it affected fewer than 1% of global queries and carried less weight than signals such as high-quality content.
