Questions this episode answers

What split testing tools should Amazon sellers use?

Daniela recommends three third party tools: PickFu for main image split testing, Product Pinion for video polls where real shoppers review your listing and give verbal feedback, and IntelliV for product image stack polls that test the order and performance of your image carousel.

What are the three core conversion pillars for an Amazon product page?

Daniela outlines SEO to get found in search results, an optimized main image to win clicks over competitors, and dialed in A plus content inside the listing to drive conversion as shoppers scroll, noting this framework has not changed from five years ago.

How is AI changing content creation for large Amazon catalogs?

AI lets agencies like Mindful Goods build master templates and then scale execution across thousands of SKUs in hours or days instead of weeks or months. It also enables faster review mining from Reddit and Amazon, image generation to replace or supplement photo shoots, and synthetic avatar based split testing.

Terms defined in this episode
Manage Your Experiments
A free split testing tool inside Amazon Seller Central that lets sellers run ongoing tests, recommended to run continuously every month for incremental gains.
Rufus
Amazon's AI powered shopping search tool that is changing how search results appear on the platform.
Product image stack poll
A poll format offered by IntelliV that tests multiple sets of product carousel images against each other to determine which images and which order best improve Amazon conversion.
