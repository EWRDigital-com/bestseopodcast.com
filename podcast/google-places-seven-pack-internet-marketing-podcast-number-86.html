Questions this episode answers

What is UBL.org and how does it help local businesses?

UBL.org stands for Universal Business Listing. For $30 per year it distributes your business profile to search engines like Google, Yahoo, and Bing, online yellow pages, social networks like Yelp, portals like MapQuest and AOL, cell phone directories including BlackBerry and iPhone, 411 directory assistance, and OnStar in-car GPS navigation.

How is LocalPac different from UBL.org for local listings?

LocalPac is a service from BruceClay.com available at LocalPac.com for $49.95 per year per business location. It lists businesses in over 350 premium directories and includes Foursquare, which UBL does not. Both services feed into the InfoUSA and Axiom databases that search engines trust.

Why is Google Places so difficult to manage for businesses with multiple doctors or locations?

Multiple listings can accumulate automatically from various databases, creating 30 or more entries for one practice. Phone verification fails when businesses use automated phone systems, and postcard verification often gets discarded. Google can also ban listings it suspects of spam without clear guidance on which rule was violated.

What is the Google Places seven-pack and why does it matter for SEO?

The seven-pack refers to the group of seven local business listings Google displays in search results. These listings now appear interspersed within organic results, meaning businesses that previously ranked on page one can be pushed to page two. Being listed as A through G in the pack is described as critical for local visibility.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Places seven-pack
A group of seven local business listings labeled A through G that Google displays within search results, sometimes pushing organic results to page two for local searches.
UBL.org
Universal Business Listing, a single entry point that distributes enhanced business profiles to search engines, yellow pages, social networks, GPS navigation, and mobile directories for $30 per year.
InfoUSA and Axiom
Primary trusted databases used by major publishers, search engines, and directory services to verify and source local business listing data.
Link Juice
SEO value passed to a business through links from various directories and listing sites, cited as a second key reason to use local listing services like UBL or LocalPac.
