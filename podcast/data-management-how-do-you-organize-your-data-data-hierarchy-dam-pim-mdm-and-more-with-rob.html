Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between a DAM and a PIM system?

A DAM, or digital asset management system, stores and manages images, artwork, and 360 views. A PIM, or product information management system, handles technical data, pricing, and product details. Both are needed together for e-commerce because companies rarely keep all of that information in one place.

Why does master data management matter for e-commerce search?

Master data management drives taxonomy, meaning it defines relationships between terms so search returns accurate results. The episode uses the example of typing a brand name and getting only the gun rather than every part with that word in the description, which required intelligent data structuring to achieve.

How should small businesses start organizing their data to scale?

Stephen Covey's principle of starting with the end in mind applies directly. Plan the hierarchy from the top down, identify what you need to measure such as price, cost, and inventory, keep data clean and complete, and structure it consistently from the start so it stays usable as the business grows.

Terms defined in this episode
DAM (Digital Asset Management)
A content management system for images and digital assets that allows tagging, organizing, and merging images with text to create web content, mailers, and catalogs.
PIM (Product Information Management)
A system that stores product technical data, pricing, and specifications, designed to work alongside a DAM so complete product information can be delivered together.
MDM (Master Data Management)
The practice of structuring and governing all core business data, including product, pricing, and customer information, to enable accurate reporting, search, and taxonomy.
Taxonomy
The structured classification of terms and their relationships, used in e-commerce search to connect broad queries to the correct products or content.
