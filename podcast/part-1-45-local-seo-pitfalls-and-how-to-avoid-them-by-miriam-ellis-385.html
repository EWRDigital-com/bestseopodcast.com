Part 1: 45 Local SEO Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them by Miriam Ellis #385

Ep. 38543 min2017-08-09

Local SEO success starts with avoiding 9 foundational business-plan pitfalls, including choosing a limiting business name, using ineligible addresses, picking undesirable locations, and setting unrealistic ranking expectations.

Key takeaways
  • Avoid indistinct or geographically limiting business names, as they restrict your search visibility and confuse potential customers.
  • Use a real staffed location with a phone number for local SEO; PO boxes and unstaffed virtual offices hurt rankings.
  • Talk to customers from their perspective, not yours, and make sure your messaging reflects what they want, not just what you sell.
  • Add a privacy policy link on every page of your website to signal trustworthiness to Google and avoid appearing spammy.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common local SEO pitfalls when naming a business?

Choosing an indistinct name that does not signal what you do, or a geographically limiting name like a suburb rather than a major city, can restrict your search visibility. The episode uses examples like a yacht club name for a clothing shop and Sugarland Muffler when the real market is all of Houston.

Should I use a virtual office or PO box for my local SEO address?

No. The episode says PO boxes and unstaffed virtual offices are ineligible locations. If you lack a real office, using your home address is better, and you can list yourself in directories that hide your address if privacy is a concern. A staffed location with a real phone number is the key requirement.

How does Google's Possum update affect local SEO for businesses near city borders?

Possum fixed many issues for businesses just outside a city, but being outside the city proper can still create ranking challenges. The episode also warns that if multiple businesses offering the same service share a building, Google may show only one or two of them to avoid treating them as the same business.

Why is content strategy important for low-content pages like testimonials or gallery pages?

The episode explains that gallery pages are often among the most visited yet do no selling, which is a mistake. Testimonials should be spread throughout the site, appearing above the fold on the home page and on other pages, so every page functions as a true landing page rather than a brochure.

Terms defined in this episode
Possum update
A Google update that fixed many local ranking issues for businesses located just outside a city boundary, but still leaves some challenges for those not in the city proper.
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term meaning a share, a like, or a follow on social media.
Ineligible location
A business address such as a PO box or unstaffed virtual office that does not meet Google local listing requirements and can harm local SEO.
Instant search
A former Google feature that displayed full search results with each letter typed, turned off due to heavy reliance on mobile devices.
