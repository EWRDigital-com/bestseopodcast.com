Marketing Success Downfalls - #SEOpodcast 241

Episode34 min2014-08-04

Content marketing success requires planning, listening to your audience, agility across platforms, loyalty to repeat customers, modesty, and consistent testing, with creativity added as a seventh essential factor.

Key takeaways
  • Plan content strategy by defining objectives, roles, and voice before publishing anything.
  • Listen to your audience through comments and surveys to learn what they actually want.
  • Be agile and repurpose content across multiple platforms while a topic is still buzzing.
  • Stay loyal to regular customers and avoid focusing only on big one-time campaigns.
  • Test ads, landing pages, and content consistently, and have proper analytics in place to measure results.

Questions this episode answers

What are the main reasons content marketing fails?

According to the article discussed in this episode, the six reasons are lack of planning, lack of listening to your audience, lack of agility, lack of focus on loyalty, lack of modesty, and lack of testing. The hosts add a seventh reason: lack of creativity, because content needs to stand out from competitors covering the same topics.

How should I listen to my audience to improve content marketing?

The hosts recommend reading comments on your published content, actively asking for comments, and surveying your audience directly. Ask whether they liked the headline, whether the content was too long or too short, and use that feedback when creating future content.

Why is agility important in content marketing?

Agility means reacting quickly and spreading content across all platforms while it is still getting traction. The hosts give the example of taking a Facebook video, clipping portions for Instagram, and then combining everything into a blog post so multiple channels benefit while the topic is still hot.

What does lack of modesty mean in content marketing?

Lack of modesty means pretending to be something your brand is not. The hosts say consumers, especially millennials, can tell what is real and what is fake. They recommend presenting your products and services in a way that honestly represents your company so you attract loyal customers.
