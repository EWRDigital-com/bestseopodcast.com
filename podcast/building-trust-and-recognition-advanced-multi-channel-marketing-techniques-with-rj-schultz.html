Questions this episode answers

What is Google's 7-11-4 rule and why does it matter for marketing?

Google's 7-11-4 rule holds that buyers need seven hours of content consumption, 11 interactions, and exposure across four different platforms before they are ready to choose a brand. Blip uses this framework to guide clients toward full-funnel, multi-channel campaigns rather than relying on a single channel.

How does Blip Billboards work and who can use it?

Blip is a scheduling algorithm that acts as an open exchange between billboard owners and advertisers, similar to how Uber connects car owners and riders. Advertisers use a second-price bid auction to buy fractional time on digital boards, letting small businesses access inventory without long-term contracts or large budgets.

What is a qualified impression according to Blip?

Blip defines a qualified impression as one delivered to a person who has already seen the advertiser's ad at least six times. Below six exposures it is counted as a general impression. Reaching the qualified threshold signals that enough trust has been built to justify adding more expensive one-to-one digital ads.

How much should a small business budget for billboard advertising and for how long?

Blip recommends a minimum 90-day commitment so an area becomes saturated enough for repeated exposure. In a mid-size market like Salt Lake City, a budget of $3,000 to $5,000 per month is cited as a general rule of thumb to reach roughly 60% of the population six times.

Terms defined in this episode
7-11-4 Rule
A framework published by Google stating that buyers need seven hours of content, 11 interactions, and exposure across four different platforms before they are ready to make a purchase decision.
Qualified Impression (QI)
A proprietary Blip term for an ad impression delivered to someone who has already seen the ad at least six times, indicating a sufficient trust baseline for conversion-focused spending.
Full Funnel Marketing
An approach that uses different advertising mediums at different stages of the buyer journey, from awareness through consideration to purchase, rather than relying on a single channel.
EAT Metric
Google's framework standing for Expertise, Authority, and Trust, later expanded to include Experience, used to evaluate content quality and site credibility.
