Questions this episode answers

What types of content improve organic search visibility?

The episode covers 10 types: related information articles, a blog hosted on your own domain, FAQ-based articles, vertical or market-specific pages, case studies, a glossary or dictionary, white papers, product manuals and instructions, learning center how-to articles, and top 10 lists.

Why should a blog be hosted on your main website instead of a separate domain?

Because blog content will eventually rank in search, and you want that traffic landing on your site where your header, sidebar, footer, and other sales material can help convert visitors. Hosting it elsewhere means hoping readers click a link back to your site.

How do white papers differ from case studies for SEO content?

Case studies focus on a specific client, product, or outcome. White papers are about your company, your process, or the service you offer more broadly. White papers work well as soft conversions, trading access for an email address to feed into a drip campaign.

Terms defined in this episode
Vertical targeting
Creating separate landing pages aimed at specific industries or niches that your product or service serves, rather than cramming all markets onto one page.
White paper
A content piece focused on your company, process, or service offering, used to capture email addresses as a soft conversion and feed prospects into a drip marketing campaign.
Soft conversion
A lower-commitment action from a site visitor, such as providing an email address in exchange for a white paper, before they become a paying customer.
Evergreen content
Content such as top 10 lists and how-to articles that remains relevant and accurate for years after it is first published.
