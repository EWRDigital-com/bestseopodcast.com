Things That You Should Stop Doing With Your Website - Internet Marketing Podcast 189

Episode27 min2013-06-07

Stop these 17 website habits, including posting promotional fluff, stealing stock images, ignoring readers, and auto-playing media, to improve SEO, user experience, and conversion rates.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Content that is 75% or more different from another page avoids a duplicate content penalty.
  • Use stock images only if necessary, and manipulate them to make them more original.
  • Duplicate content across multiple domains risks one site getting penalized by Panda.
  • Do not require user registration without explaining the value and confirming data security.
  • Test your website in more than one browser and avoid overloading pages with heavy jQuery or Flash.

Questions this episode answers

Does duplicate content across two different city-targeted domains cause a Google penalty?

Not a direct penalty, but Google will not rank both sites at the same time, and one may lose ranking. If the sites serve the same service area with less than 75% different content, one site risks getting penalized by Panda. The hosts recommend rewriting content if one site struggles to rank.

How much content needs to be different to avoid being flagged as duplicate content?

According to the hosts, content needs to be at least 75% different from any other page to avoid being considered duplicate content. Google can also detect pages assembled from multiple duplicate sources, even if no single source exceeds that threshold.

Should you link out to external sites from your website?

Yes. The hosts say linking out, for example to Wikipedia or manufacturer pages, creates a good experience for the Google user. A dead-end page with no outbound links is not considered a good experience by Google, though you should avoid linking to competitors.

Is it okay to blend advertising with content on your website?

The hosts disagree with blanket advice against it. They say cheesy or irrelevant pop-up ads should be avoided, but highlighting your own products and services within content and linking to them is fair game and is part of driving conversions from the traffic you earn.

Terms defined in this episode
Duplicate content
Content that is less than 75% different from content on another page or site, which can prevent Google from ranking both pages and risks a Panda penalty.
Guest checkout
An e-commerce option allowing users to purchase without registering, recommended when registration is not strictly necessary.
CAPTCHA
A verification step users must complete to prove they are human, which the hosts say should be readable and simple to avoid frustrating users trying to complete a call to action.
