Questions this episode answers

Should I add location keywords to my Google Places business title or description?

The hosts strongly advise against it. Adding location keywords to your business title violates Google guidelines and, while some businesses still rank with it, it can get your listing banned. Including too many location keywords in your description can also lead to a penalty. The risk is not worth it for your main listing.

What type of video should I associate with my Google Places listing?

The hosts recommend uploading your video to YouTube and then associating that YouTube video with your Google Places listing. YouTube is a free Google product, and using Google products for your listing is unlikely to hurt your ranking and may help it.

Does the age of a Google Places page affect its ranking?

According to the article discussed in this episode, the age of a Places page may matter, but mainly in the first six months. After six months, age has little effect. The hosts compare it to domain age in regular SEO, where older, established listings may be trusted more by Google.

How should I choose categories for my Google Places listing?

Start with the categories Google provides and choose the ones most closely related to your business. If none fit exactly, pick marginally related Google categories before creating your own. Custom categories can help for long tail searches but should not replace Google's own categories.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Boost
A pay-per-click advertising option for Google Local Places where the advertiser cannot control exact keywords, and keywords are based on the Google categories chosen for the listing.
301 Redirect
A server-side redirect that permanently points one URL to another, consolidating link authority and resolving duplicate content issues such as www versus non-www versions of a site.
Canonical Tag
An HTML tag that tells search engines to assign ranking value to a specified page while keeping the original page live, used as an alternative to a 301 redirect.
Google Places listing age
How long a Google Places page has existed, which may influence trust and ranking in local search results, particularly during the first six months after creation.
