Switching Domain Names and Site Credibility - #seopodcast 137

Episode32 min2012-03-08

Switching from a .co.uk to a .com domain requires 301 redirecting every page, keeping the old domain live during transition, and considering whether your target audience is UK-based before making the change.

Key takeaways
  • Use 301 redirects on every individual page when switching domains, not just a simple URL forward.
  • Keep the old domain live with redirects for a period to preserve any existing rankings.
  • If your target audience is in the UK, staying on a .co.uk domain may be the better choice.
  • Site credibility equals expertise plus trustworthiness, shown through authority links, good navigation, and contact information.
  • Anchor text should be keyword rich and match the keywords targeted on the destination page, not generic phrases like click here.
Site credibility is the sum of expertise plus trustworthiness.

Questions this episode answers

How do I switch my website from a .co.uk domain to a .com without losing SEO rankings?

Move an exact copy of your site to the .com domain, then 301 redirect each and every page on the .co.uk to the corresponding .com page. Keep the old .co.uk domain live with those redirects for a period of time to preserve any existing rankings before removing it.

Should I switch from a .co.uk to a .com domain?

It depends on your target audience. If your clients are in the UK and you already rank there, staying on .co.uk is likely the better choice. If you are targeting a US or global audience, switching to .com has value, but the transition must be handled carefully with proper 301 redirects.

What makes website content poor quality according to Google Panda guidance?

Poor quality content includes shallow pages with little value, pages whose sole purpose is advertising, keyword stuffed footers, bad titles, poor quality code, duplicate stock photos, bad grammar, factual errors, and pages with lots of words that say very little.

What is site credibility and how do you build it?

Site credibility is defined as the sum of expertise plus trustworthiness. You build it by earning authority links, maintaining high uptime, providing easy navigation, displaying physical contact information, having fast page speed, and making it easy for visitors to verify content accuracy.

Terms defined in this episode
301 redirect
A permanent redirect from one URL to another, used when switching domains to preserve rankings by pointing each old page to its corresponding new page.
anchor text
The clickable text in a hyperlink. It should be keyword rich and match the keywords targeted on the destination page rather than generic phrases like click here or learn more.
shallow content
Content that provides little value to users and offers low quality based on the intent of the query, often resulting in a high bounce rate.
Google Panda
A Google algorithm update targeting low quality and poor content on websites, named after a person involved in the project according to the episode.
