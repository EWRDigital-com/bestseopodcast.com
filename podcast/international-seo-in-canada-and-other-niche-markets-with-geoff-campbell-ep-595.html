Key takeaways
  • Use hreflang tags and human-validated translations for multi-language SEO, as automation alone is not enough.
  • A .ca domain signals Canada-only business, so use .com if any international expansion is possible.
  • Canadian postal codes use a letter-number-letter format and will break e-commerce forms built only for U.S. zip codes.
  • Long tail key phrases capture highly specialized B2B searches and can drive significant traffic even with low overall volume.
  • Organic SEO traffic consistently converts at a higher rate and stays on site longer than paid traffic.

Questions this episode answers

Should a business use a .ca or .com domain for Canadian SEO?

According to Geoff Campbell, .ca is standard in Canada but signals you will only do business there. If there is any chance of going international, a .com is preferable because .ca is limiting for international SEO, while hreflang tags help target specific countries from a .com.

How should B2B companies approach SEO when their products are highly specialized with low search volume?

The episode recommends building topical authority around parent topics, creating content clusters, and targeting long tail key phrases. Even a single long tail keyword for a niche piece of equipment can drive a large share of category traffic, as one client saw roughly 20 percent of traffic from one such term.

How important is French language SEO for the Canadian market?

French is an official Canadian language and is essential for reaching Quebec, which has a large population and distinct culture. The French spoken in Quebec has subtle differences from European French, so translations must be validated by local experts, not just automated tools.

Terms defined in this episode
hreflang tags
HTML tags that tell search engines which language and regional version of a page to serve, required for proper international multi-language SEO targeting.
topical authority
The practice of building a broad base of expert content around a subject area so search engines recognize a site as a reliable source on that topic and rank it for related searches.
long tail key phrases
Longer, more specific search queries with lower individual volume that together make up a large share of all searches and often signal high purchase intent in niche markets.
EEA (E-E-A-T)
A Google quality framework standing for experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust, used to evaluate the credibility of content, especially in specialized industries.
