Questions this episode answers

What new KPIs should SEOs track for AI search visibility?

Duane Forrester says key internal metrics include semantic density score, retrieval confidence score, zero-click AI surface presence, and machine-validated authority, but none of these are shared by platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, or Gemini. For now, manually logging query responses in a spreadsheet and analyzing trends monthly with ChatGPT is the practical approach.

What are vector embeddings and why do they matter for SEO?

Vector embeddings are mathematical representations of entities or objects stored in a vector database. The vector indicates direction, like a compass pointing to zero, and the embedding is the number. SEO relevance is determined by how close your content's embedding is to the embedding of a user query, making topical precision far more important than keyword matching.

Does LLMs.txt matter for AI search optimization?

Duane Forrester says LLMs.txt has an adoption and trustworthiness problem because no major AI platform has officially backed it, unlike robots.txt, schema.org, or sitemap.xml, which launched with explicit search engine support. He does not consider it valuable in its current state.

How is an LLM fundamentally different from a traditional search engine?

Forrester argues that a search engine forces the human to complete the final mile by choosing from a list of results, while an LLM returns a direct answer with such authority that users hand over their decision-making agency. The underlying math, process, and requirements are entirely different, not just the interface.

Terms defined in this episode
Semantic Density Score
An internal platform metric assessing whether a content chunk provides complete, deep knowledge on a topic without requiring the system to look elsewhere, distinct from semantic search as a concept.
Vector Embedding
A mathematical number representing an entity or object stored in a vector database, used to measure directional alignment between content and a user query in three-dimensional space.
Chunking
The way content is segmented for processing by LLMs, where each chunk must stand alone with sufficient information on its topic rather than spreading key facts across an entire article.
Retrieval Confidence Score
An internal LLM metric indicating how confidently a system can pull and surface a piece of content in response to a query, not currently shared publicly by any major platform.
