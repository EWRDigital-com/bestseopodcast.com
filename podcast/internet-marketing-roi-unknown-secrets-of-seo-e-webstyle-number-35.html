Questions this episode answers

How do I calculate ROI for SEO services?

Start by finding the average value of a new customer, then compare that to the monthly SEO cost. The hosts use a kitchen remodeler example where an average job is $5,000 and SEO costs $400 a month, making the investment clearly worthwhile if even one customer is gained.

What happens if Google finds hidden text on my website?

According to the hosts, Google will ban the site from its index. Hidden text can be created using CSS styling or by making text the same color as the background, and Google may use dedicated bots to find it. Losing indexing means losing all organic traffic.

Is there a penalty for duplicate content in Google?

A Google engineer the hosts reference stated there is no formal penalty, but duplicate pages are simply not included in the index. If two pages differ only by a city name, Google may show one but exclude the other, limiting reach for both targeted locations.

Terms defined in this episode
Hidden text
Text made invisible to users but readable by search engines, achieved through CSS styling or matching text color to background color. Google can ban sites caught using it.
Duplicate content
Pages or text that are identical or nearly identical across a site or across the web. Google excludes duplicated pages from its index rather than ranking them.
ROI (Return on Investment)
A measure of whether money spent on SEO or other marketing generates more revenue or profit than it costs, calculated by comparing average customer value to monthly service cost.
Content spinning
Rewriting existing content so it is unique enough to avoid being flagged as duplicate, while still covering the same subject matter.
