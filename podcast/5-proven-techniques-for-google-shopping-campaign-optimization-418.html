5 Proven Techniques For Google Shopping Campaign Optimization #418

Ep. 41839 min2018-05-07

Google Shopping campaign optimization requires excluding poor-performing search queries, adjusting bids by product and brand, using day parting and geo targeting, and optimizing product titles, descriptions, and images in the data feed.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Bid higher on individual products and brands that perform well, and move low performers to a separate campaign with a lower budget.
  • Product titles are the single most important item to optimize in a Google Shopping data feed, and should be keyword dense.
  • Use day parting and geo targeting based on actual conversion data, not assumptions, to concentrate budget where it performs best.
  • Landing page quality matters as much as the ad itself, including compelling descriptions, testimonials, and confidence-building product details.
Nothing throws your product listing into the abyss of lost impressions as poorly optimized titles.

Questions this episode answers

How do you optimize a Google Shopping campaign by excluding search queries?

Let data accumulate, then filter out queries that have received at least 50 clicks in the last 30 or 90 days without converting. Turn those off or move them to a campaign with a much lower bid, and concentrate spend on queries that do convert.

Why are product titles so important in Google Shopping feeds?

Google Shopping campaigns do not use keywords the way search ads do. Google pulls relevant products based on the shopping feed data, so product titles should be keyword dense and information rich, going well beyond what is typically used as a product title on the store itself.

How should product descriptions be written for Google Shopping feeds?

Google allows up to 5,000 characters for product descriptions, but the optimal length is between 500 and 1,000 characters. Treat them the same way you would SEO page titles and meta descriptions, and use keywords researched specifically for shopping descriptions.

Does day parting actually work for Google Shopping campaigns?

Yes, but decisions should be data driven rather than assumption based. Once enough data is collected, if conversions concentrate at certain times or in certain geographic areas, increase budget during those windows and reduce it elsewhere, regardless of what you might expect upfront.

Terms defined in this episode
Product Listing Ads (PLAs)
Ads that appear as a carousel or panel in Google search results, driven by AdWords, showing individual products from a shopping feed based on relevance to the search query.
Day Parting
The practice of running ads only during specific times of day or days of the week, based on when conversions are most likely to occur, to concentrate budget more efficiently.
Self-Liquidating Offer
A strategy where a product is sold at or below cost to acquire new customers, with the intent to make profit on back-end or repeat purchases.
Shopping Feed
A data file uploaded to Google that contains product information such as titles, descriptions, images, prices, and identifiers like GTINs, which Google uses to match products to relevant search queries.
