Google Analytics, 301 Redirects and CTA's - #seopodcast 113

Episode33 min2011-08-25

Google Analytics goal tracking tied to organic keywords reveals which terms convert, guiding SEO focus, while 301 redirects, strong CTAs, and call tracking maximize conversions from existing traffic.

Key takeaways
  • Use Google Analytics goal data to find which organic keywords convert, then build dedicated content pages for those terms.
  • 301 redirect old indexed pages to new equivalents, and analyze what the old page had that outperformed the new one.
  • Call tracking reveals not just call volume but the specific keywords driving calls, and recording calls can expose salespeople going off script.
  • Check entrance pages for missing contact information, broken navigation, and keyword-to-page continuity to reduce bounce rates.

Questions this episode answers

How do you use Google Analytics goals to improve SEO keyword targeting?

Compare goal completions to traffic by keyword. If a keyword consistently converts at a high rate but gets low traffic, it signals you should build dedicated content for it and push it up the rankings. Tying e-commerce dollar values to keywords also shows which terms drive real revenue.

What should you do with old indexed pages after a website redesign?

Apply a 301 redirect from the old page to the new replacement page. Before redirecting, analyze what content the old page had that may have ranked better than the new version, and carry that value over to avoid losing ranking authority.

What is call tracking and why does it matter for SEO campaigns?

Call tracking replaces your business phone number on the website with a forwarding number, allowing you to track which keywords drove each call, what time calls came in, where callers are from, and even record calls. One example in this episode showed recorded calls revealing salespeople going off script, causing close rates to drop from 75 percent to 25 percent.

Why does page continuity matter for landing pages in SEO?

If a user searches for a specific term, they expect to see that term reflected when they land on your page. If the landing page lacks clear references to what they searched, they will bounce. Adding a visible CTA matching the search intent, as done for a quit smoking product page, can increase sales significantly.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, the practice of optimizing a website not just for search engines but for the visitors who arrive from search, ensuring the page meets their intent and converts them.
301 Redirect
A permanent redirect from an old URL to a new one, used to preserve link value and send visitors and search engines to the correct page after a site redesign.
Call Tracking
A service that replaces a business phone number on a website with a forwarding number, enabling tracking of which keywords, times, and locations drove each incoming call, with optional call recording.
Entrance Page
The first page a visitor lands on when arriving at a website, which should match the keyword searched and include clear contact information and relevant CTAs to avoid bouncing.
