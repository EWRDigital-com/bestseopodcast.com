Questions this episode answers

Why is NAP consistency important for local SEO?

Name, address, and phone number must be identical across all listings. Even small differences, like spelling out suite versus using ST with a period, signal inconsistency to Google. A platform like Bright Local can clean up hundreds of directory listings and remove duplicates automatically.

How should a business owner respond to negative reviews?

Respond to all reviews without emotion and treat each response as a public statement. A measured, professional reply shows potential customers how the business handles problems and builds trust. Snarky or condescending responses make the owner look bad and can drive away future customers.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. These must be consistently and accurately listed across hundreds of local search engines and directories to support local SEO rankings.
Google Business Profile (GBP)
Google's platform for managing a business listing in local search and maps, supporting photos, hours, Q&A, reviews, and product or menu data.
Event Schema
A structured data type that communicates the current state of a scheduled event, with attributes including event canceled, event moved online, event postponed, event rescheduled, and event scheduled.
Hyper Local Content
On-page content that includes location-specific terms in headings, titles, metadata, image alt tags, and body text to improve relevance for local searches.
