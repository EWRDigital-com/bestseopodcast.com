Questions this episode answers

Why does technical SEO matter if I already have good content and links?

According to this episode, poor technical SEO can make a website unindexable and inaccessible, which defeats the purpose of having great content and links. Google's senior search quality strategist identifies content, links, and RankBrain as the top three factors, but technical SEO must support all of them.

How do I check if my website pages are indexed by Google?

Type 'site:' followed by your domain name with no space into the Google search bar. Google will return all pages it has indexed for your site. You can also use Google Search Console, formerly known as Google Webmaster Tools, to monitor indexing status.

What is mobile first indexing and how does it affect rankings?

Mobile first indexing means Google indexes and ranks the mobile version of a page over the desktop version. If your site is not optimized for mobile, your rankings will suffer. The hosts also warn against stripping keywords from mobile versions, as that hurts how Google crawls the site.

What is a 301 redirect and when should I use it?

A 301 redirect is a permanent redirect used when a page on your site moves to a new location. The episode contrasts it with a 302, which is for temporary redirects. The hosts say permanent 301 redirects are almost always the right choice when redirecting pages.

Terms defined in this episode
Mobile First Indexing
Google's practice of using the mobile version of a website as the primary version for indexing and ranking, meaning mobile optimization directly affects search rankings.
301 Redirect
A permanent redirect from one URL to another, used when a page moves to a new location, as opposed to a 302 which signals a temporary redirect.
Orphan Page
A page on your website that has no internal links pointing to it, making it difficult for search engine spiders to discover and index it.
Google Search Console
A free Google tool, formerly called Google Webmaster Tools, that lets website owners monitor site health, check indexing status, and identify technical SEO issues.
