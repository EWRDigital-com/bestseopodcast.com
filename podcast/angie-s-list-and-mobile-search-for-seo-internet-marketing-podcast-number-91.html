Questions this episode answers

How much of Google search volume comes from mobile in 2011?

According to the episode, mobile search accounts for 10% of Google search volume at the time of recording. Data from Performix cited in the episode projects that figure could grow to 16% of total clicks by September 2011, a rise of 6 percentage points in roughly nine months.

How do you get high quality inbound links for SEO?

The hosts recommend producing great content that others naturally want to link to, submitting to directories like UBL.org, and creating a free TED.com profile which offers PR8 follow links with anchor text you control. They caution against relying on automated programs like SEO PowerSuite in place of manual work.

What is a QR code and how is it used in mobile marketing?

A QR code is a two-dimensional barcode made of dots and squares. You scan it with a smartphone camera or barcode reader app and it returns information such as a website, Twitter feed, or Google Places listing. The episode notes them appearing on badges, business cards, flyers, magazine ads, and even T-shirts.

Should you create a separate mobile website for SEO?

The hosts and their guest Charles Lewis suggest mobile specific sites may become less necessary as smartphones like the HTC Evo and Android tablets display full websites well. The advice is to design mindfully for both screens rather than building two separate sites, while still targeting mobile keywords in your SEO strategy.

Terms defined in this episode
QR code
A two-dimensional barcode made of dots and squares that a smartphone camera or barcode reader app scans to retrieve internet content such as a website, Twitter feed, or Google Places listing.
Seven pack
The local map results labeled A through G that appear on a Google search results page, which the hosts call one of the most important parts of the search engine results page.
Bounce rate
A metric reflecting how quickly users leave a page; the episode notes it may be one of roughly 200 Google ranking factors, but a high bounce rate is only a problem if users are leaving without converting.
PR8
A PageRank score of 8, cited in the episode as the authority level of TED.com, making a free profile there a valuable source of follow links with controllable anchor text.
