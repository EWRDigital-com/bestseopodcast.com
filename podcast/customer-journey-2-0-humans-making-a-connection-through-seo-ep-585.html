Key takeaways
  • Recent, relevant reviews matter more than old ones because Google now surfaces current reviews to help people make purchasing decisions.
  • Long-tail keyword phrases let smaller sites enter the conversation closer to the conversion point, where big-brand competition is weaker.
  • Enabling past customers to share testimonials and reviews accessibly is one of the highest-value actions a business can take online right now.

About this episode

In this podcast episode, We cover the evolving trends in online user behavior, particularly their shifting search patterns. We discuss strategies to adapt to these changes, emphasizing the importance of personalized approaches and the necessity of establishing online connections in a world where people yearn for meaningful interactions. Additionally, we covers the growing role of AI in bridging this connection gap.

Questions this episode answers

How has the customer journey changed in the age of AI and online search?

Most buyers now go about 75% through the customer journey before they contact anyone. They research independently, rely heavily on recent reviews and testimonials, and expect to find relevant content at every stage from broad awareness to final price comparison, all before raising their hand.

What did Google's helpful content update do to sites using mass AI-generated content?

Google's helpful content update knocked down many sites generating massive amounts of AI content, with some sites Matt Bertram observed dropping 30 to 50 percent in visibility. Google stated it is okay with AI supporting human communication, but the content still needs to reflect the human saying it.

How can smaller businesses compete against large brands in search?

By targeting long-tail keyword phrases further down the funnel, closer to the conversion, smaller sites can slide into the conversation where big-brand competition is thinner. Adding unique customer experience, testimonials, and original images to product pages instead of stock manufacturer content also helps differentiate.

Why do recent reviews matter more than older ones for SEO and conversions?

Google has begun surfacing current, relevant reviews to help people make purchasing decisions, treating older reviews as potentially outdated. Buyers also want to see recent reviews from people who may be like-minded or geographically similar to them before deciding to buy.

Terms defined in this episode
Intimacy economy
A term used by Matt Bertram to describe a shift away from the attention economy toward people seeking genuine human connection online, influencing how content and SEO should be approached.
Helpful content update
A Google algorithm update that targeted and reduced rankings for sites publishing large volumes of non-helpful or mass AI-generated content, with some sites losing 30 to 50 percent of visibility.
Long-tail keyword phrases
More specific, lower-competition search queries that represent buyers further down the funnel and closer to a purchase decision, where smaller sites can more realistically rank.
EEAT (Experience)
Google added Experience to its quality evaluation framework, meaning content reflecting real personal experience, such as customer testimonials and original photos, carries more weight in rankings.
