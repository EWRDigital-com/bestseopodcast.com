5 Content Marketing Goals You Should Have by Aaron Agius #324

Ep. 32431 min2016-06-14

Content marketing goals should follow a five-step progression from brand awareness through traffic, lead generation, conversion, and retention, with referrals added as a practical sixth goal.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Define a clear goal before creating content so every piece has direction and a desired action.
  • The website is the hub of all internet marketing where conversions, carts, and remarketing codes live.
  • CTAs should match the search intent and content topic for continuity to move visitors through the funnel.
  • Post-sale content marketing keeps customers engaged and makes upselling easier than acquiring new clients.
  • Turning existing customers into referral sources is a natural sixth content marketing goal beyond retention.
without well-defined goals, the content you're producing is little more than noise and not going to satisfy the needs of your customers

Questions this episode answers

Why is the website considered the foundation of content marketing?

The hosts explain that the website is the hub of all internet marketing. It is where conversions happen, where the cart lives, where email sign-ups are collected, and where remarketing code is placed. All content marketing efforts should direct traffic back to the site.

How should CTAs be written to improve lead conversion?

CTAs should be based on the type of content the visitor consumed. If someone reads about ductless AC units, the CTA should say something like install your ductless AC unit so the action is consistent with both what they searched and the content they read.

Why does content marketing matter for existing customers, not just new prospects?

The hosts note that once someone is paying you it is easier to get them to pay more than to acquire a new client. Post-sale content such as exclusive offers, email campaigns, and video tutorials keeps customers engaged and creates opportunities to upsell additional products or services.

once the customer is yours, the content marketing shouldn't end. This is the stage where you're creating custom content specific to your existing customers in order to churn, nurture, and nurture those relationships and keep them coming back for more
