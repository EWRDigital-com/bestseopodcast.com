Questions this episode answers

Which three search engines should content marketers optimize for beyond just Google?

According to this episode, the three important search engines for content marketing are Google, YouTube, and Pinterest. YouTube is the second largest search engine with a billion users, and Pinterest has 200 million active monthly users, making both significant traffic sources worth dedicated SEO attention.

How should you optimize a YouTube video for search?

The episode recommends targeting platform-specific keywords in the video title, description, and transcription. Using predictive search on YouTube helps identify phrases your audience uses. Including a full website link back to the related blog post or page is also advised, and informational videos in the 10 to 12 minute range tend to rank better.

What content formats work best on Pinterest for SEO?

On Pinterest, images are the most critical element. Vertical pictures with copy overlay and pins with multiple photos perform well. The episode also recommends including a targeted keyword description, considering hashtags, and always linking back to your website so Pinterest can serve as a lead generation source.

What is predictive search and why does it matter for YouTube and Pinterest SEO?

Predictive search is the auto-suggest feature that appears as you type a query, showing related phrases. The episode advises typing different versions of your keywords in YouTube and Pinterest search bars to discover the exact terms your audience uses, then incorporating those terms into your video and pin metadata.

Terms defined in this episode
Predictive search
The feature that suggests search phrases as a user types, visible at the top of the search bar. The episode recommends using it on YouTube and Pinterest to find keyword variations your audience actually searches.
Pillar content
Important content designed to drive significant traffic and link attraction. The episode says pillar content should include image and video to maximize its impact.
Keyword stuffing
The practice of overloading content with keywords. The episode notes Google has updated its algorithm against this, but suggests YouTube has not penalized it as heavily.
