Questions this episode answers

Why should a CMS support keyword-rich URLs for SEO?

Google gives ranking value to words in a URL, so gibberish or parameter-heavy URLs hurt performance. A CMS like WordPress, with the right plugins, turns an article title such as Blue One-Armed Widgets directly into a clean, keyword-rich URL that Google credits.

How can Google Analytics help a local business decide whether to sell nationally?

By filtering visits by country and territory, you can see which states already send traffic even without targeted optimization. The episode shows a Houston rattan furniture client getting 70 California visits monthly despite no California SEO, revealing clear expansion opportunities.

What are the key CMS features an SEO needs according to this episode?

The episode lists title tag customization with rule-based patterns, static keyword-rich URLs, per-page meta tag control, the ability to use custom HTML tags such as nofollow, internal anchor text flexibility, and intelligent categorization structures as the core SEO requirements for any CMS.

Terms defined in this episode
Content Management System (CMS)
A platform that lets site owners edit website content without learning web design software like Dreamweaver. Joomla and WordPress are examples discussed in this episode.
Anchor text
The clickable, often colored and underlined words in a hyperlink. The episode stresses that anchor text should contain keywords because search engines use it as a relevance signal.
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A term used by eWebStyle meaning optimization that starts at the search engine result page, including getting phone numbers or other information to appear directly in results.
Perception protection
An informal term coined in this episode for monitoring and managing what online directories and review sites say about a business, including using Google Alerts and review platforms.
