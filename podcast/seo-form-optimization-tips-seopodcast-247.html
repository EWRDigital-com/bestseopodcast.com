SEO Form Optimization Tips - #SEOpodcast 247

Episode36 min2014-09-24

Optimizing landing page forms with fewer fields, privacy statements, above-the-fold placement, and compelling button text increases form submissions, with each added field reducing conversions by roughly 10 percent.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Place lead forms in the upper right corner above the fold to maximize submissions.
  • Each additional form field reduces submissions by roughly 10 percent, so keep forms short.
  • Include a privacy line on forms to build trust, especially in sensitive industries like legal.
  • Use a two-page lead form so the first short form captures the lead even if the second is skipped.
  • Replace or brand your CAPTCHA, or use a honeypot, to avoid driving away legitimate form submitters.

Questions this episode answers

How many fields should a landing page form have?

The hosts recommend as few fields as possible, noting roughly a 10 percent drop in form submissions per field added. For most businesses, name, phone, email, and a comments box is enough, with any extra questions saved for a follow-up call or a second form page.

Where should a lead form be placed on a landing page?

According to the episode, the best converting placement is the upper right-hand corner of the page, above the fold. A client who moved their form lower saw daily submissions drop from five or six to about two, and recovered once the form was moved back up.

Should I use CAPTCHA on my forms?

The hosts suggest avoiding standard CAPTCHA because it acts like an extra field that makes forms intimidating. They recommend either a branded CAPTCHA, where the challenge text reinforces your brand, or a honeypot field, which stops spam without adding friction for real visitors.

What should the submit button text say on a lead form?

The episode advises using compelling, action-oriented words consistent with your call to action, such as get a free quote or apply now, rather than generic words like submit. The button wording should match the action words already used in the page title to keep messaging consistent.

Terms defined in this episode
CVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
The hosts' term for making sure visitors who land on a site take the desired action, such as filling out a form.
Honeypot
A hidden form field technique used instead of CAPTCHA to catch spam bots without adding visible friction for real users.
Two-page lead form
A form strategy where a short first form captures core contact details and a longer second form collects additional information, ensuring at least partial lead data is saved.
Above the fold
The portion of a webpage visible without scrolling, where placing a lead form is said to produce the best conversion results.
