Questions this episode answers

What are shoppable posts and why do they matter for digital marketing in 2020?

Shoppable posts let users click on items inside a photo to purchase them directly. Platforms like Pinterest, Instagram, and Facebook are all moving into this space. The hosts highlight Pinterest as especially promising because it blends personal taste with buying intent, making conversion easier for both brands and consumers.

How important is page speed for SEO in 2020?

Page speed is described as one of the highest impact, lowest cost improvements a business can make. The hosts recommend fast hosting such as DigitalOcean or Google Cloud, building Progressive Web Apps, and adding AMP pages. They say Google visibly rewards faster sites even without heavy off page SEO work.

What is a Progressive Web App and why should marketers care?

A Progressive Web App (PWA) merges a traditional website with app functionality. Key benefits include offline access to content and the ability to send push notifications. The hosts note that besteopodcast.com was built as a PWA and ranked well quickly with minimal off page SEO effort.

Why is authentic video more effective than polished production video?

Data cited in the episode shows engagement and trust are three times higher with homemade style videos. The reasoning is that well produced content can feel intentional and less authentic, while off the cuff video is harder to fake, and audiences want to see behind the curtain of a business or personality.

Terms defined in this episode
Progressive Web App (PWA)
A website and app merger that allows users to download content for offline use and enables push notifications, combining benefits of both formats.
AMP Page
An Accelerated Mobile Page is a reduced content page with less media that loads faster, which Google rewards with better rankings.
Shoppable Post
A social media post where users can click directly on items in a photo to purchase them, reducing friction in the conversion process.
Three Pack
The top three local business listings shown in Google Maps results, which the hosts say captures about 30% of search market volume.
