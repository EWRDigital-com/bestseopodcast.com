YouTube can boost local SEO through geotagging videos, adding NAP in descriptions and audio, associating videos with Google Places and Google Plus local pages, and embedding videos on your own site.

Key takeaways

  • Put your website link at the very start of the video description so it appears above the fold on the YouTube page.
  • State your business name, address, and phone number in the video's audio so Google's transcription indexes your location.

Questions this episode answers

How do I geotag a YouTube video for local SEO?

After uploading, go into YouTube's video manager and find the advanced section. Search your address there to bring up a map with coordinates. If the pin is in the wrong spot, drag it to the correct location and save. This tells YouTube where the video was recorded, which matters for local search.

What should I put in a YouTube video description for local SEO?

Start with a link to your business website, then add a summary of the video. Include a transcription if possible, and close with your business name, address, city, and phone number. Also mention your location and category in the tags separated by commas.

Should I host videos on YouTube or on my own server for SEO?

The hosts recommend YouTube over self-hosting. YouTube saves your storage space, offers more sharing options, is mobile friendly, and is more familiar to users than alternatives like Vimeo. You then embed the YouTube video on your own site page and promote that page.

How do I connect YouTube videos to my Google local presence?

Add videos to your Google Places listing, where you can include up to 10 videos. Also log into Google Plus, go to Pages, manage your local page, and post the video URL there. Both are separate Google properties that give your video additional indexed locations.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. For local SEO, this should appear in video annotations, the video description, and be spoken aloud in the video audio so Google can transcribe and index it.
Geotagging a video
Using YouTube's video manager advanced settings to pin your business address coordinates to a video, telling YouTube where the video was produced for local search purposes.
YouTube channel optimization
Writing a description for your entire YouTube channel page, linking it to your website and main social media profiles, and keeping the description consistent with your website bio.
