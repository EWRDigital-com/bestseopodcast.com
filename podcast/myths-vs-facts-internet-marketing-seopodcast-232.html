Myths vs Facts - Internet Marketing - #SEOpodcast 232

Episode37 min2014-05-28

Local SEO success requires consistent NAP citations, fast page speed, strategic link building, and annual site audits, not just building a site and waiting for traffic.

Key takeaways
  • Consistent name, address, and phone number across all directories including Yelp, Foursquare, and Google Local is essential for local SEO.
  • Page speed improvements can move a site from position five to position one and increase contact form submissions by 30 percent.
  • Link building is not dead but must be strategic, natural, and relevant, avoiding mass directory submissions that can trigger manual spam actions.
  • Social media does not replace link building but drives visits, time on site, and conversions that indirectly improve rankings.
  • New sites under construction should be locked down with a login to prevent beta pages from outranking the live site in Google.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. Must be identical across all online directories and listings for local SEO consistency.
301 redirect
A permanent redirect that tells search engines and users a page has moved to a new URL, preserving link equity when a site migrates.
Manual spam action
A penalty Google applies when it detects unnatural link building or thin content, requiring a disavow file and outreach to remove.
Robots.txt
A file that blocks Googlebot from crawling specific pages but does not prevent pages already indexed from appearing in search results.
