  • Focus each page on one clear topic so Google and users understand what it covers.

Questions this episode answers

Why should SEO not be the only marketing tactic for a new business?

SEO takes time, roughly five to eight months, before a new site starts ranking. The hosts recommend running PPC alongside SEO to generate traffic in the short term, and also using social media and email newsletters if you have a list, so you are not dependent on one channel.

How does site architecture affect SEO and inbound links?

Good information architecture helps users find content quickly and makes it accessible on mobile devices. When users can find what they need easily, they are more likely to link to it. Poor structure creates a bad impression even before visitors read the content, reducing the chance they will share or link to it.

What does it mean to keep each page focused in SEO?

Each page should target one clear topic. The hosts use a plumber example: a page about water heater repair should not also cover remodeling, water repair, and faucets. Mixing topics confuses Google about what the page is about and dilutes its relevance.

Can semantic markup be applied to YouTube videos?

The hosts say YouTube limits the HTML you can use, so semantic markup cannot be added directly on YouTube. However, when embedding a YouTube video on your own page, you can wrap it in a div tag and apply semantic markup such as a video object type, which may add SEO value to the hosting page.

Terms defined in this episode
Information architecture
How content is organized and presented on a site so users and search engines can find and index it correctly, not just how visually appealing the design is.
Deep links
Links from high-authority, big brand name sites pointing to pages with great content on your site, showing trust and value that Google rewards with higher rankings.
Semantic markup
HTML tagging that gives search engines structured context about content on a page, such as labeling an embedded video as a video object with a title.
Search bait
Having the most current, accurate, and relevant information on a topic so search engines are more likely to surface your site in results, especially for fast-changing topics like sports scores or health news.
