Key takeaways
  • 99% of websites need setup work before an SEO campaign can begin, including keyword review and meta tag fixes.
  • A bounce happens when a visitor lands on a page irrelevant to their search and immediately leaves, wasting ad spend.
  • Google local listings can drive significant first-page traffic even without strong organic rankings.

Questions this episode answers

Why do SEO agencies charge a setup fee?

According to eWebStyle, 99% of sites they review are not ready for an SEO campaign. The setup fee covers fixing meta tags, adding content, correcting robot.txt files, and choosing the right keywords so the site is prepared before optimization work begins.

What is a bounce in SEO terms?

A bounce means a visitor arrives at a website and immediately leaves because the page is irrelevant or unhelpful. The hosts use the example of landing on a corporate training page when searching for comedy shows, finding nothing relevant, and going back to search results.

What is Google local and why does it matter for local businesses?

Google local is a section of Google search results showing nearby businesses with a map and lettered listings. The hosts note that a Houston comedy theater gets its biggest traffic from appearing in Google local for the term Houston Comedy, even without a top organic ranking.

Why should businesses insist on Google Analytics access from their SEO company?

Google Analytics tracks every traffic source, keyword, browser, and geographic location for free. The hosts recommend that business owners request their own access to the account so they can independently monitor their website metrics and verify the SEO work being done.

Terms defined in this episode
Bounce
When a visitor arrives at a webpage and immediately leaves because the content is not relevant to what they searched for, resulting in wasted traffic and spend.
Landing page
A page built to target a specific keyword or small set of keywords, ensuring visitors who click through from search results find content directly relevant to their search.
Sitemap
A text file listing all pages and links on a website that helps search engine crawlers and spiders index every page, including pages that may have lost internal links.
Google local
A section within Google search results that displays nearby businesses alongside a map with lettered markers, separate from both paid sponsored links and regular organic results.
