Questions this episode answers

What is Google Alerts and how can businesses use it for reputation management?

Google Alerts lets you set up notifications for any phrase, such as your company name or a competitor. Google sends you a link whenever it indexes new content containing that phrase. It can help you find customers tweeting about you, respond to complaints, and thank positive mentions.

Should a business focus on social media or search SEO?

According to this episode, businesses should maintain both. Social media helps manage customers, brand image, and customer service, while search engines are still where people go to find products and services. Dropping search in favor of social is listed as a key mistake to avoid.

What are the biggest don'ts in social media marketing?

The episode covers several don'ts: do not spam on social sites, avoid blatant marketing tactics, do not let social profiles go stale, do not badmouth competitors, do not engage in social climbing, and do not try to do too much social too fast without a plan.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Alerts
A Google tool that notifies you via a link anytime Google indexes new content containing a phrase you specify, useful for tracking your brand, competitors, or any keyword.
Perception Protection
A phrase coined by the hosts to describe online reputation management, monitoring and managing how a brand is perceived across the web.
TweetDeck
A social media monitoring tool that can surface tweets and mentions referencing your brand or content, used by the hosts to find people tweeting about their podcast.
Social climbing
The practice of pursuing celebrity or high-profile social media accounts in hopes of gaining followers or attention, which the article discussed in the episode warns against.
