Key takeaways
  • Make sure your website is ready to receive traffic before driving people to it, just like stocking shelves before a grand opening.
  • Use selective tweets with the hashtag FB to control which tweets also post to Facebook, avoiding audience bombardment.
  • Link social media ads directly to a matching landing page, not your homepage, so visitors find exactly what was promised.
  • TweetDeck aggregates Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Foursquare in one place, making it easier to post consistently across platforms.
  • Facebook pay-per-click ads can be cheaper than Google AdWords and pair well with a dedicated Facebook landing page.

Questions this episode answers

Why should a business link all social media profiles to the same website?

Chuck explains that when all social profiles link to the same site, those links become incoming links that boost the ranking of that site. Consistency also ensures visitors land on a page ready to convert them rather than getting lost across unrelated pages.

What is selective tweets and how does it help social media marketing?

Selective tweets lets you tag a tweet with the hashtag FB so it also posts to your Facebook wall. If you do not add the tag, the tweet stays only on Twitter. This prevents bombarding Facebook friends with every tweet while still sharing the most relevant content across both platforms.

What makes WordPress a good CMS for SEO?

Chuck says WordPress lets you create clean, readable URLs instead of long PHP strings, embeds titles and meta information that you simply update, produces lighter pages that load faster, and is intuitive enough for a novice. Page speed was noted as a recent Google algorithm factor.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A term used in the episode to describe optimizing a site for visitors who arrive from search engines, ensuring calls to action and conversion paths are in place before traffic arrives.
Selective Tweets
A tool that posts a tweet to Facebook only when the tweet is tagged with the hashtag FB, giving the user control over which tweets cross-post to their Facebook wall.
TweetDeck
A social media management tool that aggregates multiple accounts including Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, and Foursquare into one dashboard for coordinated posting.
CMS (Content Management System)
A platform such as WordPress, Joomla, or ModX that allows users to build and manage website pages without deep coding knowledge, though SEO-friendliness and ease of use vary by platform.
