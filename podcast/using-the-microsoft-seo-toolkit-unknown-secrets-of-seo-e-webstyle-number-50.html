Questions this episode answers

What does the Microsoft SEO Toolkit actually do?

After you download the Microsoft IIS Website Platform and the toolkit itself, you enter a URL and it spiders the site like a search engine would. It then reports on links, validation violations, duplicate content, and page load performance, with plain-English explanations and recommended fixes for each issue.

Why use the Microsoft SEO Toolkit if you already use Google Analytics?

The toolkit shows inbound links for a site, information Google stopped displaying publicly. It also consolidates features spread across Google Analytics, code validation tools, and other web development platforms into one free interface, making it a useful complement rather than a replacement.

What keyword density does the podcast recommend to avoid keyword stuffing?

The hosts recommend staying between 1 to 3 percent keyword density, meaning your target keyword should appear 1 to 3 times per every 100 words of text. Exceeding that becomes obvious to readers and search engines and is considered a borderline black hat technique.

Terms defined in this episode
CVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A practice focused on what visitors do after arriving at a website, including how to convert them into customers or newsletter subscribers, and ensuring key pages are easy to navigate to.
Keyword stuffing
Placing target keywords far too frequently in page content so it becomes obvious to readers and search engines, considered a borderline black hat technique that all search engines penalize.
Perception protection
A concept described as brand management for websites dealing with bad reviews, becoming more important as review sites like Yelp and CitySearch grow in popularity.
Keyword density
The ratio of a target keyword's appearances to total word count on a page, with 1 to 3 percent per 100 words recommended to avoid keyword stuffing.
