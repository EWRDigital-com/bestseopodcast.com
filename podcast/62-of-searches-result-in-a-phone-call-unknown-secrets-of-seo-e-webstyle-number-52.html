Questions this episode answers

What percentage of internet searches result in a phone call?

According to Ken Roberts, 62% of people who search the web for something follow it up with a phone call. He warns that businesses not offering a phone option are leaving money on the table, even if customers could otherwise complete an order entirely online.

What is an SEO citation and how does it work?

A citation is a mention of your business name and location in online content that contains no link back to your site. Ken Roberts explains that search engines can now associate that text with your business entity and treat it similarly to a backlink, especially in competitive niches.

What are the three get thems in SEO?

The three get thems are: get qualified visitors to your website, get them to take action when they arrive such as buying, calling, or filling out a form, and get them to come back for more. The hosts emphasize the third is often undervalued because repeat customers are the easiest revenue source.

Terms defined in this episode
Citation
A mention of a business name and location in online content that has no hyperlink back to the site but that search engines can associate with the business entity as a ranking signal.
LSI algorithm
Latent Semantic Indexing algorithm used by search engines to read and understand web page content the way a human brain reads, moving beyond simple keyword density analysis.
The three get thems
A framework covering three goals: get qualified visitors to your site, get them to take action, and get them to return for more business.
Theming
The search engine practice of evaluating all pages on a domain together to determine the overall topic or industry the site represents, used to decide relevance for queries.
