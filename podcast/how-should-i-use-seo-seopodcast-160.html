How should I use SEO? - #seopodcast 160

Episode30 min2012-10-10

Social signals from Facebook and Twitter do help Google rankings, physical addresses are required for Google Places listings, and meta descriptions should reword rather than duplicate page titles to avoid over-optimization penalties.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Use keyword-rich full URLs when sharing content rather than URL shorteners to preserve the SEO value of the link.
  • A WordPress plugin showing recent posts on the homepage keeps it refreshed without requiring daily manual updates to static pages.
  • Pages linked directly from the homepage are treated as more important by Google because they are fewer clicks away.
  • Repeating a page title verbatim in the meta description risks triggering Google over-optimization penalties and is better reworded instead.
Google has made it very clear that how many clicks away from the homepage defines how important a page is.

Questions this episode answers

Does a business without a physical address qualify for a Google Places page?

No. Google requires a physical address and will not accept a P.O. box. The hosts suggest using an executive suite address with a suite number, which is currently acceptable, though they note Google may crack down on that practice over time.

Do I need to update my homepage daily if I publish new blog posts every day?

Not manually. Installing a WordPress plugin that displays your latest one or two posts on the homepage keeps it refreshed automatically. The hosts also recommend adding direct links from the homepage to older popular articles so important content stays fewer clicks away.

Terms defined in this episode
Social signals
Shares, likes, and tweets that Google and Bing use as ranking factors, incorporated via an algorithm change in 2011 according to both companies.
CIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A term used in this episode for optimizing the meta description to compel searchers who see it on the results page to click through to the site.
Google Places
A local business listing service requiring a verified physical address, which the hosts note has transitioned to Google Plus local pages.
Click depth
The number of clicks required to reach a page from the homepage, which Google uses to determine how important that page is.
