About this episode

Branded vs. Non-Branded Traffic: What’s the Difference?How should you treat branded vs non-branded traffic and search strategies? Unpack the things to consider when it comes to targeting, attribution, and more.

Questions this episode answers

Should I bid on my own brand name in paid search?

Yes, especially if you are not ranking organically in search results or the map pack. Bidding on your brand name protects that traffic, lets you control where users land, and is useful when running promotions that need a dedicated landing page separate from your main site.

What is brand cannibalization in search and how do I avoid it?

Brand cannibalization happens when branded paid search spending pulls clicks away from your organic results. The hosts recommend benchmarking both paid and organic branded traffic before launching campaigns, then monitoring whether turning on branded paid ads causes a relative drop in organic traffic.

Why should branded and non-branded traffic be reported separately?

Combining them produces a blended cost-per-conversion that looks lower than it really is, because branded visitors convert far more easily than cold traffic. Separating the two shows the true cost of acquiring new customers and helps measure how well brand awareness efforts like radio or billboards are working.

Is it worth bidding on a competitor's brand name?

The hosts say it is rarely worthwhile. While the cost per click may be low, those searchers have already chosen a brand and are too far down the funnel to convert easily, making the overall campaign expensive relative to the actual opportunity to win a new customer.

Terms defined in this episode
branded traffic
Search visits from users who type a specific company or brand name into the search query, driven by brand awareness, referrals, or traditional advertising.
non-branded traffic
Search visits from users using generic or category keywords that do not include a specific brand name, representing cold or top-of-funnel audiences.
brand cannibalization
A situation where launching branded paid search ads causes a relative decrease in clicks to existing organic search results for the same brand terms.
micro moments
A term Google uses to describe specific points in a customer journey where a user actively searches for information about a product or service, representing targeted moments to reach potential buyers with ads.
