Key takeaways
  • Avoid dashes in domain names as they can look cheap and raise caution flags when seeking links and citations.
  • Use only lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens in subfolders and file names to avoid URL interpretation errors.
  • Pick either HTTP or HTTPS consistently; mixing them can lead to a Google penalty.
  • Use the vertical bar (pipe) in title tags to separate keywords, and keep titles under 70 characters.
Google ignores the dashes, but not the vertical bar.

Questions this episode answers

Is it bad to have a dash in my domain name for SEO?

According to the article discussed in this episode, dashes in domain names look cheap and may raise a caution flag when you reach out for links and citations. The hosts partially disagree, arguing that quality content and a strong brand matter more than whether a domain contains a hyphen.

In their words

Create great content. Something free. Maybe something entertaining. Something that people would want to link to. That's the white hat method to go.

Terms defined in this episode
Pipe (vertical bar) in title tags
The vertical bar character used to separate keywords in a page title tag. According to the episode, Google ignores dashes in this context but does not ignore the vertical bar, making it the preferred separator.
Negative keywords
Words added to a PPC campaign such as AdWords or Bing to prevent ads from showing for unwanted searches, typically including terms like free or DIY, helping maximize return on ad spend.
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization. Refers to what you do with a visitor after they arrive from a search engine, focusing on conversions rather than traffic or rankings.
Google Engage
A program for agencies that participate in AdWords, offering perks such as a dedicated rep, faster ad approvals, and assistance managing ads.
