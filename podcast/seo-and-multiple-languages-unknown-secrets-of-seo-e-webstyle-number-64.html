Questions this episode answers

Can I put keywords in multiple languages on one English web page for SEO?

It depends on competition. If no dedicated foreign-language page exists for that keyword, adding it to an English page can earn a ranking. Once a properly optimized native-language page appears, Google will likely favor it. Also, visitors who land on a page in the wrong language will bounce, limiting real value.

Why does Google penalize or ignore text inside images for SEO?

Google's bots cannot reliably parse text that is embedded inside image files. Matt Cutts noted they considered OCR on images but decided against it, reasoning that sites not following good web design standards should not rank well. Using actual HTML text in H1 and H2 tags is the recommended approach.

Does having keywords in a URL actually help SEO rankings?

Yes. A URL containing the target keyword gives a ranking advantage over a numeric CMS-generated URL. Combined with having the keyword in the domain name, there are effectively two additional ranking signals. WordPress is cited as improving its SEO-friendly URL generation.

What is SEVO and why does it matter for a website homepage?

SEVO stands for search engine visitor optimization. It means optimizing the experience after a visitor arrives, not just getting them to the site. A homepage loaded with loosely related articles can confuse visitors about what the business does and lacks the calls to action needed to convert passive visitors into engaged ones.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
Optimizing a website so that visitors who arrive from search engines are guided toward a clear action, such as submitting an email or calling, rather than simply landing and leaving.
Backlink spamming
Posting low-quality or irrelevant links across blogs, forums, and other sites purely to generate inbound links, a practice that can harm a site's reputation and rankings.
SEO-friendly URL
A URL that contains descriptive keywords related to the page content rather than numeric or random strings generated by a CMS, giving a ranking advantage in search results.
