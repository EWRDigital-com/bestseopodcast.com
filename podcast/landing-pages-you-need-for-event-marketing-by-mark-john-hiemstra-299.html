Landing Pages You Need for Event Marketing by Mark John Hiemstra #299

Ep. 29931 min2015-11-16

Event marketing landing pages need a unique value proposition, hero shot, benefits list, social proof, and a single focused call to action to convert visitors before, during, and after an event.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • A landing page should have one goal and one call to action, not multiple competing options.
  • Build awareness at least 90 days before an event by releasing content in stages to build anticipation.
  • After an event, repurpose the landing page with photos and new CTAs so it stays fresh and continues ranking.
  • Social proof, meaning tweets, check-ins, and hashtag feeds from real attendees, acts as digital word of mouth.
  • Testimonial videos can supplement the hero shot and give prospects a reason to attend or buy.

Questions this episode answers

What landing page elements does an event marketing page need?

According to this episode, an event landing page needs five things: a unique value proposition explaining why people should attend, a hero shot image or video, a list of benefits for attendees, social proof from past participants, and a single clear call to action with supporting reasons to act now.

How far in advance should you start promoting an event landing page?

The hosts say you should start promoting at least 90 days before the event. They compare it to musicians releasing a single months before an album, then a video, then a trailer, building anticipation through a series of emails and announcements leading up to the date.

What should you do with an event landing page after the event is over?

The episode recommends adding photos from the event, placing a secondary CTA for early registration for the next occurrence, and keeping the page live. This keeps it fresh, maintains rankings, and lets you remarket to the audience list already built up around that page.

What is social proof in the context of event marketing landing pages?

Social proof, as explained in this episode, is any social media activity generated by people other than the organizer, such as tweets, Instagram photos, check-ins, or hashtag feeds from past attendees. It acts as digital word of mouth and co-signs the event for prospects visiting the landing page.

Terms defined in this episode
Unique Value Proposition (UVP)
A clear, prominent statement on the landing page explaining why someone should attend your event or choose your product over others in the same space.
Hero Shot
An image or video on a landing page that conveys a sense of what the product, service, or event experience will be like, giving visitors a visual aid.
Social Proof
Any social media activity about your event, product, or service that came from other people, such as tweets, check-ins, or hashtag feeds, functioning as digital word of mouth.
FOMO (Fear of Missing Out)
A tactic used in calls to action, such as limited seating or first-purchase bonuses, to give prospects an urgent reason to register or buy now rather than later.
