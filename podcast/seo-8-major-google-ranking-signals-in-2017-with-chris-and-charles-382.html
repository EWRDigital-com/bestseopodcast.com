Questions this episode answers

What are the eight major Google ranking signals in 2017?

The eight signals are link score, anchor text relevance, keyword usage, content length, content comprehensiveness, page speed, mobile friendliness, and click-through rate. They fall into four categories: backlinks, content, technical SEO, and user experience, as outlined in an article from Search Engine Land posted by SEO Power Suite.

How should I build links to improve my Google ranking?

Target high-quality, low-hanging-fruit links from manufacturers, distributors, and partners in your industry. For example, if you sell a branded product, get listed with a full profile and a link on that brand's website. Avoid spammy links, which hurt your link score.

Why does anchor text variety matter for SEO?

Using only your exact target key phrase as anchor text can trigger an algorithm penalty. You need a mix of anchor text types, such as the domain name, click here, the company name, and longer phrases, so Google does not flag over-optimization of a single match.

How fast does Google expect pages to load, and how can I check?

Google expects pages to load in two seconds or less and has officially confirmed speed is a ranking signal. The hosts recommend using Pingdom to see how long each individual component of your page takes to load, and suggest taking Google PageSpeed Insights results with a grain of salt.

Terms defined in this episode
Link Score
A measure made up of every incoming link's individual quality score and the total number of links to a site, used by Google as a ranking signal.
Anchor Text Relevance
The descriptive words used in a hyperlink that tell Google what the linked page is about; too much exact-match anchor text can trigger a ranking penalty.
Rank Brain
An artificial intelligence additive to Google's algorithm that analyzes results with good user satisfaction metrics and identifies their common features to assess content comprehensiveness.
Shiko
An E-Web Results branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows.
