6 Huge B2B Social Media Marketing Mistakes to Avoid by Tamara Weintraub #339

Ep. 33936 min2016-09-23

B2B companies should avoid six social media mistakes including targeting wrong platforms, ignoring paid promotion, using one-and-done content strategies, and keeping social media separate from SEO efforts.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Focus limited social media resources on the platform your specific B2B audience actually uses and understand how they use it.
  • Repurpose one article into many posts: six headers become six tweets, six intro paragraphs become six Facebook posts.
  • Paid social ads let you reach audiences beyond existing fans and followers, something organic posts cannot do.
  • Social profiles must stay active, current, and visually consistent with your website or you lose credibility.
  • Keeping social media separate from SEO is a mistake because social presence directly affects how prospects research your company.

About this episode

There are many ways marketers can use social media. Here are some of the biggest social media marketing mistakes to avoid, and what to do instead.

Questions this episode answers

What are the six B2B social media marketing mistakes to avoid?

According to Tamara Weintraub's article discussed on the podcast, the six mistakes are: dedicating resources to the wrong platforms, having a narrow definition of social media marketing, having a one-and-done content strategy, avoiding new content formats like video, not paying to play with paid ads, and keeping social media separate from SEO.

Why do B2B companies need to pay for social media advertising?

Organic posts only reach existing fans and their friends. Paying for sponsored or boosted posts lets you target people who match your demographic but have never interacted with your brand. The hosts stress tracking goals like sign-ups or phone calls and not just chasing reach numbers.

How can a B2B business repurpose a single article for social media?

The hosts suggest turning each of the article's section headers into tweets with a supporting link, and using each intro paragraph as a Facebook post with a link back. One article can produce six tweets and six Facebook posts, totaling twelve pieces of content from a single source.

Why should B2B social media profiles stay consistent with the company website?

Prospects research companies by moving from the website to social profiles. If branding, logos, or taglines differ, or if social accounts show outdated posts and few followers, credibility is lost. All profiles should reflect the same current look, messaging, and information as the website.

Terms defined in this episode
Shikos
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows.
One-and-done content strategy
Publishing a piece of content only once and never repurposing or redistributing it, described in the episode as a major B2B social media mistake.
Pay to play
Investing in paid advertising or sponsored content on social platforms to reach both existing followers and new target audiences beyond what organic posts can access.
Expanded text ads
A Google AdWords ad format larger than standard text ads, with more characters and an extra line of space, set to replace standard text ads after January 31st according to the episode.
