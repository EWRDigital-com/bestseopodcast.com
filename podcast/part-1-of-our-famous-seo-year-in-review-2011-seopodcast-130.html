Key takeaways
  • Running both PPC and organic SEO together is recommended, as turning off PPC can hurt overall results.
  • Google Local Places rankings depend heavily on reviews collected within the last 12 months.
  • The Panda update penalized sites using duplicate or scraped content, including repurposed podcast transcripts.

Questions this episode answers

Should I run PPC and organic SEO at the same time?

Yes. The hosts recommend doing both PPC and organic SEO together. They cite a test where someone turned off PPC and tracked the impact with statistics before turning it back on, suggesting that running both simultaneously produces better overall results than relying on one alone.

How does Google Local Places ranking work and what factors matter most?

Google Local Places rankings weigh reviews, incoming links relevant to your business category, accurate address and local phone number, and the quality of the website your Places page links to. Reviews collected within the last 12 months are especially important for appearing in the A through G local listing.

What was the Google Panda update and how did it affect SEO?

The Panda update targeted sites using duplicate, scraped, or reposted content. The hosts described how their own SEO page was penalized because it used content from their podcast, which Google treated as scraped. Replacing that content moved the page to the first page for a national term.

What was Google Boost and why did it have limitations for paid search advertisers?

Google Boost was an automated AdWords-based system tied to Google Local Places categories. Its key limitation was that advertisers could not control which keywords triggered their ads. Negative keywords could only be added by emailing Google, which sent an automated reply before eventually making the correction.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Boost
An automated Google advertising product linked to Local Places that used selected categories to trigger ads, without giving advertisers direct keyword control. It no longer existed by the time of this podcast.
Quality Score
A Google PPC metric that drops when a landing page lacks good SEO, causing the advertiser to be charged more per click.
Google Panda
A Google algorithm update that penalized sites for duplicate, scraped, or reposted content, including article marketing and content farms.
Search Visitor Optimization (Sevo)
A phrase coined on this podcast referring to what you do with visitors after they arrive at your website, including calls to action and unique selling propositions.
