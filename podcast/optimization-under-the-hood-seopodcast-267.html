Optimization Under the Hood - #SEOpodcast 267

Episode22 min2015-04-01

Schema markup, secure sites, image sitemaps, and optimized social profiles are among eight under-the-hood SEO techniques from Neil Patel that Chris Burris and Charles Lewis break down and debate on this episode.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Place images or videos on the fold rather than above it to force scrolling, increase time on site, and improve user engagement.
  • Monitor your link profile regularly before a penalty hits, request removal of spammy links, and disavow only if they are not removed.
  • Schema markup appears in more than a third of all Google search results, yet only 0.3% of internet domain names use it.
  • Optimize social profiles consistently across all platforms and make sure your website actually links to the correct business pages.

Questions this episode answers

How long should you run an A-B test before making changes?

It depends on your traffic volume. If your site gets heavy daily traffic, let a full week pass before reviewing results. For low-traffic or small-budget sites, let a full month go by to gather enough data to be statistically significant.

Should you use the same keyword across your entire site?

Only if your entire site covers one topic. For businesses offering multiple unrelated services, it is better to optimize each section for its own relevant keywords, such as separate AC, heating, and plumbing pages, rather than forcing one phrase across the whole site.

Why should you add schema markup to your site?

Schema markup shows up in more than a third of all Google search results, showing Google places high weight on it. Despite that, only 0.3% of internet domain names use it, so marking up social profiles, addresses, phone numbers, events, images, and videos gives a clear ranking advantage.

Terms defined in this episode
Disavow
A tool used to tell Google to ignore specific low-authority or spam sites that are linking to your site, submitted after requesting those sites to remove their links.
Image Sitemap
An extension of your existing sitemap that adds extra information about images on your pages, helping search engines discover and index those images.
Schema Markup
Code added to a site to help search engines understand content such as addresses, phone numbers, events, and social profiles; it appears in more than a third of all Google search results.
Exact Match Domain (EMD)
A domain name that exactly matches a target keyword, described in the episode as an SEO spam technique of yesteryear, though Google still considers the URL a significant ranking factor.
