SEO Ideas for the Fall Season - #SEOpodcast 239

Episode37 min2014-07-23

To cleanse and recharge your SEO each fall, fix Webmaster Tools errors, update CMS plugins, improve page speed, build a content plan, and max out domain registration, among other targeted steps.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Fix errors in Webmaster Tools and check for manual spam penalties, submit your sitemap, and set geographic preferences while you are there.
  • Update CMS plugins and themes regularly to avoid security vulnerabilities that can lead to a hacked site.
  • Improving page speed by resizing images and condensing style sheets can directly lift rankings and increase form submissions.
  • Build a content plan with a schedule, let others preview content before posting, and ensure it is link worthy before promoting it.
  • Max out your domain registration period and keep it active to signal long term commitment to search engines.

Questions this episode answers

What should I do in Webmaster Tools to improve my SEO?

Fix crawl and indexing errors, check for manual spam penalties, submit your sitemap, and set your geographic preference. Many site owners either lack access or have never logged in, so those steps alone can reveal major issues holding back rankings.

How does page speed affect SEO and conversions?

The hosts describe a siding contractor client whose site jumped two positions and started receiving form submissions after they condensed style sheets and resized images. Page speed improvements affected both rankings and revenue, since siding is not a cheap product.

What is a link posse and how does it help content reach?

A link posse is a network of socially engaged people, around seven or eight, who share, tweet, and link to each other's content after it is posted. The key is keeping it authentic, not spammy, and only sharing content that is genuinely worth sharing.

Should I embed social media on my site instead of linking out to it?

Yes. Linking users away to Facebook or Twitter means they are gone and likely will not return, costing you both the social engagement and the potential sale. Embedding a Facebook like box or similar widget lets visitors interact with your social presence without leaving your site.

Terms defined in this episode
301 redirect
A redirect that forwards visitors and search engines from an old URL to a new one, carrying over link value and link juice, especially important when migrating a site to a new platform like WordPress.
Page speed insight
A Google developers tool where you enter a URL and receive a report on how slow your site loads and specific recommendations for improvement.
Responsive website optimization
Going beyond making a site display on multiple devices by tailoring each version for user behavior, such as tap-to-call on mobile and address links that open maps for navigation.
Manual spam penalty
A penalty applied directly by Google and visible in Webmaster Tools, displayed prominently and indicating what type of violation was found on the site.
