Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between on-site and off-site SEO work?

On-site SEO means making changes directly on your web pages, such as tweaking text, meta tags, and alt tags. Off-site SEO means building inbound links from other websites. Both are necessary for competitive terms, but off-site work alone can often rank a site for less competitive terms.

How important are alt tags for SEO?

Alt tags on images are read by Google even though the image content itself is not. Placing target keywords in alt tags, such as 'credit repair Houston,' signals to Google what subject the image and page are about, which can improve rankings for those keyword phrases.

Why should keyword-rich text not be placed inside images?

Google cannot read text that is embedded inside an image. Words like a city name in a testimonial image are invisible to search engines. Moving that text into actual HTML on the page lets Google count those words as relevant content and improves keyword relevance signals.

What is a drip campaign and how does it help a business?

A drip campaign is an automated sequence of emails sent to prospects or customers over time. According to this episode, the goal follows a visibility, credibility, profitability model: repeated contact makes you recognizable, tips demonstrate expertise, and together they move a contact toward becoming a paying client.

Terms defined in this episode
alt tag
A descriptor added to an image in a website that Google reads to understand what the image is about, making it a place to include target keywords.
on-site work
SEO changes made directly on a web page, including tweaking text, meta tags, title tags, description tags, and alt tags to improve search engine relevance.
off-site work
SEO efforts made outside a website, primarily building inbound links from other websites, which signal authority and relevance to search engines.
keyword density
A measure of how often a target keyword appears in the text of a web page, used to assess whether a page is sufficiently optimized for that term.
