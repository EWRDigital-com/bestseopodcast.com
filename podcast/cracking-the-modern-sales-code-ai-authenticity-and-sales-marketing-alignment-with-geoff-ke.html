Questions this episode answers

Why are cold sales funnels converting worse than they used to?

Geoff Ketterer says the market is in a trust recession. Buyers have been burned by bad programs and scams, so a 10-minute VSL and a single sales call no longer builds enough trust to close. Prospects now need more nurturing through formats like live webinars and podcasts before they buy.

How can AI help sales managers review calls at scale?

Geoff trains a custom GPT on sales call transcripts. The GPT analyzes each call, identifies what needs improvement, scores how the pitch landed, and assesses how the prospect felt. Results are automatically emailed to the rep who took the call, enabling coaching without the manager listening to every recording.

How do you use intent data to lower cost per acquisition on Meta ads?

Geoff's team pulls data from a source with about 200 million data points tied to specific Google search phrases, then uploads it into Meta as a custom audience. Ads only display to people who searched for the relevant product within the last seven days, cutting CPA by 40 to 70 percent across client accounts.

Terms defined in this episode
Trust recession
A market condition described by Geoff Ketterer where buyers have been burned by bad programs or scams, making them far less willing to purchase from a short VSL or a single sales call.
Setter
A salesperson who does outbound dialing to leads that entered a funnel but did not book an appointment, with the goal of getting them to schedule a call with a closer.
Custom audience
An audience uploaded into Meta ads built from hashed intent data, such as IP addresses or aggregator data, so ads only show to people who have already searched for a specific product or service.
Consultative sale
A selling approach Geoff recommends where the rep treats the interaction as a conversation rather than a scripted pitch, focusing on building trust and understanding the prospect's needs.
