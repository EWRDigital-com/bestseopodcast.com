Questions this episode answers

What were the biggest SEO themes of 2015 according to this podcast?

Content marketing dominated 2015, with the hosts describing it as the year content went from king to content god. Other major themes included mobile optimization, NAP consistency for local SEO, algorithm penalties from Penguin and Panda, and using PPC data to guide organic strategy.

How should businesses handle NAP information for local SEO?

Name, address, and phone number must be exactly the same everywhere online, including your website, social profiles like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, email signatures, Bing, Yelp, and yellow pages. Even formatting details like parentheses on phone numbers must be consistent across every listing.

How can PPC help with content strategy?

Running an open pay-per-click campaign generates data on which terms are searched most, which drain ad budget, and which drive conversions. Those high-performing terms deserve dedicated content pages, making PPC a practical research tool for guiding organic SEO content planning.

What is the recommended approach to guest blogging according to this episode?

Guest blogging is acceptable when it genuinely makes sense. Content must be relevant, placed on the right site, and of high quality. The hosts warn against publishing thin content on other sites just to get a backlink, which they suggest is what Google actually penalizes.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP consistency
Having the exact same name, address, and phone number, including identical formatting, across all online platforms, directories, and social profiles to support local SEO.
FOMO
Fear of missing out, an emotional trigger used in ad copy and meta descriptions to drive conversions, such as phrases like while supplies last.
Thin content
Low-quality, low-value content that can trigger a Panda penalty and fails to rank or engage users.
Digital PR
A content and link strategy discussed in podcast 268 focused on proactively monitoring your link profile and driving new search traffic through content.
