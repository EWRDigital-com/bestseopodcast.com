Retargeting and remarketing are powerful complements to SEO and PPC, but businesses should establish search campaigns first before adding banner remarketing to their internet marketing plan.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Set up SEO and PPC before adding remarketing, so search forms a solid foundation first.
  • Retargeting works by showing ads to prior site visitors on other websites, targeting people who already expressed interest.
  • Choose a remarketing provider with greater than 90 percent network reach so ads can follow users across the internet.

Questions this episode answers

What is retargeting or remarketing in digital marketing?

Retargeting, also called remarketing, means showing ads to people who already visited your site as they browse other websites. The hosts give examples like seeing a specific domain name ad after searching it on Register.com, or seeing a Texas Renaissance Festival banner on the Houston Chronicle site after visiting the festival page.

Should a small business start with remarketing or SEO and PPC first?

The hosts recommend building out SEO and PPC first, then adding remarketing in a later phase once the search campaign is running and proving ROI. The exception is businesses selling unknown products or services, like a dental spa, where people cannot search for something they have never heard of.

Why is the Google Keyword Tool returning fewer keyword results now?

According to an article discussed in the episode, Google limited its keyword tool to only show terms with demonstrated commercial characteristics, meaning terms tied to AdWords revenue. As an example cited, searching Facebook returned only 12 keyword suggestions, while another tool returned 2,200 for the same term.

How should a business pick a remarketing provider?

The hosts say to compare cost models, such as cost per click, and to prioritize network reach. They note that many providers have greater than 90 percent reach, meaning they can serve ads to users across a very wide range of websites, not just a narrow set of publisher sites.
