Questions this episode answers

Why can't you track podcast MP3 downloads with Google Analytics?

Google Analytics tracking code cannot be placed on an MP3 file. If listeners download the file from a platform like iTunes, that activity is outside the site owner's control. The hosts recommend moving podcast files to a platform like Podomatic.com, which provides its own download tracking.

How does tracking sales in Google Analytics help with marketing spend?

By tracking sales alongside country, keyword, and referral data, you can see which markets actually generate revenue. If all sales come from two countries out of five you are targeting, you can cut spending on the other three and invest more where conversions happen.

What is the benefit of tracking user reviews or blog comments as a Google Analytics goal?

User-generated content like reviews and comments adds content to your site without requiring you to produce it yourself. Tracking it as a goal shows how many visitors engage that way and gives you a pool of engaged users you can market to or respond to directly.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Analytics Goals
A feature in Google Analytics that lets site owners designate specific user actions, such as form submissions, file downloads, or feed subscriptions, as conversion events to monitor and measure.
Goal Funnel
A sequence of pages or steps in Google Analytics that a user follows toward completing a goal, allowing site owners to see where visitors drop off before converting.
robots.txt file
A file placed on a web server that tells search engine crawlers which pages or files they are allowed or not allowed to access, used in the episode to block an unwanted Asian spider from consuming bandwidth.
Podomatic
A podcast hosting platform mentioned in the episode as a way to track podcast download statistics that cannot be captured through Google Analytics on raw MP3 files.
