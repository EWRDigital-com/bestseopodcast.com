Questions this episode answers

Why should I check placements instead of pausing keywords in Google Display Network?

A single keyword may trigger ads on irrelevant or low-quality sites, driving up spend, but that same keyword may convert well on other placements. The fix is to exclude the bad placement and keep the keyword active rather than pausing it entirely.

Should I use image ads or text ads for remarketing on the Display Network?

Image ads are generally more effective for remarketing because the visual is familiar to someone who already visited your site. A text ad is easy to ignore, but a visual ad is harder to overlook and draws on brand recognition built during the first visit.

Why do Google Display Network campaigns get fewer conversions than search campaigns?

People on the Display Network are browsing, not actively searching for a product, so they are less ready to convert on the first click. However, if the display ad is a remarketing ad shown to a prior site visitor, the post-click conversion likelihood is higher than a cold search click.

Terms defined in this episode
Segmented remarketing lists
Separate audience lists built in AdWords based on specific user behavior, such as completing a purchase versus abandoning a cart, so each segment can be shown different ads.
Placement
A specific domain or website within the Google Display Network where an ad is shown. Advertisers can review, exclude, or target individual placements.
Bid adjustment
A percentage-based change to a keyword or campaign bid applied for a specific device, time of day, location, or audience segment to reflect where conversions are highest.
Display Network
Google's network of third-party websites where image and text ads are served to users who are browsing rather than actively searching, distinct from Google search results.
