Questions this episode answers

How long does SEO take to show results and what should I do in the meantime?

SEO is a slow process because link building, keywords, and meta tags all take time to develop. While waiting for organic results, eWebStyle recommends setting up a pay-per-click campaign to get immediate traffic and customers while the long-term SEO strategy builds momentum.

Why should I hire someone to manage my pay-per-click campaign instead of doing it myself?

Effective pay-per-click management requires one to two hours of daily attention. Most business owners cannot spare that time, and inexperienced management can waste an entire monthly budget in a day on wrong terms or wrong pages. A skilled manager can turn a $500 spend into thousands of dollars in returned business.

What are the five things to focus on when managing a pay-per-click campaign?

According to the episode, the five focus areas are your pay-per-click manager such as Google AdWords, your keywords, your analytics or stats software to track visitor behavior, keywords again, and keywords once more, stressing that keyword work is the most critical and recurring daily task.

Why does my website need to be prepared before I start a pay-per-click or SEO campaign?

A website must support the actual sales process, not just look good. The episode uses a Houston office supply company as an example: their shopping cart site looked professional but never communicated their key value of same-day delivery and personal customer service, so paid traffic would not convert without fixing that first.

Terms defined in this episode
Pay-Per-Click (PPC)
A form of search engine marketing where businesses pay for clicks on their ads. It delivers immediate traffic but requires daily monitoring of the pay-per-click manager, keywords, and analytics to avoid wasting budget.
Search Engine Marketing (SEM)
The broader category that includes pay-per-click advertising as well as other paid and promotional online marketing strategies, distinct from organic search engine optimization.
Golden Triangle
A referenced study about how users get more clicks in the first three positions on page one of Google, often cited in contrast to the click rates seen for pay-per-click ads.
Webalyzer
A free stats viewing software that shows how people found a website and tracks visitor behavior, mentioned as an alternative to Google Analytics that some clients still use.
