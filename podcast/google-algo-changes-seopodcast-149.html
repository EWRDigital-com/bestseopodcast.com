Questions this episode answers

What Google algorithm changes happened between 2010 and 2012?

The episode covers Google Places and Caffeine in 2010, then Google Instant, Instant Previews, and Social Signal. In 2011 came Attribution Update, Panda, Farmer, the Plus One button, Sitelinks changes, encrypted search, and Freshness. In 2012 the Top Heavy update and Search Plus Your World were added.

What is the Panda update and how did it affect websites?

Panda launched in February 2011 and affected sites across the board, hitting over-optimization, content quality, and links. It was so large that sub-versions like Panda 3.5 and 3.7 kept rolling out, and the hosts say most subsequent updates still contain some element of Panda.

What is Search Engine Visitor Optimization or Sevo?

Sevo stands for Search Engine Visitor Optimization. It focuses on what happens after traffic reaches a site, guiding visitors to take a desired action such as calling or emailing. The hosts describe it as conversion optimization and say they aim for users to take action as quickly as possible.

Why did Google's encrypted search hurt SEO analytics?

The October 2011 encrypted search update hid keyword data for users logged into Google to protect their privacy. The hosts note it was supposed to affect only about 3% of searches but one client saw over 33% of keywords appear as 'not provided' in analytics, and the hosts say they have never seen it under 30%.

Terms defined in this episode
Algorithm Cataclysm
A segment on this podcast dedicated to covering recent Google algorithm changes, introduced to appear early in episodes for SEO reasons.
Sevo (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A term used by eWebStyle meaning optimizing a website so that once visitors arrive, they are guided to take a conversion action such as a phone call or form submission.
Top Heavy
A 2012 Google algorithm update that penalized sites displaying too many ads above the fold before users could reach the main content.
Freshness Update
A November 2011 Google update, called Ziploc by the hosts, that prioritized newer content in industries where information changes rapidly such as news, sports, and health.
