Questions this episode answers

What does CEO identity mean in the context of business growth?

CEO identity means adopting the mindset and behaviors of the business owner you want to become before you get there. Jason Moss recommends spending five minutes each morning visualizing how a seven-figure entrepreneur would make decisions, because identity drives behavior more than strategy does.

How should entrepreneurs think about delegation versus abdication?

Delegation means handing off a task you already know how to do well so you can move up the value ladder. Abdication means outsourcing something you have not mastered yourself, hoping someone else will fix a problem you have not diagnosed. Abdication can cause serious trouble in a business.

What are the three levels of consciousness Jason Moss describes for entrepreneurs?

The three levels are willful, which is grind and hustle; intellectual, which is systems and strategic thinking; and intuitive, which is making decisions based on inner alignment. Moss credits this framework to his mentor Scott Oldford and says understanding which level your clients are at helps tailor your marketing.

Terms defined in this episode
Willful, Intellectual, Intuitive
Three operating modes entrepreneurs move through: willful is hustle-driven, intellectual is systems-and-strategy-driven, and intuitive is alignment-and-feeling-driven. Coined by Scott Oldford and applied here to marketing and sales.
Delegation vs Abdication
Delegation is handing off a mastered task to free up higher-value time. Abdication is outsourcing something the owner has not figured out, hoping another party will solve an undiagnosed problem.
