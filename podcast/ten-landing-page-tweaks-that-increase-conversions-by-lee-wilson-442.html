Questions this episode answers

What button text should I use on a landing page to improve conversions?

The episode cites Lee Wilson's recommendation to replace 'buy' or 'buy now' with softer text like 'continue.' The idea is to lower the sense of commitment so curious visitors keep moving through the funnel rather than bouncing at the first call to action.

How does lead nurturing affect ROI according to this episode?

According to MarketSherpa, cited in the episode, organizations that nurture leads see a 45% lift in ROI on average. The hosts add that while only 3% of people are ready to buy immediately, nurturing with email or SMS over 90 days can bring that figure up to 40%.

Should I use more or fewer form fields on my landing page?

It depends on the goal. Dropping down to just an email field can increase leads by around 20%. But if you want more qualified leads and need to filter out unqualified contacts, adding more fields helps. The right number depends on what you promise to deliver.

What is Wistia and why does the episode recommend it for landing page optimization?

Wistia is a paid video hosting tool mentioned in the episode for tracking micro-goals around video content. It shows how much of a video viewers watch and when they drop off, and it allows marketers to build custom audiences based on viewing behavior, giving more data than free YouTube hosting.

Terms defined in this episode
Micro-goals
Smaller tracked actions on a page beyond a final sale, such as pages visited, video watch time, brochure downloads, or terms and conditions views, used to measure engagement step by step.
Emotive commitment
The psychological weight a visitor feels when asked to take an action like clicking 'buy now.' Reducing it, such as by using the word 'continue,' keeps visitors moving forward with less resistance.
Lead nurturing
An ongoing process of following up with contacts who are not yet ready to buy, using email workflows, SMS, and other touchpoints, to stay present until they are ready to purchase.
Conversion rate optimization
The practice of making ongoing tweaks to landing pages and other site elements to turn more existing visitors into leads or customers, described in the episode as part of a monthly SEO package.
