Questions this episode answers

Should a local business focus on organic search or Google Maps rankings?

According to this episode, the answer is both, plus PPC. Appearing in all three placements at once builds credibility and raises click-through rates across all listings. For hyper-local businesses like restaurants, Google Maps with reviews may matter most, while service businesses covering a whole city also need organic rankings.

How do you run better PPC ad tests?

The hosts recommend running three or four different ads simultaneously for a full month, then comparing results. If two ads excel, find what they have in common and build a third ad that emphasizes those shared elements, while keeping the best-performing control ad running.

What is a productive bounce rate versus a non-productive one?

A productive bounce happens when a visitor lands, gets the information they need, and calls without visiting another page, which counts as a conversion. A non-productive bounce is when a visitor lands, sees nothing relevant, and leaves immediately, wasting the click and any ad spend behind it.

How should you optimize images for SEO before uploading them to WordPress?

Resize the image to the smallest size that keeps clarity, rename the file to your top keyword, then upload it. After uploading, give it an alt tag and a title that match that keyword, and place it on a page targeting the same keyword so the image is fully optimized.

Terms defined in this episode
Honeypot
A spam-prevention technique used as an alternative to CAPTCHA on forms, described in the episode as super cool without further technical detail.
Click pattern
The sequence of clicks a visitor takes from a search result through a website before converting or bouncing, visible in the visitor flow report inside Google Analytics.
SERP
Search engine results page. The hosts note it changes frequently, citing examples like authorship avatars, the plus one button, and mobile-friendly labels being added or removed.
301 redirect
A permanent redirect used to consolidate multiple URL variants of the same page so inbound link equity is not split across four different addresses.
