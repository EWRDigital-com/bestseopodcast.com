Key takeaways

Questions this episode answers

What is latent semantic indexing (LSI) and why does it matter for SEO?

LSI is the process of including related key phrases and words alongside your main target phrases to boost a site's value in search engines. For example, if you optimize for coffee makers, adding phrases like iced coffee or organic coffee signals relevance to Google.

Why should you run PPC at the same time as an SEO campaign?

PPC delivers immediate results and helps identify which keywords actually convert before you invest in long-term SEO. The hosts found that pausing a paid campaign for auto glass repair saved their client from ranking for a term that did not match the service they offered.

What meta tag length limits should you follow for SEO?

Keep the meta title tag at 70 characters or fewer and the meta description at 160 characters or fewer. Anything longer gets cut off by search engines, and the description is your first chance to prompt a searcher to click or even call.

Terms defined in this episode
Latent Semantic Indexing (LSI)
The process of including related key phrases and words alongside main target phrases to boost a site's relevance and value in search engines.
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
The practice of guiding visitors who arrive from search engines toward a desired end goal, such as calling, filling out a form, or signing up for a newsletter.
Canonicalization
Ensuring pages are not counted more than once by making one URL version the default and redirecting all other versions to it, consolidating link juice.
Long-tail key phrases
Longer, more specific search queries that tend to rank easier and attract more qualified visitors who know exactly what they are looking for.
