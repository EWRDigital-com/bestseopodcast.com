Top 5 Mindfulness tips to take action NOW during this pandemic to help your mindset and GROW your business! With Malorie Nicole #481

Ep. 48134 min2020-04-20Guest: Malorie Nicole

Entrepreneurs can protect their businesses during the pandemic by first addressing their own fear-driven mindset, then checking in with clients, finding new markets, and connecting authentically rather than freezing or over-reacting.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Work through your own fear first before making business decisions, or you will project panic onto clients and team members.
  • Call or speak directly with current clients during the crisis, not just send emails, to maintain trust and loyalty.
  • Look for adjacent markets your existing skills can serve rather than rebuilding your entire business from scratch.
  • Connection with people is easier right now than ever because everyone shares similar fears and uncertainty.
  • Soft skills, not hard tactical skills, are what will break or save most businesses during major downturns.

Questions this episode answers

What are the top 5 mindset and business tips for entrepreneurs during the pandemic?

Malorie Nicole lists: first, examine the story you are telling yourself and get out of panic mode; second, check in directly with current clients; third, look for new markets your existing skills can serve; fourth, use the moment to connect genuinely with people since everyone shares similar fears; fifth, seek mindset coaching support.

Why do soft skills matter more than hard skills during a business crisis?

Malorie Nicole argues that most business owners already have the tactical knowledge they need, but unresolved emotional patterns create paralysis and poor decision-making. Until fear and scarcity thinking are addressed, hard skills cannot be applied effectively, which is what causes businesses to fail in downturns.

How does Malorie Nicole help entrepreneurs who feel stuck in a business they no longer want?

She takes clients through an eight week process that uncovers why they think the way they think, traces blocks back to past experiences, and reworks subconscious patterns around scarcity and fear. One client left a seven figure agency he was miserable in and built a new business where he is happy and replaced his income.

Should businesses keep selling during the pandemic or pause all marketing?

Matt Bertram and Malorie Nicole both say business should not just stop. The key is being aware of how you are selling and connecting authentically. Facebook ad costs are down while usage is up, making it a good time to build presence online and reach people with a message that fits the current moment.

In their words

it's not the lack of hard skills. It's people not knowing how to work through the low times because the reality of this is in my opinion, and I'm only 30 years old, this is going to happen 10 more times before I die in my business.

Terms defined in this episode
Subconscious blocks
Neural pathways that create default stress and overwhelm patterns, identified by Malorie Nicole as the root cause of poor decision-making and paralysis in business owners.
Neuroplasticity
The brain's ability to be retrained, referenced in the episode as the foundation for coaching work that helps entrepreneurs redesign how their brain processes information.
Soft skills
Emotional and interpersonal capabilities such as processing fear and building connections, described in the episode as what will break most businesses during a downturn rather than any lack of tactical knowledge.
