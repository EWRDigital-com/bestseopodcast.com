Yahoo and Bing PPC clicks can cost far less than Google, sometimes $1 versus $5 per click, so advertisers should exhaust cheaper search engine budgets first before moving spend to Google.

Key takeaways
  • Yahoo and Bing clicks can cost $1 to $2 while comparable Google clicks cost $5, delivering more traffic for the same budget.
  • If Yahoo generates enough queries to spend your full budget, spend it there first before moving to Google.
  • Before investing in SEO, fix website problems like splash pages, expired offers, and broken links or the SEO spend is wasted.
  • Use a business domain email instead of a Hotmail address on your website to maintain credibility with visitors.
  • Keywords used internally in an industry, like MEP, may not match how customers search, so align content to customer language.

Questions this episode answers

Should I use Yahoo or Bing PPC instead of Google for paid search?

According to this episode, Yahoo and Bing clicks can cost $1 to $2 compared to $5 on Google for the same terms. If Yahoo generates enough traffic to consume your full budget, you should spend it there first. Once you exhaust cheaper inventory, move remaining budget to Google.

What should I do to my website before starting SEO?

Fix problems that damage credibility first. The hosts say to remove splash pages, update expired offers, repair broken links, replace a personal email like Hotmail with a domain email, and add clear calls to action. Spending on SEO before fixing these issues wastes money.

Does Google having more searches mean Google ads are more valuable than Yahoo ads?

No, according to this episode. For an individual searcher, the search engine they use has full influence over them. A click from Yahoo is worth the same as a click from Google. The difference is cost, not the value of the visitor who clicks your ad.

Why might a business owner need to invest in their website before doing SEO?

The hosts explain that driving traffic to a poorly built site is like a mail out with the wrong phone number. If the site has a confusing splash page, expired promotions, broken links, or a Hotmail contact email, it can lose sales even after SEO brings visitors in.

the last thing we'd want to do is make an effort doing search engine optimization and getting somebody onto the first page and have them have to struggle with this

Terms defined in this episode
Splash page
An intro or entry page, often with graphics or flash animation, that appears before the main website content. The hosts say these hurt user experience and SEO because visitors bounce instead of entering the site.
Cost per click
The amount an advertiser pays each time a user clicks their paid search ad. In the episode, Google clicks cost around $5 while Yahoo clicks cost $1 to $2 for the same terms.
Organic search placement
The unpaid, algorithmically ranked positions in search engine results. The hosts say users trust organic results about 80 percent more than paid ads.
Call to action
A prompt on a webpage that tells visitors what to do next, such as calling a phone number. The hosts note the reviewed website lacked clear calls to action.
