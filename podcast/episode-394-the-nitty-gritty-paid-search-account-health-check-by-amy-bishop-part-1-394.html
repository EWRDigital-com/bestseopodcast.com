Key takeaways
  • Understand client goals and lifetime customer value before auditing any paid search account.
  • Conversion tracking must reflect real actions like form submissions or calls, not trivial metrics like 10 seconds on a page.
  • Time of day data can reveal when all conversions occur, letting you cap spend outside those windows.
  • Campaign structure should be highly segmented so you can clearly see what is performing and control bids.
  • Ad extensions must be audited to confirm links are live and do not lead to 404 errors.
I look at what is working now and what isn't working now. I also look at what was working and why it stopped working.

Questions this episode answers

What are the eight steps in a paid search account health check?

The eight steps are: get the background on goals and targets, review conversion tracking types and values, analyze performance trends over time, assess campaign structure, examine device performance, analyze geographic performance, review ad tests for clear winners, and audit ad extensions for relevance and live links.

Why does conversion tracking setup matter so much in a PPC audit?

Poor conversion tracking can make a campaign look successful when it is not. Examples include counting 10 seconds on a page as a conversion, which produces inflated numbers of little real value. Meaningful conversions are actions like form submissions or phone calls, not trivial page visits.

How can time of day data improve paid search efficiency?

By reviewing historical conversion data by hour, you can identify windows when all conversions actually occur. In one example from the episode, every conversion happened between 6 a.m. and noon, so budget was capped to those hours to avoid wasting spend on clicks that never converted.

What should you look for when auditing ad extensions in a paid search account?

Check that all ad extension links are live and do not lead to 404 errors. Also verify the links go where intended, that extensions are relevant and compelling, and that they are active. It is easy to miss broken or outdated extensions during a routine campaign review.

Terms defined in this episode
Ad Extensions
Additional links or information attached to a paid search ad that must be audited for relevance, active status, and destination URLs that do not return 404 errors.
Conversion Tracking
The system that records which user actions count as a goal in a paid search campaign, such as form submissions or phone calls, as opposed to low-value signals like 10 seconds on a page.
Quality Score
A Google metric influenced by ad relevance, landing page optimization, and click-through rate that affects how ads are ranked and priced in AdWords.
Dayparting
Adjusting paid search bids or campaign delivery by time of day and day of week based on historical conversion data to concentrate budget on high-converting windows.
