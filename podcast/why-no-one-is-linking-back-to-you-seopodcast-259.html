Episode34 min2015-01-16

Sites fail to earn backlinks due to 11 fixable problems including website bloat, slow load times, broken media, no fresh content, atrocious writing, and outdated design, all of which also hurt overall SEO performance.

Key takeaways
  • Consolidate thin or duplicate pages to fix website bloat and make clear link targets for potential linkers.
  • Slow load times and broken media create bad first impressions that discourage anyone from linking to your site.
  • Posts need social sharing buttons, images, video, and bullet points to attract links and engagement.
  • Unattended comment sections signal you do not care about your site and discourage both links and user interaction.
  • Outdated site design using Flash or marquees loses credibility and links in a mobile-first world.

Questions this episode answers

Why won't other sites link back to my website?

According to this episode, the most common reasons include website bloat with too many similar pages, slow load time, broken media, no social sharing options, stale content, atrocious writing, untended comment sections, too many ads, unoriginal content, better content existing elsewhere, and an outdated site design.

How does website bloat hurt link building?

When you create many pages targeting similar terms, potential linkers cannot tell which page to point to. The fix is to organize, consolidate, and condense related pages, and remove thin content to avoid Panda problems and make link targets obvious.

What should a fully optimized post include to attract links?

The episode says a fully optimized post needs great text content, images, bullet points, supporting rich media, outbound links to additional resources, and social sharing icons that display share counts, all of which together make people more likely to share and link.

Terms defined in this episode
Website bloat
Having too many pages targeting similar terms that overlap in content, making it unclear where linkers should point and risking Panda penalties for thin content.
FAB
Features, Advantages, and Benefits. A content framework for addressing a user's pain point by explaining what a product or service does, why it is better, and how it directly benefits the reader.
Three-pack
Google replaced its carousel of local results with only three local listings shown below paid ads, making competition for those top three local positions extremely important.
Mobile-friendly label
A tag Google began showing next to listings in mobile search results to indicate whether a site is optimized for mobile devices, directly influencing whether users click.
