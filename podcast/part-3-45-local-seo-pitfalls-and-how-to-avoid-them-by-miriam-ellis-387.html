Questions this episode answers

What is NAP consistency and why does it matter for local SEO?

NAP stands for name, address, and phone number. Keeping this data consistent across major platforms like Google My Business, Facebook, Apple Maps, Foursquare, Yelp, and Bing is the fourth most important local search ranking factor for placement in the Google three-pack, according to the report referenced in Miriam Ellis's article.

How should a business handle negative reviews online?

Respond to every review, but do so without emotion. The hosts recommend writing out the emotional response privately first, then posting a calm version that acknowledges the customer, apologizes, and makes some kind of offer or gesture. Not responding at all is described as the worst possible action.

What tools can help find and manage local citation duplicates?

The episode mentions Moz Local, UBL, Bright Local, and Yext as services that can identify and manage duplicate or inconsistent local citations. Entering your business name and zip code in Moz Local is suggested as a starting point to check for duplicates.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. Consistent NAP data across local directories and platforms is the fourth most important local search ranking factor for the Google three-pack.
Google three-pack
The block of three local business results Google displays prominently in local search. NAP consistency and citations are key factors in determining placement there.
Duplicate listings
Multiple local business listings for the same business that divide ranking strength and can violate platform guidelines, similar to duplicate web pages diluting link value.
Google My Business trusted photographers
Photographers recognized by Google who can create verified 3D and virtual tour photos for a business listing, allowing users to take an interactive tour of the office.
