Questions this episode answers

What is topical authority in SEO?

Topical authority is a perceived authority over a niche or broad idea set, as opposed to authority over a single idea or a particular term. A site that covers all aspects of a subject, like branding across logo, PPC, social, and web, holds topical authority over a site that covers only one angle.

How do you build topical authority?

Find every question your audience asks using sources like keyword data, Google Webmaster Tools, your sales and support team, client surveys, and answerthepublic.com. Then create content in multiple formats, text, video, images, and infographics, that fully answers those questions across the topic area.

Will topical authority stop mattering as AI and voice search grow?

Dave Davies argues topical authority may diminish as Google's knowledge graph and voice devices like Google Home answer questions directly, reducing the need to visit a site. However, the hosts note that being in the knowledge graph itself signals topical authority and may become even more valuable.

Why do links still matter when building topical authority?

Links remain significant, with at least 40% of search value attributed to them according to the episode. Authoritative, well-organized content that answers real questions naturally attracts links. The article also recommends seeking links from topically relevant sites that rank for the same phrases.

Terms defined in this episode
Topical Authority
A perceived authority over a niche or broad idea set, as opposed to authority over a single idea or a particular term, built by covering all related questions within a subject area.
Knowledge Graph
A Google feature that answers user questions directly in search results, which the episode says is growing stronger with devices like Google Home and may reduce clicks to individual sites.
Answer the Public
A tool at answerthepublic.com that maps user questions around a topic into a spider graph organized by who, what, why, when, where, and how question types.
Hummingbird Update
A 2013 Google update that improved its understanding of context and relationships between words, pushing content quality toward answering user intent rather than matching keywords.
