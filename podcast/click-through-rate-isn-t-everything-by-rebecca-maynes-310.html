Click-Through Rate Isn't Everything by Rebecca Maynes #310

Ep. 31036 min2016-02-16

Display ad click-through rate is not the only metric that matters; relevancy, audience age, gender, income, and remarketing timing all drive purchases even without a direct ad click.

Key takeaways
  • Display ads are 39% more likely to influence users who are actively researching a purchase versus those with no intent to buy.
  • 53% of people said they were likely to search online for the product featured in an ad rather than click it directly.
  • Remarketing cookies let you target people who already visited your product page, making your display ads far more effective.
  • Tailor ad copy and platform by demographic: younger audiences respond to exclusivity on Instagram, older audiences respond to collection framing on Facebook.

About this episode

To be successful, advertisers need to ensure their ads are providing value to online web users, to be noticed, remembered, and engaged with, relevancy of the ad is key. Serving relevant ads that are related to a searcher’s current need or want are far more likely to capture attention than a "one-size-fits-all" approach. Questions this episode answers

Why is click-through rate not the only metric for display ads?

According to the article discussed in this episode, seeing an ad can trigger research, phone calls, walk-in visits, newsletter sign-ups, or social follows without a click ever happening. The ad is still noticed and remembered, and 53% of people said they were likely to search online for a product after seeing an ad.

When should I enable remarketing for display ads?

You should enable remarketing before running display campaigns so you can build an audience list of people who already visited your product pages. The episode explains that display ads are 39% more likely to influence users who are already researching a purchase, making remarketing the highest-value moment to show your ads.

How does audience age affect display ad performance?

The episode covers stats showing 18 to 25-year-olds are most likely to be influenced by online advertising early in the buying cycle, while adults ages 26 to 54 are 12% more likely than the prior generation to purchase as a result of seeing an ad at later buying stages.

Should you offer discounts in display ads?

The episode argues against offering discounts unless the user is about to abandon a checkout page or has already bounced and you can identify them for follow-up. If an ad is irrelevant to the searcher's needs, a coupon will have no impact, so relevancy matters more than discounting.

Terms defined in this episode
remarketing
A display advertising technique where a cookie dropped on a site visitor is used to show that person targeted ads after they leave, reaching users who have already shown interest in a product.
GTIN
A unique identifying number required by Google for product listing ads, especially for brand-name products carried by multiple merchants, mandatory as of May 16th per the episode.
algorithmic timeline
A social feed ordered by platform algorithm rather than chronological order, as announced by Twitter and discussed in the episode as a concern for users who follow many accounts.
display ads
Image-based paid ads shown across the Google Display Network, Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter that can influence users even without a direct click.
