Questions this episode answers

What did the Google data leak reveal about ranking factors?

The leaked documents confirmed that click signals, dwell time, user behavior tracked through Chrome, sandboxing of new sites, and contextual anchor text all influence rankings. Google had previously denied several of these factors publicly, which is why the leak caused significant concern about trust in the industry.

Does dwell time actually affect Google rankings?

According to the host, Google has repeatedly said dwell time is not a ranking factor, but the leaked documents and observed SEO results show it does have an effect. Google tracks how long users spend on pages through Chrome, and that behavior feeds into other ranking signals and scoring functions.

What is sandboxing in SEO and does Google actually use it?

Sandboxing is an audition period where Google artificially holds back a new website from ranking regardless of the SEO work done. Google previously denied using a sandbox, but the leaked documents confirmed it exists. The host says clients typically need to invest four to six months before seeing ranking movement.

How can negative SEO through link spam hurt a website?

If someone repeatedly sends spammy links to your site and you do not disavow them, Google assumes you want those links and may penalize you. The host says two clients were attacked this way and recommends actively monitoring Search Console and disavowing harmful links to protect your site.

Terms defined in this episode
Pogo sticking
When a user clicks on a search result and immediately returns to the search engine results page, signaling to Google that the page was not relevant, which causes that click to be downgraded.
Long click
When a user clicks on a search result and stays on the page for an extended time, signaling to Google that the content was engaging and relevant.
Sandboxing
A period where Google artificially limits rankings for new websites while it evaluates trust and signals, typically lasting several months before ranking movement occurs.
Exact match domain
A domain name that exactly matches a target keyword. The leaked documents note minor penalties for these, though the host says they can still help if backed by strong content.
