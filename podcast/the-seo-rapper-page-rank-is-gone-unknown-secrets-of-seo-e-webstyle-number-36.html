Questions this episode answers

Is Google PageRank still used in its algorithm?

According to this episode, Google removed PageRank from its Webmaster Tools and toolbar, but the hosts and guest believe the underlying algorithm still uses it. They expect a formal algorithm change to follow, similar to when Google stopped publicly valuing meta keywords after people manipulated them.

How should a business use Twitter for SEO and brand management?

The guest recommends using Twitter alongside Facebook and Google Alerts to monitor and manage your brand. Set up Google Alerts for your company name and respond immediately on Twitter when someone mentions you. Using Twitter on mobile keeps you most active and aware in real time.

Why is social media content becoming more important for search rankings?

The guest explains that Google is indexing tweets and social media posts and placing that content higher in rankings. User-generated content from Twitter and Facebook provides fresh, real-time information, and Google partnering with those platforms means your social posts can appear in search results even when your website cannot.

Terms defined in this episode
PageRank
A Google signal used to evaluate website authority based on links. Google removed it from its toolbar and Webmaster Tools, suggesting a coming algorithm change.
Google Alerts
A free Google tool that sends email notifications whenever a specified name, company, or product is newly posted anywhere on the web.
Indexable tweets
Twitter posts that Google can crawl and include in search results, giving social media content potential ranking value alongside traditional web pages.
TweetDeck
An application mentioned in the episode for managing Twitter activity, including receiving alerts and posting updates from a desktop or mobile device.
