Content Marketing Tips and Tricks - #SEOpodcast 240

Episode34 min2014-07-30

Content marketing success requires consistent visual branding, a tone true to core values, audience-focused value, strong headlines, and genuine gratitude, as framed through 13 lessons inspired by common motherly advice.

Key takeaways
  • Match your visuals to your content message and keep the look consistent across all formats.
  • Your content's tone and style should be recognizable and consistent so readers know it is you.
  • Bring real value to your audience. If content wastes their time, they will not share or remember it.
  • A strong headline matters. If it does not draw readers in, the next 1,700 words will never be read.
  • Thank your customers sincerely, not with canned messages, because they could be anywhere but chose you.

Questions this episode answers

How should content marketers handle writer's block?

The hosts recommend changing your environment, such as going to a park or coffee shop, switching your writing medium from typing to physically writing, using different ink colors to activate different parts of your brain, and recording voice memos to capture ideas when inspiration strikes away from your desk.

Why is visual design important for content marketing?

Even strong written content can fail if the graphics, layout, colors, and structure do not match the content's message. The hosts say the visual appearance must be consistent and speak the same language as the content, whether that is corporate and buttoned-up or playful and animated.

Should you follow every content marketing trend?

Not necessarily. The hosts use responsive design as an example, noting that a mobile app redirect might suit some businesses better than a fully responsive site. The advice is to understand your own business first and do what is right for it rather than copying what others are doing.

How do you keep your content audience engaged over time?

The hosts suggest directly asking your audience what they want to read, using Twitter followers, Facebook fans, or LinkedIn groups to gather real feedback before creating your next piece of content. Consistent tone, branding, and value across every format also help readers keep recognizing and returning to your content.
