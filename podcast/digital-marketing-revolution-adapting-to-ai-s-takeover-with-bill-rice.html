Questions this episode answers

How can brands get their content to show up in ChatGPT and other LLMs?

According to Bill Rice, brands should look for gaps in what LLMs already know and fill those gaps with unique, personal, and experience-based content. LLMs consume that kind of material quickly because it is distinct from the generic blog posts that already dominate their training data.

Is traditional SEO still worth investing in with AI search growing?

Bill Rice says the agency still starts with PPC to generate leads, then layers in SEO, but expects SEO and content to increasingly lead engagements because Google is indexing new pages faster and ChatGPT is actively recommending brands to users, sometimes pre-selling them before they ever visit a site.

What AI tools should marketers use right now?

Bill Rice recommends starting with core tools including ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Gemini rather than pre-baked SEO content tools. He also recommends building custom GPTs, maintaining a personal prompt database, and using Spaces to organize client projects and maintain safe history.

How do you write better prompts for AI tools?

Bill Rice describes a two-step method: draft your prompt, then feed it to the O3 reasoning model and ask it to write a better prompt. Take that improved, more detailed prompt and run it through GPT-4 or 4.5 to get higher quality output.

Terms defined in this episode
LLM content gap
Unique, personal, or experience-based content that large language models have not yet consumed, making it highly attractive for LLMs to index and surface in responses.
Agentic AI
A setup where small AI agents are built, strung together, and set in motion to handle tasks autonomously, freeing up human capacity for creative and strategic work.
Guided content creation
A process where executives are coached through a recorded session, similar to a podcast interview, and the resulting content is edited into social and marketing material capturing their unique voice.
Topical authority
An existing site credibility and category relevance that causes Google to index and rank new related content very quickly, sometimes within one to two days.
