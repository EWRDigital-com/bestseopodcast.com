Questions this episode answers

Why do my Google search rankings look different when I am logged in versus logged out?

When you are signed into a Google account, Google personalizes results based on pages you have visited before. If you regularly visit a site you track in analytics, Google may rank it higher just for you. Logging out immediately returns results to their true, unbiased positions.

Should a small business be on Twitter and Facebook?

According to this episode, only if the business has time to stay active and post relevant content. A static presence is better served by a website. Twitter is especially useful for time-sensitive promotions, as shown by a Utah burrito chain whose 80-follower offer reached more than 2,500 customers.

Why should a website come before a social media profile?

The hosts argue a website gives you a controlled destination for traffic and better control over calls to action. Social media should drive people somewhere, and that destination should be your own site, not a platform you do not control.

How powerful can a Twitter promotion be for a small business?

A Utah-based Mexican restaurant chain called Costa Vida sent a one-day free burrito offer to its 80 Twitter followers. The offer was shared among followers and their networks, and more than 2,500 people came in to redeem it, far exceeding expectations.

Terms defined in this episode
Personalized search results
When a user is signed into Google, the search engine adjusts result rankings based on that user's browsing history, potentially showing previously visited pages higher than their true organic position.
Viral video marketing
Creating and distributing a video designed to be shared widely by viewers, used as a low-cost way to promote a business or website, as discussed through the hammer pants dance YouTube example.
Twitter follower promotion
Sending a time-sensitive offer to a Twitter follower list so that followers re-share it with their own networks, multiplying reach far beyond the original audience size.
