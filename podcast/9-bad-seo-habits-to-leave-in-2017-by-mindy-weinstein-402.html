9 Bad SEO Habits to Leave in 2017 by Mindy Weinstein #402

Avoid nine outdated SEO habits including creating one page per keyword, aggressive link building, thin content, ignoring technical issues, and treating keyword rankings as the main success metric.

Key takeaways
  • Creating one page per keyword variation produces thin, duplicate content and can trigger Google penalties.
  • Buying bulk links, such as 5,000 from Fiverr, looks unnatural to Google and gets disregarded or penalized.
  • Optimize images by using descriptive file names and alt attributes instead of generic labels like xyz123.jpg.
  • Keyword rankings alone are not the real measure of success; conversions and actual client acquisition matter more.
  • Staying current on algorithm changes and webmaster guidelines is essential because SEO best practices constantly evolve.

About this episode

9 Bad SEO Habits to Leave in 2017As we enter a new decade, it’s time to say goodbye to some bad SEO habits. These are SEO tactics that just plain don’t work, or even worse, can get a website penalized. Below is a list of the top nine habits that need to be kicked to the curb.

Questions this episode answers

Why is creating one page per keyword variation a bad SEO habit?

It leads to thin or duplicated content across pages that offer little real value to users. Barry Schwartz addressed this in relation to the Google Maccabees updates, noting local landing pages built around city and service keyword combinations were a common problem.

What technical SEO problems hurt search performance most?

Mindy Weinstein and the hosts point to improper redirects, slow page load time, mobile errors, duplicate content, and unintentionally blocked pages as major issues found in most technical SEO audits. Google Search Console is recommended for identifying and fixing these.

What is the right approach to link building in 2018 according to this episode?

Focus on earning quality, relevant links through good outreach rather than buying large volumes of links. Sudden unnatural spikes in link counts are visible to Google and links gathered through schemes like bulk Fiverr purchases do not reflect organic endorsement.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Maccabees Update
A Google algorithm update discussed by Barry Schwartz that targeted sites creating many pages for keyword variations, particularly local landing pages combining city names with service terms.
AMP pages
Accelerated Mobile Pages, a feature designed to improve mobile experience. Gaming the system by redirecting AMP pages immediately to a full non-AMP site risks losing the ranking benefit AMP provides.
thin content
Pages with very little substantive text, often created to target individual keyword phrases, which can result in SEO penalties and poor user experience.
Search Console
A free Google tool that maps technical issues on a website, allowing site owners to identify and fix problems like blocked pages, redirect errors, and mobile issues.
