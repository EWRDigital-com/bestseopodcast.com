Questions this episode answers

What marketing opportunities exist in Web3 for traditional digital marketers?

According to this episode, PR and earning bylines, community management in DAOs and encrypted chats, and improving user experience are the clearest entry points. The guest says traditional Web2 marketers can help bridge Web3 to commercialization by focusing on user experience, which plagues most Web3 projects.

Is Web3 or crypto marketing too risky for agencies to pursue?

The guest says the space is extraordinary speculative by definition due to lack of commercialization, involves significant stress and risk, and has seen rampant fraud. He recommends starting lightly, choosing a range of clients, and being realistic about your own pain and reward tolerance before committing fully.

What is a DAO and why does it matter for marketers?

The episode describes DAOs as groups that often still rely on human administration, which is a vulnerability. Many Web3 freelancers and communities operate through DAOs, and community management within these structures is cited as one of the main active marketing roles in the space right now.

How does blockchain technology relate to marketing transparency?

The guest explains that Bitcoin and other public protocols create a record-keeping system that is harder to delete or edit, making data more reliable. Early Bitcoin interest came partly from affiliate and click-through marketers who were frustrated by lack of transparency in payment processing and throughput rates.

Terms defined in this episode
DAO
A decentralized autonomous organization; in this episode described as a group or community in Web3 that often still has human administration, which the guest identifies as a vulnerability.
Smart contract
Code that locks agreed-upon rules into a blockchain so that those rules are enforced automatically, removing the need for human administrators to ensure parties do what they say.
Public protocol
Open software layers like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and IPFS that provide incentives to make record-keeping harder to delete or edit, as described by Anthem Blanchard in this episode.
Commercialization
The stage at which Bitcoin or other public protocols become indispensable in commercial software, similar to how Linux underlies the LAMP stack; the guest says we have not reached this stage yet.
