Questions this episode answers

What is Google Panda 2.2 focusing on?

According to Matt Cutts, Panda 2.2 will include improved scraper protection, targeting sites that steal original content and rank close to or above the original source. Google did not make manual exceptions for sites hurt by earlier Panda updates, which caused significant frustration among webmasters.

Should I build separate websites for Australia, New Zealand, and the US or use one site?

The hosts recommend separate sites with country-coded domains for competitive markets, since local users prefer local businesses and country-coded domains add relevance. For non-competitive niches, one globally optimized site can work. Content must be substantially different across sites to avoid duplicate content issues.

Are exact match domains being devalued by Google?

Yes. The hosts note that Google is devaluing exact match domains, meaning a domain like Houstonplumber.com should not rank on page one purely because of its domain name. Existing businesses should still be able to compete, and Google appears to be moving in that direction.

What will SEO look like in the next five years?

The hosts cite an article by Sam Crocker on thestateofsearch.com suggesting off-site activities will shift toward content distribution, consumption, and interaction rather than pure link building. The hosts add that local search integration and user-generated content will grow in importance, and meta tags may lose significance.

Terms defined in this episode
Panda update
A Google algorithm update that targeted low-quality content; version 2.2 focuses on improved scraper protection and did not include manual exceptions for affected sites.
Exact match domain
A domain name that exactly matches a keyword search phrase, such as Houstonplumber.com; Google is devaluing these so they no longer rank on page one by domain name alone.
Country-coded domain
A domain extension specific to a country, such as .au for Australia, that can signal local relevance to users and search engines in that region.
Infographic
An image that presents complex information with graphics, quotes, and sample data, often used as a form of link bait to attract shares and links.
