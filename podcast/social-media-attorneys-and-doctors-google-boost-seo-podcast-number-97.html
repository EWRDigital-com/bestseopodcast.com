Questions this episode answers

What is a content farm and why is it bad for SEO?

A content farm is a site loaded with large amounts of low-quality content, created either to generate inbound links or to capture organic search traffic and monetize it through AdWords. Google declared war on content farms around this episode and began algorithmically blocking them, making them worthless or harmful for SEO.

How did Google discover that Bing was copying its search results?

Google noticed that Bing showed identical results for misspelled queries, even though Bing does not perform spell correction. Google then planted bogus queries with fake results, had employees search on Google using IE, and confirmed that Bing was collecting that click data through IE's suggested sites feature and using it in Bing results.

Why is traditional SEO no longer enough to rank on the first page?

After a major algorithmic change in October, Google began weighting local places listings, user-generated content like blogs and social media, video, and mobile alongside traditional content and links. Being on the first page today does not guarantee staying there without covering all these areas.

How should doctors or attorneys approach social media to avoid legal risk?

According to the hosts, doctors and attorneys must avoid posting specific medical or legal advice on social media because followers could act on it and sue them. Instead, they recommend focusing on community involvement, such as congratulating local sports teams or highlighting community events related to their practice.

Terms defined in this episode
Content Farm
A site loaded with large volumes of low-quality content created to generate inbound links or capture organic traffic for AdWords monetization, penalized by Google algorithmic changes.
Google Local Places
Google's local business listing system, which the hosts observed being merged into organic search results, with listings linking to internal pages rather than just homepages.
User-Generated Content
Content produced by users rather than site owners, including blogs and social media posts, which Google began weighting more heavily in rankings around the time of this episode.
Google Boost
A Google advertising product that automatically runs pay-per-click ads for a business, including for branded searches, which the hosts argued wastes budget when a site already ranks first organically.
