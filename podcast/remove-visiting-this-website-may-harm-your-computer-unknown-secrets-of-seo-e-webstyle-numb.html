Questions this episode answers

How do I remove the 'visiting this website may harm your computer' warning from Google search results?

Go into Google Webmaster Tools, find the message indicating parts of your site may be distributing malware, click for more details, then click 'request a review.' Google will review the site and remove the warning if malware is gone, rather than waiting months for automatic re-indexing.

Why are all my subpages showing the same visitor count in Google Analytics?

The likely cause is that different subpages have different Google Analytics dash numbers assigned to them. Every page on a single domain should carry the exact same Analytics account identifier. Google itself tracks which page is being viewed, so the same code should appear sitewide.

What is Ecosia and how does it relate to saving the rainforest?

Ecosia is a search engine at Ecosia.org that uses Bing and Yahoo results. It donates 80% of its profits toward saving the rainforest. A listener calculated that if Google adopted the same model, current search volume could theoretically save the entire rainforest in about 327 days.

Can a new website under 12 months old rank on the first page of Bing and Yahoo using basic SEO?

Yes, according to a listener named Robert at acrylic-splashbacks.com. His site, less than 12 months old, reached the first listing on Bing and multiple first-page positions on Yahoo by following advice from the Unknown Secrets of SEO podcast and working with an SEO contact.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Webmaster Tools malware review
A feature inside Google Webmaster Tools that lets site owners request Google re-examine a flagged site after malware is removed, speeding up restoration of normal search listings.
Google Analytics dash number
The numeric suffix appended to a Google Analytics account ID that identifies a specific property or domain. All pages within one domain should share the same dash number.
Net neutrality
