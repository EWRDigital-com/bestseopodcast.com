Part 4: 45 Local SEO Pitfalls and How to Avoid Them by Miriam Ellis #388

Ep. 38846 min2017-09-12

Local SEO review pitfalls include too few reviews, gaining them too fast, ignoring platform guidelines, skipping monitoring, and using review kiosks, all of which can harm rankings or get listings suspended.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • 84% of people trust online reviews as much as a personal recommendation, so accumulating reviews matters.
  • Getting reviews too fast looks unorganic to algorithms and may trigger spam flags on platforms like Yelp and Google.
  • Review kiosks on shared in-store IP addresses are prohibited by current guidelines and can result in reviews being removed.
  • Respond to every review, positive or negative, because it signals engagement and acts as free marketing.
  • Use a follow-up system like a customer survey call with permission to check back in seven days to improve review conversion.
Reviews aren't opt-in. Your customers are telling the story of your business whether you create a profile or not.

Questions this episode answers

Is it against the rules to ask customers to leave reviews?

Rules vary by platform. Yelp forbids business owners from asking for reviews, while Google has changed its position over time. Across all platforms, paying for reviews or using incentives that create conflicts of interest is prohibited. Always check each platform's current guidelines.

Why are review kiosks a local SEO pitfall?

Current guidelines on all major platforms specifically prohibit review kiosks. Reviews left on the same in-store IP address or Wi-Fi network can be removed by the platform. Google can also detect location via cellular, making kiosk-driven reviews risky even on mobile devices.

What is a good process for collecting customer reviews?

The hosts describe a process called CSSR, Customer Service Survey and Reviews. A business asks a client for about 10 customer contacts, calls each one with three survey questions, asks willing respondents to leave a review, then follows up in seven days with prior permission to confirm the review was submitted.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP listing
A business listing showing Name, Address, and Phone number across directories and platforms like Google My Business. Losing these listings through guideline violations harms local SEO.
CSSR
Customer Service Survey and Reviews, a process described in the episode where a marketing team calls a client's customers, asks survey questions, requests reviews from satisfied respondents, and follows up in seven days.
Review kiosk
A tablet or device placed in a business location for customers to leave reviews on-site before leaving. Current platform guidelines prohibit this practice.
