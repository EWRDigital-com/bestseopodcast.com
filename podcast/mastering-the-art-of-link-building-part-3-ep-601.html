Questions this episode answers

What is the double survey technique for link building?

The double survey technique combines two post formats: a roundup post and original research. You pick a topic lacking statistics, conduct a survey, then add quotes from industry experts. The resulting data-rich post attracts links from writers who need fresh statistics to cite in their own articles.

How does scholarship link building work for SEO?

You create a legitimate biannual scholarship page on your site, then email career counselors at high schools and colleges to notify them. They share it internally, generating EDU backlinks that diversify your link portfolio and drive additional web traffic to your site.

How can offering podcast transcriptions earn backlinks?

Find podcasts in your industry that lack transcripts, then offer to create one for free using a service like Rev.com, which costs about $1 per minute. In the transcript, add a line crediting your company. The podcast host posts the transcript and links back to your site.

Why should you avoid buying links according to this episode?

Paid links offer no ROI guarantee. If Google identifies a site selling or buying links, sites linked to it can also be penalized, dampening the link's value or triggering algorithmic penalties. Organically earned links provide more lasting link equity than purchased ones.

Terms defined in this episode
Link equity
The ranking value or link juice passed from one site to another through a backlink, influenced by the linking site's authority and the anchor text used.
EDU backlinks
Backlinks from educational institution domains, earned through strategies like scholarship pages, that diversify a site's link portfolio.
Broken link building
A tactic where you find broken or dead links pointing to defunct sites, then contact those linking pages and offer your relevant content as a replacement.
HARO
Help a Reporter Out, a service where sources respond to journalist queries to earn high-quality backlinks, rewarding the first respondent who provides a usable quote.
