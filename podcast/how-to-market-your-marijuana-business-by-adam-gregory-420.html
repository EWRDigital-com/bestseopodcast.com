How to Market Your Marijuana Business by Adam Gregory #420

Ep. 42026 min2018-05-21

Marijuana businesses face strict ad platform restrictions, so the best marketing mix combines SEO, print, lifestyle-focused social content, and industry publications like High Times and Leafly while following each state's laws.

Key takeaways
  • Facebook and Google prohibit paid ads for marijuana, firearms, and certain medical topics due to policy restrictions.
  • SEO and organic social content built around lifestyle themes are the most viable digital channels for cannabis businesses.
  • Print and direct mail remain effective alternatives when digital advertising options are blocked for restricted products.
  • Every state has different marijuana legalization laws, so marketers must conform to each state's specific rules.
  • Branding must be original, as cannabis companies have been sued for trademark infringement by copying names like Reese's Cups.

Questions this episode answers

What digital marketing strategies actually work for cannabis businesses?

SEO is described as the best approach, focused on answering questions and targeting destination travelers. Organic social media posting with lifestyle-focused content is also viable. Industry publications like High Times and Leafly, blogs, video content, and print or direct mail round out the recommended mix.

Why is geofencing mentioned as a tactic for marijuana marketing?

The hosts suggest geofencing the airport in Las Vegas as a way to reach the right customers, specifically visitors arriving in a state where marijuana is legal, rather than wasting ad spend on people in states where it is not relevant or legal.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing
Showing ads to users who previously visited a website, for example on MSN.com or Facebook via Google ad networks. Google restricts this for marijuana, DWI attorneys, and certain medical health topics due to privacy and sensitivity concerns.
Geofencing
Targeting ads to people within a defined geographic boundary, such as an airport, to reach relevant customers arriving in a specific location.
Linkable assets
Content created specifically to earn inbound links from other sites, described in the episode as a focus area for effective link building.
HIPAA violation
A health privacy breach, cited in the episode as the reason remarketing to users who searched for sensitive health topics like AIDS tests is restricted by ad platforms.
