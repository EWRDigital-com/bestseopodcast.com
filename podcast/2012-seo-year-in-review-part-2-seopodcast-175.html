Questions this episode answers

Should I target keywords with less competition or more search volume?

The hosts recommend targeting both when possible by theming page content around both terms. If forced to choose, pick the keyword that converts best, since long tail lower-volume terms often have higher close ratios because searchers are ready to buy rather than still researching.

How do I recover from a Google Panda or Penguin algorithm update?

Re-evaluate and rewrite thin or duplicate content, audit all inbound links and disavow bad ones, fix broken links, review on-page SEO including titles and tags, and resubmit your sitemap in Google Webmaster Tools so Google can re-index the improved site.

Should I use one or two Google Plus accounts for two separate professional identities?

The hosts say one account with separate business pages keeps all authority combined and simplifies management. Two accounts risks violating Google terms of service, but can work if the identities must stay completely separate, as long as you never cross-link them.

What makes a great blog post for SEO?

End posts with a question to encourage comments, link out to research sources, include video and images, make content easy to share, and ensure it is original and useful enough that people want to read and share it rather than being a duplicate or spun version of existing content.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
An eWebStyle term for conversion optimization, focused on what happens after a visitor clicks through from the search engine results page, aiming to drive phone calls, form submissions, downloads, or other conversions.
Link Disavow Tool
A Google Webmaster Tools feature that lets site owners tell Google to ignore specific inbound links, useful when bad or spammy sites link to your site without your approval.
Exact Match Domain
A domain name that matches a target keyword exactly, which the hosts note can help or harm search engine rankings depending on how it is used.
Author Tag
A Google Plus authorship signal that associates content with a specific person, which means a business page cannot be the author, only an individual Google Plus profile can hold authorship.
