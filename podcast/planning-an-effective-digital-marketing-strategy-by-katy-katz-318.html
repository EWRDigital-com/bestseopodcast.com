Questions this episode answers

How long does SEO take to show results?

According to a joint study with MIT and HubSpot customers cited in this episode, it can take 6 to 12 months for a website to start seeing gains from search engine and keyword-optimized content. The hosts estimate around 8 months on average, depending on industry and site age.

Should I run PPC ads if I already rank organically?

Yes. The episode explains that adding a PPC ad on top of an existing top organic placement generates more clicks, reinforces brand trust, and enables remarketing. A paid listing also lets you control ad text independently from your organic description to deliver complementary messages.

What is the digital marketing PB&J analogy?

Katy Katz of Search Engine Journal describes paid efforts like AdWords or boosted posts as the jelly, giving a quick sugary burst of energy right away. Organic SEO is the peanut butter, full of protein that builds steady energy for the long haul. Together they form a complete strategy.

What are the main reasons to run pay-per-click advertising?

The episode lists four reasons: PPC should produce a positive ROI when monitored regularly, it increases clicks even when you already hold a top organic position, it enables remarketing, and it reveals the actual keywords driving traffic that organic not-provided data hides.

Terms defined in this episode
Ramp-up time
The period before a campaign launches when you build strategy, establish goals, set timelines, determine metrics, and plan content direction.
Inbound marketing
A strategy, largely associated with HubSpot, where people find you when searching for things you do, primarily through SEO and content marketing, reducing cost of acquisition over time.
Remarketing
A paid advertising technique where ads are shown to users who have previously visited your site, added by the hosts to all PPC campaigns as a standard tactic.
Not provided
A keyword reporting gap in organic search where Google withholds the actual search terms users typed, making PPC a useful workaround to see which keywords drive traffic and engagement.
