About this episode

Questions this episode answers

How important are online reviews for businesses during COVID-19?

According to the episode, one review can increase conversions by 365%. Businesses should sprinkle reviews throughout their website, not just on a dedicated reviews page, and they should respond to both positive and negative reviews. About 40% of people asked for a review will provide one.

What should businesses do about unnatural links during a slowdown?

The episode recommends auditing your link profile and submitting a disavow file to remove bad links. One example site removed 1,900 unnatural links and reached a top three position within a week. Another site lost 82% of its traffic after building thousands of unnatural links.

Should businesses cut their marketing budget during a crisis like COVID-19?

The hosts argue against cutting the budget, calling it reactionary rather than strategic. They say SEO and online marketing build connection and trust, and how you maintain outreach during challenging times matters just as much as being ready to serve customers when things recover.

How can a local business update its online presence during COVID-19?

The episode recommends updating your Google My Business profile with new hours of operation, posting whether you are taking special care to avoid the spread of COVID-19, and using Google Posts to communicate changes. Businesses should also improve any underperforming e-commerce site so customers can shop online.

Terms defined in this episode
Disavow file
A file submitted to Google listing unnatural or low-quality links you want ignored, used to recover from manual penalties or algorithmic ranking drops caused by bad link profiles.
Link velocity
The rate at which new links are created pointing to a site; a sudden spike of low-quality links can trigger algorithmic penalties or a manual Google penalty.
First-party data
Analytics and sales data a business collects directly from its own customers and website visitors, used to identify trends and improve marketing targeting.
Google My Business (GMB) post
A short update published directly on a Google My Business listing, used to communicate changes like hours, safety measures, or promotions to local customers.
