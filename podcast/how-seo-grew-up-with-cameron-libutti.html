Questions this episode answers

How should local SEO strategy change when AI search is taking over informational queries?

Cameron LiButti argues that local businesses should prune informational blog traffic and focus topical maps on high-intent, ready-to-buy searchers who do not yet know the brand name. He describes cutting a client's traffic in half through pruning while their phone volume increased significantly.

Why does working inside long AI chat windows cause problems for agency workflows?

Long chat sessions cause the model to compress or compact earlier context, effectively forgetting details discussed earlier in the conversation. Cameron recommends using separate IDE windows for building versus running agents, and keeping sessions tight by closing them out rather than continuing in one long thread.

What is the biggest challenge agencies face when adopting company-wide AI systems?

Cameron LiButti identifies two overlapping problems: preventing spaghetti sprawl as individual team members spin up their own agents in every direction, and finding someone who combines operations and IT thinking, personality types that rarely overlap, to govern the overall system structure.

In their words

The businesses that get out ahead are gonna have org charts that mix both people and agents into them and have very clear, definitive, like this is where the agent stops, or this is where the human starts.

Terms defined in this episode
Visual regression AI
An automated check that visually compares a client website to the previous day's version and pings a developer if something no longer looks correct, without making changes itself.
Token management
The practice of keeping AI sessions short and focused so the model does not burn tokens re-reading a long conversation history before responding, preserving accuracy and reducing cost.
Topical map pruning
Removing or de-prioritizing low-intent informational content from a site's content strategy to concentrate ranking signals on high-intent, conversion-ready search terms.
