Questions this episode answers

Why is double opt-in important for email marketing?

Double opt-in protects businesses from spam complaints and legal action. Without it, people who hate unsolicited email can be submitted to your list and cause serious trouble. Some lawyers actively look for non-double-opt-in lists to send settlement demands, making double opt-in a legal and business necessity.

What are the three get-ems in Ken Roberts' internet profitability framework?

The three get-ems are: get qualified visitors to your website, get them to take some action like filling out a form or making a purchase, and get them to come back for more. Ken explains you cannot achieve the third without fully executing the first two through ongoing conversation and engagement.

How should businesses use email and social media together to retain customers?

Ken Roberts explains that within an email series you can introduce your Facebook page to subscribers, and from your Facebook page you can direct people back to your email campaign or a landing page. Cross-promoting these channels keeps the conversation going and meets customers where they already spend time.

In their words

people just want to know that you're aware of the problem, that you're working on the problem and that it's important to you. And all three of those things can only happen with communication.

Terms defined in this episode
Double opt-in
A confirmation step requiring a subscriber to verify their email address before being added to a list, used to avoid spam complaints and legal trouble from unsolicited email.
A to B split testing
Testing two versions of a page element, such as a link or headline, to see which one performs better, as described using Google's Picasso software example.
CIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A concept discussed in the podcast referring to optimizing what happens after visitors arrive, including calls to action, education, and newsletter sign-ups.
Three get-ems
Ken Roberts' three-step internet profitability framework: get qualified visitors to your site, get them to take action, and get them to come back for more.
