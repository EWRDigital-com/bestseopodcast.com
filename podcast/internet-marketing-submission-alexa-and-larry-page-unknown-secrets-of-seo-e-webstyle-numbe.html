Questions this episode answers

What does PageRank actually mean and where does the name come from?

PageRank is named after Larry Page, one of the co-founders of Google, not after the concept of ranking a web page. The patent for the PageRank process is assigned to Stanford University, where Page and Sergey Brin were studying, and Google holds exclusive rights to that patent.

What is Alexa ranking and how reliable is it?

Alexa is an Amazon company that offers a browser toolbar. As users with the toolbar browse the web, their data is sent to Alexa, which uses it to compare relative traffic across websites. It is not fully accurate because it only tracks toolbar users and can be skewed by the browsing habits of that specific group.

What is the difference between hits and unique visitors in web analytics?

A hit counts every item loaded from a server, including individual images on a page, so one page view with 20 images counts as 21 hits. A unique visitor is one distinct person visiting the site. The hosts use Google Analytics and note that unique visitors are a far more meaningful business metric than hits.

Why should a small business invest in SEO?

The hosts argue that SEO costs are relatively insignificant compared to other business expenses and that it functions as permission-based media, making a business findable exactly when potential customers are searching for it. If a business cannot be found during a search, customers will go to a competitor instead.

Terms defined in this episode
PageRank
A patented process named after Google co-founder Larry Page that ranks web pages based on which other pages link to them, forming the core of Google's search algorithm.
Alexa Ranking
A relative traffic ranking tool from Alexa, an Amazon company, that aggregates browsing data from users who have installed the Alexa toolbar to compare website traffic levels.
Unique Visitor
A single distinct person who visits a website; if the same person returns, they are not counted again as a unique visitor in that measurement period.
Hits
A server metric that counts every item loaded from a server, including individual images, meaning one page view can generate many hits and hits do not equal unique visitors.
