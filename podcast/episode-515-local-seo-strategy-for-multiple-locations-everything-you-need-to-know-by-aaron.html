Local SEO for multiple locations requires separate location pages, consistent NAP citations, optimized Google My Business listings, local link building, and an active review management process to rank in the local map pack.

Key takeaways
  • Each business location needs its own dedicated page with unique content, NAP, embedded map, and location-specific title tags.
  • NAP must be exactly identical everywhere online including Yelp, Yahoo Local, Bing Places, Foursquare, and Yellow Pages.
  • Google My Business listings should include URLs, photos, videos, products, services, and geotagged images for maximum completeness.
  • Local relevant links from chambers, BBB, local media, sponsorships, and community events carry strong ranking value for local SEO.
  • Video reviews are the most powerful trust signal; asking satisfied customers directly yields roughly 40% success in obtaining a review.

Questions this episode answers

Why does each business location need its own page for local SEO?

The Google My Business algorithm cross-references the GMB listing against the linked location page. Having one page serve multiple areas creates conflicting signals. One client discussed in this episode lost GMB rankings because a dual-use page conflicted with the specific address tied to their GMB listing.

How should a business respond to a bad online review?

Respond quickly and professionally, remove all emotion, avoid blaming the customer, and keep language from being inflammatory. The goal is to share your side of the story publicly, invite the customer to contact you, and ideally ask them to remove the review once the issue is resolved.

What are some effective ways to build local relevant links for multiple location SEO?

The episode recommends becoming a sponsor of local events, participating in charities, hosting meetups, joining chambers and the BBB, placing print ads that include a website, and pursuing local media or newspaper digital extensions. Guest posting was also mentioned but noted as a target of recent spam updates.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. These three data points must be exactly the same across every online listing, website page, and Google My Business profile for a business location.
Google My Business Insights
A data feature inside Google My Business that shows how customers search for a business, where they find it in search or maps, and what actions they take such as website visits, direction requests, or phone calls. Data is available for the last three months.
Structured Data
Code added to a website to help search engines understand content, enabling features like Google map panels or carousel listings. The episode references schema and Google structured data for developers as resources.
Snack Pack
The top three local business results shown in Google Maps search results. Ranking inside the snack pack is described in this episode as where a large share of local search traffic goes.
