Questions this episode answers

Why are closed captions important for YouTube SEO?

Closed captions let search engines crawl the text of your video, helping Google rank it for relevant keywords. One study showed videos with captions earned 13.48 percent more views in the first two weeks and 4 percent more views overall than uncaptioned videos, according to a YouTube study cited in the episode.

How many tags should you use on a YouTube video?

The hosts recommend using seven tags per video and keeping all tags focused on one core topic or theme. Spreading tags across multiple unrelated topics confuses Google and dilutes your search results, making it harder to rank for any single keyword.

How soon after publishing does YouTube's audition period begin?

According to the episode, the audition period typically starts on Thursday, so posting on Friday, Sunday night, or Monday is suggested to maximize early engagement. Getting likes, comments, and watches in the first seven days is critical before Google assigns the video a lasting rank position.

Should you translate your YouTube video captions into other languages?

Yes. The episode notes that 80 percent of YouTube views can come from outside the United States, much of it non-English. Search engines index translated caption files and can include your videos in search results in those languages, making translation a significant growth opportunity without re-recording the video.

Terms defined in this episode
ASR captions
YouTube's automatic speech recognition captions, cited in the episode as roughly 70 percent accurate, which the hosts suggest replacing with manually verified closed captions for better SEO.
Session watch time
A YouTube ranking factor measuring total time a viewer spends across multiple videos on a channel, not just a single video, rewarding channels that keep viewers watching several videos in sequence.
Splintering
A content marketing tactic where a single video transcript is broken into multiple formats such as blogs, infographics, white papers, and social media posts to extend its reach and SEO value.
Video SEO embed
Schema markup applied when embedding a YouTube video on a webpage, described in the episode as helping a video perform better in Google search and potentially earning two to five times more views than YouTube-only optimization.
