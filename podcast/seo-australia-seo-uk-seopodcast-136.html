Questions this episode answers

How do you get Google sitelinks to show up for your website?

According to the hosts, sitelinks relate to link sculpting in your navigation, having content-heavy static pages like About Us and Contact Us, and having enough pages of content. Google likely favors static pages that do not change frequently over blog posts when selecting sitelinks.

What are the best link building tactics for SEO?

The episode covers ten tactics including getting links from .gov and .edu sites, building business and institutional links, submitting to CSS galleries, analyzing competitor backlinks with Site Explorer, repurposing content across video and blog formats, social bookmarking, and building social media signals.

What is the difference between SEO strategy and SEO tactics?

Strategy is the basic way you accomplish your goal, covering steps like assessing site search-friendliness, evaluating competitors, profiling your audience, doing keyword research, and developing strong SEO writing. Tactics are the specific actions like link building, content sharing, and social media that execute that strategy.

Why is being a great writer not enough for SEO content?

The hosts argue that a great writer who ignores SEO principles can produce content that is useless for ranking. You need to be a great SEO writer, meaning someone who writes content that places well in search and keeps readers engaged, without stuffing keywords or writing purely for search engines.

Terms defined in this episode
Site Explorer
A tool used to identify who is linking to a competitor, what anchor phrases they use, and which pages receive those links, helping SEOs find link targets.
Link sculpting
Shaping the internal link structure of a site, particularly through navigation, to influence how search engines crawl and assign authority to pages, including sitelinks.
Social bookmarking
Submitting content to trend-driven sites so others can dig, stumble, or share it, generating social signals that may pass indirect link juice back to your site.
Sitelinks
Additional page links shown beneath a main Google search result, connected to an algorithm change by Google and influenced by site structure, static pages, and navigation.
