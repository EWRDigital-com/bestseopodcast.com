Questions this episode answers

What is a siloing strategy in SEO?

A siloing strategy points page authority and domain authority from blogs and supporting pages up to money pages. If a page with existing domain authority is updated and re-indexed, Google treats links pointing from it to new pages as more powerful, concentrating ranking strength where you need it most.

How should a small business use Google My Business for SEO?

Add three photos each of your team, building exterior, building interior, and your services. Label everything properly, post blogs and promotional offers directly in the platform, and use third-party tools to track clicks. Google My Business can function almost like a social media platform and expand your map listing visibility.

Why do citation pages sometimes fail to help SEO?

If a citation page does not get indexed by Google, it passes no SEO value. Auto-populated citation services often produce duplicate content that Google does not index. Creating unique content for each citation page and building them manually improves the indexing rate, which eWebResults puts at over 70%.

How often should a business follow up with cold leads?

Frequency depends on the sales cycle. For a short cycle like a three-day chiropractor referral campaign, hitting a lead around ten times across text, email, remarketing, and calls is reasonable. At minimum, send an email the same day, one a week later, one two weeks later, and one a month later.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Property Stacking
Using Google-owned platforms to build links that carry the high domain authority of Google itself, giving additional ranking juice to your target pages.
NAP Listings
Citations of a business Name, Address, and Phone number placed on high-domain-authority directories to build local SEO trust and links.
Remarketing Pixel
A tracking tag placed on a website that allows advertisers to show ads again to previous visitors across platforms like Facebook, AdWords, LinkedIn, Bing, and Twitter.
Siloing Strategy
An on-page SEO structure where blogs and supporting pages all point up to designated money pages, concentrating domain and page authority on the most important conversion pages.
