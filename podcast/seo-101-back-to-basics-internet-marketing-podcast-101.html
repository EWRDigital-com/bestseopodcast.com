Key takeaways
  • Ask two questions to find core keywords: what makes your business the most money, and what do you sell the most.
  • Long tail keywords, three or more words, collectively deliver more value than short, competitive single-word terms.
  • Every page needs a unique title tag with keywords so Google knows what information is on that page.
  • Never use duplicate content; pages with scraped or copied text get pushed to the back of search results.
  • Anchor text on inbound links should be your target keyword phrase, not your brand name, to build topical authority.

Questions this episode answers

What are the basic SEO steps for a new website?

The hosts list keywords first, then domain selection, URL structure, title tag review, meta tag review, content review for keyword density and originality, header tags, image alt tags, and inbound link building with keyword-rich anchor text. Each step maps directly to how Google reads and ranks a page.

Should I still buy a keyword-rich domain name even though Google is reducing its value?

The hosts say yes, at least until Google confirms it carries no weight at all. They argue a keyword-rich domain still builds user trust because searchers visually match the domain to their query, which can improve click-through rate regardless of any algorithm change.

How many keywords should I target per page?

The hosts recommend two to three keywords per page, and no more than three. Keywords placed on the same page should be closely related, such as air conditioning and AC, rather than mixing unrelated topics like heating and cooling on one page.

Why does duplicate content hurt SEO?

Google moves duplicate or scraped content to what the hosts call the back of the line in search results, making those pages unlikely to rank. They share a personal example where copying their own content to another page caused it to disappear from results until they rewrote it.

Terms defined in this episode
Long tail keyword
A keyword phrase of three or more words. It sits on the extended tail of the bell curve of search volume, meaning fewer people search for it, but combined long tail traffic can exceed short tail traffic value.
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, a term the hosts coined to describe optimizing the experience from the moment a user sees a search result, starting at the SERP, through to conversion on the site.
Keyword yield
A term coined in this episode to describe monitoring which keywords are actually bringing traffic and, further, which of those traffic-driving keywords are resulting in closed business.
Anchor text
The clickable text of an inbound link. Using a target keyword phrase as anchor text tells Google that the linked page contains information about that phrase, building topical relevance.
