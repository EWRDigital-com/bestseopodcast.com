Questions this episode answers

What is the 7-11-4 rule in digital marketing?

The 7-11-4 rule holds that a prospect must consume seven hours of content across your brand, see your brand 11 times, and encounter it on four different platforms. Those platforms can include social media, SEO, billboards, or other channels, with the goal of becoming omnipresent in their mind.

What is Matt Bertram's new coaching mastermind program?

Matt Bertram is launching a pilot coaching mastermind at MatthewBertram.com open to existing clients and non-clients alike. It offers peer support, accountability, networking, AI guidance, and access to guest experts. Pricing is low during the pilot phase and will increase once the program is established.

What did Brighton SEO speakers say about the future of search traffic?

Both keynote speakers at Brighton SEO, held in San Diego for the second year, said clicks are declining and the focus must shift from driving website traffic to occupying audience headspace. Marketers need to reach the right people with the right message across multiple platforms.

The show is pushing into YouTube Shorts under the hashtag internetmarketingsecretscom and broadening its scope from SEO to all things internet marketing. It also earned recognition as a top 10 percent podcast on Buzzsprout and is producing a book compiled from more than 700 episodes using AI.

Terms defined in this episode
7-11-4 Rule
A content and brand exposure framework requiring prospects to consume seven hours of brand content, encounter the brand 11 times, and see it on four different platforms before they are likely to convert.
Permission-based marketing
A marketing approach focused on building an audience that has opted in to receive messages, described in the episode as a key future strategy alongside community development.
Omnipresence
Being visible to your target audience across multiple platforms and touchpoints simultaneously, so your brand stays consistently in their awareness.
