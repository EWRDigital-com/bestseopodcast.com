Questions this episode answers

What are the most important on-page SEO content factors?

According to this episode, the four core content factors are EAT (expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness), keyword usage with semantic variations, SEO writing focused on readability and avoiding keyword stuffing, and visual assets like images, videos, and infographics that boost engagement and rankings.

Do meta descriptions directly affect Google rankings?

Meta descriptions are not a confirmed ranking factor, but this episode argues they have an outsized influence on click-through rates. Google often rewrites them, but a well-crafted description functions like anchor text, giving users a reason to click and helping control what appears in the SERP.

How should headers be structured for on-page SEO?

The episode recommends structuring headers like a newspaper: one H1, followed by nested subheadings. Putting unrelated content in headers or setting everything to H1 or H2 distributes link equity equally, preventing Google from understanding which topics are most important on the page.

Why are custom images better than stock photos for SEO?

When you reuse stock photography, it creates a canonical signal back to the original source, boosting that source's authority instead of yours. Custom images provide unique content, increase time on page, and the episode notes that articles with custom graphics consistently outperform those using stock art.

Terms defined in this episode
EAT
Expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. Mentioned 135 times across Google's 175-page search quality guidelines, it evaluates author credibility and on-page signals to determine how much authority a page deserves.
Keyword stuffing
A technique of overusing a target keyword on a page, sometimes hiding it in white text, to manipulate early search algorithms. Google now penalizes this practice and can demote or remove offending pages from results.
On-page SEO
Sometimes called on-site SEO, it is the process of tweaking a page's content, tags, and internal links to improve search visibility and increase traffic.
Title tags
A snippet of code that gives a webpage a title. It helps build context and demonstrate site relevancy, and in less competitive niches, having the right keyword in the title tag alone can move a page to the top of the SERP rankings.
