Questions this episode answers

How much should I spend on SEO compared to PPC?

According to this episode, organic listings receive about four times as many clicks as paid ads, so you should be willing to spend four times more on organic SEO than on pay-per-click. The hosts also warn against throwing a small random budget at either channel without a clear plan.

Can a website rank on the first page of Google without any inbound links?

The hosts describe a real case where a website with zero inbound links ranked on page one for national keyword searches. It achieved this through high-quality content, correct keyword density between 1 and 3 percent, and strong internal linking, though the hosts say they would not generally recommend building a site with no inbound links.

Why is Google Places so important for local SEO?

The hosts say the local maps A through G listing is the first thing users see on a results page and is becoming the most important spot in search results. They recommend completing your Google Places listing 100 percent, including videos uploaded via YouTube, images, reviews, and links to social media profiles.

What is Google Instant and how does it affect SEO and PPC?

Google Instant shows search results automatically as you type before you press enter. The hosts explain it affects impressions and click-through rates, and note a roughly three-second rule before an impression is counted. They also say Instant tends to surface long-tail phrases, which can reveal better keywords to optimize for.

Terms defined in this episode
Canonical (301 redirect)
A 301 redirect used to consolidate link juice by pointing one URL to another, discussed in the context of avoiding duplicate content issues.
Remarketing
A form of advertising that targets users who previously visited your website, often using banner ads served on other sites after a cookie is dropped on the visitor's browser.
Google Boost
A Google product that blends pay-per-click advertising with Google Places local listings, allowing businesses to pay for prominent placement within local search results.
Keyword density
The percentage of times a target keyword appears per 100 words of content. The episode recommends a density of 1 to 3 percent to avoid keyword stuffing penalties.
