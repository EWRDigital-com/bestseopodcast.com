Key takeaways
  • Do keyword research before any on-page SEO work, including titles, headers, and content structure.
  • 90% of sites can achieve their SEO goals using only the basics, so start there before anything complex.
  • 71% of the web audience has a Facebook account, making it the top starting point for social media marketing.
  • Business owners rarely stay consistently social, so hiring an agency or internal staff is often necessary.
  • Social media profiles on Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn all create additional ways for Google to find a business.

Questions this episode answers

Should I do keyword research before on-page SEO?

Yes. The hosts say to identify the correct keywords first, then use that data to do your on-page work, covering titles, headers, cross-linking, content, and navigation structure. They argue 90% of sites can reach their goals with just these SEO basics done in that order.

Why should a business do social media marketing for SEO?

Having Facebook, Twitter, and LinkedIn pages creates more ways for Google to find a business. The hosts also note that Google Plus activity like plus ones and circle adds directly helps SEO, and YouTube videos further reinforce overall SEO efforts.

How does eWebStyle approach building a social media campaign?

They first determine the client's goals, whether product exposure, branding, or service showcase, then choose the right platforms. They also ensure consistent branding across all profiles and handle ongoing posting, since most business owners do not have time to stay consistently social.

How much time do people spend on Facebook versus Google per day?

According to statistics cited in the episode, people spend about 33 minutes a day on Facebook and about 10 minutes a day on Google, which is one reason the hosts recommend Facebook as the lead example for starting a social media marketing campaign.
