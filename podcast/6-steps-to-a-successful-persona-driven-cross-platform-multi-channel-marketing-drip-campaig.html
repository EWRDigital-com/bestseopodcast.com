Questions this episode answers

What are the six steps of a persona-driven cross-platform multi-channel marketing drip campaign?

The six steps covered in this episode are targeting, offer, messaging, channel selection (email, phone, direct mail, remarketing, AdWords, Facebook, YouTube), timing and cadence, and measurement. Targeting is called the most important step by far, and measurement includes monitoring sales, recorded calls, and download completions.

Why is targeting the most important part of a drip campaign?

Precise targeting means your message reaches only the right people, making even imperfect copy effective. The hosts rate it nine out of ten in importance. With expensive tactics like mailing a message in a bottle at five to seven dollars each, wasting budget on the wrong audience can sink the entire campaign.

What should the irresistible offer in a drip campaign be?

The hosts recommend leading with education, such as a white paper or guide, rather than a discount on services. Education builds trust and authority, lowers the barrier of entry, and avoids asking too much too soon, similar to asking someone to coffee before proposing marriage.

How should you time and sequence a multi-channel drip campaign?

Send a direct mail piece every other week and schedule phone calls either before or shortly after each piece arrives, when it is likely still visible on the recipient's desk. Fit one or two emails or a call in between mail weeks. Use Google Analytics peak-traffic times to pick the best send windows and avoid Mondays.

Terms defined in this episode
Brand Mania Marketing
A precursor phase of the drip campaign where ads are placed in front of prospects before they receive their first email, named after the book Build Your Brand Mania.
Racking the Shotgun
A Perry Marshall concept where a provocative or bold offer is made to a room of prospects so only genuinely interested people react, identifying them as active prospects rather than suspects.
Skyscraper Technique
A content strategy by Brian Dean of Backlinko used to generate ideas for strong lead magnets to attract suspects into a campaign.
Custom Audiences
A Facebook feature that allows advertisers to upload an email list and serve ads specifically to those contacts across Facebook, YouTube, and AdWords platforms.
