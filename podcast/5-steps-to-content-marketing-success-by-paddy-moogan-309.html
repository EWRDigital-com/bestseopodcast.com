5 Steps to Content Marketing Success by Paddy Moogan #309

Ep. 30928 min2016-02-10

Content marketing success requires five ordered steps: research and idea generation, idea validation, production, promotion, and conversion tracking, with analytics guiding every decision along the way.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Research your topic and content format before creating anything, and tie it to your core service or target keyword.
  • Validate ideas against a checklist of simplicity, unexpectedness, concreteness, credibility, and emotion before producing content.
  • Spend as much time promoting content as producing it, mixing paid, earned, and owned promotion channels.
  • Check your analytics before deciding on mobile-first design, since traffic sources vary widely by business type.
  • Redefine conversions for content meant to go viral. A social follow, email signup, or remarketing pixel hit can count as a win.
spend just as much time on promotion as you do on a production

About this episode

Chris and Charles is going to discuss an article by Paddy Moogan where he shared a process for putting together a content marketing campaign. It doesn't guarantee success either, but it puts everyone in a much better position than having no process at all.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five steps to content marketing success according to Paddy Moogan?

The five steps are: research and idea generation, idea validation, production, promotion, and conversion and tracking. The hosts discuss each step from Moogan's Moz article, emphasizing that having a repeatable process is essential so you can test, measure, and improve your content marketing over time.

How should you validate a content marketing idea before producing it?

The episode references a framework from the book Made to Stick by Chip and Dan Heath. The checklist covers simplicity, unexpectedness, concreteness, credibility, and emotion. Hitting two or three of those criteria, such as the idea being credible and relevant, suggests you are on track to create strong content.

Should you always design content for mobile first?

The hosts say to check your analytics before committing to a mobile-first approach. If most of your traffic comes from desktops, optimize there first. One client example shows 97% mobile traffic for a restaurant, while service businesses like AC or plumbing companies tend to see more desktop visits.

What types of promotion channels should you use for content marketing?

The episode outlines three promotion types: paid, such as pay-per-click or boosted social posts; earned, based on your reputation and social influence; and owned, meaning your email list and existing followers. Mixing all three and boosting posts that already get strong engagement is the recommended approach.

Terms defined in this episode
Idea validation
A step in content marketing where you test your content idea against a framework covering simplicity, unexpectedness, concreteness, credibility, and emotion before moving into production.
Paid, earned, owned promotion
Three promotion channels: paid covers ads and boosted posts, earned is promotion based on your reputation and influence, and owned refers to your email list and existing social followers.
Remarketing list
An audience of past site visitors captured via a tracking pixel, which the episode counts as a valid conversion goal for content designed to generate shares rather than direct sales.
