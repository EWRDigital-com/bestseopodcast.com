Questions this episode answers

How do I improve my Google Places ranking?

According to this episode, the completeness of your Google Places listing directly affects your position in Google Maps results. You should fill out every available section, including photos, videos, coupons, hours, and payment types, and check back regularly as Google adds new sections.

Can I write multiple Google reviews for my own business from different accounts?

No. Each Google account can only write one review per business. You cannot create multiple accounts to write reviews for yourself. You need to get actual customers to write reviews, and each reviewer must have a Google account, though it does not have to be a Gmail account.

Does Google still use the keyword meta tag?

Google does not use the keyword meta tag for ranking, but if you place a keyword in that tag that does not appear on the page body, Google can penalize you for keyword stuffing. The hosts recommend removing any keywords from that tag that are not present in your page content.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Places
The renamed version of Google Local Business Center, where businesses claim and complete a listing that appears on Google Maps search results, including photos, videos, hours, and coupons.
SEVO
Search engine visitor optimization, a term discussed in this podcast series alongside traditional SEO.
Keyword stuffing
Placing keywords in content or meta tags in a way that does not make syntactical sense, or including keywords in the meta keyword tag that do not appear on the page, which Google can penalize.
QR code
A scannable barcode, described in the episode as a window decal Google sent to businesses that links directly to their Google Places listing when scanned with a phone camera.
