Long tail keywords, google position and traffic decreasing - #seopodcast 47

Episode30 min2010-03-17

Traffic drops are usually caused by competitors outranking you, lost backlinks, or seasonal patterns, not Google penalizing your site, and proper keyword selection plus quality anchor text backlinks help reverse declines.

Key takeaways
  • A drop of around 70 visitors in a month is likely seasonal or normal variance, not a sign of a serious problem.
  • Competitors cannot affect your website rankings without your permission, so focus on your own SEO work.
  • Check analytics once a month rather than daily to avoid overanalyzing data that changes constantly.
  • Use CSS instead of tables to make your page content more keyword rich for Google to index.
  • When building backlinks, prioritize quality sites relevant to your topic and use proper anchor text with your target keywords.

Questions this episode answers

Why is my Google traffic decreasing?

Traffic can drop for many reasons including competitors outranking you, lost backlinks, or seasonal patterns like post-holiday falloff. The hosts recommend focusing on keyword selection, link building, keyword utilization in content, and providing quality content rather than trying to diagnose one single cause.

Can an SEO company knock my site out of Google rankings?

No. As the hosts explain, competitors or SEO companies cannot affect your website without your permission. Unless your site was hacked, a ranking drop is caused by other factors such as competitors improving their own SEO, not by someone targeting your site directly.

Should I use the Google link operator to check my backlinks?

The hosts say no. They found their reported links dropped from thousands to just 27 using the link colon operator, and the consensus they found is not to rely on it. Google Webmaster Tools provides more data, though it is still not a fully reliable indicator of all your backlinks.

How should I set up goals in Google Analytics?

A goal is a target page in your website that visitors can only reach by completing a desired action. Common examples are a thank you page after filling out a form or a thank you page after completing a purchase. When a visitor reaches that page, Google Analytics records the goal as completed.

Terms defined in this episode
Long tail keywords
Keywords that are longer in word count and have fewer monthly searches, used as a starting point before targeting higher-competition terms.
Anchor text
The clickable keyword text of a backlink pointing to your website, which should include your target keywords to help SEO.
CSS (Cascade Style Sheets)
A coding approach that moves styling code off the main page so that the remaining on-page content becomes more keyword rich for Google to evaluate.
Search Engine Visitor Optimization
A term coined by the eWebStyle hosts referring to optimizing not just for search engine rankings but for converting search visitors once they arrive on the site.
