Things to Include in Website Audits - #seopodcasst 194

A website SEO audit should check external backlinks, CMS compatibility, image file names, alt attributes, page URLs, titles, meta information, keyword density, and Webmaster Tools settings including the View as Googlebot feature.

Key takeaways
  • Disavow external backlinks from low-quality or unwanted sites found during an audit.
  • Optimize image file names and alt attributes with your target keyphrase and hyphens.
  • Review titles, meta information, and keyword density at least every quarter.
  • Use Webmaster Tools View as Googlebot to confirm how pages are being indexed.
  • Ensure your CMS, preferably WordPress with secure login credentials, generates correct code.

Questions this episode answers

What SEO metrics should I check during a website audit?

According to this episode, an SEO audit should cover external backlinks, CMS compatibility, image file names and alt attributes, page URLs, titles, meta information, keyword density, Webmaster Tools setup in Google and Bing, and the View as Googlebot feature to confirm correct indexing.

What is the View as Googlebot feature and why does it matter for audits?

View as Googlebot is a feature inside Webmaster Tools that shows how Google is indexing your page. Using it during an audit lets you confirm that all content is presented correctly and that the right elements are being indexed first.

Terms defined in this episode
External backlinks
Sites that are not on your domain that are linking back to your site, which should be monitored and audited to identify and disavow unwanted links.
Disavow
The process of telling Google to ignore specific external backlinks pointing to your site that you do not want associated with your domain.
View as Googlebot
A Webmaster Tools feature that shows how Google indexes your page, letting you verify that content is presented and indexed the way you intend.
Alt attributes
HTML tag attributes on image elements that should be optimized to include your target keyphrase, helping search engines understand image content.
