  • Use BacklinkWatch.com as an independent tool to verify your backlinks outside of a search engine check.
  • Type 'link:yourwebsite.com' in Google search, with no space after the colon, to check backlinks.
  • PageRank is named after Larry Page and remains a fundamental part of the Google algorithm even if removed from Webmaster Tools.
  • Google Analytics Goals can now track up to 20 goals, up from 4, letting you monitor specific visitor conversion paths.
  • If your search engine placement is strong, focus on that result rather than obsessing over PageRank fluctuations.

Questions this episode answers

How do I check how many backlinks my website has?

Type 'link:yourwebsite.com' in the Google search bar with no space after the colon. You can also use BacklinkWatch.com as an independent source to verify which websites are linking back to you, giving you a second data point outside of Google.

What is PageRank and does Google still use it?

PageRank is a measure of link quality named after Larry Page, and it has been a fundamental part of the Google algorithm since its early days at Stanford. Even if Google removes it from Webmaster Tools or the toolbar, the hosts argue it will always be used in the underlying algorithm.

What is SEVO and why does it matter for SEO?

SEVO stands for search engine visitor optimization. Once traffic reaches your site, you need calls to action so visitors convert by clicking, calling, buying, or interacting. Getting traffic is only part of the process; closing the deal on the site is equally important.

What are Google Analytics Goals and how many can you set up?

Google Analytics Goals let you track specific visitor paths through your site, such as moving from a home page to a form page to a thank you page. Google increased the number of available goals from 4 to 20, giving site owners more flexibility to monitor different conversion paths and e-commerce activity.

Terms defined in this episode
PageRank
A measure of link quality named after Larry Page, fundamental to the Google algorithm, which scores a site based on the number and quality of inbound links pointing to it.
BacklinkWatch.com
A free third-party website the hosts use to check which sites link back to a given domain, serving as an independent source outside of Google search.
SEVO
Search engine visitor optimization, the process of converting site visitors into customers through calls to action after they arrive via search traffic.
Google Analytics Goals
A feature inside Google Analytics that lets you define and monitor specific visitor conversion paths on your website, with a limit of up to 20 goals.
