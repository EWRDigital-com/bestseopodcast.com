Converting Visitors into Prospects - #SEOpodcast 236

Episode34 min2014-07-09

To convert website visitors into prospects, businesses must know their audience, understand their sales process, craft a unique selling proposition, build credibility, and align their call to action with how their product actually sells.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Know your audience by identifying your biggest and best client and finding where they concentrate.
  • Understand your sales process: higher-priced products typically require a longer, multi-step conversion path.
  • A unique selling proposition tells customers exactly why they should choose you over competitors.
  • Confidence and credibility elements like licenses and certifications must be visible on your landing page.
  • Your call to action is the single most important element and must align with your proven sales funnel.
A call to action is the single most important thing you must have. If you were to have a webpage with only one thing and one thing only, it should be your call to action.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five things needed to convert website visitors into prospects?

The five things are: know your audience, know how your product sells, have a unique selling proposition, build confidence and credibility, and include a strong call to action. The hosts describe these as forming the foundation of a converting landing page or website.

Why does the call to action need to match the sales process?

Because conversion rates differ by channel. If 90 percent of deals close after a phone call, the primary CTA should drive phone calls. The hosts recommend making the phone number a required field on forms so every lead re-enters the most effective part of the funnel.

What is a unique selling proposition and how is it different from confidence and credibility?

A USP tells customers exactly why to choose you over competitors, such as a guarantee or a specific delivery promise. Confidence and credibility elements like board certifications or posted license numbers are expected minimums that do not differentiate you but must still be visible.

Terms defined in this episode
CVO (Conversion Visitor Optimization)
Stands for search engine visitor optimization, meaning what you do with a visitor after they arrive from a search engine, focused on turning that visit into a conversion.
Dynamic Keyword Insertion (DKI)
A pay-per-click technique that incorporates the exact search term a user typed into the ad they see, creating continuity that increases click-through rates.
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. Mentioning NAP in video audio and descriptions helps search engines index local business information for local placement.
Call to Action (CTA)
The single most important element on a webpage that prompts visitors to take a specific next step aligned with the business sales process, such as calling, submitting a form, or downloading a resource.
