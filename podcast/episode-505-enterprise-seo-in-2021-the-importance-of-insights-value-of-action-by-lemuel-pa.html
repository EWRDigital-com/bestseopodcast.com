  • Enterprise SEO requires cross-department advocacy and buy-in, not just page-level optimization.
  • Search insights should be framed as business intelligence for the whole organization, not just an SEO channel.
  • Data, not opinion, should drive decisions: use heat mapping, A/B testing, and analytics tools.

Questions this episode answers

What makes enterprise SEO different from regular SEO?

Enterprise SEO involves coordinating across multiple departments, securing internal advocates, planning 18-month strategies, and addressing site-wide legacy issues. It is not just optimizing individual pages or ranking for single keywords, but aligning the entire organization around search data as business intelligence.

How should SEOs present insights to large organizations?

SEOs should frame search insights as business intelligence that helps the whole organization, not just the SEO team. Summarizing findings succinctly for decision makers and showing how those findings impact sales, product, and marketing teams is key to getting buy-in across departments.

Why did search demand and digital focus increase in 2020 and 2021?

COVID forced companies that relied on conferences or in-person sales to shift online. The biggest observed trend was demand for websites that could sell products, with many enterprises moving heavily into Amazon and paid ads, while SEO demand remained consistent rather than spiking dramatically.

Terms defined in this episode
Enterprise SEO
SEO practiced at a large organizational scale, involving cross-department coordination, long-term strategy planning, site-wide analysis, and use of search data as organization-wide business intelligence.
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
The practice of improving how website visitors convert, using tools like heat mapping, A/B testing, and user session analysis to optimize page usability and drive more actions.
Remarketing
A paid advertising strategy that targets users who previously visited a website but left without converting, described in the episode as the best dollar spend for recapturing lost visitors.
Business Intelligence
The use of data and analytics, including search insights, to understand macro market trends and micro consumer behaviors in order to inform organizational decision-making.
