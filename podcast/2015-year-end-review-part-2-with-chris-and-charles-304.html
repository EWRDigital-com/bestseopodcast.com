Questions this episode answers

Should I hire an in-house SEO or an agency?

According to this episode, small businesses with limited content can hire an entry-level person internally for basic social and posting tasks. Larger businesses with e-commerce or complex campaigns should use an agency, which brings a strategy, a system, and multiple experts working simultaneously, often at a comparable or lower true cost when overhead and benefits are factored in.

How can I use Google AdWords data to improve my organic SEO?

The episode recommends taking the ad text from a well-converting AdWords campaign and reusing it as the meta description for the corresponding landing page. Since that text has already proven it converts paid traffic, applying it organically can produce similar click-through and conversion results.

What is the best practice for event marketing landing pages?

The episode advises creating a dedicated landing page for every event, loading it with maps, directions, vendor listings, and a registration button, then promoting that single URL everywhere. For recurring events, keep the same URL each year and simply update the content so the page retains its accumulated traffic and ranking authority.

How should I optimize page titles for Google rankings?

Put the main target keyword first, place the location at the end, and keep the total character count between 50 and 55. The episode highlights unoptimized page titles as one of the most common and repeated errors seen on new websites.

Terms defined in this episode
SIVO
Search engine visitor optimization, a term used in this episode to describe optimizing the experience for visitors who arrive from search engines, starting with the meta description they see on the results page.
Remarketing
Showing ads to people who have already visited your site. The episode notes click costs are usually 80% cheaper than standard ads and that it works across Google, Bing, and Facebook.
XML Sitemap
A file that helps search engines discover and index your site pages. The episode states plainly that every site needs one.
Ad Preview and Diagnosis
A Google AdWords tool that lets users define a geographic area and view both paid and organic search results for that location, useful for checking local rankings after Google removed the location selector from standard search.
