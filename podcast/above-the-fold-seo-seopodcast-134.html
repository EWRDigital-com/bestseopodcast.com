Questions this episode answers

What is the Google page layout algorithm update and how does it affect SEO?

The Google page layout algorithm update targets pages where ads, banner ads, or unrelated rotating images dominate above the fold instead of content relevant to the search query. Sites with that layout will hurt their rankings. Google's stated goal is to reduce user frustration by surfacing pages with genuinely relevant content at the top.

Should a business website run display ads like Google AdSense?

According to this episode, if your site sells a product or service you should not run display ads. Ads pull visitors away from your site and can even direct them to direct competitors. One example given was a moving company running AdSense ads that linked to other moving companies.

What is anchor text manipulation and why is it harmful for SEO?

Anchor text manipulation includes using multiple links with different anchor text on the same page that all point back to that same page, which is called circular linking. The episode says this is foolish and something Google is moving to penalize, as part of broader Panda-style action against link spam.

How is Google plus affecting organic search results?

When users are logged into Google and search, their results change based on who is in their Google plus circles and what those contacts have plus-oned. This means two people searching the same query can see different results, giving sites with active Google plus presence a potential visibility advantage.

Terms defined in this episode
Above the fold
The portion of a webpage visible without scrolling. Google's page layout algorithm update penalizes pages where ads and non-relevant images dominate this area instead of content matching the search query.
Anchor text
The clickable text inside a hyperlink on a page. Using multiple anchor text variations on the same page that all link back to that same page is considered manipulative and can be penalized.
Google page layout algorithm update
A 2012 Google algorithm change designed to lower rankings for pages that show excessive ads or irrelevant images above the fold rather than content relevant to the user's search.
Not provided
Keyword data withheld by Google when users are logged in during a search. The episode notes this figure was already 12 to 17 percent and predicted to reach around 25 percent due to Google plus and Android growth.
