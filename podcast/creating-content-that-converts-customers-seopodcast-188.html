Creating Content That Converts Customers - seopodcast 188

Episode15 min2013-05-29

Creating content that converts customers requires testing tone, choosing the right format, telling stories, adding stats, using real examples, finding the right voice, and asking readers for comments.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Test salesy versus review-style content to find which tone drives the most reader action, then build on that format.
  • A-B testing page elements matters: simply moving a form changed submissions from zero to about four per week.
  • Adding factual data with a source link makes content more credible and can attract significant pickup, like a news story.
  • Use real-time testimonials or published reviews to show that a product or service actually works.
  • End blog posts with a specific question to prompt comments, since questions tend to generate answers.
Questions tend to generate answers, right? And those answers can come in the form of content.

Questions this episode answers

What are the seven steps to creating content that converts customers?

The seven steps are: test different content tones, use the right content format, include a story, add stats and data with source links, give real-time examples like testimonials, use the right voice for your audience, and end articles by asking readers a specific question to prompt comments.

Why is A-B testing important for content and landing pages?

A-B testing removes guesswork by giving you numbers before and after a change. In one example from the episode, simply moving a form on a page increased form submissions from about zero per week to about four per week, showing a clear measurable impact.

How should you use data and statistics in content to build credibility?

Include factual data through charts or graphs and always link back to the source. Linking to the source is good for SEO and makes the post more credible. A single compelling statistic can attract major media pickup, as illustrated by the Barbara Cochran example in the episode.

What does having the right voice mean for content that converts?

It means writing in a tone your readers are already accustomed to, one you are comfortable with and can express consistently. That could include humor or even sarcasm if that fits your brand. The right voice makes content feel personal and helps readers identify your personality.

Terms defined in this episode
CTA
A call to action, such as a form, phone number, download link, or any action you want a site visitor to take after landing on a page.
A-B testing
Comparing two versions of a page or content element with numbers before and after to determine which version produces better results.
Call extension
A Google AdWords feature that adds a phone number to an ad, which the episode notes requires using Google Voice and can increase ad costs.
Link bait
Content, especially stat-driven content, that is compelling enough to attract links and pickup from other sites or media outlets.
