Facebook Ads vs. Google AdWords: Which one should you be using? Part 2 #447

Ep. 44727 min2019-02-08

Facebook ads generate demand through granular audience targeting while Google AdWords harvests existing demand, and most businesses benefit from using both platforms together as a complementary multi-channel strategy.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Facebook ads blend natively into the feed, unlike display ads, making them more effective for engagement.
  • Target audience selection matters most in any ad campaign, rated nine out of ten in effort priority.
  • Lookalike audiences on Facebook let advertisers upload a customer list and reach a much larger similar audience.
  • A-B testing tools like AdExpresso help automate image versus video and thumbnail comparisons to multiply results.
  • Bing traffic may be one-tenth of Google but can show double the conversion rate for certain audiences like baby boomers.

About this episode

Facebook Ads vs. Google AdWords: Which one should you be using? Part 2 Chris and Matt talks about what sets Google AdWords and Facebook Ads apart, how the two ad platforms work, and why you should consider using both as part of your wider digital marketing strategy.

Questions this episode answers

Should I use Facebook ads or Google AdWords for my business?

According to this episode, the answer is it depends on your strategy, but probably both. AdWords captures people already searching for your product, while Facebook ads target people who have not yet expressed intent, making them complementary tools rather than competing ones.

How does Facebook ad targeting work?

Facebook draws on the personal details users share, such as life milestones and career moves, to allow granular audience targeting. Advertisers can also upload their own customer lists and Facebook creates a lookalike audience, potentially expanding a list of 1,000 people to 10,000 or more to target.

What is a Facebook sales funnel?

As described in this episode, the funnel starts with SEO or ads driving traffic to a landing page, followed by Facebook remarketing to bring visitors back, then an offer like a downloadable, and finally email follow-up. It is a multi-step process designed to move prospects toward contacting the business.

What is A-B testing for Facebook ads and why does it matter?

A-B testing means running variations of your ads, such as image versus video or different thumbnails, to see which performs better. The hosts recommend a tool called AdExpresso to automate this process, calling it a way to multiply your results and improve ROI on the Facebook platform.

There used to be silver bullets of AdWords, of Facebook, and really now it's becoming a multi-channel strategy.

Terms defined in this episode
Lookalike Audience
A Facebook targeting feature where advertisers upload a customer list and Facebook uses its algorithms to find and target new users who share similar characteristics, potentially expanding a seed list many times over.
Harvesting Demand
A term used in this episode to describe how Google AdWords captures users who are already actively searching for a product or service, as opposed to generating new interest.
Remarketing
Showing ads to people who previously visited your website, described in the episode as being like emails without the emails, used to bring potential customers back into the sales funnel.
A-B Testing
Running two or more variations of an ad simultaneously, such as comparing an image ad to a video ad or testing different thumbnails, to determine which version drives better results.
