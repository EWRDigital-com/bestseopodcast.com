Questions this episode answers

Should I target high search volume or low competition keywords for SEO?

The hosts recommend targeting at least one high competition money phrase alongside low competition long tail phrases. Low competition words are easier to rank for and tend to bring higher quality visits, while high competition phrases build visibility for terms that convert.

What is negative SEO and should I worry about it?

Negative SEO is using SEO tactics to devalue a competitor's site, such as pointing spammy or malware site links at them. The hosts believe Google likely zeroes out bad link value rather than truly penalizing innocent sites, but the risk is real for small or new sites with few natural links.

How do I know if Google's Penguin update penalized my site?

Check your analytics comparing one month before and after April 24th. Look for traffic drops, review your keyword referral report for lost rankings, manually check your top money phrases, audit your link profile, and check Google Webmaster Central for any messages about your site.

Terms defined in this episode
Negative SEO
The art of devaluing a competitor's site using SEO tactics, such as pointing spammy or dangerous site links at their domain to trigger a penalty.
Penguin update
A Google algorithm update released April 24th targeting web spam, specifically sites with spammy inbound links including paid exact match anchor text links and comment spam.
Not provided
A Google Analytics placeholder that replaces keyword data when a user is logged into a Google account during search, affecting keyword referral reporting for site owners.
Exact match anchor text
A paid link where the clickable text matches a target keyword precisely, flagged by Penguin as a spam signal when coming from low quality or link seller sites.
