Key takeaways
  • Irrelevant links from outside your topical universe send red flags to Google's machine learning and create noise.
  • Broken link building is highly underrated: finding and replacing dead backlinks on authoritative sites earns real link equity.
  • Unique original images, infographics, and AI-generated graphics count as unique content and are more likely to earn links than stock photos.
  • LinkedIn newsletters can reach 60,000 followers in three days, making them a powerful tool for authority and traffic.
  • Less is more with linking: random profile links or low-quality citations dilute your signals more than they help.
Link building is the art of sending signals to Google from a machine learning standpoint for it to understand what you're trying to say.

Questions this episode answers

Why do random or irrelevant backlinks hurt your SEO?

According to Matt Bertram, Google's machine learning can detect when a link comes from outside your topical universe, which sends a red flag. Spammy or unrelated links water down the signals you are trying to create and confuse search engines rather than helping them understand your site.

What is broken link building and why does it matter?

Broken link building means finding dead or broken links on high-authority sites and offering a relevant replacement. Matt Bertram calls it highly underrated, noting it works roughly 15 to 20 percent of the time, and that landing even one high-quality link makes the effort worthwhile.

Are stock images bad for link building?

Matt Bertram says stock images earn no credit for your site because the original poster gets the credit. He recommends unique infographics, original photos, and AI-generated graphics, which he says Google treats as unique content and which are more likely to attract links and engagement.

What book does Matt Bertram recommend for learning link building?

Matt Bertram recommends The Ultimate Guide to Link Building by Eric Ward and Garrett French, published by Entrepreneur. He calls it one of the best books he has ever read on link building and notes a used copy was available for six dollars at the time of recording.

Terms defined in this episode
Link equity / link juice
The value or authority passed from one site to another through a backlink, which Matt Bertram describes as the reward earned through activities like broken link building.
Broken link building
A strategy of finding broken or dead links on other websites and offering your own relevant content as a replacement, in order to earn a high-quality backlink.
Dark social
Traffic and engagement that comes from offline or untraceable channels, such as radio or word of mouth, where you cannot see where the visitors are coming from online.
Hidden gems
A Google algorithm update mentioned by Matt Bertram that surfaces Reddit forums and similar community content to provide diversity of thought in search results.
