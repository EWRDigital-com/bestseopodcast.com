Key takeaways

  • Exact match (brackets) shows how many people searched for precisely that term, nothing more or less.
  • Long tail keywords (three or more words) attract searchers more likely to want your specific product or service.

Questions this episode answers

What are the keyword match types in Google's Keyword Selector tool?

There are four match types: broad match (default, very wide), phrase match (keywords must appear together in that order, in quotes), exact match (only that exact phrase, in brackets), and negative match (AdWords only, excludes a word from triggering ads). For SEO research, broad, phrase, and exact are the relevant ones.

Why should I use long tail keywords instead of short tail keywords for SEO?

Long tail keywords (three or more words) attract searchers who are more specifically looking for your product or service. Short tail terms like 'lawyer' attract broad research traffic, not buyers. Someone typing a long phrase like 'personal injury lawyer in Houston Texas' is far more likely to need your specific service.

Why does my website not display on iPhone or iPad?

If your site uses Flash for key components, it will not display on iPhone or iPad because those devices do not support Flash. The recommended fix is to detect Safari mobile browsers and serve a static non-Flash version of the page instead, since a significant share of mobile web traffic comes from iPhones.

How does Google's PPC ranking algorithm work beyond bidding?

Google no longer ranks ads solely by highest bid. It also factors in the relevancy of the landing page to the keyword being targeted. A well-matched landing page can earn you a better position at a lower cost per click, which is why using a pay-per-click expert can reduce spend while improving results.

Terms defined in this episode
Broad Match
The default keyword match type in Google's Keyword Selector tool. It returns search volume for any query containing either word in any order or combination, making results very wide and often inflated.
Exact Match
A keyword match type using brackets. It shows how many times users searched for precisely that term with no additional words before, after, or in between.
Phrase Match
A keyword match type using quotation marks. It requires the keywords to appear next to each other in that exact order, but allows additional words before or after the phrase.
Long Tail Keyword
A keyword phrase made up of three or more words. Long tail keywords attract more relevant, purchase-ready searchers compared to broad single-word or two-word short tail terms.
