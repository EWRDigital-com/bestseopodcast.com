Questions this episode answers

How do I tell if a keyword has local or national search intent?

Search the keyword in an incognito browser and check what the SERP shows. No map pack and no city or state pages means national intent. A map pack with location pages means local intent. A map pack where your physical proximity dominates the results signals hyperlocal intent.

Why does store locator architecture matter for SEO?

Brands whose store locators relied on a search box only, with no linkable state, city, and location pages, had 50% fewer keywords ranking in top positions compared to brands that built a fully linked locator architecture that bots and users could navigate.

How can a service area business with no physical location gain local pack visibility?

One option is creating a store within a store at a partner brand location, the way FedEx placed on-site services inside Walgreens stores. Another option, if lead value is high enough, is opening a dedicated physical location in target areas to become eligible for local packs.

Terms defined in this episode
Hyperlocal search intent
A query type where the searcher's physical location is the most significant factor in the results, causing the map pack to dominate the SERP, as with searches like auto insurance near me.
Service Area Business (SAB)
A business such as a plumber or roofer that targets local search queries but has no storefront, making it ineligible to appear in Google local packs without a verified physical address.
Single source of truth (location data)
One central platform, such as Yext or Uberall, where a multi-location brand manages all its Google Business Profile listings and directory data to avoid conflicting signals.
Google Business Profile (GBP)
A set of local listings for business locations that functions, as described in the episode, like a separate search engine wrapped together with national search, requiring unique and accurate content per location.
