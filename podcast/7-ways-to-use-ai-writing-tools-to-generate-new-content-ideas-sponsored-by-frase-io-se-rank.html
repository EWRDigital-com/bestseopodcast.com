AI writing tools can generate blog topic ideas, outlines, intros, meta descriptions, cold emails, and ad copy in roughly 15 to 20 minutes, but human review and expertise are required before publishing.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • AI tools work best as support for content creation, not as a replacement for primary, expert-driven writing.
  • Google considers content wholly generated by AI to be spam, so human editing is essential before publishing.
  • AI-generated facts and figures are sometimes fabricated and must be verified and replaced by the writer.
  • Running an AI template multiple times and selecting the best output improves quality significantly.
  • Early adopters of AI writing tools gain a competitive advantage over those who delay experimenting with the technology.
Google considers AI-generated content to be spam. So you shouldn't just publish whatever an AI gives you without looking at it.

Terms defined in this episode
Content clustering
A strategy where one core topic sits at the center and several related topics, often drawn from People Also Ask questions, are built around it to create overlapping, interconnected content.
AI writing assistant
A software tool that uses artificial intelligence to generate written content such as blog posts, meta descriptions, and marketing copy from user-supplied inputs like keywords, product descriptions, and tone of voice.
