8 Tips to Help You Set up a Killer Email Drip Campaign by Jason James #406

Ep. 40630 min2018-02-06

Email drip campaigns remain a powerful marketing tool when you identify goals, sequence emails correctly, avoid spammy subject lines, and use automation to build customer loyalty and drive conversions.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Identify clear goals before building any drip campaign, such as brand awareness, sales, or customer retention.
  • Send the first seven emails daily for membership or SaaS trial campaigns to maximize conversion rates.
  • Spend roughly 50% of your composing time on the subject line because it determines open rates.
  • Use the Seinfeld model for long-term sequences: tell a story about something relevant, then tie it back to your offer.
  • Track everything, avoid deleting campaign data, and re-send the same email with a different subject line to reach unopened recipients.
The number one reason for me of email marketing is the power of automation.

Questions this episode answers

How often should I send emails in a drip campaign?

According to the episode, the first seven emails of a campaign can be sent on a daily basis, especially for membership or SaaS trial scenarios. After that longer-term sequences can use a story-based approach, referencing Russell Brunson's Seinfeld model, where you share something relevant and tie it back to your offer.

Why is the subject line so important in email marketing?

The subject line determines how many people open the email. The episode recommends spending about 50% of your time on the subject line and testing three types: quirky, fact, and tease. You can resend the same email with a different subject to reach people who did not open the first version.

What is the Seinfeld email model?

Referenced in the episode via Russell Brunson, the Seinfeld model is used for longer-term email sequences. Like the show about nothing, you check in with subscribers by sharing a story or something relevant and then tie it back to what you are offering, keeping the relationship warm over time.

Terms defined in this episode
Email drip campaign
A series of pre-written emails sent automatically on a set schedule to turn cold or opted-in leads into customers through consistent, sequenced communication.
Seinfeld model
A long-term email sequence strategy, referenced via Russell Brunson, where you share a story or something relevant and tie it back to your offer, similar to a show about nothing.
