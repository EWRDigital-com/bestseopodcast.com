Questions this episode answers

How do you optimize for Bing SEO?

According to this episode, consistent SEO for Bing means great content, keyword focus, accurate meta tags, and a strong inbound linking campaign. Because Bing pulls titles and descriptions directly from your tags, those tags should be written to entice clicks and begin the sales process, not just repeat keywords.

Is Bing worth targeting for pay-per-click advertising?

Yes. The hosts say Bing holds 10% of the online search market, which represents a significant volume of traffic. They also note there is a potentially significant cost-saving reason to use Yahoo and Bing for pay-per-click compared to Google, making it worth including in campaigns.

What are Bing site links and can you control them?

Site links are the additional page links shown below a main search result listing, giving extra real estate and more sales opportunities. Unlike Google, where the webmaster tool lets you remove site links you do not want, Bing does not offer the same level of control over them.

What does it take to make money from a website today?

The hosts say you need traffic, a website that converts visitors through the right calls to action, the right products or services, good website structure, and strong customer service. Simply having a website or high traffic is no longer enough on its own.

Terms defined in this episode
Site links
Additional links to internal pages of a website displayed below the main search result listing, giving more real estate and extra sales opportunities at the search result level.
Alt tag
Text attached to an image that describes its content, visible when hovering over the image, believed in the SEO community to affect search engine optimization.
Decision engine
Microsoft's stated concept for Bing, designed to help searchers make decisions especially around shopping, travel, and health rather than just returning information.
Explore Panes
Microsoft's name for the category sections in Bing that organize searches, containing links called Quick Tabs, which Bing continually tweaks.
