Episode42 min2011-03-09

Google Local Places rankings depend most on off-page citations from trusted directories and check-ins, while on-page factors like city/state in title tags and full address help trust but rarely move rankings significantly.

Key takeaways
  • Get your Google Local Places listing into compliance by making the title match your actual business name.
  • Citations from high-quality directories like Yelp, CitySearch, and Foursquare are the most powerful off-page factor for local rankings.
  • Quality of citations matters more than quantity, so prioritize authoritative directories like DMOZ over low-value sites.
  • Tagged photos and videos associated with your business can help move your local listing position by making it appear active and trustworthy.
  • Location-based check-ins on services like Foursquare are a major ranking signal, likely counted similarly to a review by Google.

Questions this episode answers

Do you need a website to rank in Google Local Places?

No, you can rank in Google Local Places without a website. The hosts confirm they have seen businesses without websites rank alongside or above businesses that have one. However, they still strongly recommend having a website to convey quality and value to customers.

How do check-ins on services like Foursquare affect a Google Local Places listing?

Check-ins on geo-targeted services like Foursquare are described as a major local ranking signal, possibly equivalent to a review in Google's local algorithm. More check-ins can also mean more frequent crawling of your listing and more citations, which the hosts say is a very positive factor.

What is a KML file and does a local business need one?

A KML file works similarly to a sitemap but focuses on your business address, helping search engines find your location. It is recommended mainly for businesses with multiple locations, like franchises. For a single-location business, the hosts say it is not a key priority.

Terms defined in this episode
Citations
References to your business name, address, and phone number on other credible websites such as directories, Yelp, and CitySearch. The quantity and quality of these citations are described as the most powerful off-page factor for Google Local Places rankings.
KML file
A file similar to a sitemap that provides search engines with trusted location data for a business. Most beneficial for businesses with numerous locations such as franchises or restaurant chains.
My Maps
A Google application that allows users to embed or save maps from Google Maps onto other websites or blogs. The number of My Maps that include your business is listed as an off-page local ranking factor.
IYP portals
Internet Yellow Pages portals such as yellowpages.com. Citations from these and other major data providers are rated as a very high importance off-page factor for Google Local Places listings.
