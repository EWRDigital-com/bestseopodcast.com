7 Powerful Benefits of Using PPC Advertising by Corey Morris #377

Ep. 37740 min2017-06-15

PPC advertising delivers seven key benefits including fast market entry, precise targeting, full campaign control, and rich marketing data that can inform broader business and SEO strategy decisions.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • PPC can contribute to any business goal, from brand exposure to direct sales and lead generation.
  • Syncing AdWords with Google Analytics and setting up conversion goals is essential to avoid wasting budget.
  • A PPC campaign can go live within hours, while organic SEO can take six months to a year to gain traction.
  • Running a short PPC campaign reveals which keywords convert, giving data to guide SEO strategy before optimizing.
  • AdWords targeting lets you filter by gender, device, location, and time of day for highly specific audience reach.
PPC can have a major and positive impact on most businesses and brands. If you aren't doing any PPC marketing, you're likely losing out on valuable traffic and revenue.

About this episode

Pay. Per. Click. PPC advertising gets you in front of your audience instantly, with one caveat: you have to know how to use it effectively. Tune into SEO Podcast 377 and let Charles and Chris give you the 7 Reasons to Use Pay-Per-Click Ads.

Questions this episode answers

What are the seven benefits of PPC advertising covered in this episode?

The seven benefits are: PPC contributes to business goals, it is measurable and trackable, it offers quick entry compared to SEO, you are in control of targeting options, it works well with other marketing channels like content and SEO, it has incredible targeting options, and it provides a wealth of marketing data.

How does PPC help with SEO strategy?

Running a short paid campaign generates data on which keywords drive conversions and which produce bounces or bad leads. That data can then guide SEO keyword selection, effectively testing and filtering target terms before committing long-term organic optimization resources to them.

Why is setting up goals in Google Analytics important for PPC campaigns?

Without goals, you only see traffic volume and cost, not whether visitors completed a form, downloaded content, subscribed, or called. Goals tied to conversion pages or call-tracking software reveal the actual outcome of each paid click, so you can measure real return on investment.

How quickly can a PPC campaign start driving results compared to SEO?

According to the episode, a site can be built, a campaign set up, and after roughly an hour or two for Google to review and approve the ads, clicks and leads can start arriving. Organic SEO for a new site can take six months to a year to achieve comparable positioning.
