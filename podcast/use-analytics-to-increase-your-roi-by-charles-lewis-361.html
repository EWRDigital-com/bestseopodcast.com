Questions this episode answers

What are the seven Google Analytics reports that help increase ROI?

The seven reports covered are: All Traffic, Device Overview, New vs. Returning, Channel Report, Pages Report, Locations Report, and Demographics Report. Each one reveals a different aspect of how visitors find and interact with your site, helping you decide where to focus marketing resources.

How should I set up goals in Google Analytics?

Goals should be tied directly to revenue-generating actions, such as a contact form submission landing on a thank-you page, a phone call over a meaningful duration like 30 or 60 seconds, or reaching a key page like a pre-qualification form. Avoid goals like time on site or random page views.

Why does the Demographics report need to be enabled manually in Google Analytics?

The Demographics report is not available by default and does not collect historical data. You must check the box to enable it before it starts gathering age and gender data. Once enabled, you can find it under Audience, Demographics, Overview in the left sidebar.

How can the Locations report help a business considering opening a new location?

The Locations report shows which geographic areas are sending traffic and conversions to your site. If you receive strong engagement and conversions from an area where you have no physical presence, that data can serve as an indicator of where a new location might succeed.

Terms defined in this episode
All Traffic Report
A Google Analytics report found under Acquisition, All Traffic, Source or Medium that shows which sources are sending traffic and how each source engages, including conversions.
Channel Report
A Google Analytics report under Acquisition, All Traffic, Channels that groups traffic by marketing channel type such as organic, paid search, social, or referral, rather than by individual source.
Attribution
The challenge of determining which traffic source or marketing touchpoint deserves credit for a conversion when a visitor interacts with multiple channels before converting.
