Questions this episode answers

What were the top five SEO ranking factors identified in the 2009 year in review?

The five factors were: keyword-focused anchor text from external links, external link popularity, diversity of link sources, keyword use in the title tag, and trustworthiness of the domain based on link distance from trusted domains. Three of the five relate directly to links.

How can you influence the anchor text that another website uses when linking to you?

You can contact the linking site by email or phone and ask them to use specific keyword-focused anchor text instead of a generic word or your brand abbreviation. The episode suggests building relationships and possibly offering something in return, such as a reciprocal link or a discount.

Why does diversity of link sources matter for SEO?

If all external links come from the same domain, Google assigns less value because it is easy to create a site whose only purpose is to link back to your own site. Links from a wide variety of different domains signal more genuine, unbiased endorsement.

What is SEVO and why does it matter?

SEVO stands for Search Engine Visitor Optimization. The episode explains it as balancing what pleases the search engine bot with what delivers a good experience to the human visitor. Ranking on page one means nothing if the site has no content and visitors immediately bounce.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword-focused anchor text
The actual clickable words used in a hyperlink pointing to your site, ideally containing the target keywords such as 'blue one-arm widgets' rather than a generic label.
External link popularity
The total count and quality of inbound links from other domains pointing to your website, treated by Google as a signal of authority and relevance.
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, the practice of balancing the needs of search engine crawlers with a quality experience for human visitors.
Link distance from trusted domains
How many link hops separate your site from a recognized high-authority domain like MSN. Fewer hops means greater trustworthiness in Google's eyes.
