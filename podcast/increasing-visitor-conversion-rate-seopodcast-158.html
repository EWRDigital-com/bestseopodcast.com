Questions this episode answers

What is internal page rank sculpting in SEO?

Internal page rank sculpting means linking from your existing high-PR, well-ranking pages to a target page you want to rank. In the episode, linking to a 'home movers' page from strong existing pages raised its PR enough to rank in search results even though the page was not in the main navigation.

Why should social media share buttons go at the bottom of an article?

Placing share buttons at the bottom means readers have already consumed and appreciated the content before being asked to share it. The hosts note that nine times out of ten a reader who finishes an article is far more willing to share it than someone who sees the prompt before reading a word.

How do you choose the right keywords for your website?

Choose keywords based on what your customers actually type, not internal industry terms. The episode uses HVAC versus AC repair as an example: HVAC is trade jargon used to order parts, while AC repair is what customers search for. Consider whether keywords should be local, regional, or national based on your business focus.

Why is having one company handle both website design and SEO marketing important?

When separate companies handle design and traffic separately, neither is accountable if visitors do not convert into customers. One company responsible for both can ensure that traffic, site structure, and content all work together to produce actual business results, not just a pretty site or raw visitor numbers.

Terms defined in this episode
Internal Page Rank Sculpting
The practice of linking from high-authority pages on your own site to a target page in order to pass page rank to it and improve that page's ranking in search results.
Over-optimization
Creating excessive separate pages targeting keywords when content could be merged, or stuffing keywords and fluff content, which can trigger Google penalties and hurt user experience.
Breadcrumbs
Navigational elements on a website that help users understand their location within a site and move around quickly, especially on sites with many pages.
Bounce
When a visitor lands on a page and immediately leaves because the content does not match what they were searching for, such as landing on the wrong page due to an incorrect AdWords campaign or poorly optimized site.
