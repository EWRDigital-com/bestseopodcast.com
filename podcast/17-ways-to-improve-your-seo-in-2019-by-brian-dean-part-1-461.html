Questions this episode answers

What is snippet bait in SEO?

Snippet bait is a strategy where you write a short block of content specifically designed to fit inside Google's featured snippet box. You first identify a keyword you already rank for on page one, determine the type of snippet needed such as definition, list, or table, then craft content matched to that format.

What is a ghost post and how does it help with keyword research?

A ghost post is a page published on your site without being promoted to social networks or elsewhere. Brian Dean used this to test whether the keyword free SEO tools was truly competitive. The page ranked quickly, proving some keywords tools label as difficult are actually easy to rank for.

How do you get site links to appear in Google search results?

According to Brian Dean's technique covered in this episode, adding a table of contents with jump links to your page can trigger Google to display site links. Once his page ranked in the top five, Google added site links, boosting his organic click-through rate.

Why does commercial intent matter more than search volume for keyword targeting?

A keyword with only 350 searches per month but a $25 cost per click in paid search signals a high concentration of buyers. Brian Dean targeted link building services for this reason, finding it less competitive than tools suggested while still attracting high-value commercial traffic.

Terms defined in this episode
Snippet bait
Content written specifically to match the character count and format of a Google featured snippet box, targeting a keyword the page already ranks for on page one.
Ghost post
A page published on a website without any social promotion, used to test real-world keyword difficulty independent of third-party tool scores.
Dwell time
The amount of time a visitor spends on a page before returning to search results, treated in this episode as a Google ranking factor improved by adding video.
Commercial intent
An indicator of how likely a keyword is to attract buyers rather than researchers, often measured by cost per click in paid search advertising.
