Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between hits and visits in Webalyzer?

A hit is recorded every time the web server is requested for any file, including images and CSS, so one visitor to a page with two images and a CSS file counts as four hits. A visit counts the actual person who came to the site, making visits the more meaningful metric for understanding real audience size.

Why are entry pages important for SEO and PPC?

Entry pages show which pages people first land on when they arrive at your site, whether from a search engine or a direct URL. Knowing this helps you optimize those pages with better calls to action, correct keywords, and relevant content so visitors stay and convert.

What are search strings in Webalyzer and why do they matter?

Search strings are the keywords people typed into a search engine that brought them to your site. Webalyzer shows the top search strings, which reveals how customers already find you. This is valuable for starting or refining a PPC or SEO campaign around terms with proven traffic.

Why should you keep old page URLs live when renaming pages for SEO?

If Google has already indexed a page under its old URL, renaming it without keeping the old page causes visitors who click that Google result to get a 404 error. Leaving the old page live prevents that broken experience until Google updates its index.

Terms defined in this episode
Webalyzer
A free server-log analysis tool that processes web server logs to show hits, files, pages, visits, referrers, search strings, entry pages, and exit pages for a website.
Referrer
A website that links to your page and actually sends a visitor through that link. It only appears in reports when someone clicks the link and arrives at your site.
User Agent
The browser or software a visitor uses to access a website, such as Internet Explorer, Firefox, or Chrome, as recorded in server logs.
404 Error
A response code meaning the requested page was not found on the server, often caused by a page being renamed or deleted while the old URL remains indexed in Google.
