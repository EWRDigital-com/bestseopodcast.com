Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between ranking and positioning in SEO?

Ranking varies depending on the user's IP address and location, making it hard to gauge consistently. Positioning refers to where a site appears in specific result areas, such as top three organic results, local listings, or paid ads, giving marketers more control over their data.

How should you solicit Google reviews without getting penalized?

Ask everyone publicly and accept any review that comes in, positive or negative. Never incentivize reviews with discounts or gifts, and never have multiple people write reviews from the same IP address, as that can result in all reviews being removed.

Why is segmenting remarketing lists important?

Segmenting ensures people see ads relevant to what they actually browsed. For example, if a site offers both services and products, separate lists let you show service ads to service visitors and product ads to product visitors, improving relevance and conversion rates.

Terms defined in this episode
Dynamic Keyword Insertion (DKI)
A PPC feature where a searcher's keyword is automatically inserted into the ad copy, making the ad more relevant by mirroring the exact phrase the user searched for.
Remarketing segmentation
Dividing site visitors into separate retargeting lists based on their behavior or interests so each group sees ads relevant to what they looked at.
Citations
Mentions of a business used to establish its online presence. The episode describes citations as paramount and the difference between a well-placed page and a non-well-placed page in competitive industries.
Review gate
The practice of incentivizing people to leave positive reviews, such as offering discounts or gift cards, which violates review platform guidelines.
