Questions this episode answers

What digital marketing strategies work best for B2B companies?

The episode recommends building a high-converting, mobile-friendly website first, then adding SEO, email marketing, organic and paid social, PPC, and influencer marketing in layers. Starting with SEO and email automation is emphasized as the strongest foundation before adding other channels.

How is B2B digital marketing different from B2C marketing?

B2B involves multiple stakeholders and longer sales cycles, so marketers need separate buyer personas for each decision maker. B2B buyers are influenced by logic, ROI, and data, though the episode notes people still make emotional decisions and respond to storytelling.

Should B2B companies run both SEO and PPC at the same time?

Yes. The episode explains that PPC helps inform the algorithm when SEO rankings are not yet strong, so the two work together. Having one team or agency manage both is recommended to avoid silos and ensure constant communication between campaigns.

How important is influencer marketing for B2B companies?

The episode says influencer marketing functions like PR and becomes valuable once other foundations are in place. Finding influencers with an engaged audience that matches potential customers matters more than follower count, with 10,000 followers cited as a general threshold.

Terms defined in this episode
Buyer Persona
A profile representing a target customer segment, used to guide B2B sales and marketing strategies and map pain points to the solutions being offered.
Account-Based Marketing
A B2B strategy that targets specific accounts or companies rather than broad audiences, often paired with account-based selling.
Lead Scoring
A method inside a CRM that assigns values to leads so that automation can trigger targeted campaigns or drip emails when a threshold is reached.
Lookalike Audiences
Ad targeting that finds new potential customers who share characteristics with an existing customer segment, used in paid social media campaigns.
