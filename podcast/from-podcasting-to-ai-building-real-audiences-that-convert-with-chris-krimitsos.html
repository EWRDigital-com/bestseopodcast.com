Questions this episode answers

How should a B2B brand get started with podcast or creator partnerships?

Start a YouTube channel as your home base and distribute shorts from it everywhere else. If you lack in-house talent, hire a creator you like to run the channel. Begin with a 90-day test, give the creator parameters but not micromanagement, and extend to six months before judging results.

How do you measure whether a creator partnership is actually working?

Look beyond click-through rates and affiliate links. Train your team to ask callers how they found you, because many buyers search independently after hearing an influencer. Gabe of the Let's Play Through golf channel drove far more sales to Play Better than affiliate codes alone captured.

Why is human-created long-form content important for LLM and AI search visibility?

LLMs need human interaction to train their data sets and synthetic data does not substitute for that. Podcasting and long-form video are less saturated by AI-generated spam than written content, making them valuable signals for AI platforms weighting human creators higher.

What is the biggest mistake brands make when working with creators?

They play scared and over-control the content, dictating details down to the commas and periods. One example in the episode involved an ad agency correcting a creator ten times over a shade of blue for Chase Bank, exhausting the creator and undermining the campaign's performance.

Terms defined in this episode
7114 rule
A content framework referencing a Google paper: create seven hours of content, have your brand seen 11 times across four different channels to build brand association with a decision maker.
Creator on the rise
A metric-based term for creators whose audience numbers are growing rapidly, valued by brands because their fan bases are excited and more willing to try new things.
LLM visibility
Being discoverable and cited within large language model platforms like ChatGPT, treated in this episode as the near-term evolution beyond traditional Google SEO.
NIL (Name Image and Likeness)
A term originating in college sports allowing individuals to monetize their personal brand through partnerships, now extending to content creators and influencer deals.
