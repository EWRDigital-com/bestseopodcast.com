Key takeaways
  • Domain authority is measured on a 100 point scale; scores in the 90s are strong, scores in the 20s need improvement.
  • Site speed matters critically once load time crosses the four to five second threshold, hurting both rankings and user experience.
  • Backlinks remain important but plus ones and reviews may carry equal or greater value in some cases.
  • A high bounce rate can signal a good user experience if visitors call or convert without browsing multiple pages.
  • Ask clients which analytics reports they actually want to see, rather than sending data they do not use.

Questions this episode answers

What are the eight most important SEO data points for a website?

According to Neil Patel as discussed in this episode, they are domain authority, site speed, backlinks, number of indexed pages, social media interaction, site sessions, organic visits, and bounce rate. Each one can be found using free tools such as Google Analytics, Open Site Explorer, or Google Webmaster Tools.

Where can I check my website's domain authority?

The episode recommends using Open Site Explorer. Enter your URL there to find your domain authority score, which Moz measures on a 100 point scale. Scores in the 20s are considered low, while scores in the 90s indicate strong authority.

Is a high bounce rate always bad for SEO?

Not necessarily. The hosts explain that if a visitor finds what they need, calls the business, and leaves without viewing a second page, that is a good bounce. The concern is a bad bounce where users return to search results and click a competitor, which signals a poor experience to search engines.

What is the difference between sessions and visits in Google Analytics?

Google Analytics replaced visits and unique visitors with sessions and unique sessions. A session covers a user interacting with the site across multiple pages, and even if the user leaves and returns within a short period they can still be counted in the same session, making sessions a broader measure than a simple visit count.

Terms defined in this episode
Domain Authority
A Moz metric measured on a 100 point scale that indicates the overall strength and ranking potential of a domain name.
Bounce Rate
The percentage of visitors who leave a website after viewing only one page, which can indicate either a poor experience or a successful single-page conversion.
Organic Visits
Visitors referred to a site by an unpaid search engine result, found in Google Analytics under the Acquisitions tab.
Site Session
A Google Analytics measurement of a user interacting with a site across one or more pages, replacing the older visits metric.
