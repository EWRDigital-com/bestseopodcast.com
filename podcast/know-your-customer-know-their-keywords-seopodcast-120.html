Know your Customer, Know their Keywords - #seopodcast 120

Episode27 min2011-10-25

Effective keyword research starts with interviewing your client to uncover industry jargon searchers never use, then combining base terms, service terms, and geo-targets to build complete keyword lists for both SEO and paid search.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Interview clients to uncover industry-specific terms, like MEP or gondola shelving, that you would never find on your own.
  • Combine base terms, service modifiers, and geo-targets using a keyword combination tool to generate a full list and avoid gaps.
  • Build two separate lists: one geo-targeted for pay-per-click ads and one without cities for content writing.
  • Match your keywords to consumer intent, entertainment or problem-solving, and include action words like buy, free, or download accordingly.
  • Keep your keywords in sync with your call-to-action so ad text, title, and meta description all reflect the same search phrase.
In real estate, it's location, location, location. In SEO, it's keyword, keyword, keyword.

Questions this episode answers

Why should you interview a client before doing keyword research?

Clients use industry-specific terms that general keyword tools never surface. For example, the hosts discovered the term MEP, meaning mechanical, electrical, and plumbing drawings, only because a client mentioned it. Without that interview, that keyword would have been missed entirely.

What is gondola shelving and why does it matter for keyword research?

Gondola shelving is the industry term for the display shelving found in grocery stores. A client selling this type of shelving had optimized for that term, but most buyers search for store shelving or display shelving instead, showing how industry jargon can hurt search volume if used without research.

How do you build a comprehensive keyword list for both SEO and pay-per-click?

Start with a base of product or service terms, add service modifiers like repair or maintenance, then add geo-targets like city names. A combination tool merges all three columns into a full list. Use the geo-targeted version for pay-per-click and the non-geo version for content writing.

How does consumer intent affect which keywords you should target?

The hosts explain that searchers are either seeking entertainment or trying to solve a problem. If your goal is newsletter signups, you may not need buy in your keywords. If you sell products, buy or free download may be essential. Matching keywords to the desired visitor action improves campaign results.

Terms defined in this episode
MEP
An industry abbreviation for mechanical, electrical, and plumbing, used primarily on the commercial and engineering side of the trades, not in residential or retail contexts.
Gondola shelving
The industry term for the display shelving units found in grocery and retail stores. General consumers search for store shelving or display shelving rather than this trade term.
Consumer intent
The underlying goal of a searcher, described in this episode as either seeking entertainment or trying to solve a current or future problem, which should guide keyword selection.
Geo-targeting
Adding location names such as city or town to keyword phrases so that search ads and content can be directed at users in specific geographic areas.
