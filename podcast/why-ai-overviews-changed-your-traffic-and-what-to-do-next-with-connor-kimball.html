Questions this episode answers

Why did my website traffic drop after Google launched AI overviews?

Google launched AI overviews for the majority of queries around March or April 2025. Sites with heavily informational blogs got hit hard because users could read a quick answer in the overview without clicking through. Transactional and commercial content was less affected because users still had to visit the page to view and purchase products.

What type of content performs best in AI search right now?

Competitor comparison or battle-card style content is performing best. One example from Carrot showed only two Google clicks in a month despite thousands of impressions, yet that same page was the most cited page in AI with dozens of referral clicks from AI platforms, making it the top content tactic the guest is seeing in that niche.

How should I measure success in AI search if most users do not click through from the LLM?

Track share of voice and brand mentions across LLMs, not just referral clicks. Users who see a brand in an LLM typically then search for it on Google, go directly to the URL, or respond to a later ad. Every client observed increasing AI referral traffic also saw a similar impact on organic and direct traffic.

Is AEO the same thing as traditional SEO?

Most practitioners would say about 80% of AEO is SEO, according to the guest. However, AEO specifically prioritizes high-intent, buyer-specific, and transactional content. The underlying technology of LLMs is fundamentally different from traditional search, so while tactics overlap, the approach and content focus must shift.

Terms defined in this episode
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)
The guest's preferred term for optimizing content for AI-driven answer engines, described as roughly 80% overlapping with traditional SEO but focused specifically on high-intent, buyer-specific content that LLMs surface to users.
LLM visibility
A catch-all term used in the episode referring to marketing and analytics work aimed at ensuring a brand is found, cited, and accurately represented by large language model search platforms.
AI Overviews
Google's feature that displays AI-generated answer summaries at the top of search results, launched broadly around March or April 2025, which reduced click-through traffic to informational blog content.
Battle-card content
A blog post format that directly compares two competing products or companies, covering differences in features, pricing, geography, and ideal use cases. The episode identifies this as the most cited content type in AI search currently.
