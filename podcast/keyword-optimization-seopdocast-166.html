Keyword Optimization - #seopdocast 166

Episode28 min2012-11-12

Keyword optimization for niche rankings requires research, grouping related phrases, assigning each group to a specific URL, performing on-site SEO, and building links to those pages before and after site launches.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Group related keyword phrases together and assign each group to one specific URL on your site.
  • Use Google Webmaster Tools and Google Analytics to discover keywords you already rank for but may not know about.
  • Perform on-site SEO including titles, headers, meta info, images, video, and content for each assigned URL.
  • Set up correct SEO configuration on your blog platform from day one because fixing years of backdated posts is costly.
  • Build links to pages after optimizing them, using social submissions and standard link building methods.
In real estate, the three most important things are location, location, location. And in internet marketing, the three most important things are keywords, keywords, keywords.

About this episode

In this week's podcast, we first go over a few details and tricks on national seo campaigns, and more specifically, how optimizing for local environments on the national scale will help out the campaign significantly. We then move on to keyword optimization and rankings. We finally finish off the podcast by talking about how to increase niche keyword rankings.

Questions this episode answers

How do I find keywords my site already ranks for?

The episode recommends using Google Webmaster Tools to see which pages are listed and what position they hold, and checking Google Analytics keywords expanded to 500 results. The hosts also mention running Web Position Reports to get rankings across search engines for both target and long-tail phrases.

How should I group keywords for SEO?

Group related keyword phrases together, for example translated rap lyrics, rap lyric translation, and hip-hop translation lyrics would form one group. Then assign that entire group to a single specific URL and optimize that page for all the phrases in the group.

What on-site SEO elements should be optimized for each keyword group?

The episode lists titles, headers, meta information, images, video, and content as the elements that should all relate to the assigned group of key phrases for each URL. Getting these right from day one on a blog is stressed as especially important.

What were the Google algorithm updates discussed in this episode?

Two updates are covered. A Penguin update on October 5th affected 0.7 percent of English language queries and targeted low-quality inbound links. A Top Heavy update on October 9th, also called the Page Layout Algorithm, also affected 0.7 percent of queries and targeted sites with too many ads and images above the fold.
