Maintaining your Ad Campaign - #seopodcast 179

Episode23 min2013-02-19

Managing a low-budget PPC campaign requires ad scheduling, negative keywords, budget shifting between campaigns, device targeting, and location targeting to maximize conversions and reduce wasted spend.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Run ads only during peak conversion hours to avoid depleting a small budget before the day ends.
  • Add negative keywords at the campaign level to block irrelevant traffic across all ad groups at once.
  • Shift budget away from non-converting campaigns toward campaigns that are generating leads, then reanalyze.
  • Use device targeting to reach mobile users for time-sensitive services like windshield repair.
  • Check analytics for high-converting locations and concentrate budget there, then rinse and repeat.
If you have a small budget, then that means sporadically, sometimes in the morning, sometimes in the afternoon, sometimes at night.

Questions this episode answers

How should I use negative keywords in a low-budget AdWords campaign?

Add negatives at the campaign level so they apply across all ad groups. If you have a large negative list and need more precision, add negatives at the ad group level instead. Pair negative keywords with the right match types to block irrelevant traffic without cutting off valid searches.

When should I shift budget between PPC campaigns or platforms?

Pause campaigns that are spending but not converting, and move that money toward campaigns that are generating leads. Also consider shifting to Bing, where cost per click is lower and the audience may be closer to a purchasing decision, then monitor and reanalyze results.

Why does ad scheduling matter for a small PPC budget?

With a small budget, ads shown all day run out of money by midday, leaving no coverage for the rest of the day. Scheduling ads to run during peak conversion hours, such as 9 to 3 for phone-call-driven campaigns, ensures the budget is spent when it is most likely to generate results.

How does device targeting help PPC performance?

AdWords lets you target desktops, mobile phones, tablets, and specific tablet types. For services where need is immediate, like windshield repair, targeting mobile devices is critical because users are likely already on the go and ready to make a decision right away.

Terms defined in this episode
Ad Scheduling
An AdWords feature that controls when ads are shown, allowing advertisers to run ads only during hours when conversions are most likely.
Negative Keywords
Words or phrases added to a PPC campaign to prevent ads from showing on irrelevant searches, applicable at the campaign or ad group level.
Display Network
A Google advertising network where ads appear on sites beyond google.com, recommended in this episode primarily for remarketing to past site visitors.
Remarketing
A display network strategy that shows ads specifically to users who have already visited your site, increasing the likelihood they convert on a return visit.
