Newspaper and cold calling advertising are not dead if they deliver positive ROI, but businesses must track returns and shift budgets toward online marketing as audiences move to search engines.

Key takeaways
  • If a marketing channel doubles your money, keep investing in it regardless of whether others call it dead.
  • Track ROI on every channel, including pay-per-click, cold calling, and newspaper, before cutting or scaling spend.
  • A professionally managed pay-per-click campaign can deliver ten times the results of an improperly managed one.
  • Your website is often the first impression a potential client has, so invest in making it reflect your quality.
  • As marketing budgets grow, diversify into branding channels like radio, TV, and conventions, not just search.

About this episode

We discuss older techniques for growing your business. Has Google brought the end to Cold Calling? How about newspaper advertising, is this tantamount to throwing your money away? How about yellow pages? Are the yellow pages dead?

Questions this episode answers

Is newspaper advertising still worth it for local businesses?

According to the episode, newspaper advertising can still be worth it if it delivers positive ROI. A Dallas real estate agent invested $100,000 in local newspaper ads and said it worked for his market. The hosts advise keeping any channel that returns more than you spend, while also building an online presence.

How do you know when to stop spending more on pay-per-click?

The hosts say you should track ROI closely. If spending $100,000 on pay-per-click only returns $110,000 in profit, that 10% return may not be worth it. Scaling back to $50,000 and getting $100,000 back would be a smarter decision, making it essential to monitor results constantly.

Why hire a professional to manage pay-per-click instead of doing it yourself?

The hosts say a properly managed pay-per-click campaign can get ten times the results of an improperly managed one. They charge $400 to manage campaigns and argue that spending $400 of a $500 budget on management can make the remaining $100 perform as well as a full $1,000 unmanaged budget.

Does a small company really need a quality website?

Yes, according to the hosts. They point to their partner company SES Research, which manufactures carbon nanomaterials, and say people assume it is a 10 to 50 person company because of the quality of its website. A professionally designed site gives instant credibility and first impressions matter significantly.

Terms defined in this episode
SEM
Search engine marketing, which the hosts describe as the broader category that includes both pay-per-click advertising and search engine optimization.
ROI
Return on investment, used throughout the episode to measure whether a marketing channel like cold calling, newspaper ads, or pay-per-click is generating more money than it costs.
Pay-per-click
A form of search engine marketing where advertisers pay each time a user clicks their ad, discussed as a channel that can waste money if not monitored daily for keyword performance and bid costs.
Branding
Described in the episode as both internal culture and external messaging, where all company communications carry a cohesive, coherent message to build recognition across channels like radio, TV, and conventions.
