Questions this episode answers

How should businesses respond to Google Places reviews?

Google now lets verified Google Places business owners publicly respond to reviews written by Google Maps users. The episode recommends responding to both positive and negative reviews. Business owners cannot respond to reviews pulled from third-party sources like Yelp or CitySearch, only to reviews written directly on Google.

Is it better to put all website content on one page or separate pages?

The hosts recommend separate pages for each topic. Putting everything on one long page with anchor links dilutes inbound anchor text across subjects, making it harder for Google to understand what each piece of content is about. Individual pages allow focused link equity per topic.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Sandbox
A period where a new domain is held back in Google rankings. The episode says getting an initial page live helps a site exit the sandbox faster.
Google Caffeine
A Google indexing update described in the episode as a glutton for content that indexes content faster, gives weight to fresh and relevant content, and reads content contextually.
Google Places
A local business listing product from Google where verified owners can now publicly respond to reviews written by Google Maps users.
Anchor text dilution
When inbound links pointing to one page carry mixed anchor text about different subjects, reducing the SEO signal strength for any single topic on that page.
