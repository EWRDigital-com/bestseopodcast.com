A successful PPC campaign requires defining a realistic budget, building targeted keyword lists with expert help, writing keyword-matched ad copy, and sending clicks to relevant landing pages integrated into your website.

Key takeaways
  • Define a budget before launching any PPC campaign, since both under-budgeting and over-budgeting cause campaigns to fail.
  • Get expert help choosing keywords by first answering what your business sells most and makes the most on.
  • Use separate ad campaigns for distinct services so keywords and ad copy stay relevant to each audience.
  • Your PPC ad must contain the specific keyword the user typed, and the landing page must match that keyword.
  • Landing pages integrated into your website reduce cost per click because Google rewards relevance and page rank.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common reasons a PPC campaign fails?

According to this episode, a PPC campaign fails for two reasons: being under-budgeted, which means too few clicks to generate results, or being over-budgeted, meaning you pay more than necessary. Getting 20 clicks a month from a small budget, for example, is described as substantially under-budgeted.

How should I choose keywords for a PPC campaign?

The hosts recommend starting by understanding what your business sells most and makes the most on. Then build a keyword list and hand it to a qualified professional for review. Industry-specific terms matter, as shown by the example of MEP, meaning mechanical electrical plumbing contractors, which general marketers would not have known to target.

Why does a PPC ad need to match the landing page content?

Google measures relevance between the ad, the keyword, and the destination page. When the landing page matches the search term and ranks well for it, Google charges less per click because it prefers sending users to relevant content. A mismatch forces advertisers to pay more and can result in ads being rejected.

Should I handle PPC setup myself or hire an expert?

The hosts say virtually every step, including setting a budget, finalizing keywords, writing ad copy, and building landing pages, benefits from expert help. They note that one client was paying eighteen dollars a click before getting professional help, suggesting outside expertise can significantly reduce wasted spend.

Terms defined in this episode
MEP
Mechanical electrical plumbing contractors, an industry-specific keyword that target clients use but that outside marketers would not think to include in a campaign.
Cost per click
The amount an advertiser pays each time a user clicks a PPC ad. Relevance between the ad, keyword, and landing page can reduce this cost because Google prefers sending users to relevant pages.
Landing page
A destination page a PPC ad links to. It must contain the keywords and information the user searched for, and it should be integrated into the website rather than standing alone to rank well and reduce click costs.
Facebook Places
A Facebook feature that allows users to check in to locations, described in this episode as a more social competitor to Google Places and a move that competes with Foursquare.
