Questions this episode answers

How should web designers handle calls to action on a business website?

Identify one or two specific actions you want visitors to take, such as calling, filling out a form, or making a purchase, and make those actions immediately obvious. Clients should decide this goal before the site is built, and everything on the page should guide the visitor toward that single action.

Why should businesses replace Flash with jQuery or HTML5?

Flash does not display on iPhones or iPads, and roughly 50 percent of mobile users are on Apple devices. jQuery or HTML5 can replicate the same visual effects, loads faster, and lets site owners update images directly in HTML without specialized flash software.

How does email newsletter marketing compare to Facebook for driving business leads?

Facebook posts can be missed if a follower scrolls past them, but email newsletters are a direct push to people who have already opted in. Christina Hawkins reports getting three or four responses requesting services with each newsletter send, making it more reliable for generating inquiries.

What is the right way to use keywords and heading tags for on-page SEO?

Use one H1 per page and a couple of H2s for subheadings, and naturally place key phrases in the content without spamming. Bold or strong tags can highlight important words. The goal is a clear reading experience for users while giving search engines a well-structured content hierarchy.

Terms defined in this episode
SIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
What you do with a visitor after they arrive at your website from a search engine, focused on converting that traffic into business.
Strong tag vs. bold tag
Strong is the preferred HTML element over bold for emphasizing text because it carries semantic meaning that search engines recognize.
H1 tag
The primary heading on a webpage. The episode states only one H1 should be used per page, with H2s used for subsections.
Pay-per-click (PPC)
A paid advertising model where businesses pay for clicks on ads. The hosts note it fails when done incorrectly or with too small a budget, not because the channel itself does not work.
