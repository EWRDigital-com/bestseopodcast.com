Improving Ad Copy Conversion - #seopodcast 226

Episode21 min2014-04-02

To improve ad copy conversions, focus on tweaking headlines, highlighting benefits over features, A/B testing everything, using power words, speaking your audience's language, and reconsidering your offer if nothing works.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Headlines are the make-or-break element of any ad or search result, so tweak them when impressions are high but clicks are low.
  • In PPC ads with limited characters, focus on the benefit to the customer, not the features or the process.
  • A/B split test all ad copy, use the best performer as your control, and keep testing from there.
  • If your ad copy is not converting, the problem may be the offer itself, not the text. Test multiple offers.

Questions this episode answers

What is the most important element to fix first in a poorly performing PPC ad?

According to this episode, the headline is the most critical element. If you are getting high impressions but low clicks, the headline likely needs tweaking because it is the first opportunity to communicate what a user will get when they click your link.

Should you answer objections inside the ad copy itself?

The hosts suggest that for PPC ads with limited space, it is better to address objections on the landing page rather than inside the ad. Once a user clicks, they should immediately see the benefit restated and then have their likely objections addressed through video, bullet points, infographics, or other formats.

How do power words help ad copy conversions?

Power words like 'free,' 'instantly,' and 'new' draw attention and make ads more compelling. The episode also recommends matching the action word to what you want the user to do, for example using 'download' instead of 'get' when promoting a white paper download.

What should you do if tweaking ad copy text does not improve conversions?

The episode advises reconsidering the offer itself. The product or service being promoted may not be competitive, or a competitor may be executing it better. The recommendation is to review your offerings, create something new, and then A/B test multiple offers.

Terms defined in this episode
A/B split test
A method of testing two versions of an ad or page against each other, using the best-performing version as the control and continuing to test for further improvement.
Power words
Words used inside ads such as 'free,' 'instantly,' and 'new' that draw reader attention and improve ad performance.
FAB (Feature Advantage Benefit)
A sales framework covering features, advantages, and benefits. In PPC ad copy with limited characters, the episode recommends focusing only on the benefit.
Organic impressions
The number of times a site appears in search results, tracked via webmaster tools search queries. Rising impressions indicate a site is ranking for more queries and being seen more often.
