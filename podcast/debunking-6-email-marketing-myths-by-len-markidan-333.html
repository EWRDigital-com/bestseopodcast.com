Questions this episode answers

What is the best day to send marketing emails?

According to HubSpot data cited in this episode, Tuesday is actually the worst day to send marketing emails, despite being widely recommended. The hosts found Friday afternoons work best for their list, generating opens that continued through the following Monday, Tuesday, and Wednesday.

Should marketing emails always be short?

Not necessarily. The episode explains that email length depends on your audience and purpose. If you are selling a product or service, short and direct works best. If you provide information, long-form emails may be more appropriate. Testing and measuring results is recommended.

Are email unsubscribes bad for your marketing?

No. The episode argues unsubscribes are beneficial because they remove people unlikely to buy, saving money since most email software charges by list size. Savvy marketers even strategically encourage unqualified leads to unsubscribe to maximize ROI, though bulk unsubscribes in a short period are a warning sign.

Can you send the same marketing email more than once?

Yes. The episode describes a tactic where you change the subject line of an email you already sent and resend it a week later only to subscribers who did not open the original. This gets more value from content you already invested time in creating.

Terms defined in this episode
Non-opens
Subscribers on your email list who did not open a particular email, who can be targeted with a follow-up send using a new subject line to increase overall engagement.
Email drip campaign
An automated sequence of emails sent to prospects or customers over time, which can be plain text or branded depending on where the recipient is in the sales process.
Shikos
A term used by the hosts referring to shares, likes, or follows on social media platforms.
