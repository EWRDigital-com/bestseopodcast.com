Episode37 min2015-07-20

Showing vulnerability, using emotional content, scheduling a content calendar, and repurposing content across platforms like SlideShare and Instagram are among 13 marketing tactics covered in this episode.

Key takeaways
  • Show vulnerability in content by sharing mistakes and how you fixed them, which builds trust with potential clients.
  • Use negative headlines paired with solutions to boost click-through rates, since negativity draws attention online.
  • Schedule a content calendar 90 days out so posts align with seasonal events and audience anticipation.
  • Repurpose blog content into SlideShare decks, newsletters, Instagram posts, and podcasts to maximize reach.
  • Use overt asks, telling your audience directly to retweet, share, or subscribe, because fans respond when asked.

Questions this episode answers

How can I use Instagram to promote written blog content?

Take the featured image from your blog post and use it as your Instagram image. Put the post title as meme text on the image, and place the link to the post in your bio and in the comments. This creates visual consistency and drives traffic back to your site.

Should startups publish content on their own blog or on other sites first?

The hosts favor publishing on your own domain because your site holds your calls to action, sales funnel, phone number, newsletter signup, and social links. Guest posting elsewhere can work for beginners, but a shorter remix with links back to your site is the preferred approach.

What is the marketing takeaway from Google and Twitter renewing their relationship and showing tweets in search results?

Make sure you are tweeting regularly and that your tweets include keywords you want to rank for. Tweets appearing in a mobile carousel in Google search results mean keyword-rich, valuable tweets with links back to your site can now surface in search.
