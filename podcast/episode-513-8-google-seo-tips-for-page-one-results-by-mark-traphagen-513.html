Questions this episode answers

What are threshold pages in SEO?

Threshold pages are pages that currently rank just below page one, typically on page two or page three. They are the easiest candidates to push onto page one because they are already close, so SEOs should identify them using a keyword ranking tool, sort by search volume, and optimize those pages first.

How does internal linking help with Google rankings?

Internal linking helps Google identify and understand the content on your site, signals which pages are most important, and guides how Google spiders the site. It also reflects the customer journey and user interaction patterns, which Google uses as ranking signals alongside dwell time and click behavior.

How should you research competitive content to improve rankings?

Compare your page against pages that outrank you by checking whether their content is better written or more comprehensive, whether they have stronger internal and external link profiles, and whether they include extras like images, charts, or videos. The goal is to reverse engineer what competitors do well and match or exceed it.

Why does placing a page higher in site navigation help it rank better?

Google measures a page's importance partly by its distance from the homepage through internal navigation. Pages linked higher in the menu structure are seen as more important, and they receive more link authority transferred from the homepage, which typically carries the most authority on a site.

Terms defined in this episode
Threshold pages
Pages that rank just below page one, on page two or three, which are the closest and easiest to push onto page one with targeted optimization.
Server-side optimization
Technical SEO improvements made at the web server level, including site speed enhancements tied to Web Vitals updates, which require back-end server access.
Link juice
The authority or ranking power passed from one page to another through links, including from a high-authority homepage to pages linked within the navigation.
Core Updates
Major Google algorithm changes, sometimes called FRED or Core Updates, that affect how pages are ranked, including shifts in weighting for homepages and EAT-related signals.
