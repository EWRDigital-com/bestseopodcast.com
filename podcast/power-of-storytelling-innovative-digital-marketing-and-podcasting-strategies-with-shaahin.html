Questions this episode answers

Why are podcasts better than other content formats for digital marketing?

According to Shaahin Cheyene, podcasts are evergreen, meaning they live forever, and produce original content that AI cannot yet replicate. They build authority, support every stage of a funnel, and generate raw material that can be repurposed into blogs, social clips, and YouTube videos.

How does the YouTube live stream strategy work for organic growth?

Cheyene explains that YouTube treats 24-7 live streams as potentially timely and news-relevant, so the algorithm pushes them higher in feeds without requiring paid ads. His company Viral Mirage combines this with backlinks and blog posting to grow channels within 90 days.

What is the 7-11-4 rule in digital marketing?

The 7-11-4 rule, mentioned by host Matt Bertram, means giving prospects seven hours of content, having them see the brand 11 times, across four different channels. Podcasting is cited as the most efficient format for generating that volume of content.

How can a guest appearance on someone else's podcast become your own content?

Cheyene advises asking the host for the video after recording, then posting it on your own channel, turning it into a blog with pictures and clips, distributing clips across social media, and adding it to your own podcast feed, producing a full episode without hosting a show yourself.

Terms defined in this episode
7-11-4 Rule
A content marketing formula requiring seven hours of content exposure, 11 brand impressions, and presence on four different channels to build audience trust.
Evergreen Content
Content such as podcast episodes that remains discoverable and relevant indefinitely after publication rather than expiring like time-sensitive posts.
Social Proof
Third-party validation, such as Amazon reviews or podcast appearances, that builds consumer trust more effectively than self-promotion because it comes from outside the brand.
YouTube Live Stream SEO
A tactic where running 24-7 live streams signals potential timeliness to YouTube's algorithm, prompting it to surface the content higher in feeds without paid promotion.
