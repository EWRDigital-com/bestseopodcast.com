Landing Page Design Tips - #seopodcast 214

Episode22 min2014-01-10

Effective landing pages require both SEO and strong user experience, with a focused product match, clear call to action, demographic-appropriate design, full-site access, and a mobile version.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • SEO and user experience must both be strong on a landing page, as neglecting either kills traffic or conversions.
  • A landing page should show only the specific product or offer the paid click promised, preventing bounce from confusion.
  • Design the landing page to match your target demographic in color, tone, and imagery to improve conversions.
  • Always give users a path back to the full site from a standalone landing page, including privacy policy and FAQs.
  • Google projected that by 2015, 80% of searches would come from mobile, so a mobile landing page version is essential.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most important elements of a good landing page for SEO and user experience?

According to this episode, a good landing page needs a striking headline, easy-to-scan layout with bullet points, a clear call to action, and an optimized keyword-rich page title. The hosts add that the page must be focused on one product, match the target demographic, link back to the full site, and have a mobile version.

Why should a landing page be focused on only one product?

If a paid-ad landing page shows multiple products, a scanning visitor may notice the wrong ones and bounce, wasting the ad spend. The episode stresses that the first thing a visitor should see is confirmation they are looking at the exact product they searched for.

Why does a landing page need a mobile version?

The hosts cite a Google projection that around 2015, 80% of searches would come from mobile devices. They also note that for their own clients, over 50% of traffic already comes from mobile, making a mobile-optimized landing page critical for any marketed page.

Should you remove navigation from a landing page?

Removing navigation is sometimes acceptable, but the hosts say you must still provide access back to the full site. Visitors may want to check a privacy policy, FAQs, or videos after converting, and without those links they simply cannot get there.

Terms defined in this episode
Quality Score
A metric relevant to paid ads where the landing page must be optimized; the episode notes that SEO on the landing page directly affects this score.
Landing Page
A specific page a user arrives on after clicking an ad, which should be focused on one product or offer, match the user demographic, and include a clear call to action.
Call to Action
A clear, concise instruction on a landing page telling the user exactly what to do, such as call, fill out a form, subscribe, or download something.
User Experience (UX)
How easily a visitor can find what they need on a site; the episode treats it as equally important as SEO, since poor UX prevents conversions even with high traffic.
