Local Search Strategy Tips - #seopodcast 191

Episode28 min2013-06-27

Local SEO success requires combining online tactics like Google Places and mobile PPC with offline efforts like community events, consistent content, reputation management, and measuring every result.

Key takeaways
  • Offline efforts like seminars, meet-and-greets, and radio ads with QR codes directly support your local online strategy.
  • 60% of local searches happen on mobile devices, so mobile PPC ads should focus hard on driving phone calls.
  • Localize content to your specific service area, referencing local landmarks or events to build emotional connection with readers.
  • Measure everything including traffic, bounce rate, form submissions, and phone calls to know what works.
  • Reputation management requires monitoring with tools like Google Alerts and fixing problems quickly, not just tracking them.

Questions this episode answers

What offline tactics help with a local SEO strategy?

Hosting seminars, organizing community events, and running radio or TV ads that include your website address and a QR code all support local online visibility. These efforts build brand familiarity, generate social sharing, and create additional citation and link opportunities for your local search presence.

Why is mobile important for local search campaigns?

Most local searches happen on a mobile device. People searching locally on mobile are typically ready to take action, whether calling or navigating to a location. Local PPC ads on mobile should be focused on driving phone calls rather than form fills, which are difficult on phones.

How should you manage your online reputation for local SEO?

Set up Google Alerts to monitor mentions of your business. If you find complaints, fix the underlying problem. Going the extra mile with personal notes, gift cards, or phone calls to satisfied customers generates positive reviews and referrals that strengthen your local search strategy.

What does it mean to localize your content for local SEO?

Rather than using generic content, write about the specific area you serve. Reference local landmarks, highways, or community events so that nearby readers feel an immediate connection. This emotional relevance encourages them to read further and take the action you are prompting them toward.
