Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between absolute unique visitors and new visitors in Google Analytics?

An absolute unique visitor is someone who has never visited the site before on that browser and IP address. A new visitor is someone who returns to the site after closing their browser, starting a fresh session. Returning visitors come back to the site without ever having closed the browser session.

Does web hosting server location affect SEO rankings?

According to this episode, server location has little direct SEO impact. Google understands servers can be anywhere in the world. Speed can have some effect, but the hosts note Google said speed differences affected less than 10 percent of search results. Being on a server flagged for email spamming can negatively affect SEO.

How can you use Google Analytics visitor data to gauge your marketing efforts?

Set your analytics calendar to a specific visit date, then check your keyword logs and content pages visited on that date. This lets you see which channel brought visitors, whether PPC, SEO, or social media, and helps you determine which marketing efforts are working best.

What is universalbusinesslisting.org and why is it mentioned in this episode?

Universalbusinesslisting.org, found at ubl.org, is a service that gets your business listed in many directories at once. The hosts say it costs about 30 dollars a year and can list your site in around 150 U.S. based directories, which helps build link juice and local relevance.

Terms defined in this episode
Absolute Unique Visitor
A visitor counted only once per browser and IP address combination, representing someone who has never visited the site before on that browser.
New Visit
A visit recorded when a user returns to a site after closing and reopening their browser, creating a fresh browser session, even if they visited earlier the same day.
Returning Visitor
A visitor who navigates away from a site and comes back without closing the browser, so the original browser session remained open.
Google Local Listings
Location-based results Google surfaces for regionally relevant searches, sometimes shown as a six-pack of local business results interspersed in search results.
