Key takeaways
  • Start with a website audit covering on-page factors like keyword density and headers, and off-page factors like backlink quality.
  • SEO is a zero-sum game, so a competitive analysis revealing rivals' keyword gaps is essential to the business case.
  • Define a target audience persona before optimizing anything, because strategy without a clear target produces random results.
  • Bring marketing, IT, and sales into the plan early to build internal champions and remove roadblocks.
  • Support financial projections with data: Google drives 92 percent of web searches and covers 267 million unique U.S. visitors monthly.

Questions this episode answers

What tools can I use to conduct an SEO website audit?

The episode mentions several options including Ahrefs AWT as a free tool, plus SEO Clarity, Screaming Frog, SEMrush, Ubersuggest, and Majestic for backlink analysis. The hosts note that most tools excel at one thing, so using a variety to triangulate findings is the common approach.

Why does an SEO business case need financial projections?

Executives typically focus on return on investment. The episode cites that Google accounts for 92 percent of web searches and 267 million unique U.S. visitors, and that moving from position three to position one can produce an eightfold increase in traffic, giving concrete numbers to project revenue uplift.

How should internal stakeholders be included in an SEO plan?

The episode recommends bringing marketing, IT, and sales into the plan, educating each group on their role, and explaining how SEO goals connect to company-wide objectives. Getting IT on board is highlighted as especially important since IT requests can otherwise become roadblocks.

What metrics should be tracked to measure SEO success?

The episode lists organic traffic, keyword ranking, click-through rate, bounce rate, conversion rate, time on page, average order value, lifetime value, store visits, phone calls, and form submissions. Tying these to revenue attribution on the front end is emphasized as critical for proving SEO value.

Terms defined in this episode
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
Tactical improvements made to a website to increase the percentage of visitors who complete a desired action, such as a purchase or form submission.
Target audience persona
A fictional character representing a typical customer, used to guide content and SEO strategy by identifying what that person searches for and why they visit a site.
Zero-sum game (SEO)
The idea that search traffic is finite: traffic a site earns is traffic competitors do not earn, making competitive analysis essential.
Lifetime value (LTV)
The total revenue a business can expect from a single customer over time, which SEO supports by keeping the brand visible for repeat searches.
