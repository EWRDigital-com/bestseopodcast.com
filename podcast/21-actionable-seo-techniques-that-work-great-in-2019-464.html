Questions this episode answers

How can updating old blog posts improve SEO?

According to Brian Dean's technique covered in this episode, refreshing and republishing old content, then re-promoting it to your list and on social media, can increase organic traffic by around 111 percent. The hosts also note they unindex low-performing posts and rewrite or combine them into stronger skyscraper posts for clients.

How do you find broken link building opportunities on Wikipedia?

Wikipedia marks outdated references with the label 'dead link' before editors remove them. You can search for your topic phrase combined with the phrase 'dead link' on Wikipedia to find those locations and pitch your content as a replacement, making it a scalable and white hat strategy.

Why should you link out to authority sites in your content?

The Hilltop algorithm signal tells Google whether a page is a hub of information, and that status is determined by the quality and relevance of its outbound links. The episode recommends linking to at least three quality relevant sources in every piece of content published.

Terms defined in this episode
RankBrain
Google's AI system introduced in October 2015 that ranks pages based on how well they satisfy users, using signals like organic click-through rate and dwell time.
Progressive Web App (PWA)
A website that merges app functionality with a traditional web presence, allowing users to download and use it like an app directly from the browser.
Hilltop
An older Google algorithm signal that determines whether a page is a hub of information based on the quality and relevance of its outbound links.
Skyscraper technique
A content strategy of creating a more comprehensive, higher-value piece of content than existing top-ranking pages in order to earn backlinks and outrank competitors.
