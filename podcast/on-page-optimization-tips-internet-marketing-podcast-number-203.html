Key takeaways
  • Content must be uniquely valuable, giving visitors something useful they can take away and share.
  • Page titles, H1 through H6 headers, body text, and URLs all need targeted keywords.
  • Image alt tags, titles, and descriptions remain effective and should always be filled out.
  • Meta descriptions do not directly affect rankings but influence click-through on search results pages.
  • Authorship, schema markup, and rich snippets are now must-haves, not optional extras.

Questions this episode answers

Does the meta description affect Google rankings?

The hosts explain that Google does not use the meta description directly in its ranking algorithm, but it is often displayed on the search results page. They recommend treating it as your first sales opportunity and including a subtle call to action tied to the keyword and your offering.

Why are authorship, schema, and rich snippets important for on-page SEO?

The hosts say these are now must-haves. Authorship tells Google who wrote the content and pages with authorship set up get clicked more. Schema markup helps search engines understand what the content is about. Rich snippets require good media, images, and videos that are both user-accessible and indexable by search engines.

What should you know about internal and external links for on-page SEO?

Internal links help users navigate, improve crawlability, and are endorsed by Google. External links pointing to your site should come from proper sources. Outbound links from your site are valuable when they point to relevant, non-competitive pages, because the goal is providing a good experience rather than letting pages dead-end.

Terms defined in this episode
Uniquely Valuable Content
Content that is not only unique and non-duplicate but also provides real usefulness and takeaways that prompt visitors to share it.
Authorship
A setup that tells Google who wrote a piece of content, described in this episode as about a year and a half old at time of recording, which also increases click-through rates on search result pages.
Schema
An advanced markup that helps search engines recognize what a page's content is really about, which Yoast for WordPress can help implement.
Rich Snippets
Search result enhancements that depend on having good media such as images and videos, citations, and other elements that are both user-visible and indexable by search engines.
