10 Different Ways To Get Organic Page 1 Google Rankings Ep. 560

Ep. 55928 min2022-12-265,540 plays

There are 10 ways to reach Google page one organically, including plain text results, video rich results, carousel rich results, and featured snippets, each requiring distinct optimization strategies beyond traditional blue-link SEO.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Plain text organic rankings are harder than ever due to increased competition, algorithm complexity, and the growing uniqueness of search queries.
  • Video rich results from YouTube feature prominently in Google search because Google owns YouTube, the second biggest search engine.
  • Carousel rich results apply to only four content types: courses, movies, recipes, and restaurants, and require proper Google markup.
  • Featured snippets display roughly 50 words of text and can appear at position zero, sometimes above paid results.
  • To rank for featured snippets, write concise single-paragraph answers to specific informational queries relevant to your business.
75% of all searches are new searches now.

About this episode

Discover 10 ways to go beyond the old 10 blue links and get more traffic from Google's enhanced organic search results.

Questions this episode answers

What is position zero in Google search?

Position zero refers to featured snippets, which are enhanced answers that appear at the top of search results, sometimes even above paid results. They typically display about 50 words of text, a page title, a URL, and a featured image thumbnail.

How do you optimize content for Google featured snippets?

Answer informational queries relevant to your business in a concise paragraph format. Research what questions people are asking, identify gaps where no snippet appears or existing results are low quality, and write a single paragraph that directly and specifically answers the target question.

Why is it harder than ever to rank organically in Google today?

The hosts cite three main reasons: the algorithm has grown significantly more complex, online competition has increased, and 75% of all searches are now unique, meaning people use search engines in new and varied ways that are harder to target with traditional SEO.

Terms defined in this episode
Featured Snippet
An enhanced answer appearing at the top of Google search results, sometimes above paid results, displaying approximately 50 words of text, a page title, URL, and featured image thumbnail, also called position zero.
Knowledge Panel
An information panel about entities such as people, places, organizations, movies, and music that is automatically generated by Google to provide quick information.
Carousel Rich Results
A Google rich result type available for four specific content categories: courses, movies, recipes, and restaurants, requiring proper Google markup to appear.
Google Business Profile
Formerly known as Google My Business, a Google program that allows local businesses to manage their online presence when someone searches for their business, a local product, or a local service.
