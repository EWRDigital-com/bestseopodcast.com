Questions this episode answers

Why should I run SEO and PPC at the same time instead of choosing one?

Running both together means different audiences see you in paid and organic results with very little overlap, so total traffic increases. PPC also sends user-behavior signals to search engines that can accelerate organic ranking movement, and both channels share keyword and content insights that reduce wasted spend.

How does PPC help with keyword research for SEO?

Google Keyword Planner only surfaces paid keywords, hiding high-converting organic terms. Running PPC ads lets you test whether a keyword actually drives conversions quickly and cheaply before you invest months of SEO effort, and it exposes keyword intent mismatches before they become costly mistakes.

What is search incrementality?

Search incrementality, referenced from a Search Engine Land article in this episode, describes the finding that when PPC ads are stopped, SEO performance also drops even though nothing about the organic strategy changed. It suggests the two channels reinforce each other rather than simply competing for the same clicks.

How important is retargeting and what data supports it?

Retargeting is described as extremely valuable because only about 2% of visitors convert on the first visit. Most converters return roughly 10 times before buying, so spending a small amount to serve ads on multiple platforms to past visitors significantly improves overall conversion rates.

Terms defined in this episode
Search Incrementality
A phenomenon where stopping PPC ads causes SEO performance to also drop, suggesting the two channels actively reinforce each other rather than working in isolation.
Zero Party Data
Data a consumer intentionally and proactively shares with a brand, which becomes more important as third-party cookies are phased out.
First Party Data
Data collected directly by a business from its own audience, such as email lists, used as an alternative to third-party tracking cookies.
Keyword Trap
A word or phrase that sounds relevant to a business but has a dual meaning or mismatched search intent, causing wasted SEO or PPC spend on the wrong audience.
