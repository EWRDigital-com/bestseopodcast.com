Questions this episode answers

How important are online reviews for local SEO?

The episode cites that 88% of consumers trust online reviews as much as personal recommendations. The hosts recommend proactively seeking reviews, including review links in newsletters or emails, and ensuring strong review profiles on Yelp, Google, and BBB to influence local search visibility.

What is NAP consistency and why does it matter for local SEO?

NAP stands for name, address, and phone number. The episode stresses that this information must appear exactly the same everywhere, including your website, Yelp, Foursquare, Google Plus, Bing Places, Yahoo Places, Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram, because business address and location is the primary information sought by local searchers.

Should a local business build a mobile app or focus on a mobile website?

The episode says 50% of mobile users prefer a mobile browser to a mobile app, citing research from Bright Local. The hosts advise that if your goals can be accomplished on your site, you likely do not need an app, and a well-optimized mobile site is usually sufficient.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP Consistency
Name, address, and phone number kept exactly the same across every online platform, including website, Yelp, Foursquare, Google Plus, Bing Places, and social media, because it is the primary information local searchers seek.
Ad Extensions
Additional information added to paid ads such as address, directions link, or call button; the episode notes 68% of users engage with local information inside ads.
Watch Time
A Google-patented search ranking factor measuring the time a user spends on a webpage or watching a video, used to signal content quality and relevance.
CSSR
Customer Service Survey and Reviews, a process described in the episode where clients are called with survey questions and, if the experience was positive, asked to leave a review with a follow-up seven days later.
