Google+ and Local SEO - #seopodcast 156

Episode27 min2012-09-06

Google merged Google Places pages into Google+ Local pages, requiring local businesses to update listings with matched email addresses, local phone numbers, and complete images to maintain SEO visibility in Maps and mobile search.

Key takeaways
  • Use the same email address for your Google+ Local page as your existing Google Places page to avoid losing data during migration.
  • Link to your contact or locations page in the website field of your listing, not your homepage, for stronger local relevance signals.
  • Fill every available field in your Places or Google+ Local listing, including all image and video slots, to maximize completeness.
  • Use a phone number with the local area code of the city you are targeting in your Google+ Local listing.
  • Maintain both your Google+ business page and Google+ Local page separately until Google rolls out the ability to merge them.

Questions this episode answers

What happened to Google Places pages and how does it affect local SEO?

Google converted all existing Google Places pages into Google+ Local pages. These new pages are indexed and integrated into Google Search, Google Maps, and Google Mobile, making them more important for local SEO than the old Places pages, which were not indexed.

How should I set up or migrate my Google Places listing to Google+ Local?

If you already have a verified Places page, keep the information current and upload as many images as possible. If you also create a Google+ Local page, use the exact same email address as your Places account so data carries over correctly during Google's migration rollout.

What information is most important to include in a Google+ Local listing?

Use your real company name without stuffing keywords into the title, choose keyword-rich categories, include a local area code phone number for your target city, link to your contact or locations page instead of your homepage, and upload as many images as possible to complete the listing.

Can you merge a Google+ business page with a Google+ Local page?

At the time of this episode, Google had not yet released a merge feature. Businesses with both a Google+ business page and a Google+ Local page had to maintain them separately, while a Foursquare check-in, for example, could only appear on the Local page and not the business page.

Terms defined in this episode
Google+ Local page
A page that replaced Google Places listings, indexed by Google and integrated into Google Search, Google Maps, and Google Mobile, giving local businesses a social and searchable presence.
Google Places page
An older directory-style listing that showed up in Maps and local search but was not indexed; Google converted all existing Places pages into Google+ Local pages.
Zagat reviews
A review system Google incorporated into Google+ Local pages to replace the previous one-through-five star rating system, populated by Google users where no formal Zagat review exists.
301 redirect
A permanent redirect used to consolidate multiple URL variations of a site, such as with and without www or a trailing slash, into a single canonical address.
