Google Analytics has eight key SEO reports including organic search, landing pages, conversion goals, assisted conversions, site speed, site search, mobile overview, and custom dashboards, but conversions must be manually configured in the admin goals section first.

Key takeaways
  • Set up conversion goals in the Google Analytics admin section before relying on any conversion data in reports.
  • Use assisted conversions under multi-channel funnels to see the full attribution picture, not just last-click data.
  • Check site search terms to discover what visitors cannot find on your site, then create or improve those pages.

Questions this episode answers

Where do I find the organic search report in Google Analytics?

Go to Acquisition, then Overview, then click through to Organic Search. This shows a summary of your top channels and how each performs in terms of traffic volume, behavior, and conversions, though you must set up conversion goals first for conversion data to appear.

What is assisted conversions in Google Analytics and why does it matter for SEO?

Assisted conversions are found under Conversions, Multi-Channel Funnels, Assisted Conversions. They show when a user first found your site via organic search but converted later through a different channel. This gives a fuller attribution picture than last-click conversion data alone.

How can Google Analytics site search data help SEO?

The site search report under Behavior, Site Search, Search Terms shows what visitors type into your on-site search bar. Repeated searches for a topic can reveal content gaps or navigation problems, and those search terms may point to new pages you should create.

What bounce rate benchmarks should SEO managers use?

According to the hosts, a bounce rate over 80 is a concern, while a rate around 60 or lower means the page is performing well. They recommend reviewing bounce rate on a page-by-page basis each month rather than only looking at the site-wide average.

Terms defined in this episode
Assisted Conversions
Conversions where a channel, such as organic search or PPC, played a role in the path to conversion but was not the final click. Found in Google Analytics under Conversions, Multi-Channel Funnels.
Attribution Model
A framework for assigning credit to different marketing touchpoints in a conversion path. Common models include last touch, first touch, and multi-touch, each weighting channels differently.
Not Provided
The label Google Analytics shows instead of organic keyword data, caused when users are logged into a Google account or when the site uses HTTPS with an SSL certificate.
Segments
Filters in Google Analytics that let you isolate a specific subset of traffic, such as only organic or only paid, across any report to compare behavior and conversions by channel.
