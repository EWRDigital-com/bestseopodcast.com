#SEOPodcast 284 - Improve Your Click-Through Rate

Episode37 min2015-08-31

Email newsletter click-through rates improve when marketers use buttons for CTAs, personalize messages, add time sensitivity, link images back to their site, and embed videos on site pages rather than YouTube.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Use big buttons instead of text links for calls to action to make clicks obvious and increase click-through rates.
  • Link all images in newsletters back to a page on your own site, not just floating unlinked images.
  • Embed videos on your website and link there from newsletters so visitors land on your remarketing list.
  • Personalize newsletters by segmenting lists, such as separating commercial from residential clients, and always include the reader's name.
  • Add time sensitivity and FOMO to newsletters, such as a 48-hour deadline, because emotion drives conversions.

Questions this episode answers

Should I link email newsletter videos to YouTube or my own website?

Link to a page on your own website where the video is embedded, not directly to YouTube. Doing this gets visitors onto your site so they are added to your remarketing list, allowing you to track behavior and retarget them with follow-up ads.

Why is remarketing recommended for all online marketing strategies?

Remarketing is cheaper, with cost per click typically 70 to 80 percent lower than the Google Search Network, and it keeps your brand in front of people who have already visited your site. The hosts say it is a necessary element of any online marketing strategy.

How does personalizing an email newsletter improve performance?

Segmenting your list so each group receives content relevant to them, for example separate newsletters for commercial versus residential customers, makes the email more actionable. Always include the reader's name and avoid generic placeholder text that signals the newsletter was not proofread.

Terms defined in this episode
Click-Through Rate (CTR)
The percentage of email recipients who click a link or button inside a newsletter, used to measure how well the email drives traffic back to a site.
Remarketing
Showing ads to people who have previously visited your site; the episode notes cost per click is usually 70 to 80 percent cheaper than standard Google Search Network ads.
FOMO
Fear of missing out, described in the episode as a powerful emotion that drives conversions when newsletters include time-sensitive offers.
