Questions this episode answers

What type of press release gets the most consistent media pickup?

An industry survey press release consistently generates earned media. Mickie Kennedy says that when he coaches clients through this format, they typically receive eight to fourteen earned media articles per release. You send the survey to small trade associations, gather at least 100 responses, and build the press release around the single biggest surprising finding.

How does a press release help with SEO link building?

When journalists write original articles based on a press release, those articles link back to the company. Kennedy and host Matt Bertram both note that earned links carry full link equity, while shortcuts like link farms pass only partial or no equity. Original data and story-driven releases are what prompt journalists to link in the first place.

Why do most press releases fail to get media coverage?

Kennedy says 97% of press releases on the wire yield no earned media because they are self-centered, listing product features without a story arc, use case, or original data. Journalists need building blocks for a story that will entertain or educate their audience, and releases that do not provide that are ignored.

What is the best way to structure a press release for a product launch?

Kennedy recommends incorporating a use case study showing measurable results, pairing it with relevant public data to illustrate industry stakes, and framing everything as a story arc rather than a feature list. This gives journalists the raw material to write an engaging article rather than a product announcement nobody will run.

Terms defined in this episode
Earned media
Coverage where a journalist independently writes an original article about a company based on a press release, as distinct from syndication where the release is simply replicated on partner websites.
Syndication
The automatic replication of a press release on third-party websites such as Yahoo Finance or Fox Business, which occurs every time a release goes over the wire but does not count as earned media.
Boilerplate
A section at the bottom of a press release labeled about the company where the sender writes an elevator pitch and can include an origin story nugget to create a human interest hook for journalists.
Story arc
A narrative structure in a press release that builds context, establishes stakes with data or a use case, and reaches a satisfying conclusion, mirroring the format journalists use in published articles.
