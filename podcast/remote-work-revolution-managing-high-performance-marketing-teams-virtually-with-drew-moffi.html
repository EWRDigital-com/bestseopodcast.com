Key takeaways
  • Slack and Zoom were not built for remote work and leave employees feeling isolated without spontaneous collaboration.
  • Async-only communication goes against human nature and creates friction during hiring and onboarding.
  • Remote workers tend to work longer hours because they skip commutes, which improves business efficiency over time.
  • Hiring within the same hemisphere or time zone is critical for real overlap and synchronous communication.
  • Leaders should make themselves visibly available in the virtual office and be overly transparent with remote team members.
At the core, business owners really need to remember that no software is going to make you a trusting boss, but a really successful company is a trusting organization.

Questions this episode answers

What is KumoSpace and how does it work for remote teams?

KumoSpace is a virtual office platform with a 2D top-down map of an office space. Users move avatars around using arrow keys, and spatial audio activates based on proximity. Teams can see who is available, join open conversations, or close a conference room door for privacy, replicating in-person office dynamics.

What are the biggest downsides of remote work that KumoSpace tries to solve?

The main downsides are isolation, lack of impromptu collaboration, and not knowing what colleagues are doing. Tools like Slack and Zoom were not built for remote work and cannot replicate shoulder-tap conversations or the visibility of knowing who is available and interacting at any moment.

Should small businesses go back to the office or stay remote?

According to Drew Moffitt, remote work offers broader talent pools and longer productive hours, while the office provides mentorship and spontaneous collaboration. Most employees dislike commutes and uninspiring office environments. If collaboration benefits can be replicated virtually, remote work offers more overall advantages for small businesses.

How should leaders onboard and manage remote employees effectively?

Moffitt recommends making yourself visibly available in a shared virtual space, encouraging new hires to reach out directly to any team member without scheduling, and being overly transparent. He says new KumoSpace hires are producing work within one week, which he describes as very quick for a remote environment.

Terms defined in this episode
Async absolutists
People who default to asynchronous text communication like Slack or email as the only way to interact, avoiding real-time voice or video conversation entirely, which Moffitt cautions against for remote teams.
Synchronous communication
Real-time communication such as voice or video conversation, as opposed to text messages or email, described in the episode as essential for collaboration and human connection in remote work.
Spatial audio
Audio functionality in KumoSpace where the range of who you hear depends on your position in the virtual space, mimicking how sound works in a physical office environment.
Staff augmentation
Adding remote individuals, freelancers, or agency teams to supplement an in-house marketing function, discussed in the episode as a common approach for businesses that cannot afford a full internal marketing department.
