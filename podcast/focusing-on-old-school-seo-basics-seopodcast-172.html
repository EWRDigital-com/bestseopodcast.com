Episode27 min2012-12-17

Old school SEO basics like titles, meta information, quality content, and cross-linking remain effective despite algorithm changes, but marketers must watch anchor text profiles and stay current on updates like Panda refreshes.

Key takeaways
  • Titles, meta information, good content, and cross-linking are still effective SEO basics despite Google algorithm changes.
  • Be cautious with exact match anchor text in link profiles because Google monitors how sites build links.
  • 85% of consumers search for local businesses online, yet 63% of small businesses have no website.
  • 44% of smartphone users search for products while standing in the aisle, making mobile optimization critical for brick-and-mortar businesses.
  • An integrated approach combining web design and traffic generation is more effective than hiring separate companies for each.

Questions this episode answers

How important is mobile optimization for local and retail businesses?

Very important. The episode cites that 66% of smartphone users make purchasing decisions on mobile, 38% do so at the store, and 44% search while in the aisle where the product is sold. Mobile can drive foot traffic and increase in-store conversions.

Terms defined in this episode
Not Provided Phrases
Keyword data hidden from site owners in analytics when users are logged into a Google account, which the hosts observed increasing with each algorithm update.
Penguin
A Google algorithm penalty referenced in the episode that affected at least one listener's site, apparently triggered by paying for low-quality links such as those sold on Fiverr.
Panda Refresh
A periodic update to Google's Panda algorithm; the episode discusses Panda refresh 22, said to affect 0.8% of searches.
Geographic Target in Webmaster Tools
A Google Webmaster Tools setting that helps distinguish where a site should be listed geographically, discussed as part of a broader local optimization effort.
