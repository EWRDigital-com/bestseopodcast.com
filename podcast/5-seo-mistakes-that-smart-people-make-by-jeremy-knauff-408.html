5 SEO Mistakes That Smart People Make by Jeremy Knauff #408

Ep. 40833 min2018-03-06

Smart SEO practitioners fail by chasing bots over humans, skipping strategy for tactics, ignoring KPIs, tracking performance poorly, and following questionable online sources, according to a Search Engine Journal article by Jeremy Knauff.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Write content for human visitors first, not search bots, because satisfying visitors drives both conversions and rankings.
  • Build SEO around a themed strategy before choosing tactics, since unrelated keywords require separate campaigns.
  • Set KPIs before testing, not after, so you collect the right data to prove which path delivers value.
  • Track conversions as the primary KPI, because a high bounce rate page that generates calls is still effective.
  • Get SEO information from trusted publications like Search Engine Journal, Search Engine Land, and Search Engine Round Table.
If you can't satisfy your visitors, they won't buy from you. And if they won't buy from you, is there any real value in ranking well for that particular phrase?

Terms defined in this episode
KPI (Key Performance Indicator)
A metric used to measure whether an SEO or marketing campaign is meeting its goals. The episode argues conversions are the most important KPI, ahead of hits, links, or time on page.
Semantic index
An evolution in SEO targeting beyond single keywords and synonyms, focusing on which topics are associated with each other to build thematic authority.
Continuity
The practice of matching the language in an ad or meta description to the language on the landing page so visitors immediately find what they clicked to see.
Negative SEO
Harm caused to a site's rankings when it receives low-quality or irrelevant inbound links, such as pet-related links pointing to an engineering website.
