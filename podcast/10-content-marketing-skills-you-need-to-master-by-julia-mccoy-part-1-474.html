Key takeaways
  • Know your audience precisely before writing; use Google Analytics, Quora, Reddit, and surveys to uncover fears, desires, and demographics.
  • You can write authority content without a formal degree by reading deeply in a niche and teaching what you learn.
  • Every piece of content needs a specific goal, such as growing an email list, driving blog traffic, or building authority.
  • Spy on competitors to find content gaps, then present your content uniquely, such as adding video when no one else has it.
  • Target long-tail, low-competition keywords first to build domain authority before going after high-competition head terms.

Questions this episode answers

What are the top content marketing skills to master for SEO success?

The episode covers five of ten skills from a Search Engine Journal article: audience detective work using tools like Google Analytics, Quora, and Reddit; subject matter expert interviewing; writing every piece with a clear goal; knowing how to stand out from competitors; and keyword research savviness with a focus on long-tail phrases.

Why should marketers target long-tail keywords instead of high-competition terms?

Long-tail and low-competition keywords are easier to rank for and still drive targeted visitors. Building authority on these phrases first creates a foundation to eventually compete for bigger terms. Even low search volume keywords can justify a full year of SEO spend if one conversion is a high-ticket deal.

How can you write authority content without being a credentialed expert?

The episode says you do not need a degree in a niche to produce authority content. Reading extensively in a specific niche builds enough expertise, and teaching the material, whether through writing, training, or podcasting, is when knowledge becomes truly concrete and articulate.

How does paid advertising compare to SEO according to the episode?

The episode cites a statistic that there is a 26% chance a target audience will not see paid ads due to ad blockers, meaning only about 75% of people see paid ads while organic content reaches the full audience. The hosts argue that a core marketing budget should go toward SEO for long-term value.

Terms defined in this episode
Long-tail keywords
Longer, more specific keyword phrases with lower search volume and less competition that help build site authority before targeting high-competition head terms.
Subject matter expert (SME) interviewing
The practice of interviewing a recognized expert to fill content knowledge gaps and lend credibility to an article, using open-ended questions and a prepared content outline.
Savoir faire (savviness) in keyword research
The episode uses this phrase to mean not just the mechanical ability to find keywords, but the strategic skill to choose the right ones, particularly long-tail and low-competition phrases.
SpyFu
A PPC and SEO competitive research tool mentioned in the episode as a way to spy on competitor keyword strategies and find content gaps.
