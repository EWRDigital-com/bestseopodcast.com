#SEOpodcast 280 - 4 Things People Don't Understand About SEO

Episode40 min2015-07-29

SEO must be built into every business decision from the start, not treated as a quick fix, because it is a long-term, data-driven strategy that no plugin or gut instinct can replace.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • SEO should be integrated from the very beginning of a project, including when choosing a domain name and planning content.
  • Plugins like Yoast organize SEO work but cannot do the work for you; good content, links, and active maintenance are still required.
  • Paid search supplements SEO for new sites by generating conversion data before organic rankings are established, typically within three to six months.
  • Good SEO is data driven, requiring monthly reporting and client conferences to evaluate which keywords and pages generated actual conversions.
  • SEO insights can reveal unexpected business development opportunities, such as identifying a high-demand service line from incoming lead patterns.

Questions this episode answers

Why can't a WordPress plugin like Yoast replace an SEO expert?

According to the hosts, a plugin organizes and simplifies SEO tasks but you still have to write good content, build links, submit sitemaps, optimize images, and stay socially active. The plugin cannot do that work for you, and an unused or outdated plugin can even invite security vulnerabilities.

How does paid search fit into an SEO strategy?

The hosts explain that a new site will not rank organically on day one and can take three to six months. Running a paid campaign for 30 to 60 days generates conversion data, showing which keywords and pages perform best before investing in long-term organic optimization.

What does it mean that good SEO is data driven?

The hosts say decisions should be based on digital analytics, not gut instinct. They hold monthly client conferences to review reports, identify which keywords produced conversions, determine which conversions became actual leads, and then adjust the strategy for the next 30 days based on those findings.

Can SEO help with business development beyond just rankings?

Yes. The hosts share an example with Manning Pool Service, where SEO reports revealed that most pool inspection leads were coming from real estate agents. That data led the agency to recommend opening a dedicated department for realtor pool inspections, creating a new revenue line for the client.

Terms defined in this episode
SIBO analysis
Search Engine Visitor Optimization analysis, a detailed review focused on what a visitor does after arriving from a search engine, including technical issues and conversion improvements.
TrueView
A YouTube ad format where an advertiser receives value when a viewer watches the full video and clicks through, and which the hosts note is substantially cheaper per click than other ad types.
Conversion Rate Optimization (CVO)
The hosts use CVO and search engine visitor optimization interchangeably to describe improving existing pages so that traffic from search or paid campaigns turns into actual leads or sales.
Thin content
Short or low-quality pages that can trigger a Google Panda penalty; the hosts recommend combining short pages into longer ones and improving content quality before a Panda refresh.
