Questions this episode answers

Does shared hosting affect your SEO rankings?

It can, but only if the other sites on your server are doing really bad things like spamming or have been blacklisted. Google knows thousands of sites can share one IP, so one misbehaving neighbor is unlikely to hurt you. If budget allows, a dedicated server removes the risk entirely.

How should you optimize a PPC landing page for SEO?

Host it on the same domain to help with quality score, include your unique selling proposition and a clear call to action, and avoid internal links that distract visitors. Pages built this way for paid ads can still rank well organically if the on-page SEO is solid.

What is news jacking and when should you use it for content marketing?

News jacking means taking a current news story and repurposing it for your own content. This episode says you should only do it when you can add something new, such as a different perspective or insight not already covered, otherwise there is no value for the reader.

Terms defined in this episode
News jacking
Taking a current news story and repurposing it with your own input, a different perspective, or a devil's advocate position to add value beyond what has already been published.
User-generated content
Content created by your clients or fans, such as photos or posts about their experience with your product or service, which you can then repurpose on your own platforms.
Google Phantom
A name given to the rollout process tied into Panda updates, where a Google algorithm update takes approximately 10 days to complete its full rollout.
Repop
A term coined in this episode for the pop-up Google introduced when clicking on Google reviews in search results, showing the latest reviews with a link for more information instead of redirecting to the Places page.
