Key takeaways
  • Title tags are the number one element algorithms use to determine what a page is about, not a tiny factor.
  • Image alt text is consistently missed: compress images, rename files with target keywords, and fill in descriptive alt text.
  • Link equity from a homepage flows to every linked page equally, so strategic interlinking matters for directing SEO value.
  • URLs should be short, keyword-focused, and clearly reflect site structure rather than auto-generated gobbledygook.

Questions this episode answers

How often should you re-optimize underperforming content for SEO?

Matt Bertram recommends conducting on-page SEO reviews once a month or more, depending on the size of the website and the size of your team. Google changes its algorithm constantly, so pages often need to be revisited and adjusted as competitive landscapes and ranking signals shift.

Why does image alt text matter for SEO?

Google cannot visually interpret images the way humans do, so it relies on alt text to understand what an image shows. Alt text also makes content accessible to visually impaired users. The image file name itself should also be renamed to include relevant keywords, not left as auto-generated names.

What is link equity and why does interlinking strategy matter?

Link equity refers to the SEO value a page can pass to other pages through links. Matt Bertram explains that a homepage is the strongest provider of link equity, and linking out to every page equally tells search spiders all pages are equal, which wastes that value and hurts rankings.

Should you use custom images instead of stock photos on blog pages?

Matt Bertram says creating custom images adds value to existing blog posts. Stock photos carry generic, non-keyword file names and do not signal originality to Google. A custom image with a keyword-relevant file name and matching alt text sends stronger relevance signals to search engines.

Terms defined in this episode
Link Equity
The SEO value a page can pass to other pages through links, described in the episode as 10 points on a page that should be strategically directed rather than spread equally to every linked page.
Image Alt Text
A text description placed behind a visual element that tells search engines and assistive tools what the image depicts, used because Google cannot visually interpret images on its own.
Slug
The portion of a URL that identifies a specific page, which should use clear, short, keyword-focused words rather than auto-generated strings.
Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (AWT)
A free tool from Ahrefs that scans a website for 100 plus common SEO issues, reviews backlink data, and shows what keywords a site ranks for compared to competitors.
