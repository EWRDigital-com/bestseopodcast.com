Questions this episode answers

What is position zero and why does it matter for SEO?

Position zero is the featured snippet at the top of Google search results. The hosts say it raises trust levels and conversion rates significantly. All the best practices required to reach it, such as schema markup and strong on-page SEO, also support higher organic rankings even if the featured snippet itself is not always attained.

Is voice search worth optimizing for as a business?

The hosts argue that most voice searches, such as weather and directions, lack purchasing intent, so position zero rarely delivers direct business value. However, understanding voice search trends matters because Comscore expects voice to account for up to 50% of global search soon, and ignoring the shift risks falling behind.

How does schema markup help with position zero and voice search?

Schema markup tells Google exactly what a page is about without requiring interpretation. The hosts say it is a key step toward featured snippets and that voice search assistants pull from structured data. Despite its importance, most clients who come to EWR Digital have never had schema implemented by previous SEO providers.

What SEO tactics does EWR Digital recommend for getting into the featured snippet?

The hosts recommend implementing schema markup, ensuring proper H1 tags, title tags, meta descriptions, and alt tags, and geotagging images. They emphasize that web designers often skip these SEO fundamentals, leaving sites unable to rank well, and that structured data is the layer most commonly missing when new clients arrive.

Terms defined in this episode
Position Zero
The featured snippet that appears at the very top of Google search results, above all ranked pages, considered a coveted spot that raises trust and conversion rates.
Schema Markup
Structured data added to a page that explicitly tells Google what the page and its sections are about, removing the need for Google to interpret the content on its own.
Featured Snippet
A selected search result displayed at the top of Google results in a special box, equivalent to position zero, often used by voice assistants to answer queries.
Search Intent
The underlying purpose behind a search query, such as finding information versus making a purchase, which determines how much business value a ranking can deliver.
