How to Succeed with an Effective Cost Per Click - #seopodcast 224

Episode33 min2014-03-19

To win in high-cost-per-click paid search, use proper conversion tracking, negative keyword lists, retargeting, intent-based bid adjustments, and dedicated landing pages that match ad copy.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Set up call tracking and sync analytics with AdWords to identify which keywords drive real conversions.
  • Retargeting clicks cost far less than new search clicks, sometimes two or three dollars versus $28 or more.
  • Bid higher on keywords that show clear purchase intent, such as quote for AC replacement over general searches.
  • Send paid traffic to a dedicated landing page matching the ad copy, not the homepage, to improve conversions.

Questions this episode answers

How should I split my paid search budget between Google AdWords and other platforms?

The hosts recommend roughly a 70/30 split, with 70 percent going to AdWords and the remaining 30 percent to Bing. If the Bing budget cannot be maximized, the remainder can go to Facebook or LinkedIn depending on where the target clients spend their time.

What are broad match keywords and should I use them in a competitive PPC campaign?

Broad match keywords allow Google to show your ads for loosely related searches. The hosts advise staying away from broad match in high-cost environments because you lose control over what triggers your ads. They recommend using it only to discover what might work or when search volume is too low for alternatives.

What is a bid modifier in Google AdWords and how does it help mobile campaigns?

A bid modifier is a tool in AdWords that lets you increase or decrease bids for specific conditions such as device type. If your site is optimized for mobile and your main call to action is a phone call or directions, increasing the bid modifier for mobile can improve visibility among users who are ready to act.

Why does retargeting cost so much less than standard paid search clicks?

Retargeting uses banner ads through Google's display network to reach people who already visited your site. Because display inventory is cheaper than search inventory, the hosts note a retargeting click can cost two or three dollars compared to over $28 for the same visitor through standard paid search.

Terms defined in this episode
Cost Per Click (CPC)
The amount an advertiser pays each time a user clicks their ad. The hosts explain it tends to be high when the end product or service is expensive or high value.
Negative Keywords
Words added to a PPC campaign to prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches. The hosts cite terms like DIY and free as default negatives for service providers.
Bid Modifier
A setting in AdWords that lets advertisers increase or decrease bids based on factors like device type, allowing higher bids on mobile when conversions are stronger there.
Retargeting (Remarketing)
Showing ads to users who previously visited your site, typically through banner ads on Google's display network, at a much lower cost per click than standard paid search.
