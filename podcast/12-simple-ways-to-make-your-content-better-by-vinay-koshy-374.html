12 Simple Ways to Make Your Content Better by Vinay Koshy #374

Ep. 37440 min2017-05-23

To make content better, focus on your audience, fact-check claims, add research, use visuals, optimize on-page SEO, build internal links, and regularly update existing pages to keep them accurate and engaging.

Key takeaways
  • Understand your audience's intent, pain points, and language before writing any content.
  • Fact-check everything and cite credible sources to build reader trust and thought leadership.
  • Use visuals like charts, images, or video because people will watch a three-minute video before reading three paragraphs.
  • Tweak headlines to drive clicks, and test headline performance using AdWords campaigns.
  • Update existing pages that still rank and get traffic to keep them accurate and relevant.

Questions this episode answers

Why is updating old content important for SEO?

The hosts explain that existing pages can become outdated, but if they still rank and receive traffic, updating them with current information, new visuals, and fresh perspectives keeps them relevant. They recommend checking analytics to identify evergreen content worth refreshing.

How should you use visuals to improve content engagement?

According to the episode, visuals like charts, graphs, images, and video illustrate points more effectively than text alone. One case study cited in the article found that adding custom graphics increased average time on site by 46% and increased traffic by 962%.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows on social media platforms.
on-page SEO checklist
A set of optimization criteria for a page including meta description with keyword, keyword in page title, image alt tags, word count, outbound links, internal links, and embedded tagged videos.
internal linking
Linking from one page or post on a site to another related page, helping search engines identify important pages and providing context through anchor text variations of target keywords.
evergreen content
Existing content that remains relevant over time and continues to receive traffic, making it a candidate for updates to improve accuracy and search performance.
