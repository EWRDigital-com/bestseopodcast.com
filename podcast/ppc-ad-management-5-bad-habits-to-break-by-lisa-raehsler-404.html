Key takeaways
  • Never leave ads on indefinite rotation without returning to analyze conversion data, not just click-through rates.
  • Always switch Google's default geo-targeting to show ads only to people located in your target area.
  • Build a negative keyword list before launching a campaign to avoid wasting budget on irrelevant clicks.
  • Exclude mobile game apps from display campaigns by adding AdSense for mobile apps to exclusions.
  • Ad extensions should be planned strategically to avoid duplicate information and to claim more page real estate.

Questions this episode answers

Why is rotating PPC ads indefinitely considered a bad habit?

When ads rotate indefinitely, the decision of which ad to show is based on clicks, not conversions. Advertisers should monitor rotation data themselves and decide which ad drives actual conversions, such as a buy now call to action that gets fewer clicks but more purchases than a softer read more prompt.

How do you stop Google Ads from showing to people outside your service area?

Google's default geo-targeting includes people who show interest in an area, not just those located there. Advertisers should manually switch this setting to target only people in the geographic location, and use exclusions to remove areas like major airports where irrelevant searchers may be present.

What is the best practice for negative keywords in PPC campaigns?

Negative keywords should be researched and added before a campaign launches. Using only exact match negatives, which is Google's default, excludes too little. The recommendation is to use phrase match and broad match negatives as well, since Google can serve ads in unexpected ways.

What are the three types of ad extensions and what do they each do?

According to the article discussed, site links connect to deeper content, structured snippets highlight a specific aspect of a product or header with no link, and call outs highlight features or benefits also with no link. Using them strategically avoids duplicate information and captures more page real estate.

Terms defined in this episode
Rotate Ads More Evenly
A Google Ads setting that distributes ad impressions evenly across multiple ads so advertisers can collect data, though Google notes it is not recommended for most advertisers.
Negative Keywords
Keywords added to a PPC campaign to prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches. Using only exact match negatives excludes too little, so phrase and broad match negatives are also recommended.
Ad Extensions
Additional pieces of information added to PPC ads, including site links, structured snippets, and call outs, that expand the ad and take up more real estate on the search results page.
Geographic Location Targeting
A campaign setting in AdWords and Bing Ads that controls where ads are shown. Google's default includes people with interest in an area, not just those physically located there.
