Key takeaways

Questions this episode answers

How did Shaunda Necole grow the Vegas Right Now site using SEO?

She focused on a tight niche of things to do in Las Vegas, created content only around topics people were actively searching for, stayed consistently on-theme across all platforms, and built E-E-A-T signals by sharing her 20 years of Las Vegas experience in bios, alt text, captions, and about pages everywhere online.

Why is a consistent bio across platforms important for SEO?

Google reads your presence across Pinterest, Facebook, YouTube, Instagram, and your website to determine your topical authority. If your messaging is inconsistent, Google can get confused about who you are and what you cover, which weakens its confidence in surfacing your content in search results.

What revenue streams does a niche content site like Vegas Right Now use?

The site uses affiliate links to attractions and products, ad revenue partners that show relevant ads to visitors, and original products. In Shaunda Necole's case, consistent blogging led directly to a publisher book deal for 100 Things to Do in Las Vegas Before You Die, coming out March 1st of 2025.

What is Google E-E-A-T and why does it matter for content creators?

E-E-A-T stands for experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. Google uses these signals to decide whose content to surface. Creators can demonstrate E-E-A-T by appearing in photos at the places they write about, maintaining a professional bio, and consistently publishing on-niche content across all platforms.

Terms defined in this episode
Google E-E-A-T
Experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness, the signals Google uses to evaluate whether a content creator should be trusted and surfaced in search results.
Topical authority
The degree to which Google recognizes a site or person as a reliable, focused expert on a specific subject area, built by consistently publishing on-niche content and signaling expertise everywhere online.
Entity SEO
The practice of helping Google connect and correctly identify all online properties belonging to the same person or brand, using signals like schema markup and consistent bios, so Google understands how everything is related.
Long tail key phrases
Highly specific, lower-volume search queries that tend to convert well; described in the episode as honey holes that require site expertise and traffic data to discover, not just standard keyword tools.
