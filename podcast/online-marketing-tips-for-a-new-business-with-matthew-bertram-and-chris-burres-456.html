Key takeaways
  • Plan for a minimum of $10K to enter online marketing, plus another $5K to $10K to sustain early momentum.
  • Build about 10 lead sources so no single source like referrals or one ad platform can collapse your business.
  • SEO takes 90 to 120 days before traffic appears, so patience and budget runway are essential for new businesses.
  • Your business name and domain name must clearly signal what you do, ideally within eight seconds of someone encountering it.
  • Start with a high-converting website, then layer in SEO, paid ads, email drips, and remarketing in that order.

Questions this episode answers

How much money does a new business need to start online marketing?

According to the hosts, a new business needs a minimum of $10,000 to build initial digital assets and get into the game. They recommend an additional $5,000 to $10,000 to sustain momentum, and suggest reinvesting 15 to 30 percent of revenue once the business is generating income.

How long does SEO take to show results for a new business?

The hosts say new businesses should expect 90 to 120 days before seeing traffic from SEO, and frame the realistic timeline as four to six months to reach the first page of Google from a starting point of zero online presence.

Why is relying only on referrals risky for a business?

Referrals are described as the best type of clients, but relying exclusively on one or two referral sources is dangerous. If those sources retire or stop referring, the business loses its lead pipeline. The hosts recommend building about 10 separate lead sources to stay stable.

Terms defined in this episode
Profit Plan
A consulting process the hosts describe as taking a business from potential marketing problems to a profitable online marketing strategy, typically starting with a 15 to 30 minute consult.
Remarketing
An advertising tactic mentioned as a later-stage step where ads are shown to people who previously visited a website but did not convert, used after a site reaches sufficient traffic.
Keyword Arbitrage
A concept the hosts use to describe finding keywords that have proven advertiser demand but low organic competition, creating an opportunity to rank for traffic without high cost.
Gulf Stream
An analogy used by the hosts to describe how a website, once it reaches the top positions on Google, tends to maintain its ranking and continue generating traffic consistently.
