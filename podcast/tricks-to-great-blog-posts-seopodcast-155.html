Tricks to Great Blog Posts - #seopodcast 155

Episode28 min2012-08-23

Writing better blog posts for SEO requires studying your audience's social media language, using trending hashtags, doing interviews, and using Facebook Insights to understand what readers share and engage with.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Study the words your audience uses on social media and include them in blog posts to improve search relevance.
  • Including trending hashtags in your post title can help it appear in hashtag searches on search engines.
  • Doing or getting interviewed, then writing a post about it and linking back, multiplies reach and builds links.
  • Avoid stock video in blog posts when possible since original media performs better than content everyone has already seen.
  • Keyword-rich domain names are far less impactful than they used to be, so branding should take priority over keyword domains.

Questions this episode answers

Should I use keywords in my domain name for SEO?

According to this episode, keyword-rich domain names are no longer very impactful after recent algorithm changes. If a keyword domain is short and naturally fits your brand, it is fine, but branding your company name is a better priority. The hosts cite ranking well for SEO Houston without the keyword in their domain as proof.

How can social media help me write better blog posts?

The episode recommends studying the specific words your audience uses on social media and including those terms in your posts, since people likely search those same words. It also suggests using Facebook Insights to identify what types of posts your audience shares most, then tailoring new content to match that.

Should you use industry jargon in blog posts?

It depends on your audience. If you are targeting consumers, plain language works better. For example, use air conditioning instead of HVAC for a consumer audience. If you are targeting B2B buyers or industry professionals, jargon may be exactly what they are searching for, so include both terms.

How do interviews help with blog post SEO?

The episode suggests writing a post about an upcoming interview first, then having the interviewer link back to that post. This drives traffic from people who watch the interview video. Video also helps ranking. The hosts used this approach with thebusinessmakers.com to gain keyword anchor text links back to their site.

Terms defined in this episode
Facebook Insights
Described in the episode as the Google Analytics of Facebook, showing who likes and shares your content and how many people had access to a given post on their wall.
keyword anchor text
A clickable link where the visible text contains a target keyword, used to pass relevance signals back to the linked page, as described when the hosts requested specific link text from thebusinessmakers.com.
trending hashtags
Hashtags actively being searched and used across social platforms; including them in a blog post title can cause the post to appear in hashtag searches on search engines, not just on Twitter.
