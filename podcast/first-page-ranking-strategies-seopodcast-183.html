Key takeaways
  • Link building strategy must differ between e-commerce sites and service providers because their audiences and sales cycles are different.
  • Outreach for links should be personal and targeted, not mass spam with identical anchor text sent to every webmaster.
  • Use Google Alerts to find freshly posted content in your niche, then comment, rebut, or contact the author for a link.
  • Running both PPC and SEO at the same time gives more listings on the search page, and more is better.
  • Content type should match the niche: videos and surveys for candle sellers, before-and-after photos for roofing repair providers.

Questions this episode answers

How should link building strategy differ between an e-commerce site and a local service business?

An e-commerce site selling products can use videos, surveys, and blog posts to attract links, while a local service provider like a roofer benefits more from before-and-after photos and instructional videos. The sales cycle and audience differ, so the content and outreach tactics must also differ.

How can Google Alerts help with link building?

You set up Google Alerts for keywords related to your client's services and location. When new content is posted on those topics, Google emails you a link. You can then comment on blog posts, write a rebuttal on your own site, or contact the blog owner to contribute a post and earn a backlink.

Should a business keep running PPC after SEO gets them to the first page?

Yes. The hosts argue that more listings on the search results page is always better, comparing it to having both a large and small Yellow Pages ad. They advise clients to run both PPC and SEO simultaneously rather than stopping what is already working.

Are EDU backlinks still valuable for SEO?

EDU backlinks are still coveted for their trust factor and PageRank, and finance links also retain some value. However, Google has stated it no longer places as much weight on EDU links as it once did, so their value has decreased compared to earlier.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Alerts
A Google tool that emails you links to newly posted online content matching keywords you designate, useful for finding link building opportunities in your niche.
EDU backlinks
Links from .edu domain websites, which are highly coveted for their trust factor and PageRank, though Google has reduced the weight it places on them.
Dynamic keyword insertion
An AdWords feature that automatically inserts a matching keyword, such as a competitor name, into ad text at the time the ad is triggered by a search.
Referral traffic
Website visits that come from links on other sites, visible in analytics; identifying these sources can reveal where to focus link building efforts.
