Tips For Top-Performing Brand Awareness Campaigns On Facebook Ads Ep. 537

Ep. 53740 min2022-05-093,268 plays

Facebook brand awareness campaigns work best for mid-to-large budgets using Reach, Brand Awareness, or Video Views objectives, but retargeting is the highest-ROI Facebook tactic for businesses of any size.

Key takeaways
  • Retargeting is the most efficient marketing spend; start when your site hits 100 to 300 monthly visitors.
  • Facebook offers three upper-funnel objectives: Reach, Brand Awareness, and Video Views, each optimizing for different KPIs.
  • Ad exhaustion is a real problem on Facebook; rotating creatives frequently lowers cost per impression and conversion.
  • Lookalike audiences built from existing customer lists can shortcut machine-learning optimization and drive outsized results.
  • The Facebook pixel slows websites; remove it if you are not actively running retargeting campaigns.

Questions this episode answers

What are the three Facebook campaign objectives for brand awareness and how do they differ?

Reach shows ads to the widest audience as cheaply as possible and lets you set a frequency goal. Brand Awareness targets people Facebook thinks are more likely to recall the ad and provides an estimated ad recall lift metric. Video Views optimizes for people most likely to watch or complete the video, giving access to detailed video engagement metrics.

When should a business start Facebook retargeting?

According to the hosts, businesses should start retargeting once they reach roughly 100 to 300 people visiting their website. Facebook retargeting shows ads to people who already visited the site, meaning they have already expressed interest, making it the most cost-effective ad spend available.

What is estimated ad recall lift on Facebook and how do you get it?

Estimated ad recall lift shows how many people Facebook estimates would remember your ad if asked within two days. Campaigns spending at least $30,000 or more over their duration are also eligible for a brand survey test that asks users directly whether they recall seeing your ad.

Why should you remove the Facebook pixel if you are not running retargeting?

The hosts report that the Facebook pixel has been loading slowly and that removing it speeds up websites considerably. Since data suggests users rarely convert on their first visit, they recommend keeping the pixel only when you are actively running retargeting campaigns to bring those visitors back.

Terms defined in this episode
Estimated Ad Recall Lift
A Facebook-exclusive metric showing how many people Facebook estimates would remember your ad if asked within two days of seeing it.
Ad Exhaustion
A state where an audience has seen the same ad so many times that engagement and conversion rates drop, requiring creative rotation to restore performance.
Lookalike Audience
A Facebook targeting list built from an existing customer base that the platform expands to roughly three times the original list size by finding users with similar behaviors and attributes.
Through Play
A Facebook video metric counting the number of times a video plays to completion or for at least 15 seconds, used to gauge how well video content holds viewer attention.
