5 More Super- Common SEO Mistakes Content Marketers Make by Stephan Spencer #328

Ep. 32836 min2016-07-08

Content marketers most often fail at SEO by skipping systematic outreach, using wrong keywords, breaking sites during migrations, ignoring analytics, and believing outdated myths like SEO being a one-time activity.

Key takeaways
  • Systematic outreach requires a repeatable production schedule, not one-off promotion efforts.
  • Keyword research must match how your specific audience actually searches, including long-tail and voice queries.
  • Moving a site without proper 301 redirects destroys link juice and rankings that are hard to recover.
  • Analytics reports like Pages and Behavior Flow reveal which content people find and engage with most.
  • SEO is never a one-time activity; it demands consistent link building, content, and technical improvements.
in a perfect world, great content will spontaneously yield loads of great backlinks. In the real world, however, you need to promote your content to the right audience.

About this episode

This week Chris and Chuck talk about an article from Search Engine Land, 5 more super-common SEO mistakes content marketers make by Stephan Spencer. In this article. Stephan addresses some more common but avoidable SEO problems for content marketers to be aware of.

Questions this episode answers

What is systematic outreach in content marketing SEO?

Systematic outreach means having a repeatable process for promoting content every time you publish. This includes a production schedule covering research, writing, editing, and publishing days, plus channels like paid, social, and email marketing to push the content to the right audience.

Why does moving a website hurt SEO rankings?

Migrating a site without correct 301 redirects causes old pages to lose their link juice and authority. If popular pages had inbound links and the URL changes, Google may devalue the site for broken links and bad pages, making it very difficult and time-consuming to recover rankings.

How should content marketers use analytics to improve SEO?

The hosts recommend the Google Analytics Pages report sorted by time spent, filtered for blog content, to find what audiences engage with most. The Behavior Flow report then shows how visitors arrived at those pages, informing the next content strategy and promotion plan.

Is duplicate content actually penalized by Google?

The hosts debate this. Google's stated approach is to identify the original and ignore the rest rather than issue a formal penalty. In practice, duplicate content in competitive markets like e-commerce rarely ranks well, while in low-competition small markets it may still surface as the most relevant result.

Terms defined in this episode
301 redirect
A permanent redirect that passes link juice from an old URL to a new one, used during site migrations to preserve page authority.
long-tail phrase
A more specific, lower-volume search query, such as a brand name plus a service type, that signals higher purchase intent than a broad term.
link juice
The ranking value or authority passed from inbound links to a page, which can be lost if URLs are incorrectly redirected or decommissioned.
systematic outreach
A planned, repeatable process for promoting content to the right audience using channels like paid, social, email, video, or infographics on a consistent schedule.
