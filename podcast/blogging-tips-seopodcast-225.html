Blogging Tips - #Seopodcast 225

Episode27 min2014-03-25

Consistent blogging, writing for your audience rather than search engines, and responding to comments are the core fixes for a blog that fails to build readership and rank well.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Post on a consistent, predictable schedule to build an audience that knows when to expect new content.
  • Write for your audience first, not for search engines, while keeping keyword strategy in mind for titles and headers.
  • Respond to every comment, especially questions, which can also be spun into new blog posts.
  • Limit pop-ups to a single appearance per visit and use cookies to avoid showing subscribe prompts to people who already subscribed.
You're not writing for the search. You're writing for your audience.

Questions this episode answers

Do blog comments affect SEO quality?

Yes, according to this episode, comments are indexed and can dilute SEO quality, especially if they are spammy or include unrelated links. The advice is to delete bad comments, respond to good ones with relevant replies, and manage keyword density across the comment section.

Should I use keyword research to plan all my blog content?

The hosts say keyword research should drive your content strategy in terms of what to write about and what titles to use, but the content itself must read naturally and conversationally. Keyword stuffing purely to hit a density target is what to avoid.

Are guest posts a good blogging strategy?

The hosts advise strongly against relying on guest posts, citing Matt Cutts saying guest posts are dead. If you allow them, you must personally vouch for the author and the content must be very high quality, or it risks being flagged as a low-quality backlink attempt.

How often should a blog or podcast publish new content?

The episode recommends publishing on a specific, predictable schedule, such as a set day and time each week. The hosts point to HubSpot as an example of committing to a consistent release time, which builds the kind of followership comparable to a regular TV show.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing script
A free piece of code from Google AdWords placed on your site to begin building a list of past visitors so you can target them with ads later at a lower cost than search network bidding.
User-generated content
Content created by site visitors, such as blog comments, which can contribute to or dilute a page's SEO quality depending on whether it is relevant and spam-free.
Keyword density
The proportion of keyword usage within a page's content, which should be managed naturally rather than stuffed artificially, especially in comment sections and blog posts.
Top-heavy site
A site with a large number of ads placed at the top and quality content pushed toward the bottom, which Google devalues according to its layout algorithm.
