Questions this episode answers

What is the Manual Actions tab in Google Webmaster Tools?

The Manual Actions tab, found under the search traffic section in Google Webmaster Tools, tells you specifically why your site is not ranking. It can flag issues like user-generated comment spam, thin content, or unnatural inbound links, and it tells you what to fix to recover your rankings.

How can I benefit from a high-authority guest blogger writing for my site?

Have the author set up Google authorship and add your site as a contributor on their Google Plus page so their authorship shows on your site. Also ask them to mention and link to your site from their own platform. Be careful to avoid posts stuffed with links, which Google is actively penalizing.

Why is site structure important for SEO?

Good site structure through link sculpting, clear navigation, and user-friendly layout keeps visitors moving through your site easily. This supports conversions, improves user experience, and can increase your rankings. The disavow tool also now falls under link sculpting to remove unnatural inbound links.

What does integration mean in the context of SEO and internet marketing?

Integration means connecting all social profiles, online activities, and offline events back to your website as the central hub. Twitter, Facebook, LinkedIn, and Google Plus should all link to your site, and even offline events should be featured on the site so attendees can check in and connect digitally.

Terms defined in this episode
Manual Actions
A tab in Google Webmaster Tools under the search traffic section that shows why a site received a manual spam action, such as thin content, user-generated comment spam, or unnatural inbound links.
Link Sculpting
Organizing a site's internal links by order of importance to create fluid, user-friendly navigation that supports conversions and improves user experience and rankings.
Disavow Tool
A Google Webmaster Tools feature that lets site owners flag unnatural or spammy inbound links so Google can discount them, now considered part of the link sculpting process.
Author Rank
The authority or ranking influence associated with a content author through Google authorship, which requires the author to add a site as a contributor on their Google Plus page.
