A cryptocurrency marketing plan starts with web design, SEO, and video storytelling, then layers in paid ads, press releases, display campaigns, and remarketing, all driven by audience data and clear business goals.

Key takeaways
  • Define your target avatar and coin purpose before choosing any marketing channel.
  • SEO as a base layer helps people find your coin through organic informational searches.
  • Remarketing is the most cost effective ad spend once your site reaches a traffic threshold.
  • Paid ads like Google AdWords reveal which keyword phrases actually drive conversions.
  • Press releases build credibility and push unrelated search results off your first page.

Questions this episode answers

What is the first step in a cryptocurrency marketing plan?

According to this episode, the first step is defining your target avatar, understanding who you are reaching and what problem your coin solves. Only after that do you move into web design, SEO, and video, because every channel decision flows from those foundational assumptions.

Why is remarketing important for a new crypto coin launch?

Remarketing targets people who already visited your site, meaning they have already shown interest. The hosts cite a 400 percent increase in engagement and say you can adjust messaging and offer strength over a full year, making it the most cost effective ad spend available.

Can you run Google or Facebook paid ads for a cryptocurrency ICO?

The hosts note that during the ICO boom around 2017, Google and Facebook were not allowing ICO advertising. They suggest a workaround of positioning ads around education, such as how to invest in crypto, rather than directly promoting coin purchases.

What role do press releases play in crypto marketing?

Press releases help announce a new coin to a wide audience. When journalists or bloggers write articles based on the release and link back to your site, those backlinks provide SEO value. The hosts also note that a press release appearing in search results beneath your site adds credibility.

Terms defined in this episode
ICO
An initial coin offering, which is the process of seeking investors for a new cryptocurrency coin before or at launch.
Remarketing
Serving targeted ads to users who previously visited your website, allowing you to adjust messaging frequency and offer strength over time, up to a full year after the initial visit.
Display campaigns
Banner ads that appear on news sites and other web properties, used for brand awareness, seeding new markets, and retargeting, typically bought on a cost per thousand impressions basis.
Utility coin
A type of cryptocurrency designed to solve a specific problem or power a specific platform, such as Filecoin, which the hosts describe as enabling decentralized online data storage.
