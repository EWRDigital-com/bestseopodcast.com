Questions this episode answers

What should a Google Places listing include to be 100% complete?

According to the hosts, a fully complete Google Places listing should have ten images, five videos, up to five categories, hours of operation, and payment forms accepted. A complete listing helps your ranking in the A through G local results on Google.

Why does the Google Golden Triangle matter for local map results?

An eye tracking study on Google Maps, written by Matt McGee, found that listings with social components like reviews and images follow the classic Google Golden Triangle attention pattern. Listings without those elements cause user attention to shift down toward richer results.

How can a business use Foursquare for local marketing?

The hosts recommend adding your business to Foursquare, adding friends, linking your account to Twitter and Facebook, posting shouts and tips when checking in, and rewarding check-ins with benefits like free items or custom badges to drive engagement and top-of-mind awareness.

How significant is mobile search for local businesses?

The hosts cite a stat that 15% of Google queries come from mobile devices with local intent. They say this alone is a strong reason to keep your Google Places page updated and to have a mobile version of your website.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Golden Triangle
A heat map study pattern showing that user attention concentrates on the first three search results, with the first position receiving the most focus.
Google Places listing
A business profile on Google that can include images, videos, categories, hours, and payment forms, and that appears in local A through G map results.
Foursquare mayor
The Foursquare user who has checked in at a particular location more than anyone else, earning that location's mayor status.
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, the focus of the hosts' companion video podcast covering website analysis.
