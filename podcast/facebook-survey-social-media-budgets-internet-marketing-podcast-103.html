Facebook Survey, Social Media Budgets - Internet Marketing Podcast 103

Episode43 min2011-05-17

Social media works for every industry including industrial sectors, but businesses should add social media budgets on top of existing marketing spend and prioritize platforms where their target audience is most active, especially LinkedIn for B2B.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Industrial sector professionals favor LinkedIn at 63% usage, followed by Facebook at 59%, with Twitter at only 15%.
  • Do not pull from your existing marketing budget to fund social media. Add to it instead.
  • Understand how your target audience uses social media before choosing which platforms to invest in.
  • Blogs and forums are foundational social media tools that build industry authority and earn trust from technical audiences.
  • Social media is not truly free because time spent on it has an opportunity cost versus sales, customer service, or follow-up.
Social is not free, right? Because it distracts you from other things you could be doing.

Questions this episode answers

What should a business define before launching a social media campaign?

The hosts list several questions from the article: how much your target audience participates in social media and which platforms they use, what your goals are, what resources including people, time, and budget you have, and how you will measure return on investment.

Terms defined in this episode
B2B vs B2C
B2B means business to business, where sales go to other companies. B2C means business to consumer, covering direct or retail sales to individuals.
SSL certificate
A credential that enables HTTPS on a website, encrypting all interactions between the browser and the server. The episode notes Mozilla was pushing to strengthen the certification process.
Page Speed test
A tool at pagespeed.googlelabs.com where you enter your website URL and receive a speed performance score.
Facebook business page
A professional Facebook presence for a company or product, distinct from a personal account, used to post content, attract followers, and prompt actions like calls or downloads.
