Questions this episode answers

What are the best ways to collect email addresses for a small business?

The episode lists 29 methods sourced from TypePad.com, including putting a signup offer on business cards, collecting at trade shows, adding opt-in forms to every website page, hosting events like Lunch and Learns, offering birthday clubs, and using Facebook and Twitter to promote newsletter signups.

What is a joint venture in the context of email marketing?

A joint venture involves partnering with a complementary business to send promotional emails to each other's lists without sharing the actual list. Each party agrees to send a set number of emails for the other, allowing cross-promotion to a relevant audience and potential new email subscribers.

Why is cloaking bad for SEO?

The hosts cite a news story where Google's own AdWords help pages were caught cloaking, showing different content to users versus the Google bot. Google banned those pages automatically, demonstrating that cloaking is a serious violation that can result in pages being removed from search results.

Terms defined in this episode
CIVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, eWebStyle's term for optimizing calls to action on a site, such as capturing email addresses or phone numbers from visitors.
Cloaking
An SEO violation where a website shows a different page to users than to search engine bots; the episode notes Google banned its own AdWords pages for this practice.
Link baiting
Creating high-value content, such as a top-10 tips article, that other sites are motivated to link to, earning inbound links naturally.
Opt-in form
A form placed on a website or email that invites visitors to subscribe to a newsletter or download a resource, used to collect email addresses as a call to action.
