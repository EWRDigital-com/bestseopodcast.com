Questions this episode answers

What is Google Boost and how does it work for local businesses?

Google Boost is pay-per-click advertising for Google Places. It can change a business pin from red to blue and surface the listing in pay-per-click results. Advertisers cannot control which keywords trigger their ad, as Google manages and optimizes keywords across the entire business category.

How do I get my Google Places listing to rank on the first page of Google?

The hosts recommend making your listing 100 percent complete, including videos, logos, web addresses, contact information, product descriptions, and hours. Getting customer reviews on your Google Places page is also considered important, as businesses with reviews likely outrank those without any.

What changed about the Google search results page layout around late October 2010?

The Google Places map moved from the center of the page to the right side, above the sponsored links. The Places listings also grew larger and began filling the entire first page of results for local searches, reducing the visibility of traditional organic listings.

In their words

Individual Boost advertisers can't add, edit, or delete keywords from their Boost ad. However, the keywords are managed and optimized over time for all customers within a business category.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Boost
A pay-per-click advertising product tied to Google Places that changes a business pin from red to blue and can surface the listing in paid search results. Advertisers cannot control individual keywords.
Google Places
Google's local business listing product that displays a map with pins and business information in search results. A complete listing includes pictures, videos, hours, contact info, and reviews.
Google Places Tag
An earlier Google Places sponsored feature described as a little yellow tab that could include a video or coupon, distinct from Google Boost.
Local Search
Search queries with local intent where Google surfaces a map and nearby business listings, which the hosts describe as increasingly dominating the first page of search results.
