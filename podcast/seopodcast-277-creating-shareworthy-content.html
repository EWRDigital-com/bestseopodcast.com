#SEOpodcast 277 - Creating Shareworthy Content

Episode35 min2015-06-23

Creating share-worthy content requires ideation, strong titles, thorough research, time investment, team feedback, a commitment to excellence, and active promotion to reach the right audience.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Spend real time on ideation before writing, using client issues, current events, and product context as starting points.
  • Develop several title options and test them for emotional pull, keeping keywords and the 55-character Google truncation limit in mind.
  • Back up claims with outbound links and credible sources to build authority and trust with readers.
  • Get specific feedback from multiple people before publishing, asking about tone, images, length, and complexity.
  • If you would not share the content yourself, do not post it. Promote it manually to targeted individuals for best results.
Share-worthy content isn't fast food. You must offer your target audience a fine dining experience and it'll take time to cook up a meal your audience will enjoy.

Questions this episode answers

What are the seven things content creators do to make share-worthy content?

According to the article discussed in this episode, the seven steps are: ideation, focusing on titles, doing thorough research, taking the time needed, getting feedback, striving for excellence, and taking steps to improve visibility and promote the content to your target audience.

Why is getting feedback important before publishing content?

Different team members, such as sales, customer service, and even your best customers, each bring a unique perspective. Specific feedback on tone, images, length, and complexity lets you make adjustments that improve quality and increase the chances the content gets shared.

What is the Google Phantom algorithm update and what does it target?

Google Phantom is an algorithm update, not officially announced by Google, that targets how-to content. Sites filled with thin, non-relevant how-to articles are impacted. Building links through thin how-to content posted across wikis and similar sites is likely to stop working as a result.

Terms defined in this episode
Ideation
The process of setting aside time to brainstorm content ideas before writing, drawing from products, client issues, and current industry events.
Thin content
Content that is low in value, poorly researched, or created quickly without substance, which can result in a search engine penalty.
Google Phantom
An algorithm update targeting how-to content sites with thin, non-relevant articles, identified by researchers though not officially published by Google.
Schema markup
Code added to a website that helps Google easily identify information such as a business location, important for local and restaurant search visibility.
