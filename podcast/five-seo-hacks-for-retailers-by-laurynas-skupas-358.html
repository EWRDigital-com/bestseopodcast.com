Five SEO Hacks for Retailers by Laurynas Skupas #358

Ep. 35833 min2017-02-01

Retailers can improve SEO rankings in 2017 by using LSI keywords, writing thousand-word product descriptions, adding neuromarketing prefixes to titles, keeping coupon pages fresh, and implementing product review schema markup.

Key takeaways
  • Use LSI keywords, which are secondary phrases related to your main keyword, to help product pages rank for more queries.
  • Write product descriptions of a thousand words or more to differentiate from competitors using identical manufacturer copy.

Questions this episode answers

What are LSI keywords and how do retailers use them?

LSI stands for Latent Semantic Indexing. LSI keywords are phrases or words closely related to the main keyword on a page. For retailers, they function as secondary key phrases, such as using street names for products like Space Jams instead of Jordan 11s, to capture additional search queries.

Why should e-commerce product pages have long descriptions?

Many retailers selling the same products share identical manufacturer descriptions, creating duplicate content that hurts rankings. Writing a unique description of a thousand words or more, filled with good data, examples, and expertise, helps differentiate a page and, according to SERP data from SCM Rush, longer content tends to rank best in Google.

How does product review schema help e-commerce SEO?

Implementing schema markup on original testimonials and reviews, not ones already posted on third-party sites like Yelp or Google, allows those reviews to appear as rich snippets in search results. Retailers can also mark up product pricing and availability to give searchers more information before they click.

What neuromarketing trick can boost click-through rates for retailer pages?

Adding prefixes called click magnets, such as cheap, buy, or 30% off, before the main keyword in a page title can increase CTR and surface the page for long-tail queries. The idea is to put shoppers in a buying mindset before they even click, but the offer must be real to avoid a high bounce rate.
