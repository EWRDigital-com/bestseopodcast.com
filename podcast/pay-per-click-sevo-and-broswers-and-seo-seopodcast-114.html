Questions this episode answers

How should I decide how much to bid on pay-per-click keywords?

The hosts recommend identifying which keywords actually close sales, even if they have a low click-through rate, and bidding more aggressively on those terms. If a keyword closes every time, it is worth spending more money to keep it visible on the first page.

What is the new AdWords segmentation feature discussed in this episode?

AdWords introduced a segmentation option that lets advertisers choose whether their ads appear at the top of search results or on the right side. The hosts note higher bids tend to earn top placement while lower bids aimed at budget management likely land on the right side.

Why do the hosts criticize Microsoft AdCenter in this episode?

They are frustrated that Microsoft AdCenter requires Internet Explorer to manage pay-per-click accounts and does not work in Chrome or Firefox. They argue any product should run across all standard browsers, pointing out that sites built with standard HTML work correctly in every browser except older IE versions.

What are Google Plus circles and why do the hosts like them?

Circles are Google Plus's way of organizing the people you share content with into named groups such as friends, family, or coworkers. The hosts prefer them over Facebook because adding someone to a circle is integral to the signup process and requires only a hover and drag, with no extra click needed to save.

Terms defined in this episode
AdWords segmentation
A feature that lets advertisers choose whether ads display at the top of search results or on the right side, separate from standard bid-based placement.
Google Plus circles
Google Plus's system for organizing contacts into named groups so users can selectively share content with specific sets of people.
Google Apps
A Google service that lets users use Google products like Gmail under their own domain name rather than a standard Gmail address.
Highest percent closing keywords
Keywords that convert site visitors into paying clients at the highest rate, which the hosts argue deserve priority ranking and higher pay-per-click bids.
