Questions this episode answers

Why should a small business create a Facebook fan page for SEO purposes?

Google is actively watching Facebook because Facebook surpassed Google in traffic. Half of Facebook users like at least one brand page, and 25% do so to receive discounts or promotions. Creating a fan page with offers gives businesses visibility in a space Google monitors closely.

What is the Google Golden Triangle and why does it still matter?

The Google Golden Triangle is research tracking eye and mouse movement via heat maps to show where users look on search results pages. It matters because Google has since added sponsored ads at the top, local places listings, and videos, chunking the page and changing how users scan results.

How does chunking search results pages affect sponsored ad clicks?

When Google adds local places listings, videos, and top sponsored ads, the page breaks into distinct sections. Users treat each chunk as something to explore, so sponsored ads become just another section to check, which the research suggests increases time spent on the page and attention to paid listings.

Why do most people like or follow a brand on Facebook?

According to the article from Web Pro News cited in this episode, 25% of people like a brand page to receive discounts and promotions, making it the top reason. The second biggest reason is to show others they support the brand, and about 10% do it because the page is fun and entertaining.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Golden Triangle
A heat map study tracking eye and mouse movement on Google search results pages, showing the triangular zone where users most often look, originally before sponsored ads were added across the top of results.
Universal Search
Google's approach of mixing organic listings with local places, videos, and sponsored ads in chunked sections on a single search results page, increasing user time on the page.
Facebook fan page
A business or brand profile page on Facebook that users can like, used to share promotions and build an audience, distinct from a personal profile.
