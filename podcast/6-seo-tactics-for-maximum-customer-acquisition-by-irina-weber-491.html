Questions this episode answers

What are the six SEO tactics for maximum customer acquisition covered in this episode?

The six tactics are: keyword research to attract new customers, improving customer experience on your website, repurposing and refurbishing content, using social media, participating in forum marketing for real customer feedback, and promoting upcoming customer events to build links and engagement.

Why is keyword intent important for customer acquisition in SEO?

Keyword intent determines whether a searcher is a potential buyer or just learning. For example, someone searching for SEO is likely learning, while someone searching for SEO agency is looking to hire. Targeting the right intent is essential for converting traffic into actual customers.

How often should businesses contact their customers to maintain loyalty?

The hosts suggest at minimum monthly contact, noting that 80% of customers forget who you are within 90 days. Daily communication is also mentioned as effective if the content delivers genuine value, shifting the focus from frequency concerns to whether enough value is being delivered.

Is organic social media an effective customer acquisition channel for most businesses?

The hosts say organic social media is hard to scale and has not shown a clear positive ROI for their clients with standard posting. They recommend limiting focus to two to three channels, committing fully, and supplementing organic efforts with paid strategies for businesses that need a social presence.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword intent
The underlying purpose behind a search query, distinguishing whether someone wants to learn, compare, or buy, which directly affects whether that traffic will convert into customers.
Long tail keywords
Specific, lower-volume search phrases that typically attract fewer visitors but convert at higher rates than broad terms.
Conversion rate optimization (CRO)
The practice of improving a website or landing page so that more of the traffic it receives actually takes a desired action, such as making a purchase or signing up.
Forum marketing
Participating in online forums or groups such as Facebook groups to provide value, gather customer feedback, and position a business as a solution provider without direct selling.
