Key takeaways
  • Turn off keywords that spend money without ever generating a conversion.
  • Never bid on broad keyword terms exclusively or you will exhaust your budget quickly.
  • Search and display campaigns should always be kept separate in campaign settings.
  • On-page landing page optimization is the most underrated aspect of a PPC account health check.
  • Decreasing bids on maxed-out budgets can sometimes generate more overall exposure by freeing spend for other placements.
What's the value in reducing the cost of clicks to your website by 10% to a web page that converts at a zero rate? There's no value. Zero.

Questions this episode answers

What should you look for when auditing keywords and negatives in a paid search account?

Check for keywords spending money without converting and turn them off. Use strong negative keyword lists as a foundation. Run competitor keywords in a separate ad group. Avoid double bidding by negating keywords in one ad group that you are targeting in another.

How does budget optimization work in a paid search health check?

Group similar performing campaigns with similar goals together in a shared budget to avoid overspending on a single target. Also check whether maxed-out budgets could generate more exposure by reallocating bids, and watch low-volume keywords so daily caps do not cause you to miss clicks on high-traffic days.

Why is landing page optimization considered the most underrated part of PPC?

Many PPC managers focus only on click volume or click-through rate without addressing whether the landing page actually converts. Reducing cost per click by 10% to a page that converts at zero still means wasted spend, so improving the landing page experience is critical to real results.

Terms defined in this episode
ROAS
Return on ad spend, a metric used when evaluating overall budget optimization in paid search campaigns.
Negative keywords
Keywords added to a paid search campaign to block irrelevant searches, preventing wasted spend on terms that do not convert.
Shared budget
A budget setting in paid search that groups similar performing campaigns with similar goals so they draw from one pool of spend.
Shiko
A share, a like, or a follow on social media, used by the hosts as a shorthand for social engagement actions.
