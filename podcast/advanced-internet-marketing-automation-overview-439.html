Questions this episode answers

Why are Facebook Messenger bots better than email for marketing open rates?

According to a Statista report cited in the episode, emails get an open rate of 5.9 to 18% with a 2% average click-through rate, while Messenger bots achieve an 80% open rate with a 30% or more click-through rate, making them far more likely to be seen and acted on.

What is IFTTT and how can small businesses use it for social media?

IFTTT is a free service that links social and web accounts together. A business owner can post a blog, and IFTTT automatically shares it to Pinterest, Facebook, Twitter, and other platforms. The episode notes it is also part of eWebResults' three-month SEO strategy for clients.

Does direct mail still work as a marketing channel?

Yes, according to the episode. The team describes a 10-week, three-to-four-step campaign using hand-addressed lumpy mail pieces sent every couple of weeks to a targeted prospect list, combined with email follow-up and remarketing, producing results strong enough to add it to their own client process.

In their words

it takes four times as much money to grab a new customer as it does to a customer you already had. But the remarketing piece is in between that.

Terms defined in this episode
Lumpy mail
A direct mail piece that contains a physical object inside the envelope, sparking curiosity and higher open rates than flat postcards.
Remarketing segmentation
Dividing website visitors into time-based audience groups such as 24-hour, 7-day, and 30-day windows and showing each group progressively stronger offers.
Lead scoring
Assigning quality or activity scores to contacts based on actions they take, such as viewing certain pages, so email sequences can trigger appropriately.
IFTTT
A free automation service that connects apps and accounts so that one action, such as publishing a blog post, automatically triggers posts across multiple social platforms.
