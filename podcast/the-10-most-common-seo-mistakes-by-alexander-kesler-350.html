Questions this episode answers

What is the most common SEO mistake businesses make with keywords?

Businesses often choose keywords based on internal industry jargon rather than what customers actually search. The fix is to use tools like Google Keyword Planner, SEMrush, or Moz, query past clients, and check Webmaster Tools query data to find terms that drive real traffic and conversions.

Why is keyword stuffing still a problem and what does it do to rankings?

Despite being an old tactic, keyword stuffing is still practiced. Search engines flag it as spammy, which hurts SEO performance. It also creates a poor reading experience for users and can trigger a Panda penalty, causing more damage than any ranking benefit.

Should I use a separate mobile site or a fully responsive site?

The hosts recommend a fully responsive site. A separate mobile site tends to lose content, navigation, remarketing code, and other marketing elements. A responsive site delivers the full experience across phones, tablets, and desktops, though you should review how it looks on each device.

Why do analytics matter for SEO even though they do not directly affect rankings?

Analytics do not directly affect rankings, but the decisions made from that data do. Regular review reveals which traffic sources, devices, and keywords drive conversions. One example given is a client who nearly canceled a radio ad until analytics showed it generated 3 conversions from 20 clicks over 60 days.
