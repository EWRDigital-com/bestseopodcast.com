How to create an effective digital marketing strategy by Tereza Litsa #348

Ep. 34838 min2016-11-23

An effective digital marketing strategy requires five steps: define goals, analyze existing media assets, plan action steps, execute the plan, and measure results to make ongoing adjustments.

Key takeaways
  • 47% of businesses have no documented digital marketing strategy, which risks long-term goals.
  • Analyze owned, earned, and paid media separately before planning any campaign actions.
  • Share the finalized plan with every party involved to avoid costly communication gaps.
  • Execute before the plan is perfect, because real data from action beats no data at all.
  • Measure everything including visits, engagement, conversions, and cost, then adjust strategy continuously.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five steps to creating an effective digital marketing strategy?

According to the article covered in this episode, the five steps are: define your business goals, analyze your existing owned, earned, and paid media assets, plan how to turn goals into action, execute the plan with room for creativity, and measure results at every stage to improve over time.

What is owned, earned, and paid media in a digital marketing strategy?

Owned media includes assets you control such as your website and social profiles. Earned media is coverage you did not pay for, such as reviews, social shares, and guest blog posts. Paid media is any channel where you spend money to place content or ads.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, or follows on any social media platform.
Owned media
Digital media assets you control directly, such as your website, social profiles, and email lists.
Earned media
Digital coverage you did not pay for and do not own, including reviews, social shares, guest blogging, and PR mentions.
KPIs
Key performance indicators, the measurable metrics used to evaluate whether a digital marketing strategy is meeting its defined goals.
