Questions this episode answers

What is the Google Openness Update and when did it happen?

The Openness Update rolled out in November 2023 and made business hours a stronger local ranking factor in Google Maps. Businesses that are open longer, or open when a search is performed, now rank higher in non-navigational local searches than businesses shown as closed.

Should I change my business hours to 24/7 to rank better in Google Maps?

No. Listing false business hours can get your Google Business Profile suspended. Google still weighs the accuracy of business hours and is incorporating AI to detect real patterns. The host recommends extending hours legitimately, such as using an overnight answering service, rather than falsifying listings.

How do I test whether the Openness Update is affecting my local rankings?

Run two baseline ranking scans using the same tool, one when your business is open and one when it is closed, then compare them side by side. Tools like Local Falcon and SEMrush have local ranking factor tools. You will likely see significantly lower rankings during closed hours.

How important is Google Business Profile for driving website traffic?

According to data cited in the episode, Google Business Profile represents about a third of organic traffic. Verified businesses average 1,800 monthly views, with 84% coming from discovery searches. Local businesses average 105 monthly website visits from verified Google Business Profiles.

Terms defined in this episode
Openness Update
A Google Maps algorithm update from November 2023 that made business hours a stronger ranking signal, boosting visibility for businesses that are open when a local search is performed.
Google Business Profile (GBP)
Google's free business listing platform, formerly called Google My Business, which drives local map rankings, discovery searches, calls, direction requests, and website clicks.
Local Pack
The set of three local business results Google shows in Maps and local search, which the Openness Update now influences based on whether a business is currently open.
Non-navigational search
A general local search that does not include a specific business name, the type of query most affected by the Openness Update ranking factor.
