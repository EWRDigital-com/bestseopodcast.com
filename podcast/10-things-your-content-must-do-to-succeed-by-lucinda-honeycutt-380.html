10 Things Your Content Must Do to Succeed by Lucinda Honeycutt #380

Ep. 38038 min2017-07-07

Key takeaways
  • Research your audience's device type, language, and buyer persona before writing a single word.
  • Weave keywords in naturally and add a Facebook comment plugin to boost both search and social optimization.
  • Address frequently asked questions or industry concerns to guarantee your content provides real value.
  • Include a call to action in every content piece, whether a blog post, video, white paper, or remarketing ad.
  • Creating content without distributing it via sponsored posts, blog aggregators, or newsletters means almost no one will read it.

Questions this episode answers

What are the 10 things content must do to succeed according to Lucinda Honeycutt?

The 10 things are: speak to the right audience, be optimized for search and social, provide value, tell a story, inform, educate, engage, persuade, be optimized for conversions with a CTA, and be distributed correctly across channels like social posts, newsletters, and blog aggregators.

How should you optimize content for both search and social?

Do basic SEO covering titles, headers, meta, alt tags, links, site speed, and mobile friendliness for search. For social, use a consistent hashtag in your content and meta, and consider replacing WordPress comments with a Facebook comment plugin to drive engagement directly from your site.

Why is informing readers especially important for businesses with long sales cycles?

Buyers in longer processes, such as home remodels, visit a site multiple times while researching. Informing them about relevant concerns and challenges on each visit raises the likelihood they will fill out a contact form, download an e-book, or call, because you have built trust across multiple touch points.

What does it mean to address the FAB in persuasive content?

FAB stands for feature, advantage, and benefits. Attaching these three elements to the information, engagement, and education already provided in your content makes it easier to persuade readers to convert, because they can clearly see what the product does, why it matters, and how it helps them.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows across social platforms.
SCAG (Single Keyword Ad Group)
An advanced paid search technique where one ad group contains only one keyword topic, including its broad, exact, and phrase match versions, with one or two ads referencing that phrase, intended to raise quality score.
FAB
Feature, advantage, and benefits. A persuasion framework used in content to show readers what a product does, why it matters, and how it benefits them.
CTA (Call to Action)
A button, form, phone number, or other prompt placed in content that tells readers exactly what action to take next, relevant to both the content and the platform.
