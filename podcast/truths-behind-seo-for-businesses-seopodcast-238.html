Key takeaways
  • Continuity from search query to ad to landing page to CTA improves conversions.

Questions this episode answers

Why do businesses lose their search rankings after achieving them?

According to the hosts, businesses get complacent once they reach page one and stop the ongoing work that earned them that position. Just as Google rewards consistent effort, it will drop sites that stop producing good content, earning authority backlinks, and maintaining social signals.

What does it mean that offline and online marketing are codependent?

What people say about a business offline, such as negative word of mouth, can end up as online reviews and affect search visibility. Conversely, what people find online affects offline sales. The hosts recommend syncing both, such as encouraging check-ins with incentives like discounts.

How should mobile SEO go beyond just having a responsive website?

The hosts say businesses should optimize their responsiveness by thinking about what mobile users actually need. For example, phone numbers and directions should be prominent on mobile, while full navigation menus can be de-emphasized, since mobile visitors are usually looking for quick contact or location info.

Terms defined in this episode
continuity
The principle that the search query, ad, landing page, CTA, and follow-up all reflect the same offer and experience for the user.
site-wide links
Links that appear across many or all pages of a site, which can have a damaging SEO effect especially when anchor text is duplicated across duplicate content.
content scraper reporting page
A Google tool where you submit the scraper site URL, the original content URL, and the SERP result page so Google can investigate and adjust rankings accordingly.
