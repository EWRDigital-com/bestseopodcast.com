Anchor Text Best Practices for Google by Roger Montti #416

Ep. 41628 min2018-04-23

Anchor text best practices today require mixing exact match, long tail, naked URLs, and company name links at roughly 25% each to avoid appearing manipulative to Google.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • A 75% exact match anchor text ratio looks unnatural to Google and risks penalties.
  • Surrounding text around a link matters, not just the anchor text itself, per Bill Sloski.
  • The modification engine re-ranks SERPs based on implicit user behavior like clicks and time on page.
  • Geographic anchor text triggers extra Google scrutiny, so vary location phrasing like Houston Texas versus Houston TX.
  • Step one in link building is simply doing it, since most sites are already behind on anchor text diversity.

About this episode

Anchor Text Best Practices for GoogleAnchor text is the text used in the links to your site. It used to be that if you acquire enough links with the appropriate keywords in the anchor text that your site will begin to rank for those keywords. But at some point websites experienced penalizations for having statistically unnatural ratios of anchor text links. Here are the best practice for the use of anchor text today.

Questions this episode answers

What is the best anchor text ratio for backlinks?

The hosts suggest a rough 25% split across four types: exact match keyword anchor text, long tail phrases that include the anchor text, naked URLs, and company name. No expert in the article provided an official ratio, so this is a practical guideline based on experience.

Why can you not just copy anchor text ratios from top-ranking competitors?

Roger Montti argues that sites rank for many reasons beyond anchor text, including user experience, brand recognition, geographic location, and user intent signals. Copying ratios assumes a causal relationship that may not exist, so the extracted ratios may be meaningless as a direct guide.

Does the text surrounding a link affect its ranking impact?

Yes. Bill Sloski, described as a preeminent expert on search engine patents, points out that Google may look at a window of words around the linked anchor text. Irrelevant surrounding text can reduce the link's impact, and links placed in the first paragraph carry even more weight.

What is the modification engine in Google search?

The modification engine is a secondary layer that re-ranks search results based on implicit user feedback such as click behavior and time on page. After Google's core algorithm sets an initial ranking, user interactions cause Google to adjust those results, similar to how Facebook tests content with a small audience first.

Terms defined in this episode
Anchor Text
The clickable text used in a hyperlink pointing to a site. Exact match anchor text mirrors a target keyword exactly, and unnatural ratios of it can trigger Google penalties.
Modification Engine
A Google system that re-ranks search results after initial ranking by using implicit user feedback such as clicks and time on page, described in a Google patent on modifying search result ranking based on implicit user feedback.
Naked Link
A backlink where the anchor text is the raw URL itself rather than a keyword or brand name, used as one component of a natural anchor text mix.
Exact Match Anchor
A backlink whose anchor text is an exact keyword phrase being targeted. Having 75% exact match anchors is considered unnatural by experts cited in the episode.
