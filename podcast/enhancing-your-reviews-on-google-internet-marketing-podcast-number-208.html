Questions this episode answers

How do Google Plus circles affect SEO rankings?

The hosts explain that being placed in named circles by multiple users helps Google understand your areas of authority. If many users put you in circles labeled internet marketing or SEO, Google associates you with those topics and may raise your rankings for related searches.

How do you set up Google authorship on your website?

Log into your Google Plus page, go to the About section, and add contributor links to every site where you publish content. Then on each post on those sites, add a link back to your Google Plus page using the tag REL equals author so Google can confirm you as the original author.

Why are Google Plus reviews important for local SEO?

Google Plus reviews show up directly in Google search results and on third-party sites. Studies cited in the episode show sites with more reviews rank higher and get more clicks, since users trust businesses with more reviews and are more likely to click on a listing with 30 reviews over one with three.

What is the difference between a Google Plus Local page and a Google Plus business page?

A Google Plus Local page replaces the old Google Places listing and is tied to your physical business location and verification process. A Google Plus business page is a separate social presence similar to a Facebook fan page. The hosts say you need both, which requires entering much of the same information twice.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. The hosts say this information must be consistent across your Google Plus Local page, your business page, your website, and anywhere else online where your company information appears.
Google Authorship
A setup linking your Google Plus profile to content you publish, allowing Google to display your avatar next to search listings, claiming ownership of that content and helping prevent duplicate content issues.
Google Plus Local
The replacement for Google Places that lets business owners create and verify a location-based page, claim existing listings, and collect Google reviews that appear in search results.
Plus One
A Google Plus voting signal applied to a person, website, or individual post. The hosts describe it as similar to a backlink in value and say it can get a page indexed faster than submitting to Webmaster Tools.
