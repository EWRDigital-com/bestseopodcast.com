#SEOpodcast 269 - Driving Conversions with Emotion

Episode35 min2015-04-13

Emotions drive purchasing decisions first and logic is used to justify them afterward, so marketers should build confidence, trust, positive feelings, and attachment into their sites to lift conversions.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Customers make purchase decisions emotionally and use logic only to justify what they already decided.
  • Simplify your checkout flow so visitors feel capable and confident, not confused or frustrated.
  • Use testimonials, video reviews, and guarantees to reduce buyer anxiety and manage cognitive dissonance.
  • Under-promise and over-deliver in stages, such as promising one free gift and delivering three, to create loyal repeat customers.
Logical decisions are used to support the decision you've already made.

Questions this episode answers

How do emotions drive conversions on a website?

According to this episode, people make purchasing decisions based on emotion first, then use logic to justify those decisions. Fear of missing out, desire, and positive feelings like getting a good deal all push visitors to convert, while negative emotions like confusion or distrust push them away.

What is the halo effect in marketing and how do you use it?

The halo effect is people's tendency to form a positive impression of something because it is associated with something else they know and like. Marketers can apply it by displaying logos of well-known clients, using celebrity spokespersons, or cross-marketing with trusted brands to build credibility.

Terms defined in this episode
Self-efficacy
Self-confidence in the user's ability to complete a task. In conversion design, it means building a site so visitors feel the checkout or process is easy and they can accomplish it.
Halo effect
People's tendency to form a positive impression of something because it is associated with something else they know and like, used in marketing through brand associations and client logos.
Cognitive dissonance
A tension that occurs when people face a purchasing decision. It can prevent conversions if anxiety or fear of regret is not addressed through guarantees, testimonials, and trust signals.
FOMO
Fear of missing out. Described in the episode as a top producer of conversions because it drives people to act before they lose the chance to obtain something.
