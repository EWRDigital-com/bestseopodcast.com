Key takeaways
  • Each local business needs a dedicated page per main service and per physical location, with original optimized content.
  • Name, address, and phone number should appear on every single page of a local business website.
  • A page without a call to action is a page without a point, so place CTAs throughout, not just at the end.
  • Avoid link building shortcuts; focus on high-quality links to build a sustainable foundation for your money site.
  • When rebranding or switching domains, transition slowly, such as 20 percent at a time, to avoid sudden ranking drops.

Questions this episode answers

Why is a multi-site approach bad for local SEO?

Having a separate website for each service area spreads marketing efforts too thin, leads to thin or duplicate content, and creates name, address, and phone number inconsistencies that cause local ranking problems. It can also be fundamentally dishonest if a business is faking multiple locations.

What contact information should every page of a local business website include?

According to the article covered in this episode, every page should include name, address, and phone number, either in the header or footer. A highly available contact page is also essential, and the hosts add that a contact form should be on every page so visitors never have to navigate away to reach you.

How should a local business handle a domain change or rebrand without losing rankings?

Transition slowly rather than doing a hard switch. The hosts recommend duplicating the site and moving traffic in increments, such as 20 percent at a time, so you do not lose all traffic at once. Keeping the site live during testing is critical, because taking it down and putting it back up can trigger Google to flag the site.

Terms defined in this episode
Siloing
A website architecture strategy that groups related content, such as different services or locations, into distinct sections to improve organization and SEO relevance for larger sites.
Process fluency
A cognitive bias referenced via a Rand Fishkin article where information that is easier to mentally process, such as rhyming phrases, is more readily believed and remembered by users.
Multi-site approach
The practice of building a separate website for each service area or location, which the episode identifies as a local SEO pitfall due to thin content, NAP inconsistencies, and diluted SEO effort.
NAP
Name, address, and phone number, the core contact details that must appear consistently on every page of a local business website and across all online listings.
