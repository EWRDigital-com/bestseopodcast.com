#SEOpodcast 276 - Meta Description Tips

Episode25 min2015-06-10

Writing killer meta descriptions boosts CTR by invoking curiosity, using numbers, stating your USP, copying paid ad copy, and analyzing competitors, giving you a second path to more website traffic beyond ranking alone.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Copy paid ad copy for meta descriptions because PPC advertisers continuously test and optimize their text for clicks.
  • Use numbers in meta descriptions and titles because numbers draw attention and increase the likelihood of a click.
  • Invoke curiosity with phrases like 'Find out how' or 'Ever wondered' to push searchers toward clicking.
  • Include your unique selling proposition in the meta description to differentiate from competitors in the search results.
  • SEO is an ongoing process, so track results, keep tweaking, and mix and match meta description strategies over time.

Questions this episode answers

What are the best tips for writing meta descriptions that increase click-through rate?

The episode covers eight tips: copy paid ads, copy organic competitors, invoke curiosity, lead with benefits over features, use numbers, mention your unique selling proposition, include your target keywords, and mix and match all of the above while tracking results and tweaking over time.

Why should you copy paid ad copy when writing meta descriptions?

People running paid ads are constantly testing their ad text for clicks, so their copy tends to be optimized. The hosts suggest using AdWords campaigns to identify high-CTR phrases and then applying that language directly to your organic meta descriptions.

What is a USP and why does it belong in a meta description?

USP stands for unique selling proposition, meaning what makes your business different from competitors. Including it in a meta description highlights your differentiator to searchers at the moment they are choosing between listings, for example noting a lawyer is a former judge.

Terms defined in this episode
Meta Description
A brief text snippet shown in the search engine results page that describes a page's content and can influence whether searchers click through to your site.
CTR (Click-Through Rate)
The rate at which people click on a search result listing, which the episode presents as a second path to more traffic alongside improving search rank.
USP (Unique Selling Proposition)
What makes a business different from its competitors, highlighted in the meta description to stand out in search results.
FAB
A sales framework standing for Features, Advantages, and Benefits, used in the episode to suggest going beyond just benefits when writing meta descriptions.
