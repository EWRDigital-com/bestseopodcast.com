Questions this episode answers

How ready are businesses for voice search?

A study of 73,000 businesses found that over 96% of business locations fail to list their information correctly across voice search directories. The most common errors involved opening hours, website, location, and street map data, which mirrors longstanding local SEO problems with NAP consistency.

What is WNAPO and why does it matter for voice search?

WNAPO stands for website, name, address, phone number, and opening hours. It expands the traditional NAP concept used in local SEO. Every detail must be identical across all directories because Google looks for consistency to confirm a business listing is trustworthy and accurate.

How is voice search different from typed search?

Voice searches are more conversational and question-based, for example asking how do you make cold brew at home instead of typing make cold brew. On home devices like Google Home or Alexa, only one answer is returned with no visible credit given to the source website.

What should businesses do to optimize for voice search?

Businesses should ensure their WNAPO information is consistent across all directories, log into Google My Business and review the Insights section, maximize photos and videos, fill out all services, and optimize for near me and location-based queries since those search types have grown over 150% in recent years.

Terms defined in this episode
WNAPO
An acronym coined in this episode meaning website, name, address, phone number, and opening hours. It expands the traditional NAP local SEO concept and must be consistent across all business listings.
Answer engine
A term from Backlinko describing Google as a platform where users ask for direct answers rather than searching for information, reflecting the shift driven by voice search behavior.
Near me searches
Location-based queries where users look for businesses or services nearby. Growth in near me now searches has increased by over 150% over the last two years according to the episode.
Three pack / snack pack
The group of three local business results Google displays in map-based search results, representing a major opportunity since voice search on home devices returns only one result instead of three.
