Questions this episode answers

What is CIVO and how is it different from SEO?

CIVO stands for search engine visitor optimization. The hosts coined the term to describe building and optimizing a website for the human visitor, not just search engines. They argue it begins at the SERP, meaning even your search result listing should help users take action before they ever click.

What should always appear above the fold on a homepage?

According to the hosts, the area above the fold should include your logo, business name, contact information such as a phone number or email, your target keywords, and at least one call to action. Flash banners that consume the entire fold area without these elements waste prime real estate.

What are breadcrumbs and when should you use them?

Breadcrumbs are navigation links, usually at the top of a page, that show a user the hierarchy of pages they have traveled through on a site. The hosts recommend using them when a site has many subcategories, so users can easily move back up to a parent category without getting lost.

Why do Facebook, Twitter, and YouTube logos on a website matter for CIVO?

The hosts say displaying those logos breeds familiarity and signals reliability and credibility. Even if the accounts are not heavily used, visitors see those logos and conclude the business is serious enough to maintain a presence on major social platforms, which builds immediate trust.

Terms defined in this episode
CIVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
A term coined by eWebStyle meaning the practice of designing and optimizing a website for the human visitor first, with the principle that CIVO begins at the search engine results page.
Above the fold
The portion of a webpage visible without scrolling, borrowed from print advertising where the top half of a folded newspaper is the first thing seen. The hosts call it prime real estate for keywords, contact info, and calls to action.
Breadcrumbs
A navigation aid displayed at the top of a page showing the hierarchical path a user has taken through a site, helping them move between subcategory levels without losing their place.
Long tail keyword
A keyword phrase made up of three or more words. The hosts stress understanding the difference between short tail and long tail keywords when using a keyword selector tool for SEO campaigns.
