Key takeaways
  • Keyword research should be a continuous daily process, not a one-time blog post or redesign task.
  • Match keywords to funnel stage: awareness, consideration, and decision phrases attract very different visitors with different conversion intent.
  • Use AdWords PPC to test keyword ideas before committing to a long-term SEO strategy, letting data choose winners.
  • Start keyword discovery from an already popular page, then branch into related and seasonal long-tail variations with real search volume.
  • Plan content calendars around seasonal keyword peaks in advance, since search volume for topics like Black Friday TVs spikes predictably.

Questions this episode answers

How should you structure a keyword research spreadsheet for enterprise SEO?

Put the page URL on the left side of the spreadsheet and the target keyword phrase on the right, then add columns for search volume and competition. This maps each page to its keyword and gives context to the volume numbers by tying them to funnel stage and conversion intent.

Why is funnel stage important in keyword research?

Different keywords signal where a searcher is in the buying process. Broad terms like Las Vegas attract awareness traffic that rarely converts, while specific terms like Vegas hotels pricing attract people ready to buy. Targeting bottom-funnel keywords brings visitors already primed to convert.

How can PPC data improve organic keyword research?

Load candidate keywords into AdWords, run ads, and let performance data show which terms actually convert before investing in a long SEO campaign. You can A-B test keywords, ad copy, titles, and meta descriptions so data, not guesswork, determines the right targets.

What is the existing page topics method for finding new keywords?

Pick a popular existing page, identify its core ingredient or theme, research related keyword variations, and look for patterns such as seasonal modifiers. The whiskey cocktails example in the episode showed how searching related terms revealed seasonal variations like whiskey cocktails for summer and fall with real search volume.

Terms defined in this episode
Funnel stage keyword mapping
Organizing keywords by where they fall in the customer journey: awareness, consideration, or decision, so content targets visitors at each buying phase.
Long tail keywords
Specific, lower-volume keyword phrases that attract a more targeted audience and, according to the episode, convert better than broad head terms.
Ahrefs Webmaster Tools (AWT)
A free tool from Ahrefs that gives website owners access to almost half of Ahrefs product functionality at no cost and without requiring a credit card.
Micro moments
A term Google uses, referenced in the episode, to describe the individual touch points a customer encounters as they move through the decision making process.
