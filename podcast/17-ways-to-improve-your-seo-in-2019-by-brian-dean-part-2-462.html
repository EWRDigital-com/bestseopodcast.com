Questions this episode answers

What is broken link building and how does it work for SEO?

Broken link building involves finding outbound links on high-authority sites that no longer work, then contacting the site owner to replace the dead link with your relevant content. Tools like Ahrefs can help locate broken links, and one strong link from an authoritative site can outperform many weaker links.

How can YouTube videos improve your Google search rankings?

YouTube videos can appear at or near the top of search results for certain keywords, sometimes in position zero, giving you additional SERP real estate beyond your organic page listing. The hosts note that roughly 98% of video results in Google are YouTube videos, making it the preferred platform.

Why should you target topic plus statistics keywords?

Statistics keywords attract reporters, bloggers, and journalists who are looking for data to cite in their own content. By ranking for phrases like student loan debt statistics, you become a link source for content creators who do the linking work naturally when they reference your data.

What does optimizing old content for user intent mean?

It means reviewing existing posts, combining related ones, and rewriting them to match what searchers actually want to accomplish. The hosts describe merging and re-indexing blogs so Google sees content that holds user attention, which reduces bounce rates and supports higher rankings.

Terms defined in this episode
SERP real estate
The total visible space a website occupies on a search engine results page, which can include organic listings, map results, ads, and video results.
User intent
What a searcher is trying to accomplish when they perform a specific Google search. If a site does not match that intent, Google is less likely to rank it.
Broken link building
An SEO tactic where you find dead outbound links on relevant websites and ask the site owner to replace the broken link with a link to your working, relevant content.
Content partnership
A collaboration between multiple content creators or influencers who co-create or contribute to shared content and then each promote it to their own audiences.
