What You Need to Know about Podcast Advertising in 2020 with Lori Lefcourt #485

Ep. 48540 min2020-05-20Guest: Lori Lefcourt

Podcast advertising is growing fast in 2020 as brands shift budgets from live events, and hosts can monetize at any audience size by building trust through authentic, voice-driven ad reads that outperform static Instagram posts.

Key takeaways
  • One podcast episode can be repurposed into blog posts, Twitter screenshots, Instagram videos, and more, creating a full content workflow.
  • Podcast ad trust drives higher conversion rates than picture-based Instagram ads, especially outside fashion categories.
  • You can pitch sponsors before launching by building a media kit and showing projected listener numbers to local or niche brands.

Questions this episode answers

How do you monetize a podcast when you are just starting out?

According to Lori Lefcourt, there is no required download threshold. You can pitch sponsors before you even launch by creating a media kit with projected listener numbers. Ossa also offers a free course with eight modules built by podcasters who are already monetizing, and the network is open to women at any audience size.

What is Ossa and how does it help podcasters?

Ossa is a free podcast network and platform focused on helping women monetize their shows. It grew out of a blog called Like A Boss Girls that had 1.2 million organic Facebook followers. Podcasters can join other networks simultaneously; Ossa only asks that brand deals it arranges stay within the platform.

Why is podcast advertising considered more effective than Instagram posts?

Lori Lefcourt explains that a host speaking personally about a product in conversation builds far more trust than a static picture with caption text that listeners scroll past. She notes a statistic suggesting brands doing podcast advertising could see a 32% growth in sales, though she cautions against taking that figure too literally.

How do you repurpose a single podcast episode into multiple pieces of content?

The episode outlines a weekly process: record the podcast on Monday, write two blog posts from the first and second halves on Tuesday, pull a quotable sentence for a Twitter post on Wednesday, and create an Instagram video on Thursday. Audience comments can also be repurposed into follow-up posts.

In their words

I look at podcasting as a pillar piece of content. And what that means is that is the one piece of content that's going to give you a lot of other pieces of content

Lori Lefcourt
Terms defined in this episode
Pillar piece of content
A single long-form asset, such as a podcast episode, that serves as the source for many smaller pieces of content distributed across blogs, social networks, and video platforms.
Podcast advertising
Paid brand integrations within podcast episodes where a host personally endorses a product or service, valued for the trust and conversion rates it produces compared to display or image-based ads.
Media kit
A document a podcaster creates to pitch prospective sponsors, showing projected audience size and demographics in order to secure sponsorships before or shortly after launch.
