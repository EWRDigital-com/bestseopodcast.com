Questions this episode answers

Does using a templated WordPress theme hurt your SEO rankings?

A template itself does not hurt rankings as long as it is coded properly. The main risks are spammy links hidden in free theme footers, slow load times from heavy scripting, and plugin conflicts on updates. Using a clean base like the WordPress 2011 theme and adding unique content, title tags, and URLs keeps you safe.

Should Groupon work for my small business?

Groupon works best if you need foot traffic, want branding, and have a high upsell rate so customers buy more than the discounted item. It tends to hurt businesses like restaurants where the margin on the discounted product is already negative and customers do not return after redeeming the deal.

What is a PPC quality score and why does it matter?

Quality score is how Google determines whether your ads and landing pages connect to what a user searched. It factors in ad text, keyword selection, and landing page optimization. A higher quality score results in a lower cost per click and better ad position, so keeping all three elements consistent and relevant is critical.

What does Google replacing Local Places with Google Plus mean for businesses?

Local listings that previously appeared in search results will now link to the company's Google Plus page instead of a separate Google Places page. Businesses should create or update their G Plus company page and use it actively, since engagement may influence ranking in local results.

Terms defined in this episode
Link profile
The full picture of who is linking to your site, what you link to, and the value of the sites involved, which affects how your site performs through algorithm changes like Penguin.
Quality score
Google's measure of how well your PPC ads, keywords, and landing pages connect to a user's search, with a higher score yielding lower cost per click and better ad position.
Negative keywords
Keywords added to a PPC campaign to prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches, such as adding a competitor brand name you do not carry.

A recurring podcast segment covering recent or fundamental changes in how Google and other search engines operate.
