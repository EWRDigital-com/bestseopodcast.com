Preventing Costly Site Usability Mistakes - #sepodcast 223

Episode26 min2014-03-12

Six site usability mistakes kill conversions: lack of simplicity, designing for looks over function, missing or misspelled words, too many options, unclear calls to action, and no communication channels.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Target your most profitable keywords first, then optimize for less profitable ones after generating revenue.
  • Give site visitors fewer choices, more options causes decision paralysis and lost sales, as shown by the jam study.
  • Make your call to action obvious with a bright color and a clear reason for the visitor to act.
  • For smaller e-commerce sites, provide a phone number, address, and contact method or visitors will buy from a competitor.
  • Design sites for your audience's actual needs, not for personal aesthetic preferences or to impress with flashy visuals.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common usability mistakes that hurt website conversions?

According to the episode, the six mistakes are: not keeping it simple, designing for looks rather than usefulness, forgetting or misspelling important words, giving too many options, failing to make the desired action obvious, and providing no communication channels for visitors or customers.

Why do too many options hurt conversions on a website?

A psychological study on a university campus found that a stand selling four types of jam outsold a stand with 30 types by a significant margin. More choices make the option of doing nothing more attractive, so reducing options on a site leads to more decisions and more purchases.

How should an e-commerce site handle customer communication to avoid losing sales?

The hosts say smaller e-commerce brands must provide a phone number, a physical address, and a way for customers to resolve order issues. Without these, visitors will bounce and buy from a competitor. A live chat feature is also recommended if someone is available to staff it.

Why is it important to use the right descriptive words on a product or service page?

Missing a key descriptive word, like the word tankless when advertising tankless water heaters, means you attract the wrong visitors and miss your most profitable customers. It also connects to keyword strategy: ranking for the wrong term wastes optimization effort on lower-value traffic.

Terms defined in this episode
CTA (Call to Action)
The specific action you want a site visitor to take, such as calling a phone number or clicking a download link, which should be made visually obvious and limited to avoid overwhelming the visitor.
Conversion
The point at which a site visitor completes a desired action, such as a purchase or form submission. Usability mistakes like too many options or missing contact info directly reduce conversion rates.
Mobile commerce
Shopping or purchasing conducted on a mobile device. The hosts note it is growing and requires especially simple site navigation and easy tapping for product discovery.
