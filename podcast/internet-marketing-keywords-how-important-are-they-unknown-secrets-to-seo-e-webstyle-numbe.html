  • The most valuable keyword targets may not be obvious; an orthopedic surgery center benefited most from targeting referring doctors, not direct patients.
  • SEO providers must understand what a client sells most and what generates the most profit, because those are often different things.

Questions this episode answers

Why are keywords so important for SEO and pay-per-click?

Keywords guide every aspect of both on-page and off-page optimization. Without the right keywords, even technically strong SEO work will fail to bring valuable, qualified traffic. The hosts describe keyword selection as the foundation above everything else in any search engine marketing effort.

How should a business choose the right SEO keywords?

An SEO provider must deeply understand the client's business, including what they sell most and what is most profitable. The episode uses an orthopedic surgery center as an example, where the most valuable keywords turned out to target referring doctors rather than direct patients.

What is a common internal linking mistake that hurts SEO?

Using generic anchor text like 'home' for internal links is a classic mistake. Google's spiders read link text to understand what is on the destination page, so using a keyword phrase like 'plumbing in Houston' as anchor text tells Google what the linked page is actually about.

How does pay-per-click budgeting work on Google?

Advertisers set a bid amount per click and a total budget. Each time someone clicks the ad, that amount is deducted from the budget. The hosts say careful keyword and bid analysis can extend the value of a budget by 5 to 10 times compared to unmanaged spending.

Terms defined in this episode
Search Engine Marketing (SEM)
All marketing related to search engines, including search engine optimization, pay-per-click, search engine submission, and pay-for-inclusion.
On-page optimization
SEO work done directly on a website, including keyword placement in body text, header tags, title tags, and internal link anchor text.
Off-page optimization
SEO work done outside a website, the most common example being acquiring inbound links from other sites.
Pay-per-click
A form of search engine marketing where advertisers bid on keywords and are charged a set amount each time a user clicks their sponsored ad.
