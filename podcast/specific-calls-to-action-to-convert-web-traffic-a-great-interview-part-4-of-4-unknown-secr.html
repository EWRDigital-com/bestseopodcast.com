Questions this episode answers

Why should I put a text link pyramid at the bottom of my website?

If your top navigation is built in Flash or JavaScript, Google may not be able to follow those links and will not know your other pages exist. A text link pyramid at the bottom lets Google discover and crawl all internal pages, ensuring they all receive link juice.

Are reciprocal link exchanges still good for SEO?

According to this episode, reciprocal links are largely a zero-sum game. Google sees your link going out and their link coming back, and the value cancels out. The focus should be on earning one-way inbound links, especially from high-authority sites.

Should I display pricing on my website?

The hosts suggest testing both approaches. Showing prices can attract comparison shoppers who are not ready to buy. For service industries with credibility concerns, removing prices and motivating visitors to call may improve lead quality, since the sale often requires a conversation first.

Do paid banner ads count as inbound links for SEO?

Google's stated policy is that paid ads should not count as ranking links. The hosts note Google does not want site owners paying high-PR websites to insert keyword-rich text links, as that is considered manipulation of their system. Run ad campaigns for direct traffic, not for link credit.

Terms defined in this episode
Link pyramid
A block of plain text hyperlinks placed at the bottom of a webpage, using keyword-rich anchor text, so search engines can find and crawl all internal pages even when the main navigation is Flash or JavaScript.
Link juice
The ranking power or authority a website can accumulate through inbound links, which has a maximum ceiling that bad internal linking practices can prevent a site from reaching.
Drip campaign
An automated series of follow-up emails sent to a prospect after they submit their email address, used here as a way to nurture leads and track how many convert to customers.
Page rank (PR)
Google's numerical measure of a webpage's authority and value, referenced in the episode when evaluating whether a reciprocal link partner is a high-quality or low-quality site.
