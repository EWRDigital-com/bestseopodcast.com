#SEOpodcast 275 - Content Marketing Tips on a Budget

Episode36 min2015-06-03

Content curation, hosting curated content on your own site, adding original insight, giving credit, avoiding over-syndication, and aligning content with your sales funnel are the core budget-friendly content marketing strategies covered in this episode.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Host all curated content on your own site, not off-site, because your site is where conversions and remarketing happen.
  • Always add your own insight or opinion when curating content, not just regurgitating what someone else wrote.
  • Give credit and link back to the original source whenever you curate content from another publisher.
  • Be mindful of straight syndication to avoid duplicate content issues that hurt search rankings.
  • Curate content specifically to support your sales funnel so visitors are primed to convert when contacted.
Your site is home base, ladies and gentlemen. Don't ever forget that.

Questions this episode answers

What is content curation and how does it differ from stealing content?

Content curation means researching published content on a topic, then discussing and publishing it with your own meaning and additions. It is not article spinning or site jacking. You take someone else's content, research it, break it down, add to it, make it your own, and publish it because it relates to what you offer.

How do you avoid duplicate content issues when curating?

Avoid straight syndication by editorializing the content and taking a position in your blog post with a link to the original. Simply reposting the same content across channels creates duplicate content problems. Add a different perspective, supporting facts, or a controversial angle to make the content distinct and worth engaging with.

Should curated content live on social media or your own website?

It should live on your own site and be promoted through social media. Your site is where conversions happen, where your remarketing script lives, and where your sales funnel is designed to operate. Social activity should point people back to your site, including all curated content.

How do you find content to curate if your industry is slow-moving or dry?

Check your manufacturers and distributors, as they often have strong existing content you can curate. The episode uses a pool service client as an example, where product brochures and distributor materials provided a starting point for curated posts tied to that specific business.

Terms defined in this episode
Content curation
Researching published content about a topic, then adding your own insight, meaning, and resources to it before publishing it on your site. It is not article spinning or copying someone else's content without adding value.
Straight syndication
Taking content and reposting it across multiple channels without modification, which can cause over-syndication and duplicate content problems on the web.
Sales funnel content alignment
Creating and curating content that supports each stage of your sales process so that by the time a visitor converts, they are already mentally prepared for the next step your business needs them to take.
