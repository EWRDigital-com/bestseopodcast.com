Google Plus and Youtube Videos and SEO - Internet Marketing Podcast 117

Episode28 min2011-09-26

Video marketing on YouTube benefits from keyword-focused titles, descriptive URLs in video text, HTML links in descriptions, and branding overlays, all of which help with SEO and audience growth.

Key takeaways
  • Put your target keyword in the video title and avoid stuffing multiple keywords into one title.
  • If you need to rank for multiple keywords, create separate videos for each keyword rather than diluting one title.
  • Include your URL as text in the video and as the first link in the video description.
  • Add branding overlays to videos the same way TV shows display logos throughout a broadcast.
  • Ask the question your viewer is searching at the start of the video, then answer it, following Matt Cutts' approach.

Questions this episode answers

How should I write titles for YouTube videos to help with SEO?

Describe the video accurately and include your target keyword in the title. Avoid stuffing multiple keywords into one title because that causes keyword dilution. If you need to rank for several keywords, create separate videos, each targeting one keyword, so each title retains its value.

Should I include a URL or link in my YouTube video?

Yes. The hosts recommend including the URL as text inside the video itself and placing an HTML link as the first item in the video description, ideally pointing to the page where the video is embedded. This helps with indexing and gives viewers a clear destination.

How can Google Hangouts and YouTube be used together for video sharing?

YouTube now lets you start a Google Hangout directly from a video page using a share button beneath the video. This lets you bring contacts into a Hangout to watch the video together, with shared play and pause controls, rather than just sending them a link.

What are low-cost ways to create video content for a website or YouTube channel?

Beyond filming yourself, the hosts suggest screen capture software to record your monitor while narrating. Examples include demonstrating a website's navigation, explaining a bug to a programmer, or giving a tour of an e-commerce site with many categories, all without needing a large production setup.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword Dilution
When multiple keywords are packed into a single page or video title, reducing the ranking strength for any one of those keywords, as explained in the context of a client with four or five keywords in one title.
Sitelinks
Additional page links that appear beneath a main Google search result, taking up screen space and pulling from a site's structure, with snippets that can be improved by the site owner.
SIVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, a term used by the hosts to describe a companion podcast and framework focused on website analysis beyond standard SEO.
