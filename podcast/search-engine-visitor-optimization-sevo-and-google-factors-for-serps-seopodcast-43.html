Questions this episode answers

How many ranking signals does Google use and what are the most important ones?

Google uses over 200 ranking signals. The hosts highlight keyword use in the title tag, keyword use as the first word of the title tag, having keywords in the root domain name, and unique quality content as the most important. They sourced this from SEOmoz's Search Engine Ranking Factors Version 3.

What is SEVO and why does it matter for SEO?

SEVO stands for Search Engine Visitor Optimization. It means designing pages so that visitors who arrive via a specific keyword actually find that keyword and relevant content on the landing page. If a visitor searched for back pain and the page says nothing about back pain, they will bounce.

What is cloaking and why is it a negative ranking factor?

Cloaking means showing different content to Google's crawl robots than to human visitors. The SEOmoz article cited in the episode flags cloaking with malicious or manipulative intent as a negative ranking factor because it degrades the user experience Google is trying to protect.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
Designing a landing page so that visitors who arrived via a specific keyword find that keyword and relevant content on the page, reducing bounce rate.
Anchor text
The clickable text used in a hyperlink that points back to your website; controlling it to match target keywords adds SEO value.
Cloaking
Showing one version of page content to search engine robots and a different version to human users; flagged as a negative ranking factor when done with malicious or manipulative intent.
Title tag
An HTML element that names a webpage; placing target keywords in the title tag, especially as the first words, is one of the most important on-page ranking factors.
