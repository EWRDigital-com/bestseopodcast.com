Key takeaways
  • Use Google's keyword selector tool to discover synonym keywords you would never think of, like HVAC terms for a plumbing business.
  • A single obvious keyword may have far fewer searches than a slight variation, such as copier Houston versus copier repair Houston.
  • Regional businesses should combine service keywords with location terms to avoid wasting effort on national traffic.
  • The tool's trend data shows monthly search volume over 12 months, revealing seasonal spikes useful for adjusting campaigns.
  • Viewing advertiser bid prices inside the tool helps identify which keywords deliver the most bang for the buck.

About this episode

We cover Google's Keyword selector tool. There are many things to look at regarding this tool. We explain how you can use this tool to identify keywords that will be easy to get on the first page. We discuss all the ins and outs to turning your efforts using this tool into cash.

Questions this episode answers

What is Google's keyword selector tool and how do you access it?

It is Google's AdWords keyword tool, accessible by typing 'Google keyword usage' into Google search. It is free, and was originally designed to support PPC advertisers. You can enter descriptive words or phrases, or paste in a website URL, and it returns related keyword ideas with monthly search volume data.

How can the Google keyword tool help a local service business find better keywords?

By entering a broad term like 'find a plumber,' the tool surfaces synonyms such as plumbing contractor, mechanical contractor, and HVAC contractor. It also shows search volumes so a local business can prioritize high-value regional terms like 'toilet repair Houston' rather than broad national phrases.

How do you use the keyword tool to understand seasonal trends?

The tool shows traffic per month for the last 12 months for each keyword. For example, searching 'Roofers Houston' revealed a large spike in September and October after a hurricane, showing that campaigns should be adjusted by season or after local events.

Should a local plumber try to rank for a broad term like 'find a plumber'?

According to the hosts, a typical regional plumber should not chase broad terms like 'find a plumber' because the roughly 2,900 monthly searches come from across the country, meaning most visitors are outside the plumber's service area and the effort produces poor return on investment.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword selector tool
Google's free AdWords tool that generates keyword ideas, monthly search volumes, synonym suggestions, advertiser bid prices, and 12-month trend data for any entered phrase or website URL.
CSV format
A comma delimited file format that the Google keyword tool uses for downloading keyword lists, described as something Excel is comfortable working with.
CAPTCHA
A character recognition challenge on the Google keyword tool that prevents automated programs or bots from repeatedly scraping keyword data from the page.
ROI (Return on Investment)
The core metric the hosts use to decide which keywords to pursue, weighing the effort of SEO or PPC spend against the traffic and revenue value a keyword is likely to generate.
