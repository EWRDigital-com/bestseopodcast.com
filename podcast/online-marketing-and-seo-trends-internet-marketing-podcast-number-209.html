Questions this episode answers

What is Google Hummingbird and how does it affect SEO?

Google Hummingbird is an algorithm update designed to return results that are faster, more precise, and more fine-tuned. It especially helps longer-tail searches by surfacing pages more relevant to a full search query, and it benefits voice search because spoken queries tend to be longer phrases.

What does 100% not provided mean for SEO and keyword tracking?

Google moved to 100% not-provided keyword data, meaning organic keyword referral data is hidden from analytics. This makes it extremely difficult to determine which keywords drive traffic or sales. The hosts suggest leaning on Webmaster Tools and paid keyword data tools to compensate.

Is it legal to write fake reviews for your own business?

No. The episode cites fines of $75,000 and $43,000 issued to two companies for writing fake reviews, which is considered deceptive trade. The hosts call the practice astroturfing and say it is simply not the right approach regardless of legality.

Terms defined in this episode
Astroturfing
Creating bogus or fake reviews for your own business online, named after fake grass. Two companies were fined $75,000 and $43,000 respectively for this practice, which is considered deceptive trade.
Not Provided
A Google change that hides organic keyword referral data from analytics, making it impossible to see which search keywords are driving traffic or sales. The hosts describe this as 100% not provided.
Google Hummingbird
A Google algorithm update designed to return faster, more precise results, with particular benefit for long-tail searches and voice search queries that tend to be six or seven words or longer.
Google Authorship
A setup that lets content creators claim their original content so Google associates it with them, displays their avatar in search results, and can increase click-through rates.
