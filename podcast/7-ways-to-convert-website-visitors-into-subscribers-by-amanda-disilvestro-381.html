7 Ways to Convert Website Visitors into Subscribers by Amanda DiSilvestro #381

Ep. 38134 min2017-07-11

To convert website visitors into subscribers, create quality content matching search intent, use the right CTAs, design appealing sign-up forms, use lightboxes, and analyze your content performance consistently.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Quality content must be optimized with the right phrases, CTAs, images, and structure to match searcher intent.
  • A phone number alone is not a CTA. Give people a reason to call, like 'call for a free consultation'.
  • Exit intent lightbox pop-ups draw attention and can increase email subscriptions when paired with a clear offer.
  • Advertise your subscription by solving real problems in content, then gate additional tips behind sign-up.
  • Analyze analytics to learn which content drives conversions, then rinse and repeat what works best.
Getting 100,000 visits to your website can feel awesome, but if your conversion rate is low, all that traffic won't do you much good.

About this episode

You get traffic, but are you converting that traffic into subscribers? Chris and Charles cover 7 pieces to the subscriber puzzle in this week's SEO Podcast 381.

Questions this episode answers

How can I increase email subscribers on my website?

The episode recommends seven tactics: create quality content matching search intent, use the right CTAs tied to your sales process, design visually appealing sign-up forms, vary content types based on your resources, use lightboxes and exit intent pop-ups, advertise your subscription by solving problems, and analyze your content performance in analytics.

What makes a good call to action for subscriptions?

According to the episode, a CTA must give people a reason to take action. A phone number alone is not a CTA. It should prompt a specific behavior like 'call for a free consultation' or 'sign up for free shipping,' matching the site's sales process and the visitor's expectations.

Do exit intent pop-ups actually work for getting subscribers?

The episode says exit intent lightbox pop-ups work really well. They trigger when a visitor's mouse moves toward the close button, display a focused offer like a free guide or discount, and draw the visitor's attention even if they are looking for the close button.

Why should I analyze my content after publishing it?

Analytics reveal which content drives conversions, what traffic sources and devices visitors use, and how long they stay on a page. Understanding this lets you identify what worked, model future content after successful pieces, and avoid repeating tactics that did not drive engagement or subscriptions.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An e-web branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows across social platforms.
Exit intent pop-up
A lightbox that triggers when a visitor's mouse moves toward the browser close button, displaying a subscription or offer before the visitor leaves the site.
Lightbox
A feature that darkens the screen and displays a focused pop-up, used to highlight CTAs or subscription forms and capture visitor attention.
Soft lead catcher
A low-commitment conversion tool, such as an email sign-up form, that lets visitors get information without fully committing to a purchase or consultation.
