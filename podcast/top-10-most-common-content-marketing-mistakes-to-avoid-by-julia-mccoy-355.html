Key takeaways
  • Maintain a consistent blog, even starting at just once a month, to generate fresh site content and repurpose into social posts.
  • The inbox is still the most visited place on the internet, so email every new blog post to your subscriber list.
  • Always include links back to your website when posting on social media, or visitors cannot reach your content.
  • Quality content beats quantity, thin or low-quality posts risk algorithmic penalties like Panda.
  • The average content marketer uses 13 tactics for creation and distribution, so repurpose content across multiple channels.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common content marketing mistakes to avoid in 2017?

According to Julia McCoy via Search Engine Journal, the top 10 mistakes are: no consistent blog, ignoring email marketing, misusing social media, failing to proofread, not focusing on SEO, choosing quantity over quality, neglecting relevant content, using only one tactic, having no content goals, and not optimizing for mobile.

What does it mean to use only one tactic in content marketing, and why is it a mistake?

Relying on a single channel or format, such as only posting videos on YouTube, limits both reach and creative power. Repurposing one piece of content into videos, blog posts, transcriptions, and newsletters across multiple platforms maximizes the resources already invested in creating it.
