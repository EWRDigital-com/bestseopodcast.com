Penguin and Google Image Search - #seopodcast 148

Episode24 min2012-06-19

Google's March algorithm updates targeted anchor text over-optimization, improved image search quality, began indexing keyboard symbols like hashtags, and expanded the freshness update to all queries beyond news traffic.

Key takeaways
  • Use varied anchor text with long-tail phrases instead of repeating exact-match keywords, as Google reduced value for over-optimized anchor text.
  • Google's image search update now surfaces high-quality images even from lower-quality pages, rewarding good image sizing and strong landing pages.
  • Google began indexing symbols including hashtags, dollar signs, and the at symbol, making symbol-based branding more searchable.
  • The freshness algorithm update, previously limited to news queries, was rolled out to all search queries in March.
  • Include outbound links on your pages because Google values pages that do not dead-end and provide good user research experiences.

Questions this episode answers

What did Google's Penguin update target and how can it hurt a website?

The Penguin update targeted spam, including spammy inbound links and spammy on-site practices. Sites with a lot of spam links or spammy content could see their rankings drop as a result of the update, described in the episode as getting penguinized.

Terms defined in this episode
Anchor text
The clickable text inside a hyperlink. In HTML, the A tag stands for anchor, and the words inside that tag linking to another page are the anchor text.
Penguin update
A Google algorithm update that targeted spam, including sites with spammy inbound links or spammy on-site content, penalizing their search rankings.
Freshness update
A Google algorithm update designed to display the latest and most recent information for a given search query, initially applied to news traffic and later expanded to all queries.
Over-optimization
A practice where a site uses the same exact-match keyword phrases repeatedly in anchor text and links, which Google's updates have moved to penalize or devalue.
