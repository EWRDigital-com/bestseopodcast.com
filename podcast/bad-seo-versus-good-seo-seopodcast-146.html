Bad SEO versus Good SEO - #seopodcast 146

Episode28 min2012-06-05

For ecommerce and service businesses, doing SEO and PPC together is the best approach, since SEO builds brand visibility over time while PPC delivers fast, controllable traffic immediately.

Key takeaways
  • Do both SEO and PPC simultaneously when budget allows, starting small if needed.
  • Ecommerce sites face duplicate content challenges, but Google Shopping is a quick win for product visibility.
  • Wrong keyword targeting wastes months of SEO effort and can bring the wrong customers entirely.
  • PPC is the fastest way to lose money if campaigns are not properly managed and monitored.
  • Retargeting in paid search is highly effective but must be set up correctly to avoid burning budget.

Questions this episode answers

Should I do SEO or PPC for my website?

The hosts recommend doing both at the same time if budget allows. SEO builds brand strength and visibility but takes two to eight months depending on competition. PPC can go live within days and generate immediate traffic, making them complementary rather than competing strategies.

What are the biggest risks of running a PPC campaign?

Badly managed PPC is the fastest way to lose money. One client in the episode went from spending 400 dollars a day to 5,000 a day over a weekend by accidentally enabling the display network and raising his budget, resulting in roughly 10,000 dollars spent before anyone caught the error.

What is negative SEO?

Negative SEO is the practice of doing harmful things to a competitor's site in order to reduce their rankings so your own ranking increases. The episode references Dan Thies, who publicly commented on the practice and subsequently lost his own rankings, reportedly due to bad links on his site.

Terms defined in this episode
Negative SEO
The practice of doing harmful things to a competitor's site to reduce their ranking so your own ranking will increase.
Retargeting
A paid search feature that allows advertisers to show ads to people who previously visited their site, displayed on other sites, down to a granular level such as visitors to a specific page.
Display Network
A Google AdWords option that shows ads across a broad network of sites, which can rapidly increase spend if enabled without proper controls.
Google Shopping Network
A Google feature where product listings can be submitted, described in the episode as the quickest way to get ecommerce products visible online.
