7 Reasons Why Content Needs Amazing Images, Videos & Visuals Ep. 572

Ep. 57229 min2023-05-083,584 plays

Visual content is essential for SEO and engagement because visuals are processed 600,000 times faster than text, drive 94% more views, and made up 82% of all global web traffic in 2022.

Key takeaways
  • Web content with visuals receives up to 94% more views than content without them.
  • In 2022, video traffic made up 82% of all global web traffic on earth.
  • Adding visuals to a presentation improves recall by 65% several days later and 80% within a few hours.
  • All digital assets a company owns should be branded before publication.
  • Content can be repurposed across formats: blog posts, infographics, podcasts, shorts, and collages.

About this episode

Learn why you should use amazing images, videos, and visuals to help convey ideas and captivate audiences with high-quality online content.

Questions this episode answers

Why do websites need images and videos for SEO?

According to the episode, web content containing visuals receives up to 94% more views, visuals are processed 600,000 times faster than text, and in 2022 video made up 82% of all global web traffic. Visuals also improve recall and help capture short attention spans.

How much does adding visuals improve content recall?

The episode states that adding visuals to a presentation improves a human's ability to recall it by 65% several days later, and by 80% within just a few hours. Most humans retain visual information better and longer than written words alone.

What is image SEO and why does it still matter?

Image SEO means properly labeling and organizing images so search algorithms understand what they depict. The hosts note that Google, as explained by John Mueller, cannot read images as well as text, so labeling images correctly and providing clear contextual signals remains important.

How can marketers repurpose content using rich media?

The episode recommends taking survey data or articles and turning them into blog posts, pie charts, infographics, animated videos, or podcasts. Those podcasts can then become shorts, clips, and collages, which can be turned back into blog posts to maximize content value.

Terms defined in this episode
Image SEO
The practice of labeling and organizing images with proper signals so that search engine algorithms can understand what an image depicts, since algorithms cannot read images as well as text.
Rich media
Content types beyond plain text, including images, videos, infographics, and animations, that improve engagement, recall, and organic visibility.
Content repurposing
Reworking existing content into different formats, such as turning a blog post into an infographic, podcast, short video, or collage, to extend its reach and usefulness.
