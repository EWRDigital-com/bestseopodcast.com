Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between a business coach and a business consultant?

According to Paul Lemberg, a coach tells you what to do, holds you accountable, and stays involved long term, sometimes up to five years. A consultant may tell you what to do or do the work themselves but does not leave you with a go-forward strategy and typically does not maintain ongoing accountability.

How does Paul Lemberg guarantee business results?

Lemberg structures his client agreements so that if a client completes 85 percent of the agreed actions and still does not get the promised results, he takes responsibility because he gave the wrong advice. If the client does the work, he guarantees the results will follow.

What types of businesses does Paul Lemberg prefer to coach?

Lemberg says his favorite clients are e-commerce companies, especially supplements companies, and software or SaaS businesses. He also works well with healthcare businesses such as dentists and chiropractors. He avoids lawyers, real estate agents, insurance companies, and other agency-based businesses.

What marketing tactics does Paul Lemberg recommend for coaches or consultants who only need a few clients?

Lemberg recommends lumpy mail, such as sending a physical package or rolled-up dollar bills with a handwritten card, combined with a follow-up phone call. He says this approach can achieve a 100 percent response rate and works well when targeting a small dream list of high-value prospects.

Terms defined in this episode
Lumpy mail
A direct mail strategy that involves sending a physical package or unusual item, such as rolled-up dollar bills, to stand out and prompt a response from a targeted prospect.
Dream 100
A concept Chet Holmes used to frame a market by identifying a specific list of ideal target prospects, defined however the business chooses, rather than by broad demographic criteria.
Sales Voodoo
A Paul Lemberg program designed for companies selling products or services above $5,000 that want to build a direct sales team to reach prospects who are not responding to marketing campaigns.
