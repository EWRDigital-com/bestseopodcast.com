Questions this episode answers

What is Quantcast and when should a website owner use it?

Quantcast attaches code to your site and provides detailed demographic data about visitors including age, income, race, and whether they have children. It is most valuable when your site is in the top million for traffic and when advertisers or marketers want to evaluate your site for a large display ad buy.

Why is Alexa rank considered unreliable for measuring website traffic?

Alexa only tracks users who have its toolbar installed, which skews results toward a narrow tech-savvy audience. This means most ordinary visitors to any site go uncounted, making Alexa scores an inaccurate representation of true traffic for the general web.

What is Google PageRank and why does the podcast recommend paying attention to it?

Google PageRank is a per-page score from zero to ten that reflects how important Google considers that page, based on the likelihood of a random internet user landing on it via random links. The hosts recommend it over Alexa or Quantcast for day-to-day SEO focus.

What happened in the Google versus Rosetta Stone trademark lawsuit over paid search ads?

Rosetta Stone sued Google over the practice of selling trademarked brand names as pay-per-click ad keywords to competitors. The court dismissed the case without even hearing it, allowing Google to continue selling trademark terms like Rosetta Stone to competing advertisers.

Terms defined in this episode
Quantcast
A free analytics service that provides detailed demographic data about website visitors, including age, income, and family status, used primarily by marketers evaluating sites for large ad buys.
Google PageRank
A Google scoring system from zero to ten applied to individual web pages, representing the likelihood of a random internet user landing on that page by following random links.
Alexa Rank
A per-website score assigned by Amazon-owned Alexa that measures relative traffic, but only among users who have the Alexa toolbar installed, which skews its accuracy.
Revenue Metrics
The specific analytics measurements a business uses to track how website activity translates into income or leads, considered the most important numbers to monitor.
