Questions this episode answers

How does eWeb Results approach SEO strategy for new clients?

They dedicate the first month to strategy and research, then potentially add a full month of technical SEO before producing deliverables. The goal is to restructure the site fundamentally so it ranks faster, rather than applying quick fixes to a poorly built foundation.

Why should businesses target long-tail keywords instead of high-volume core keywords?

High-volume core keywords are extremely competitive and take unrealistic time to rank for. Targeting long-tail phrases with 30 to 40 monthly searches builds domain authority and generates early leads, eventually surrounding and supporting the core keyword you ultimately want to rank for.

Is conversion optimization part of SEO services?

According to the hosts, conversion optimization is a fundamentally different discipline and skill set from SEO. Sites can have strong traffic and still fail to convert, so addressing on-page analytics and conversion issues is something they believe should be charged for separately.

How does eWeb Results define what it will cost to reach first position in Google?

They are building a dashboard that uses different APIs and metrics to provide a range estimate. Rather than giving a flat number, the approach ties required activity, like content creation and competitive link analysis, to the number so clients understand what it will take relative to competitors.

Terms defined in this episode
Long-tail key phrases
Specific, lower-volume search queries, around 30 to 40 monthly searches, that are less competitive and carry targeted intent, used to build authority before going after high-volume core keywords.
Technical SEO
A foundational phase of SEO work focused on restructuring a website, including taxonomy and internal linking, to make it more capable of ranking quickly rather than applying surface-level fixes.
Conversion optimization
A discipline separate from SEO focused on ensuring that traffic arriving at a website actually converts into leads or sales, involving on-page analytics and user behavior analysis.
Domain authority
A metric used alongside total backlinks and domain age to assess how competitive a site is and how hard it will be to outrank it for a given keyword.
