Questions this episode answers

Is PPC advertising worth it for law firms?

PPC can be very expensive in the legal industry, with high cost-per-click rates. It works best for urgent needs like DWI cases. Firms should pair it with CallRail tracking, a high-converting landing page, and remarketing where allowed, rather than treating it as a standalone solution.

How can law firms collect more client reviews?

The hosts recommend a service called pro testimonial, which lets clients record a video review from their phone without downloading an app. The software adds an intro, music, and a call to action automatically, making it easier to collect positive video reviews at scale.

Terms defined in this episode
Same As schema markup
A schema markup tag used to link a brand's various online profiles so Google recognizes them as the same entity and measures them together.
EAT (Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)
Google quality rater guidelines criteria, referenced in the episode as the framework law firms should follow when building their online brand and content.
Conversion Rate Optimization (CRO)
The process of improving a website so a higher percentage of visitors take a desired action, described as essential before investing heavily in PPC ads.
Web 2.0 directories
Third-party listing and review sites such as Yelp, Trustpilot, Avvo, and similar platforms that aggregate leads and sell them, but can also be evaluated for traffic value and used as a cost-effective alternative to direct PPC.
