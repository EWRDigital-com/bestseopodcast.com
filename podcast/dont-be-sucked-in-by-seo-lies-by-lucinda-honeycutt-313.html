Questions this episode answers

Can an SEO agency guarantee a number one Google ranking?

No. As discussed in this episode, a 2013 study showed 91.5% of traffic goes to page one, making top rankings highly competitive. No SEO company can control search rankings, and any agency that guarantees number one placement should be avoided immediately.

How long does SEO actually take to show results?

According to this episode, SEO can take anywhere from three, six, nine, twelve months or longer, depending on the industry, site age, domain age, and competition level. A brand new site in a competitive market like legal or HVAC services in a major city should expect an extended timeline.

Should a local business pursue national backlinks and outreach?

The hosts argue yes, when the opportunity is available and the resource cost is low. A relevant backlink from a major national site like HGTV can improve SEO authority and increase consumer trust, even for a small local kitchen and bath design company.

Terms defined in this episode
Double opt-in
A subscription confirmation method where users verify their sign-up, resulting in a significantly higher email open rate because subscribers are more genuinely interested.
PLA (Product Listing Ads)
Paid product ads Google features in search results, increasingly prominent after Google removed right-hand side paid ads from desktop search.
Right to be forgotten
A policy originating in the UK where Google removes certain irrelevant or outdated links from search results, now geolocated so users in the UK cannot bypass it by going to google.com.
Over-optimization penalty
An algorithm penalty Google can apply when a site has tried too many SEO tactics aggressively, potentially harming rankings rather than improving them.
