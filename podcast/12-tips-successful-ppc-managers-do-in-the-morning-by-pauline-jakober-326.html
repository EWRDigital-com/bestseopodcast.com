Key takeaways
  • Add negative keywords daily to avoid paying for irrelevant clicks, especially on large budgets.
  • Exclude irrelevant Google Display Network sites and add managed placements where your audience actually browses.
  • Check ad spend daily to catch overspending, underspending, or expired payment methods before they waste budget.
  • Track the threshold of pain by digging into analytics before pausing campaigns that may assist other conversions.
  • Newly launched campaigns need daily review for the first few days to catch missed settings and add negatives.

Questions this episode answers

What should PPC managers check every morning?

According to the article covered in this episode, managers should add negative keywords, exclude irrelevant display network sites, check ad spend, track leads, review top spenders, get a bird's eye view of the account, verify ad positions, track the threshold of pain, follow PPC channel performance, read AdWords alerts, identify spend fluctuations, and watch newly launched campaigns.

How often should smaller budget PPC accounts be reviewed?

The hosts note the article is geared toward high-level, larger budget accounts. For clients spending under two or three thousand dollars a month, they suggest these 12 tasks may not all be needed daily but should be done at least weekly if daily review is not possible.

What is the threshold of pain in PPC management?

The threshold of pain is the point where you are nearing the edge of comfortable spending on a campaign, ad group, or keyword. Before pausing, the recommendation is to dig into Google Analytics to check for assisted conversions, since pausing could cut off part of a conversion funnel.

Terms defined in this episode
Negative keywords
Keywords you exclude from a PPC campaign so your ads do not show for irrelevant searches, preventing wasted spend on clicks that are not relevant to your business.
Google Display Network (GDN)
Google's network of sites and apps where display and remarketing ads can appear. Irrelevant placements, including mobile apps, can drain budget through accidental clicks.
Threshold of pain
The point in a PPC campaign where spend on a keyword or ad group approaches the limit of acceptable investment without clear returns, triggering a deeper analytics review before pausing.
C-cubed (Conversion, Conversion, Conversion)
A framework mentioned by the hosts tracking three conversion stages: clicking an ad, completing an on-site action like a form fill, and ultimately becoming a paying client.
