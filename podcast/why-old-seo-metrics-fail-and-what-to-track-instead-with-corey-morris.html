Why Old SEO Metrics Fail And What To Track Instead With Corey Morris

Episode55 min2026-02-15Guest: Corey Morris

Key moments
Key takeaways
  • Anchor reporting to revenue or profit first, then work backwards to connect search activity to those numbers.
  • Modern SEO strategy, not 2020-era tactics, still influences LLM visibility, so updating existing content and practices matters.
  • GA4 requires custom configuration to track LLM-driven traffic; it does not do this accurately out of the box.
  • Brand differentiation and clear entity definition are now table stakes for both search engines and AI systems.
  • Clients care about forward-looking indicators and projections, not historical dashboards they never open.

Questions this episode answers

What KPIs should SEO agencies use instead of keyword rankings?

Corey Morris recommends starting with revenue or profitability as the anchor metric, then connecting backwards through leads, lead quality, and customer lifetime value. For B2B clients, validating clean CRM data tied to search-driven leads is the priority over traditional rank-tracking metrics.

Does traditional SEO still work for LLM visibility in 2026?

According to Corey Morris, modern up-to-date SEO still influences LLMs. He estimates about 80 percent of a current SEO strategy applies to LLM visibility, but SEO practices from 2022 or earlier do not carry over the same way to AI search systems.

How should agencies track traffic coming from AI tools like ChatGPT or Grok?

Voltage built custom configurations inside GA4 and Looker dashboards to categorize and attribute LLM-sourced traffic. Corey notes GA4 does not track this out of the box, and his team published a step-by-step article with 15 screenshots covering the setup process.

What is the biggest mistake brands make with digital marketing budgets?

Corey Morris says brands often spend heavily on paid search or SEO before fixing foundational gaps in messaging, product development, or attribution. One prospect his team audited thought they were 20 percent profitable on paid leads but were actually 20 percent negative due to attribution problems.

Terms defined in this episode
LLM visibility
The degree to which a brand or piece of content is surfaced or referenced by large language model systems such as ChatGPT or Perplexity, distinct from traditional search engine rankings.
AI influence without attribution
When an AI system applies a brand's framework or ideas in its responses without citing or crediting the original source, making that contribution invisible to standard analytics.
GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)
A term used in the industry to describe optimization strategies aimed specifically at large language models and generative AI search, debated as distinct from or overlapping with traditional SEO.
START planning process
Corey Morris's five-step digital marketing planning acronym covered in his book The Digital Marketing Success Plan, used as a six-week to two-month foundational strategy process before execution begins.
