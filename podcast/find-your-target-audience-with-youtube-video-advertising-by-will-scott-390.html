Find your target audience with YouTube video advertising by Will Scott #390

Ep. 39035 min2017-10-09

YouTube video advertising helps businesses find target audiences because it reaches more 18-to-49-year-olds than any U.S. cable network, offers granular targeting, and delivers detailed analytics for testing and measuring ad performance.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • YouTube reaches more 18-to-49-year-olds than any cable network in the U.S., making it a major platform for advertisers.
  • Affinity audiences let advertisers target by interests and can multiply an initial audience by 2x or 3x.
  • Older demographics use YouTube mainly to solve problems via how-to searches, while younger users consume content passively.
  • If viewers skip your YouTube ad, you do not pay, so skippable ads reward relevant, well-targeted creative.
  • Repurpose videos across platforms but customize duration, pacing, and dimensions rather than cross-posting identical content.
YouTube videos offer more analytics than any other video platform in history.

About this episode

Advertising on YouTube is a cost-effective way to reach potential customers for nearly any product or business. YouTube users consume more than a billion hours of video content per day on the site. Within that large volume of traffic, advertisers are able to target specific demographics and regions. Tune in to this episode to find out why so many businesses are turning to YouTube for online marketing.

Questions this episode answers

Why should businesses use YouTube video advertising to find their target audience?

YouTube has a billion users, reaches more 18-to-49-year-olds than any U.S. cable network, and lets advertisers target by region, demographics, affinity audiences, specific channels, or even single videos. In 2017, 20% of all U.S. video ad revenues were projected to go to YouTube.

How does YouTube's skippable ad format benefit advertisers?

Viewers can skip ads after the first few seconds, and advertisers only pay when viewers watch through. Millennial viewers skip ads nearly 60% of the time, so skippable ads filter out uninterested viewers and make advertisers focus on holding the attention of actual prospects.

How do older and younger audiences differ in how they use YouTube?

Younger audiences return regularly to follow shows and personalities and consume content for entertainment. Older audiences are more likely to arrive via Google search looking for how-to information or to solve a specific problem, often reaching YouTube through embedded videos or search results.

Terms defined in this episode
Affinity audiences
A targeting option that groups users by interests, such as auto-enthusiasts or do-it-yourselfers, usable on YouTube, Facebook, and Google to reach people similar to an existing customer or remarketing list.
Discovery ads
Paid YouTube search ads, as described by Dane Golden, used to promote how-to videos to users actively searching for information on the platform.
Consumer journey
The path a potential customer travels from unawareness through attention, interest, decision, and action, referenced in the episode using the AIDA framework.
Long tail searchability
The ability of YouTube videos to keep being found through search over time, which Dane Golden contrasts with feed-based platforms like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, and LinkedIn where videos do not persist as long.
