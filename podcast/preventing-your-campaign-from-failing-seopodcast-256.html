Preventing Your Campaign From Failing - #SEOpodcast 256

Episode30 min2014-12-05

An SEO campaign fails when it lacks realistic goals, proper keyword research, a professional website, relevant content, and data tracking, according to seven reasons outlined from a Search Engine Watch article.

Key takeaways
  • Set realistic SEO goals and start with one top product or service, then rinse and repeat if it works.
  • Service providers like plumbers may see results in three to five months, while lawyers in major cities may wait 12 months.
  • Choosing keywords based on what you think customers search, without verifying real data, leads to wrong traffic.
  • A high bounce rate is not always bad, especially when paired with a strong conversion rate.
  • Tracking analytics is half the job. Know traffic sources, exit pages, and the conversion path to find where to improve.

Questions this episode answers

How long does SEO take to show results?

According to this episode, service providers like plumbers or roofers may see results in roughly three to five months with aggressive SEO. Professionals in competitive fields like personal injury law or accounting in major cities should expect eight to twelve months of consistent blogging, content, and social activity before ranking on the first page of Google.

How do you know if your SEO campaign is bringing the right traffic?

The episode recommends looking at bounce rate from organic search traffic as a quick test, but warns to weigh it against conversion rate and industry context. Also check exit pages in analytics to see where visitors leave, and trace the full conversion path to spot where people drop off.

What makes a good SEO content strategy?

The episode says content should be original, relevant to your industry and customers, and timely. It should answer questions and address concerns prospective customers typically have. Content that speaks to the pain of the customer and explains features, advantages, and benefits is most likely to engage visitors and turn them into paying customers.

What are the most important steps for image optimization in SEO?

The episode lists four steps: resize the image to the best clarity at the smallest file size, rename the file using a target keyword, add an alt tag, and add a title tag. Doing at least these four things is described as pretty good image optimization.

Terms defined in this episode
Bounce Rate
The rate at which visitors leave a site after viewing only one page. The episode notes a high bounce rate is not always bad and must be weighed against conversion rate and industry type.
NFC (Near Field Communication)
A short-range wireless technology used in tools like Google Wallet, Apple Pay, and toll road easy tags, described in the episode as a growing technology included in smart gear.
Organic Search Traffic
Natural traffic arriving from search engines like Google or Bing, as opposed to paid traffic. The episode uses it as the basis for evaluating bounce rate and campaign quality.
Robots.txt
A file that controls which parts of a site search engine crawlers can access. The episode notes that blocking JavaScript or CSS in this file can harm how Google renders and indexes content.
