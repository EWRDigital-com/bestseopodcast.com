Questions this episode answers

What is a drip email campaign and how does it work?

A drip campaign is an automated sequence of emails triggered by actions like a form submission, a download, or a click. It sends pre-written touchpoints over time, for example introducing team members after a networking meeting, so you stay top of mind without manual follow-up every time.

How many email touchpoints does it take to close a sale?

According to the episode, the average number of touchpoints needed to close a deal is seven to eight, and it can go as high as eleven. Yet 90% of salespeople stop after the third follow-up, which is the gap automation is designed to fill.

What types of drip campaigns should a business set up first?

The episode recommends four core drips: a sales networking drip after meeting someone, a post-presentation drip to push prospects to the next step, a nurture drip for longer sales cycles, and a new-customer induction drip to prevent buyer's remorse and set expectations.

How often should you send emails in a nurture sequence?

The hosts suggest matching frequency to how closely you want the recipient to know you. They recommend never going beyond once a month for any contact, and note that the data shows more frequent communication produces more engaged, raving-fan customers.

Terms defined in this episode
Drip Email Campaign
An automated sequence of pre-written emails sent to a contact over time, triggered by actions like form submissions or downloads, designed to nurture prospects through the sales process.
Remarketing / Retargeting
Showing ads to people who have already visited your website across platforms like LinkedIn, Facebook, Twitter, and Google sites to bring them back and move them toward a conversion.
DISC Profile
A four-quadrant personality framework where D is decisive, I is relationship and excitement focused, S is steady and long-term relationship focused, and C is detail and data oriented, used to tailor communication style.
Lead Nurture Sequence
A longer-term drip campaign for prospects who have not yet converted, keeping the business top of mind typically on a monthly basis until the prospect is ready to buy.
