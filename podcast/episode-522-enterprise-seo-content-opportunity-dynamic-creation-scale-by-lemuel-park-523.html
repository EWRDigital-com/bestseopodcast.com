Questions this episode answers

What is EAT and how should it be implemented in enterprise SEO content?

EAT stands for expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. Google evaluates the expertise of the content creator, the authority of the content and domain via links and citations, and trustworthiness via sources like the BBB. Credentials should go on a dedicated author bio page that individual content pages link to, not crammed into each article.

How important is page speed for SEO rankings?

Page speed carries significant weight in Google rankings. According to data cited in the episode, if a page load time increases from one to three seconds, bounce rates increase by 32%. Managed hosting and proper server-side setup are recommended as foundational investments, especially for those on a budget.

Why does enterprise SEO require intelligent automation?

Enterprise sites can have hundreds of thousands of pages and potentially millions of products. Automation handles data extraction, analysis, and auditing at scale, freeing human teams to focus on strategy and high-impact decisions. Tools like BrightEdge provide prioritized recommendations and can identify technical issues automatically.

What does a multi-channel SEO content strategy involve at the enterprise level?

It requires understanding exactly who you are reaching on each channel, what need you are solving, and where that user is in the customer journey. Each piece of content must be optimized so its purpose is clear to the search engine, and cross-channel data unified in one reporting tool helps teams see the full picture.

Terms defined in this episode
EAT
Expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. Google uses these factors to evaluate content quality, looking at the content creator's credentials, domain authority via links and citations, and brand trustworthiness via sources such as the BBB.
BrightEdge
An enterprise-level SEO tool that consolidates keyword data, multi-location ranking analysis, and AI-driven recommendations into one platform, described in the episode as accurate and useful for grouping related search terms.
Intelligent Automation
The use of AI and machine learning within SEO tools to handle data extraction, site auditing, and prioritized recommendations at scale, allowing human teams to focus on strategy.
Omni-channel
A marketing approach where content is coordinated across multiple channels, recognizing that customers may be at different stages of the sales funnel depending on which channel they use.
