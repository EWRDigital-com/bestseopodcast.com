Key takeaways
  • Spamming followers is the top social media mistake and will get accounts blocked, removed, and flagged.
  • Not promoting your social media accounts, such as missing Facebook or Twitter links on your website, limits growth.
  • Giving audiences a clear reason to follow or check in, like discounts or rewards, drives real engagement.
  • About 60% of social media posts contain a link, and posts without links have less value to followers.
  • Google Plus One had just rolled out on websites, but the hosts doubted it would catch on due to lack of platform.

Questions this episode answers

What are the top social media mistakes beginners make?

According to this episode, the four main mistakes are spamming followers, not updating frequently enough, failing to promote your social media accounts on your website and offline materials, and using social media only to promote yourself without giving followers a reason to engage.

How should businesses give people a reason to follow or check in on social media?

The hosts recommend offering tangible incentives such as check-in discounts or free items after repeat visits. The example given is offering a free DVD to church members who check in three consecutive times, mirroring loyalty strategies used by businesses like Subway.

Is Google Plus One worth investing in for SEO?

The hosts were skeptical. One host retracted a previous statement that it would take a while to catch on and said it would not catch on at all, citing Google's failure at previous social products like Buzz and Wave, and its lack of marketing push for new features.

Why is including links important in social media posts?

The episode states that about 60% of social media posts contain a link, and the hosts say links are what drive clicks as they scroll through Twitter and Facebook feeds. Posts without links, like status updates about mundane activities, offer little value to followers.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Plus One
A social endorsement button Google rolled out on websites during this episode, allowing users to plus one pages directly rather than only from search results.
MCC (My Client Center)
A Google tool that lets agencies manage multiple pay-per-click accounts in one place.
TFB (Team Follow Back)
A group on Twitter where users tag their profiles with TFB to signal they will automatically follow anyone who follows them, used to inflate follower counts.
Google Places
Google's local business listing product, which at the time pulled reviews from sources like Yelp and CitySearch and required PIN verification by postcard.
