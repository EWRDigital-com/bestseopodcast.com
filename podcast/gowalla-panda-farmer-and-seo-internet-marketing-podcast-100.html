GoWalla, Panda Farmer and SEO - Internet Marketing Podcast 100

Episode40 min2011-04-22

Google's Farmer Panda update targets content farms and scraped content, so SEOs should focus on quality user experience, vary article lengths, monitor crawl frequency, and avoid article marketing for link building.

Key takeaways
  • Vary article word counts to avoid resembling content farms, where uniform lengths like 250 to 255 words signal automated low-quality content.
  • Monitor how often Googlebot crawls your site. Infrequent crawls signal Google finds your content irrelevant to the average user.
  • Article marketing is outdated. Instead, write guest articles on credible sites to earn quality backlinks.
  • Cloaking is always bad. Showing different content to Googlebot versus users, for any reason, can get your site banned.
  • Matt Cutts advises to not chase the algorithm but make the algorithm chase you by consistently providing a good user experience.

Questions this episode answers

What did Google's Farmer Panda update target?

The Farmer Panda update targeted low-quality and scraped content, including article farms where every article had nearly identical word counts around 250 to 260 words. The update was entirely algorithmic with no manual changes, and Matt Cutts noted the searcher feedback was overwhelmingly positive.

What is white hat cloaking and is it allowed by Google?

White hat cloaking is an oxymoron according to Matt Cutts. Any code that special cases content for the Googlebot by user agent or IP address is considered cloaking and Google may take action against the site. Showing different content by geographic location, however, is not considered cloaking.

How does crawl frequency relate to content quality?

According to this episode, if Google crawls your site infrequently, it likely means Google has determined your content is not valuable or useful to its users. The recommendation is to re-evaluate your content and ensure you are providing a genuinely useful experience, not just to wait for the next crawl.

Terms defined in this episode
Content Farm
A site producing large volumes of low-quality articles, often with uniform word counts around 250 to 260 words, targeted by the Farmer Panda algorithm update.
Cloaking
Showing different content to the Googlebot than to regular users, identified by user agent or IP address. Google considers this a violation and may ban the site.
Link Baiting
A white hat technique of creating compelling content such as lists, graphs, or videos that people naturally want to link to.
Scraped Content
Content copied or republished from another source, including your own content hosted elsewhere, which Google's algorithm identifies and may penalize.
