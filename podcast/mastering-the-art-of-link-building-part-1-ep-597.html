Key takeaways
  • Most small businesses do not need off-page link building; on-page SEO should come first.
  • Spammy or PBN-generated links create a temporary traffic bump followed by a significant drop and possible penalties.
  • A good link should be niche-specific or from a high-quality news site, with a clear about-us page and contact information.
  • Buying links from sites that also sell links to others puts you in a bad neighborhood that Google can detect.
  • Social media profiles, citations, and email newsletters are lower-risk off-page signals to pursue before paid link services.

Questions this episode answers

Do small businesses need to buy links to rank on Google?

According to Matt Bertram, most small businesses do not need off-page link building at all. A properly optimized on-page foundation, combined with social media profiles, citations, and content syndication, can generate enough link equity to reach the first page even in competitive industries.

What are the risks of using cheap or managed link building services?

Cheap services often run spammy links or build private blog networks, creating a temporary ranking bump followed by a large drop. Google's algorithm never forgets links, so you may later need to disavow them. One client described in the episode spent around $200,000 and retained no value after leaving the agency.

How do you evaluate whether a link is worth having?

Matt Bertram recommends checking if the linking site is niche-specific or a recognized news outlet, reviewing its about-us page for a real description and contact information, and using tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs to see what keywords the site actually ranks for and whether its content matches your vertical.

What is swarm theory in the context of SEO?

Bertram describes swarm theory as the way search engine spiders behave when a sudden spike of new links is generated. Many spiders visit the site at once, but if they find little of real value, they leave, producing a temporary ranking pop followed by a notable dip.

Terms defined in this episode
Private Blog Network (PBN)
A network of sites built and controlled by an SEO agency used to manufacture link equity pointing to a client site, which Bertram describes as manipulative and leaving clients with no retained value if they leave the agency.
Swarm Theory
The behavior where a spike in new links draws a swarm of search engine spiders to a site; if the site lacks real value, the spiders leave and rankings drop after a brief bump.
Disavow
A process of telling Google to ignore specific links pointing to your site, used when toxic or spammy links are harming rankings.
Link Equity
The ranking value or juice passed from one page to another through a hyperlink, which Google distributes based on link placement, site authority, and topical relevance.
