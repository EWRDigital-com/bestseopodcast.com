Questions this episode answers

Will Google penalize my site for using AI-generated content?

Google does not penalize AI content outright, but it does penalize scaled content abuse. Sites that rapidly publish large volumes of AI-generated pages have been manually deindexed. Keeping content helpful, adding value beyond words, and avoiding rapid bulk publishing reduces that risk significantly.

How accurate are AI content detectors like Originality.ai?

According to Jon Gillham, AI detectors are highly accurate but not perfect. They produce a binary classification of AI or human and a confidence score in that prediction. On any individual piece they can produce false positives or false negatives, but across large data sets they are reliable for trend analysis.

What should a marketer do to use AI content responsibly without risking a Google penalty?

Jon Gillham recommends three steps: get the business risk owner to formally agree on where AI is and is not allowed, add value beyond just words through tools, graphs, or primary data, and avoid the temptation of shortcuts that scale AI content without human editorial review in the loop.

Terms defined in this episode
Scaled Content Abuse
Google's term for sites that rapidly publish a large number of pages, which triggers a secondary review and can result in a manual deindexation penalty, especially when that content is AI-generated.
LLM Visibility
A framework for optimizing content so that large language models surface and cite a brand or solution as the optimal answer, distinct from traditional Google SEO ranking.
AI Detector Confidence Score
A probability score paired with a binary AI-or-human classification that indicates how confident the detector is in its prediction, not a percentage split of AI versus human content within a document.
Human in the Loop
An editorial practice where a human reviewer checks, edits, and approves AI-generated content before publication to ensure quality and correct optimization targets.
