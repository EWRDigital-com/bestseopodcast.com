Episode 514: Here’s How You Can Master Your Next Seasonal Digital Marketing Campaign by Ann Smarty #514

Ep. 51447 min2021-07-192,898 plays

Mastering a seasonal digital marketing campaign requires checking current rankings early, creating and scheduling content assets in advance, analyzing past campaign performance, and building an editorial calendar at least two months before peak season.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Check seasonal page rankings now, even off-season, because those pages stay indexed and can build authority year-round.
  • Start creating and scheduling seasonal content assets six to nine months in advance, before your busy season arrives.
  • Evaluate past seasonal campaign pages in Google Analytics to find traffic and conversion gaps worth fixing before the next cycle.
  • Consider starting a branded tradition or coining a unique term so searchers stay in your marketing funnel rather than a generic one.
  • Build an editorial calendar and plan ad spend increases around peak seasonal windows, not a flat weekly budget.

About this episode

Here’s How You Can Master Your Next Seasonal Digital Marketing CampaignSeasonal marketing can get really wrong unless you start preparing early! Here’s how to utilize slower months to create an effective seasonal marketing campaign.

Questions this episode answers

When should I start preparing for a seasonal SEO campaign?

The hosts recommend starting at least two months before the target season, because a post or link typically takes about 60 days to come to full strength. Planning right after the previous year's sale is even better, since you can debrief on what worked and schedule assets six to nine months out.

Should I take down seasonal landing pages after the holiday passes?

No. The article by Ann Smarty recommends leaving seasonal pages up year-round. Those pages can maintain good rankings during the off-season. If a page goes blank, a 301 redirect to the active sale page is a practical fix to preserve ranking equity.

What tools does Ann Smarty recommend for seasonal digital marketing campaigns?

The episode covers several tools Smarty mentions: Text Optimizer for semantic content analysis, Boosted by Lighttricks for creating festive videos in multiple formats, Namify for campaign naming inspiration, CoSchedule for editorial calendar and social scheduling, Ahrefs and Similar Web for competitive research, and Google Analytics for conversion analysis.

How can social signals and branding help a seasonal SEO campaign?

The hosts explain that social signals impact SEO and that Google indexes platforms like Facebook and pays attention to Twitter. Running campaigns across multiple channels, including traditional media like radio, can increase branded search volume, which signals brand strength to Google and lifts overall rankings.

Terms defined in this episode
Splintering
Repurposing a single piece of content across multiple channels and platforms to extend its reach and SEO impact, rather than publishing it only on a blog.
Semantic analysis
A method used by tools like Text Optimizer to identify related concepts and angles for content, reflecting how Google understands meaning and context beyond exact keywords.
Editorial calendar
A scheduling plan that maps out when seasonal content assets and social media updates will be created and published, used to avoid last-minute campaign preparation.
