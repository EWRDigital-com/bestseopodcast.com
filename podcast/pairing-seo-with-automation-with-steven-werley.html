Questions this episode answers

How can sales teams use AI automation to recover lost revenue from unworked leads?

According to the episode, for every 100K closed a business may lose roughly 150K from prospects not properly followed up with. A Slack bot that messages closers with the next highest priority action, specific email drafts, and call flow guidance can help recover that revenue without overloading the sales team.

What tools are recommended for AI-powered cold outreach and data enrichment?

The episode recommends RB2B to identify anonymous website visitors, Clay to enrich that data with cell numbers, confirmed emails, and hiring signals, and then routing that information into a CRM or Slack channel. Apollo is mentioned as an alternative list source for outreach.

Why is domain knowledge critical when building AI workflows for a business?

The episode argues that generic AI tools are not dialed in to a specific company process. The business owner holds unique domain knowledge, and if that knowledge is not built into the AI prompts and assistants, the output is no different from what any competitor could produce.

Terms defined in this episode
Human in the loop
A workflow design where AI completes a task 60 to 80 percent of the way and a human reviews and finalizes the output before it is acted on, ensuring quality without full manual effort.
Agentic workflow
An automation where an AI assistant acts autonomously via API, such as grabbing a call transcript from a webhook, sending it to an LLM for feedback, and dropping notes into a deal record without manual steps.
LLM visibility
A framework described by the host for ensuring a business or brand is discoverable and cited by large language models like ChatGPT and Perplexity, treated as a distinct discipline from traditional SEO.
Data enrichment
The process of taking limited contact information and using a platform like Clay to find additional details such as cell numbers, business emails, company profiles, and hiring signals relevant to a sales outreach.
