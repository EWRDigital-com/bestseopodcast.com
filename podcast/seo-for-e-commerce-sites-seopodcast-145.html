Questions this episode answers

How can ecommerce sites use video to improve SEO rankings?

Create unique product videos instead of reusing manufacturer demos. Post them on your own YouTube channel with your own title. Update videos whenever a product changes, and close each video with a prompt that encourages comments and shares to generate fresh user content.

Why is it sometimes worth running an ecommerce campaign at a small loss?

The value of a customer is almost never limited to a single purchase. If a customer buys twice a year at the same amount, an initial loss becomes profitable over time. Using newsletters and coupons to encourage repeat purchases makes the lifetime value far exceed the initial acquisition cost.

Terms defined in this episode
Duplicate content
Content that is identical or nearly identical across multiple pages or sites, common on ecommerce sites that copy manufacturer product descriptions, images, and titles.
Google Fresh update
A Google algorithm update focused on rewarding new, fresh, and updated content in search rankings, referred to in the episode as Ziploc.
Google Alerts
A Google product that sends email notifications whenever new content matching a specified search phrase is indexed, useful for brand monitoring and tracking industry conversations.
Ecommerce tracking
A Google Analytics feature that ties store sales data to marketing campaigns, allowing revenue measurement down to a specific dollar amount per campaign or keyword.
