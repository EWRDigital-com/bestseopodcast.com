#SEOPodcast287 - 5 Ways to Instantly Improve Landing Page Conversions

Episode28 min2015-09-14

Landing page conversions improve when you test button colors, add time-sensitive CTAs, use heat maps, incentivize actions, and split test everything long enough to gather meaningful data.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Test button colors against a constant color over 30-day periods to find what converts best.
  • Time-sensitive CTAs use emotions like fear of missing out to drive action on landing pages.
  • Incentivize every call to action; if you give people no reason to act, they won't.
  • Split test everything and give tests enough time, at least two weeks, to collect real data.
  • Create a feedback loop with clients through monthly calls to match analytics data to actual business results.

Questions this episode answers

How should I test landing page button colors?

Run one color against a constant control color for 30 days, then swap in a new color against the same control for another 30 days. Whichever color converts best in each period guides your adjustment. Your button should also contrast with the overall page design so it stands out.

Should I remove navigation from SEO landing pages the same way I would for paid landing pages?

Not necessarily. For paid traffic, removing navigation makes sense because you are paying for each click and want one action. For SEO-optimized pages that rank organically, keeping navigation available gives users who landed on a secondary-intent page a way to find what they actually need, which also improves the experience for Google users.

What is the best way to incentivize a newsletter sign-up call to action?

Give something of clear value in exchange. Examples from the episode include offering free information on getting a hard money loan, a 5% discount on a purchase, or a coupon for free donuts and free coffee at the next visit. People are reluctant to share their email without a tangible reason.

How long should a split test run before I evaluate results?

The hosts recommend at least two weeks, driving as much traffic as your budget allows. Running a test for only one day with around 20 visitors does not produce enough data. They also suggest creating an annotation in analytics marking the start date, what you tested, and what the control and variable were.

Terms defined in this episode
Split testing
Running two versions of a landing page element simultaneously to measure which one converts better, requiring enough traffic and time to produce meaningful data.
Time-sensitive call to action
A CTA that creates urgency through a countdown clock or limited supply, using FOMO, fear of missing out, to prompt visitors to act.
Heat maps
Technology that shows where and why people are clicking on a page, helping marketers understand how visitors interact with a landing page.
Offline CTA
An online promotion such as a printable coupon or free ticket offer that encourages people who find a business online to visit its physical location.
