Questions this episode answers

What should I focus on to rank my site in 2024 with AI changing search?

Matt Bertram recommends zeroing in on E-E-A-T, which stands for experience, expertise, authoritativeness, and trust, and adding website maintenance including core web vitals as a fifth factor. He also stresses differentiating yourself with testimonials, case studies, portfolios, and a strong about-us page.

Has AI fundamentally changed what Google looks for in a website?

According to Matt Bertram, AI raises the bar but has not changed the core goal: a good experience for the user. The fundamentals of E-E-A-T and site maintenance still apply, though your unique online presence and digital identity are becoming increasingly important.

What is the role of authorship and online presence in SEO today?

Matt Bertram notes that authorship was rolled out and rolled back by Google but likely still plays a role. He emphasizes that your overall online presence and digital ID are becoming more important ranking signals and plans to cover that topic in depth in future episodes.

Terms defined in this episode
E-E-A-T
Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust, the framework Matt Bertram says Google uses to evaluate content quality and a core factor for ranking.
Core Web Vitals
Technical performance metrics tied to website maintenance that Matt Bertram groups with E-E-A-T as essential ranking factors.
Digital ID
A term Matt Bertram uses to describe a brand's overall online presence, which he argues will become a more important ranking signal going forward.
