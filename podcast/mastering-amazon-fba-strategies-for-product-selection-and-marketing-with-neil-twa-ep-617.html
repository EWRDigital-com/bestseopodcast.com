Questions this episode answers

Why does having more reviews not guarantee Amazon ranking success?

According to Neil Twa, Amazon's A9 engine weighs profitability data heavily. A product with only five reviews but higher profit per unit can outrank a competitor with 5,000 reviews if its data set shows stronger margins, because Amazon prioritizes products that make it more profit.

What is the IDQ score on Amazon and why does it matter at launch?

Neil Twa explains the IDQ score starts at 100 when an ASIN is uploaded and declines from there. Sending outside traffic or running PPC too early, before 90 days of organic traction, can damage the score in the first 7, 21, and 30 days, potentially requiring a seller to take the listing down and start over.

How can a local brick-and-mortar store use Amazon to get more customers?

Amazon rolled out a fulfilled-by-merchant local program around October 2021. A local seller lists in-stock products, specifies their zip code, and a pick-up-in-store button appears on the listing. Buyers within roughly 20 to 50 miles can purchase on Amazon and collect the item the same day.

What is Amazon's brand referral program and how does it reduce fees?

The brand referral program lets brand-registered sellers share a special link from social media. If a standard Amazon referral fee is 15 percent, purchases through that link cost only 5 percent, giving the seller a 10 percent kickback. It positively signals social intent to the algorithm without risking the listing's ranking.

Terms defined in this episode
A9 engine
Amazon's core search and ranking algorithm, which weighs product data including profitability, organic buyer intent, and keyword relevance to decide which listings to surface and promote.
IDQ score
An Amazon listing quality metric that starts at 100 when an ASIN is uploaded and decreases over time. Premature PPC or off-platform traffic can damage it within the first 7, 21, and 30 days.
Cosmo
Amazon's upcoming large language model system described as a replacement to the A9 engine, designed to use viewing and behavior data to deliver hyper-relevant, intent-based product targeting.
Brand referral link
A trackable link available to brand-registered Amazon sellers that reduces the standard referral fee when a sale originates from an external source such as social media, and positively signals social intent to Amazon's algorithm.
