10 Tips for Your Holiday PPC Campaign by Kelsey Jones #344

Ep. 34445 min2016-10-26

Holiday PPC campaigns require reviewing last year's data, increasing budgets by at least 50 percent, using festive ad copy, enabling dynamic remarketing, and automating rules while still monitoring spend closely.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Review historical AdWords data from prior holiday quarters before spending a single dollar on new campaigns.
  • Expect to at least double your PPC spend, or increase it by a minimum of 50 percent, during the holiday season.
  • Ad copy and landing pages must match: if the ad says Black Friday promotion, the landing page must show one.
  • Dynamic remarketing shows visitors the exact products they viewed, which outperforms generic remarketing ads for retail.
  • Automate campaign rules in AdWords or Bing to save time, but still schedule regular monitoring sessions during peak season.
The holidays is the wrong time to try and institute a kind of set it and forget it PPC policy.

Questions this episode answers

When should I start my holiday PPC campaign?

According to the episode, the holiday season kicks off in October, and Google found that 26 percent of shoppers start before Halloween. The hosts say that if you have not started planning by then, you are already behind and should be executing, not still planning, once the season begins.

How much should I increase my PPC budget for the holidays?

The hosts say to expect to at least double your spend or increase it by a minimum of 50 percent. For example, a 3,000 dollar monthly budget should be prepared to reach 6,000 dollars. They also recommend spending the budget in shorter windows so you can identify non-performing keywords faster.

What ad extensions should I use during the holiday season?

The episode highlights four extensions from Kelsey Jones at Search Engine Journal: site link extensions to give visitors navigation options, call extensions so mobile users can call directly, location extensions for physical stores, and review extensions to show top reviews on the search results page before a click.

Why is dynamic remarketing better than generic remarketing for e-commerce during the holidays?

Generic remarketing shows a broad brand ad, which the hosts say is better suited to service providers. For retail, dynamic remarketing shows the specific products a visitor already viewed, making them far more likely to return and convert. The hosts recommend starting with your most profitable products first.

Additional lines of text or links shown beneath a search ad on the results page, set up inside AdWords as extensions, that direct users to specific pages of a site.
