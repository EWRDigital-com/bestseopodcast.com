Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between national, regional, and local SEO keyword targeting?

National terms are generic, like typing Coca-Cola with no location. Regional terms add a state or broad area, like Coca-Cola Texas. Local terms include a specific city, like Coca-Cola Houston. Businesses should match their targeting to wherever they can actually serve customers.

Why is SEO more cost effective than newspaper or Yellow Pages advertising for reaching multiple cities?

A business targeting multiple cities would need separate advertising accounts and payments for every newspaper, radio station, or Yellow Pages directory in each city. One SEO campaign can cover all those markets at a fraction of the combined cost, and results can be tracked digitally.

Should I post my business video on my own website or on YouTube for better SEO results?

The hosts recommend posting to YouTube and embedding that video on your site. YouTube's very high page rank means a properly keyworded video description there can rank better than the same video hosted only on your own site, while also drawing from YouTube's millions of daily visitors.

What is outsourcing SEO and what should businesses watch out for?

Outsourcing SEO means hiring another company, often overseas, to handle optimization work. The hosts at eWebStyle say it requires careful attention and plan a follow-up episode covering specific do's and don'ts, including risks when the client may not know who is actually doing the work.

Terms defined in this episode
Page Rank
Named after Larry Page, it is a measure of a website's authority. More web pages on a site can increase its page rank potential, but improper internal linking can prevent a site from reaching that maximum.
Unique Visitors
A count of individual people visiting a website, distinct from hits, which count every server request including each image on a page. New unique visitors is the most meaningful traffic metric according to the hosts.
Video Submission
The practice of uploading videos to multiple video-hosting sites such as YouTube so they appear in search results and drive traffic back to a business website.
SEO Sabotage
A deliberate act of undermining a competitor's or client's search engine optimization, referenced by the hosts as something they have personally experienced and that changed their procedures at eWebStyle.
