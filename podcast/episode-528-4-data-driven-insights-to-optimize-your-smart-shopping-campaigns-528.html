Key takeaways
  • POAS (profit on ad spend) often reveals better decisions than ROAS, especially when in-house products carry higher margins than branded resale items.
  • Machine learning in Smart Shopping performs best when enough spend and data volume exist; lower budgets or hyper-local campaigns limit its effectiveness.

Questions this episode answers

What is POAS and how does it differ from ROAS in Smart Shopping campaigns?

POAS stands for profit on ad spend. Unlike ROAS, which measures revenue generated per ad dollar, POAS factors in product margins. In the episode's example, a branded resale bag had a 755 percent ROAS but only 69 percent POAS, while an in-house bag had 400 percent ROAS but 137 percent POAS, making it the more profitable choice.

How does product pricing affect Smart Shopping campaign performance?

According to data discussed in the episode, pricing a product 7 to 15 percent below the average market price drives significantly more impressions and clicks. However, higher-priced products can yield better conversion rates, so optimizing for ROI rather than clicks alone is recommended.

What types of data should advertisers combine to improve Smart Shopping campaigns?

The episode outlines three data types: Google data such as conversions, costs, and impressions; company data including margins, stock levels, and KPIs; and competition data available through tools like SEMrush or Ahrefs. Combining these gives a fuller picture for decision-making.

What are the key success factors for managing Smart Shopping campaigns at an enterprise level?

The episode lists getting clear on business objectives first, knowing what to measure before spending, aligning marketing with inventory and pricing across all departments, staying open to experimentation, and using the right software to surface actionable insights from campaign data.

Terms defined in this episode
POAS (Profit on Ad Spend)
A metric that combines Google ad cost data with company margin data to measure actual profit generated per ad dollar spent, rather than just revenue.
ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)
A metric measuring how much revenue is generated for every dollar spent on advertising, without accounting for product profit margins.
Smart Shopping
A Google Ads format that uses machine learning and a product feed to automatically display ads across Google Search, Display, YouTube, and Gmail.
AWT (Ahrefs Webmaster Tools)
A free tool from Ahrefs that scans a website for 100 plus SEO issues, provides backlink data, and shows which keywords the site ranks for.
