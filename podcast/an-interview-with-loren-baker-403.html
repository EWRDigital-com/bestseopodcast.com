An Interview With Loren Baker #403

Ep. 40348 min2018-01-18Guest: Loren Baker

Loren Baker, founder of Search Engine Journal, predicts Google will drop the nofollow attribute, featured snippets will reduce clicks, and Google My Business will become a primary SEO KPI in 2018.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Google My Business messaging and video features are becoming direct lead-generation tools, replacing traditional site visit KPIs.
  • Nofollow links still drive referral traffic and may offer long-term SEO value even if Google drops the attribute.
  • Mobile conversions lag desktop but will shift as platforms like Shopify and Amazon simplify one-click purchasing.
  • Doorway pages with spun city-state content are losing ground to sites with real, location-specific written content.
  • Delegating editorial control to strong editors, as Loren Baker did at SEJ, can grow a publication beyond its founder.

Questions this episode answers

What is Loren Baker's biggest SEO prediction for 2018?

Baker predicts Google will stop recognizing the nofollow attribute in links, which has existed for 12 years. He argues Google already has tools like disavow and Google Search Console to identify quality sites, and that influential publishers like Forbes and TechCrunch have nofollowed nearly everything, limiting discovery.

How will featured snippets affect SEO click traffic in 2018?

Baker expects Google to cite sources differently, moving the link to an 'according to' reference at the top and merging knowledge graph data into the answer. This will reduce clicks to source sites, especially on mobile, as users get answers without needing to visit a page.

Should businesses focus on AMP pages for mobile SEO?

Baker says he dislikes AMP because it forces sites into a restricted template, limits creative flexibility, and demands ongoing development resources. He prefers building a fast, CMS-based mobile experience that matches AMP speed without being locked into the AMP protocol.

Why does Google My Business matter more in 2018 for local businesses?

Baker explains that Google now lets users call, get directions, or send messages directly through the Google interface, making those actions the new SEO KPIs instead of site visits. He urges brick-and-mortar and small businesses to keep their Google My Business profiles current.

keep your eyes on Google My Business and making sure your Google My Business profile is updated and you're taking advantages of the new things rolling out such as messaging and video.

Terms defined in this episode
nofollow attribute
An HTML link attribute that has existed for 12 years, telling Google not to pass authority through a link. Baker predicts Google may stop recognizing it as major publishers have applied it site-wide.
featured snippet
A Google search result that pulls an answer directly from a source page, increasingly showing an 'according to' citation at the top and merging knowledge graph data, which reduces clicks to the original site.
doorway page
A page that hangs off a site meant as a landing page for PPC or email campaigns, sometimes misused for SEO with spun city-state content that Google is now penalizing in favor of real location-specific writing.
AMP (Accelerated Mobile Pages)
A Google-backed mobile page protocol that loads fast but restricts design creativity and requires dedicated development. Baker argues a fast CMS-based mobile site can match AMP performance without the protocol's limitations.
