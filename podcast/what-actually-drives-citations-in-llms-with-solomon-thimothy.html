Questions this episode answers

What actually drives citations and brand mentions in LLMs like ChatGPT?

According to Solomon Thimothy, consistent brand citations across quality media sources drive LLM mentions. This includes podcast appearances, press releases, YouTube videos, and structured website content with schema markup and FAQ sections. LLMs follow patterns similar to traditional SEO but pull from a wider range of media beyond just indexed web pages.

Do Google rankings determine whether you show up in ChatGPT or Perplexity?

No. Solomon Thimothy found that Google AI overview, ChatGPT, and Perplexity each produce different brand visibility percentages for the same prompt. A brand can appear frequently in ChatGPT while barely appearing in Google, meaning Google rankings do not automatically transfer to LLM visibility.

How can a small business improve its visibility in AI search tools?

Solomon Thimothy recommends adding schema markup, building out FAQ sections with clear answers, launching a YouTube channel, earning press mentions, and appearing on podcasts. He also stresses defining exactly who you serve and what you do so LLMs can match your brand to specific prompts at the top, middle, and bottom of the funnel.

Why does content in iframe embeds hurt LLM visibility?

LLMs cannot read iframe embeds, so any important content placed inside an iframe is invisible to AI systems. Matt Bertram noted this during the episode, adding that unless the iframe content is labeled with schema markup, it will not be picked up or cited by generative engines.

Terms defined in this episode
AEO (Answer Engine Optimization)
Optimizing content so it is surfaced by answer engines including Google, ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini, treating those platforms as destinations that deliver direct answers rather than blue links.
GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)
Optimization focused specifically on generative AI engines and LLMs, overlapping with AEO but centered on how generative systems select and rank brand information.
LLM Visibility
A term used in this episode to describe how well a brand is recognized, cited, and surfaced by large language models across top, middle, and bottom of funnel prompts.
Citations (in LLM context)
Brand mentions in media, podcasts, press releases, and other sources that LLMs ingest during training or grounding to determine which brands to surface in response to a prompt.
