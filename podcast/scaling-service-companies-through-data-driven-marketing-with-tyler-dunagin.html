Scaling Service Companies Through Data-Driven Marketing with Tyler Dunagin

Service companies scale faster by targeting property management firms with high lifetime value contracts, testing marketing offers with real data, and building a hub-and-spoke brand ecosystem around one core client relationship.

Key takeaways
  • Land one large property management client instead of thousands of homeowners, since a single client LTV can reach $5 million.
  • Build adjacent service brands once you are inside a niche, using existing client budgets and vendor observations to spot the next opportunity.
  • Data interpretation, not gut instinct, is the core superpower; you are inherently biased about what your target actually wants.
  • The 7-11-4 framework from Google research says prospects need 7 hours of content, 11 brand impressions, across 4 platforms before buying.

Questions this episode answers

How did Tyler Dunagin scale TurnServe into multiple companies?

Tyler built TurnServe as a parent platform with over 10 LLCs underneath, each producing millions of dollars. He spotted adjacent services by observing vendors on client job sites, analyzed client budgets to find spending gaps, and expanded from painting into flooring, bathtub refinishing, and a patented product line.

How do you find your ideal customer profile before spending heavily on marketing?

Tyler recommends testing multiple versions of your website or offer, tracking who actually converts, and letting the data correct your assumptions. His team ran over 70 website versions and discovered property managers, not property owners, were the real buyers, which dramatically improved their funnel.

What is the biggest unknown secret of internet marketing according to Tyler Dunagin?

Tyler says the biggest secret is understanding exactly who you are marketing to and interpreting the data honestly. He warns that entrepreneurs are inherently biased about what their target wants, and that aiming even slightly off target means missing entirely, no matter how strong the product or service.

Terms defined in this episode
LTV (Lifetime Value)
The total revenue a single client generates over the full relationship; Tyler cites a single property management client LTV of $500,000 to $5 million.
Edge Strategy
A concept from the book of the same name where a business already operating in a space looks at what additional services or products are within arm's reach of its existing resources and client relationships.
7-11-4 Framework
A Google-researched rule stating a buyer needs seven hours of content, eleven brand name exposures, and contact across four platforms before trusting a brand enough to purchase.
Hub and Spoke Ecosystem
A business structure where one parent platform connects multiple subsidiary brands so that landing one client benefits all companies in the network through cross-selling and shared resources.
