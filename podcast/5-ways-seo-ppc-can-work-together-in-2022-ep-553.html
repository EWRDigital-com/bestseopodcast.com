5 Ways SEO & PPC Can Work Together in 2022 Ep. 553

Ep. 55324 min2022-10-293,977 plays

SEO and PPC work best together by dominating SERPs, sharing keyword data, using common tools, remarketing to organic visitors, and avoiding paid spend on keywords you already own organically.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Appearing in both paid and organic results doubles your chance of earning a click for a given query.
  • Use PPC as a fast testing ground for keywords, then apply winners to long-term organic content.
  • Remarketing to organic visitors has a much higher CTR and conversion rate; spend first dollars there.
  • If no competitor bids on a keyword you already rank first for organically, skip paying for that click.
  • A dollar a day in remarketing ads builds brand recall without consuming large budgets, but never turn campaigns fully off.
when these two tactics collaborate, companies see better results, SERPs are dominated and ROI is increased

About this episode

PPC and SEO can be seen as SERP competitors. However, by combining paid search and organic marketing, companies can obtain better performance.

Questions this episode answers

Why should SEO and PPC teams share keyword data?

PPC offers a fast playground to test keywords and see how they perform in ad copy. The best performing keywords can then be added to organic content for long-term success, and existing SEO data can help launch new paid campaigns with confidence.

How does remarketing connect SEO and PPC?

Organic traffic accounts for 53% of web traffic but SEO does a poor job of bringing back users who leave without converting. Paid remarketing retargets those visitors, produces a much higher CTR and conversion rate, and usually does not require a large budget because the audience is limited.

When should you stop bidding on a keyword in PPC?

If no PPC competitors are bidding on a keyword and you already hold the top organic position, you will earn that click for free. In that case, spending money on the click is unnecessary, and the budget is better used elsewhere.

Terms defined in this episode
SERPs
Search engine result pages, which are a combination of paid ads and organic results.
Remarketing
A paid search tactic that enables marketers to retarget audiences who have already visited their site, producing a higher CTR than cold targeting.
LSI
Latent semantic indexing, referenced in the episode as a machine learning aspect of content clustering tools that group related keyword data.
Dollar a day strategy
Running ads at roughly one dollar per day to maintain consistency, preserve algorithm data, and build market recall without turning campaigns off.
