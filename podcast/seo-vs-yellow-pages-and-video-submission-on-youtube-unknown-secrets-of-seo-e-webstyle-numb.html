Questions this episode answers

Why is SEO better than Yellow Pages advertising for reaching multiple regional markets?

To reach multiple cities via Yellow Pages, a business must set up separate advertising accounts in every directory for every city, which becomes cost prohibitive. An SEO campaign can target all those cities at once for a fraction of the cost, and every result is trackable, unlike a print ad.

How should a business choose between a national, regional, or local SEO keyword strategy?

If a product or service can be delivered to anyone anywhere, like a downloadable e-book, national terms make sense. If a business only serves a specific city or metro area, local terms such as Plumbing Sugar Land or Plumber Baytown are more appropriate and cost effective.

Why should businesses submit videos to YouTube instead of only hosting them on their own website?

YouTube receives millions of visitors every day and carries very high page rank, so a video with the right keyword description can rank better on YouTube than the same video hosted on a smaller business website. The video can also be embedded on the business site while using YouTube's bandwidth.

What is outsourcing SEO and what should businesses watch out for?

Outsourcing SEO means hiring another company, often overseas, to perform optimization work, sometimes without the client knowing. The hosts flagged it as a sensitive topic with specific risks to watch for, and promised a full discussion of dos and don'ts in the next episode.

Terms defined in this episode
Page Rank
A Google metric named after Larry Page, not the term web page. More web pages on a site can increase its page rank potential, but improper internal linking can prevent a site from reaching that maximum.
Hits vs. Unique Visitors
Hits count every server request including each image on a page, so one page with 20 images equals 20 hits. Unique visitors, especially new unique visitors, is the more meaningful traffic metric.
Viral Video
A video that spreads widely on its own, cited in the episode using the Mentos and Diet Coke explosion example as a campaign that boosted product awareness without being intentional advertising.
Outsourcing SEO
Hiring a separate company, typically overseas such as in India, to perform SEO tasks, sometimes arranged so the end client believes the original agency is doing the work directly.
