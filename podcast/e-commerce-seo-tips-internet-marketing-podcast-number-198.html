E-Commerce SEO Tips - Internet Marketing Podcast Number 198

Episode31 min2013-08-12

E-commerce SEO requires exhaustive keyword research per product, strong on-page fundamentals, a regularly updated blog, authority links, and responding publicly to every customer review to build trust and rankings.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Exhaust all keyword variations for every product, such as black camera, black Olympus camera, and so on.
  • On-page SEO on e-commerce sites must be manually controlled because the platform auto-generates URLs and titles.
  • Add a blog on the same domain and link corresponding blog posts to product pages to support each other.
  • Place social sharing buttons at the cart and checkout pages, where buyers are most likely to share.
  • Respond to negative reviews publicly and fast, because Google sees the response and so do future customers.
Addressing negative reviews in public, and when I say in public, I mean in a comment section or on a review section, actually has more value than sometimes than a positive review.

Questions this episode answers

Where should e-commerce sites put social sharing buttons?

According to the hosts, the best places are the cart page and the checkout page, because the shopper has already shown enough interest to add the product. A pop-up prompting a share right after adding to cart is also suggested, and offering a small discount for a like or review can further drive engagement.

Should e-commerce sites build microsites to target different geographic areas?

The hosts advise against separate microsites. Instead, they recommend adding location-specific pages or subfolders on the existing site, linking from already-ranking pages to the new location pages, and incorporating the target city name into on-page content and titles before trying subdomains or separate sites.

How important are customer reviews compared to backlinks for e-commerce SEO?

The hosts say reviews have more value than links for e-commerce sites. A seller with several customer reviews is seen as more valuable to both users and Google than one with a single link from a relevant source. They recommend pursuing authority links but prioritizing the accumulation of genuine customer reviews.

Why should e-commerce sellers list products in Google Merchant and on Amazon or eBay?

Mobile searches for products surface local and Google database results first, often before standard web results. Being in Google Merchant ensures a product appears in those early results. The hosts also suggest Amazon and eBay stores so sellers compete in all venues where buyers are already searching, especially for niche or abstract products.

Terms defined in this episode
microsite
A separate, smaller standalone website distinct from the main domain, as opposed to a subfolder or subdomain on the existing site.
authority links
Backlinks from highly credible and relevant sources, such as a manufacturer like Samsung or Logitech, that carry strong trust signals for Google.
Google Merchant
Google's product listing platform that allows e-commerce sellers to appear in Google Shop results, especially prominent in mobile searches for products.
on-page SEO
The practice of optimizing individual page elements including URLs, titles, image tags, and written content to target the right keywords and improve search rankings.
