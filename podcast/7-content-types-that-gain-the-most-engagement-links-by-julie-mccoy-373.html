Questions this episode answers

What are the seven content types that get the most engagement and links according to Julia McCoy?

The seven types are authoritative blog posts, infographics, standout opinion pieces, how-to content, original research pieces, trending content, and videos. Each type serves a different purpose, from building authority to earning backlinks through data-driven research or capitalizing on viral trends.

Why do infographics earn so many links and shares?

Infographics earn links and shares because they communicate information visually and quickly. The hosts recommend creating infographics that address a specific concern of your target audience rather than a generic list of services, making them more relevant and shareable for people who actually need that information.

How should you use trending content for SEO and engagement?

Check Twitter each morning for trending topics, find anything related to your industry, and then write an opinion piece, how-to post, or authoritative blog post using those trending terms and hashtags. You can also tag the people who started the trend to put your content in front of an already-interested audience.

Does link juice pass through a 301 redirect?

Yes, link juice does pass through a 301 redirect, though some articles suggest there may be a loss of as much as 5% per redirect. The hosts recommend always using a 301 rather than a 302 or other redirect type, and note that search engines prefer a redirect over a 404 error experience.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement. It stands for shares, likes, and follows on social media platforms.
301 redirect
A permanent redirect from one URL to another that passes link juice to the destination, preferred by search engines over a 404 error or other redirect types.
Expanded text ads
A Bing and Google ad format that replaced standard text ads, giving advertisers a title, two lines of description, two additional lines of description, and optional extensions instead of the older shorter format.
Trending content
Content created around topics that are currently going viral on platforms like Twitter, designed for social media and intended to drive fast, high-volume traffic in a short period of time.
