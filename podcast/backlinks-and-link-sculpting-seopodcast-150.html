Backlinks and Link Sculpting - #seopodcast 150

Episode17 min2012-07-10

Article spinning and paid link schemes are risky and short-lived SEO tactics; Google's Panda and Penguin updates directly targeted these strategies, and sustainable SEO requires quality links and inbound marketing.

Key takeaways
  • Article spinning to build backlinks is a short-term tactic that Google's Panda and Penguin updates directly targeted and penalized.
  • Buying paid links can get a site completely removed from Google's index, as happened to the agency iAcquire.
  • Understanding your link profile, knowing what makes a good link, and monitoring SEO are core steps to surviving a Penguin update.
  • No-indexing thin content pages directs Google to your high-quality pages instead of letting low-value pages hurt your site.
  • Avoid changing URLs whenever possible, and always use 301 redirects if a URL change is unavoidable.

Questions this episode answers

Is article spinning a good backlink strategy for SEO?

No. Google's second Panda update specifically targeted sites using spun articles to build links, and the Penguin update further penalized such tactics. The hosts say the same effort is better spent creating real value through blogging, podcasting, and earning genuine links.

What are the seven steps to survive a Google Penguin update according to the episode?

The seven steps from a Chris Warren SEO Moz article are: understand your link profile, learn what makes a good link, map the path Googlebot takes through your site, no-index low-value thin content pages, almost never change your URLs, set up SEO monitoring with alerts, and embrace inbound marketing.

What is link sculpting in SEO?

According to the episode, link sculpting refers to how a site is structured so that Googlebot enters through the homepage and indexes other pages based on the sculpting, site map, and robots file. Knowing and controlling that path ensures the right pages get crawled and indexed.

Terms defined in this episode
Article Spinning
A tactic where the same article is rewritten in multiple versions, posted on free domains, and those domains point links back to a main site to artificially build backlinks.
Link Sculpting
The practice of structuring a website's internal links and site map so that Googlebot follows a specific path when crawling and indexing pages.
Thin Content
A page with very little text or value, such as a single paragraph, that Google may use to negatively judge a site's overall quality if it is indexed.
Inbound Marketing
A progression from traditional SEO that addresses not only traffic coming to a site but what that traffic does when it arrives, including purchasing, sharing, and navigating multiple pages.
