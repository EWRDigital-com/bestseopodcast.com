Link Building and Link Disavowing - #seopodcast 157

Episode38 min2012-09-16

For startups, strong SEO requires identifying target keywords first, then pairing consistent branding, fresh content, committed social networking, Google Analytics setup, link building through directories and guest posts, and regular press releases.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Identify target keywords before building your website so every page is structured around what people actually search.
  • Commit to social networking daily, even two or three tweets and a Facebook post, because consistency compounds over time.
  • Install Google Analytics on every site and set up goals tied to thank-you pages to measure real conversions.
  • Build links through curated directories, thoughtful blog comments, and guest posts on relevant, high-traffic sites.
  • Press releases are easy link and awareness opportunities for startups and should be socialized across blog, email, and social channels.

Questions this episode answers

Why should startups identify keywords before doing anything else for SEO?

The hosts explain that keywords define what pages you need because every product or service has keywords associated with it. Knowing your keywords first means your website structure, branding, social profiles, and content are all built around terms people actually use to find what you offer.

How does link building through blog comments work without being spammy?

According to the hosts, comments must actually contribute to the article being discussed. After going back and forth in a genuine conversation, it becomes acceptable to mention a related post on your own site with a link. Simply dropping your URL without context is considered spammy and unhelpful.

What is the value of guest posting for SEO?

Guest posting on a relevant, non-spammy blog with real traffic earns a quality inbound link and exposes your content to that blog's existing audience. The hosts note you get a chance to tap into someone else's network and should always write a high-quality post or risk being called out in the comments.

Should you worry about negative SEO from bad inbound links?

The hosts argue that Google likely cannot penalize you for bad inbound links because that would make negative SEO too easy. A competitor could simply link to you from low-quality sites. Their view is Google ignores those links rather than using them against you, making disavow tools of limited practical value.

Terms defined in this episode
Link Disavow
A tool that lets site owners tell a search engine they have no affiliation with a specific inbound link, without removing that link from the linking site. Bing added this feature to its webmaster tools.
Google Analytics Goals
Tracked conversions inside Google Analytics, such as reaching a thank-you page after a form submission, that show how many visitors completed a desired action on a site.
Social Signals
Activity on social networking platforms that Google and Bing have acknowledged they factor into search rankings, making consistent posting and engagement important for SEO.
Guest Posting
Writing and publishing content on another person's blog to earn an inbound link and exposure to that blog's audience, effective only when the host blog is relevant and has real traffic.
