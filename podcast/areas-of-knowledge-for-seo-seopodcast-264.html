Key takeaways
  • Create content you would personally link to, with value, substance, images, and video, before publishing it.
  • Run PPC campaigns first to find which keywords convert best, then optimize organically for those terms.
  • Responsive design is essential: MTV saw a 92% increase in mobile visits after switching to it.
  • Your NAP (name, address, phone number) should be supported by local content pages on your own site.
  • Offline marketing like TV ads drives branded searches, so your SEO and traditional marketing must stay in sync.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most important on-page SEO elements according to this episode?

The episode lists page content, title tags, URL structuring, and H1s as the four core on-page SEO factors. The hosts add alt tags, meta descriptions, great optimized content, and rich media like images, video, infographics, and downloadable files such as PDFs and eBooks.

How should SEOs approach keyword research now that semantic search is growing?

The hosts say keywords are still important but content should be written in a conversational style users can engage with. Avoid obsessing over keyword density. Place the keyword in the title and H1, and think about how voice queries break down into keyword phrases like location and service type.

How can PPC help with SEO strategy?

Running paid campaigns lets you test which keyword terms convert most and get the most clicks before committing to organic optimization. PPC also boosts credibility in search results, and the hosts note that seeing both a paid and organic listing for the same site increases clicks on the organic result.

Why does traditional offline marketing matter for SEO?

TV and other offline ads drive people to Google a brand name directly. Those branded searches signal credibility to Google. The hosts recommend having the SEO team supply the right hashtags and messaging for ads so online and offline efforts stay consistent.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, address, and phone number. Every business should have consistent NAP across profiles like Yelp, Foursquare, and G Plus, supported by local content pages on the site.
Semantic search
Conversational or intent-based search that goes beyond exact keywords. The hosts describe it as how a voice query like 'where is the best restaurant' gets broken down into keyword phrases by the search engine.
Link profile
The overall structure of links around a site, including number of incoming and outbound links, anchor text variety, whether links are paid or natural, and how many come from the same source.
Phablet
A device that is part phone and part tablet, described in the episode as a really big phone or a really small tablet.
