Why Your Business Should Be On Social Media - Internet Marketing Podcast Number 201

Episode30 min2013-09-09

Every business should be on social media because platform choice and content strategy matter more than industry type, and consistent posting builds SEO authority and customer trust at little to no cost.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Businesses in any industry should be on social media, with platform choice determined by their specific business type.
  • Tweets and posts should be 60 to 70 percent industry related and 30 to 40 percent business related.
  • Avoid posting about politics, religion, or strong personal opinions on business social media accounts.
  • Responding to comments and questions on social media is essential, especially when posts generate customer interest.
  • Google treats the business that consistently posts relevant content as the expert and authority on that subject.

Questions this episode answers

Should every business be on social media regardless of industry?

Yes, according to the hosts of this episode. They argue there is a social platform suited to every business type. For example, brick and mortar businesses should be on Foursquare, product businesses should use Instagram and Pinterest, and all businesses should be on LinkedIn.

Can you automatically cross-post tweets to Google Plus?

No, you cannot automatically send tweets to Google Plus. The hosts note there is no tool or API integration that supports this. However, a listener suggested that Google Plus posts can be set up to appear on Twitter, which could then flow to Facebook.

What is Google's stance on guest blogging and press release links for SEO?

Google flagged large-scale guest posting with exact match anchor text and optimized press release links as suspect. Google says those links should be nofollowed, and if they are not, Google will manually spam them. The hosts recommend only soliciting articles from decent, authentic authors.

Terms defined in this episode
GWT (Google Webmaster Tools)
A platform where site owners and managers can monitor their site, find issues like malware or bad links, and manage SEO health.
Nofollow
A link attribute Google recommends for guest blog and press release links to signal they should not pass SEO value, or Google will manually discount them.
Exact match anchor text
Hyperlink text that matches a target keyword precisely, which Google now treats as suspect when repeated across many guest blog or PR site submissions.
Algorithm cataclysm
The podcast segment covering recent Google algorithm updates, including a multi-week release targeting links in guest blogs and PR sites.
