Questions this episode answers

What are definitive guides as a linkable asset and why do they attract backlinks?

Definitive guides are comprehensive, authoritative resources on a topic. Bloggers and journalists prefer linking to one definitive guide rather than multiple smaller posts, making them magnets for backlinks. The key requirement is that the creator must be an expert on the subject matter to pull one off successfully.

How do you build links to a definitive guide or tutorial?

The episode recommends staying on the radar of bloggers and journalists who are likely to link, keeping the guide regularly updated, telling your subscribers, running paid ads like Facebook ads, performing outreach to relevant industry people, and reaching out to sites linking to weaker guides to suggest they link to yours instead.

What is a coined term as a linkable asset and how do you promote it?

A coined term is a newly invented phrase or concept, like the skyscraper technique, that others start referencing and linking to. To promote it, set up alerts for the term to find people using it, and guest post on high-traffic industry blogs to popularize the term and earn links.

What does Joshua Hardwick say is the overall key to making linkable assets work?

Hardwick concludes that many tactics for different linkable asset types overlap, and the common thread is letting as many relevant people as possible know about the asset. If the asset is built well and widely distributed, people are supposed to naturally link to it.

Terms defined in this episode
Linkable asset
A piece of content or a tool specifically created to attract backlinks, such as infographics, online calculators, awards, studies, definitive guides, or coined terms.
Coined term
A newly invented word or phrase, like the skyscraper technique, that becomes associated with its creator and earns links as others reference and discuss it.
Linkerati
A term used in the episode to describe bloggers and journalists who are likely to link to your content and whose attention you should stay on.
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, a coined term used by the hosts meaning what you do with a visitor after they arrive from a search engine.
