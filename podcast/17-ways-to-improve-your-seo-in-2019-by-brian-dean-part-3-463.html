Questions this episode answers

What is link intersect and how does it help with link building?

Link intersect means finding websites that link to multiple competitors at once. If several of your competitors share a backlink from the same site, that site is likely willing to link broadly in your space, making it a strong and realistic outreach target for earning your own backlink.

How do you find brand new keywords to target before competitors do?

Stay current on trends using Google Trends, Google News, and Reddit. Brian Dean's example is writing early on the term voice search, which landed his guide on the first page of Google within a week. The hosts also cite Bitcoin advertising and geofencing as topics they targeted early.

What is QuestionDB and how is it used for SEO keyword research?

QuestionDB is a tool that surfaces questions people ask on Reddit, laid out in a simple table. Because it pulls from Reddit specifically, it surfaces keyword and topic ideas that most other keyword tools miss, making it useful for finding question-based search queries.

Why should you try to rank for competitor brand name terms?

Brand names are searched far more than generic category terms. The episode cites MailChimp being searched 280 times more than email marketing software. Appearing alongside a well-known brand during a buyer's journey repeatedly can build familiarity and make potential customers more likely to consider your product.

Terms defined in this episode
Link Intersect
A technique for finding sites that link to multiple competitors simultaneously, signaling those sites are likely candidates to also link to you.
Concept Visuals
Simple graphics that explain a single concept visually, used to attract links when other sites embed or reference the image. Brian Dean's term for this type of asset.
QuestionDB
A keyword research tool that pulls questions people ask on Reddit and displays them in a simple table, revealing topic ideas most tools do not surface.
