#SEOPodcast 282 - Local SEO for Multi-Location Businesses

Episode34 min2015-08-21

Multi-location businesses wreck local SEO by neglecting per-location website pages, unverified Google My Business listings, inconsistent NAP citations, missing local content, and unmanaged reviews, according to Search Engine Land.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Each business location needs its own website page with a unique address, phone number, staff photos, and location-specific content.
  • Google My Business must be verified for every location so business information in Search and Maps stays accurate and editable.
  • NAP consistency, name, address, and phone, must be exact across every directory, down to abbreviations and punctuation.
  • Local content such as service-job blog posts with geo-specific keywords signals relevance to Google for each service area.
  • Proactively pursue positive reviews by contacting customers directly and intercepting unhappy clients before they post negative feedback.

Questions this episode answers

Why does every business location need its own website page?

Each location page should carry that location's address, phone number, staff photos, and unique content about the surrounding community. Without dedicated pages, search engines cannot connect the site to specific local searches, and duplicate content across locations prevents any of those pages from ranking.

What is NAP consistency and why does it matter for local SEO?

NAP stands for name, address, and phone. Every mention of a business across directories is a citation, and inconsistencies, such as different abbreviations for street or varying phone formats, confuse search engines and hurt local rankings. The hosts recommend using the USPS website to confirm the official address format and applying it everywhere.

How should multi-location businesses handle Google My Business?

Every location needs a verified Google My Business listing. Until a listing is verified, the business owner cannot correct wrong phone numbers, hours, or categories that Google may display. After verification, businesses should also claim the custom URL for branding and sync the listing with other Google products like YouTube.

What is the best way to get positive local reviews without risking negative ones?

The hosts recommend a structured customer survey process through a third party rather than mass email blasts asking for reviews. This approach surfaces unhappy customers privately so the business can resolve issues first, then follow up for a positive review once the experience has improved.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP consistency
Name, address, and phone kept identical across every online directory and citation, including matching abbreviations, punctuation, and suite number formatting.
Citation
Every mention of a business around the web, which may feature all or part of the NAP, according to the article by Marcus Miller at Search Engine Land.
Google My Business
A Google product businesses must set up and verify to control how their information appears in Google Search, Maps, and Google Plus.
Local content
Content specifically created to target a local area, such as a blog post about a service job performed in a named neighborhood, including geo-specific keywords and photos.
