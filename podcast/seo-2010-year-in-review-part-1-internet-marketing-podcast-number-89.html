Questions this episode answers

Can you legally bid on a competitor's trademark name in Google pay-per-click ads?

Yes. Google won most of the court cases on this issue, and advertisers are allowed to bid on trademarked names. The hosts used Rosetta Stone as an example, noting that competing language-learning products can legally bid on that trademark name in PPC.

What are Quantcast and Comscore used for in digital advertising?

Quantcast and Comscore rank websites by traffic and audience characteristics. When buying display ads on large sites, those publishers will ask for your Quantcast and Comscore numbers. Quantcast can show average visitor age, income, and household data for a given site.

Why should I focus on long-tail keywords instead of short-tail keywords?

Short-tail keywords are generic and highly competitive. Long-tail keywords attract fewer visitors individually but those visitors are more qualified and specifically targeted. Summing all long-tail keyword traffic can in theory outperform a single short-tail keyword in both volume and conversion quality.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search engine visitor optimization, a phrase used by the hosts to describe optimizing the experience for visitors who arrive from search engines, with the goal of improving ROI.
Contextual relevance
A concept introduced by SEO guest Ken Roberts describing how content must be topically relevant to its context, which became more critical after Google launched its Caffeine indexing update.
Quantcast
A web measurement service that ranks websites by traffic and can break down audience demographics such as age range, income level, and household size for a given site.
Long-tail keyword
A longer, more specific keyword phrase that attracts lower individual traffic but more qualified visitors, for example personal injury attorney truck accident Houston Texas versus the short-tail attorney Houston.
