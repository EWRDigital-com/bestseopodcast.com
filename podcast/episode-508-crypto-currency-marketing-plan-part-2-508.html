Questions this episode answers

Why is remarketing so important for a cryptocurrency marketing plan?

Because 94% of website visitors leave and never return, remarketing lets you re-engage those window shoppers across video streaming, social media pixels, SMS, and email. The hosts cite a 400% increase in engagement from retargeting and call it probably the single best use of paid marketing dollars.

What marketing channels work for crypto projects when paid ads are blocked?

Facebook and Google often reject crypto ads. The hosts say SEO and influencer and social media marketing become the two primary buckets. They also recommend Reddit community participation, Quora answers, podcasts, and chatbots as alternative ways to drive traffic and leads.

How should a crypto project use influencer marketing?

The hosts recommend using a tool called Intellifluence to find influencers and evaluate engagement over follower count. They stress converting influencers into genuine believers rather than paid promoters, warning that audiences can tell the difference, and citing examples like John McAfee and Tron as cautionary cases.

How often should a business send marketing emails?

The hosts say email frequency should reflect the closeness of the relationship you want with the customer, ranging from daily to monthly. Data cited in the episode shows that the more emails sent with valuable content, the higher the engagement and conversion rate, and people keep email addresses on average eight years.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing
Targeting people who previously visited your website but left, using pixels, SMS, email, or app-based IP targeting to bring them back and tell them a story over time.
Intellifluence
A platform where marketers post projects and search influencers by niche, location, expected pay, and audience size to arrange posts, Instagram content, or videos.
Lead Scoring
Ranking potential customers based on engagement signals such as emails given, pages visited, or content downloaded, then placing them in targeted drip campaigns.
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
The practice of making incremental improvements to a website, such as reducing form fields or adding chatbots, to increase the percentage of visitors who take a desired action.
