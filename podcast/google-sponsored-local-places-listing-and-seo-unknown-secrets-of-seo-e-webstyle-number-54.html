Questions this episode answers

What is Google Sponsored Local Places and how much does it cost?

Google Sponsored Local Places is a program where businesses pay $25 a month to have a small yellow Post-it style highlight added to their Google local listing on standard Google search results. At the time of this episode it was only available in San Jose and Houston.

Is it worth paying for Google Sponsored Local ads if no local listings show for your keyword?

No. According to the hosts, if Google does not display any local listings for your keyword category on the first page, the sponsored ad simply does not appear, meaning you pay $25 for no additional visibility. The ad only adds value when local listings are already showing.

How should someone with no SEO experience break into the SEO industry?

The hosts recommend starting a blog on a competitive topic, optimizing it to rank on the first page, and using that as tangible proof of skill in job interviews. Showing real results matters more to employers than a degree or GPA alone.

Why does keyword research matter so much for local SEO?

A keyword tool can surface unexpected high-volume terms with zero competition. In the episode, Shade Houston had the highest search volume for a window treatment business but zero advertisers were targeting it, meaning a business that optimized for that term would face no competition.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Sponsored Local Places
A paid program where businesses pay $25 a month to add a yellow highlighted Post-it style badge to their Google local listing in standard search results, available at the time only in San Jose and Houston.
Google Local Listing
A section on standard Google search results showing five to ten local businesses related to a search query, distinct from Google Maps or Google Images.
Keyword Competition Level
A metric in Google's keyword analysis tool that shows how many advertisers are bidding or optimizing for a given search term, helping identify high-volume terms that no one is targeting yet.
