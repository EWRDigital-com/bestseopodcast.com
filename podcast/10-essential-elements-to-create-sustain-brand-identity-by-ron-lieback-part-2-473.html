10 Essential Elements to Create & Sustain Brand Identity by Ron Lieback Part 2 #473

Ep. 47334 min2020-02-04

Building a lasting brand identity requires avoiding self-promotion, staying positive, keeping promises, extending branding everywhere, and continuously evolving, as covered in Ron Lieback's 10-element framework discussed across two episodes.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Avoid bragging about outdated accomplishments; focus brand messaging on the future while using case studies to build confidence.
  • Positive thinking measurably improves performance, including doctors diagnosing conditions with 30% higher accuracy when in a positive mindset.
  • Make promises and deliver on them consistently to turn customers into raving fans who spread word of mouth.
  • Extend your brand into every touchpoint, from email signatures to business cards to any content you publish.
  • Brands that fail to evolve get left behind; the strongest brands change with the times and become the only choice.
The goal is to create a business or personal brand identity so strong that you're the only choice.

About this episode

10 Essential Elements to Create & Sustain Brand IdentityHere’s some tips on how to make sure all perceptions about you or your business are positive, as important, make sure those perceptions remain positive.

Questions this episode answers

Why should brands avoid focusing only on past accomplishments?

According to the episode, brands that brag only about old accomplishments signal they are stuck in the past. The brands that build the strongest identity communicate innovative services and products with a focus on the future, while still using case studies and testimonials to build confidence.

How does staying positive affect business and brand performance?

The episode cites a corporate training example where doctors with a positive mindset diagnosed conditions with 30% higher accuracy. The hosts argue that positivity also benefits entire industries, and that negative criticism or attacks on competitors tend to drag the whole industry down.

What does it mean to extend your brand at every opportunity?

Ron Lieback's ninth element means placing your brand identity in every possible touchpoint, including email signatures, business cards, and any content or commercials you run. The hosts use the Apple 'Sent from iPhone' example as a case study of brilliant, consistent brand extension.

What is the risk of doing marketing before you have a good product?

The hosts warn that marketing an inferior product or service simply amplifies its flaws and accelerates word spreading about poor quality. They recommend ensuring you have a solid product and business model before investing in SEO, online PR, or any marketing activities.

Terms defined in this episode
Brand Identity
The collection of elements, promises, and consistent signals a business presents across every touchpoint that shapes how customers perceive and choose it, ideally making it the only choice in a buyer's mind.
Raving Fans
A concept from the book Raving Fans describing customers so satisfied they actively promote a business, reducing the need for a formal marketing department.

The differentiating factor that sets a product, service, or brand apart from competitors, discussed in the episode as critical for avoiding a race to the bottom on price.
