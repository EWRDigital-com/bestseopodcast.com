Questions this episode answers

How can a business show up in ChatGPT or AI search results?

According to this episode, you can put data into ChatGPT about your business and it gets pulled into the larger data set over time. The host also points to schema markup, rich snippets, and the knowledge graph as strategies that work for both AI and voice search visibility.

Is AI search actually going to replace Google?

The host does not think AI search will fully replace Google, comparing it to how Netflix disrupted Redbox but did not eliminate it entirely. The adoption curve for AI search is expected to take longer than many predict, though it is already reshaping how search works.

What happens to social media marketing if TikTok gets banned in the US?

The host says everything will flow over to Instagram if TikTok is banned in the United States, noting that TikTok is already banned in countries like India. He calls Instagram really, really important as a result and notes YouTube remains the second largest search engine globally.

Terms defined in this episode
AI Overview
A Google feature that displays AI-generated answers at the top of search results. Google implemented it in response to losing search traffic to AI tools like ChatGPT.
Schema Markup
Structured data added to a website that helps Google and AI tools create entities and better understand associations between a business and related topics.
Knowledge Graph
Google's system of entities and associations that schema markup and rich snippets help a business connect to, improving visibility in search and AI results.
Large Language Model (LLM)
AI systems like ChatGPT that answer user queries directly. The host notes the same strategies used for LLM visibility also apply to voice and image search.
