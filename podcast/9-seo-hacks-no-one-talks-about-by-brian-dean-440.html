Questions this episode answers

Why should SEOs check SERP features before targeting a keyword?

When a keyword triggers many SERP features like the Knowledge Graph, shopping results, or video lists, organic click-through volume drops sharply. According to Ahrefs data cited in the episode, only 35% of searchers click an organic result for such terms.

What is a link roundup and how do you get featured in one?

Link roundups are weekly or monthly blog posts that collect the best recent content in a niche. To get featured, search your keyword plus words like link roundup or best of, then send a short professional email explaining why your content is a good fit for the list.

Terms defined in this episode
SERP features
Elements on a search results page beyond standard organic listings, such as the Knowledge Graph, shopping results, and video lists, which can reduce organic click-through rates.
Link roundup
A weekly or monthly blog post that links to the best content recently published in a given industry or topic area.
Bounce rate
The rate at which visitors leave a page quickly after arriving. Pages with low bounce rates tend to outrank pages with high bounce rates, based on an analysis of one million Google search results cited in the episode.
Quality score
A Google Ads metric tied to relevancy and click-through rate that affects both ad placement and cost per click.
