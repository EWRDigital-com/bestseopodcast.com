Questions this episode answers

What is a sales funnel?

A sales funnel combines traffic from sources like Facebook, Google AdWords, YouTube, and Instagram with high-converting landing pages and follow-up sequences such as email and SMS. It is described as a three-part system: traffic, the landing page, and the product or service being sold at the end.

How should upsells and downsells be structured in a sales funnel?

Upsells and downsells should be congruent with the original purchase and add genuine value rather than simply discounting the price. The recommendation is to increase perceived value by adding bonuses instead of cutting the price, because selling on value is stronger than selling on price.

What is the AIDA funnel structure mentioned in the episode?

AIDA stands for awareness, interest, desire, decision, and action. The hosts recommend starting campaigns with an awareness-type offer rather than going straight for a conversion, because most advertisers on Facebook and AdWords already push for an immediate sale, so a staged approach helps a brand stand out.

Should you use paid ads or SEO first for a sales funnel?

The episode recommends SEO as the core marketing strategy, followed by remarketing, then paid ads on top. Paid ads can be used first to get SEO momentum going and to generate demand, then tapered off as SEO takes over, since organic rankings last after ads are turned off.

Terms defined in this episode
Sales Funnel
A system combining traffic sources such as Facebook and AdWords, high-converting landing pages, and email or SMS follow-up sequences to move prospects toward a purchase.
Trip Wire
A low-cost front-end offer, such as a $7 product, used primarily to collect buyer information so the seller can market higher-priced products on the back end.
Irresistible Offer
An offer structured so that the perceived value of what is included far exceeds the price being asked, making it very difficult for a prospect to say no.
AIDA Funnel
A campaign structure based on awareness, interest, desire, decision, and action, used to warm up cold audiences before asking for a conversion.
