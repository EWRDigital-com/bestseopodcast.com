7 Tips For Better Local SEO Reporting by Matthew Bertram Ep. 554

Ep. 55433 min2022-10-294,318 plays

Local SEO reporting improves when agencies simplify reports, choose metrics tied to business goals, segment keywords and traffic by location and device, and always end with actionable next steps.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Simplify reports by setting clear goals and eliminating metrics unrelated to the client's actual business goals.
  • Segment keywords by location using tools like SE Ranking or SEMrush to get accurate local rank data.
  • Mobile search traffic is arguably more important than desktop for local SEO campaigns.
  • Google Business Profile listings can be a higher source of conversions and foot traffic than a business website.
  • End every report with actionable recommendations so clients know exactly what needs to happen next.
Complicated reports with unnecessary data distract from the campaign successes that you're actually trying to share.

About this episode

Make your report comprehensive for local business clients. Here's how to set up your SEO reports to help local clients.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most important metrics to include in a local SEO report?

According to this episode, most local businesses care most about traffic and conversions. Matt Bertram recommends six metrics from Google Analytics: top landing pages, location by city, overall sessions, top traffic channels by sessions, traffic sources and medium, and new versus returning customers.

Why should local SEO reports segment traffic by device?

Mobile search traffic is argued to be more important than desktop for local SEO, especially when factoring in click to call and mobile ordering. The episode notes traffic ratios have shifted from roughly 70 percent desktop to at least 80 to 20 or even 90 to 10 in favor of mobile.

How should reporting periods be chosen for local SEO campaigns?

The episode recommends matching the reporting period to how often data is reviewed, whether every two weeks or every 30 days. It also advises comparing against the same period from the prior year to account for seasonality rather than only looking week over week.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Business Profile
Formerly known as GMB, this listing can be a significantly higher source of conversions and foot traffic to a business than their actual website, and is key to ranking in near me searches.
Local Pack
A set of local search results highlighted by Google; keyword tools like SE Ranking offer data on whether a business appears in the local pack.
Unlinked Brand Mentions
References to a brand online that do not include a hyperlink back to the site; listed in the episode as an additional KPI to include in local SEO reports.
Position Tracking
A feature in tools like SE Ranking that monitors keyword rankings for searches in a specific geographic area such as a metropolitan area or set of GPS coordinates.
