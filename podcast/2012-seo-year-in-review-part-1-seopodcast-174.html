Questions this episode answers

How did eWebStyle avoid the Google Penguin penalty in 2012?

According to the hosts, they avoided the Penguin penalty because their linking was legitimate and diversified. They did not rely heavily on exact match anchor text, and they began changing their approach as soon as Google signaled that exact match domains and manipulative linking could be penalized.

What is link sculpting and why does it matter for SEO?

Link sculpting refers to organizing the links on a page so that primary keyword-rich navigation links appear first, which causes search engine spiders to index those pages first. The hosts explain that sub-navigation links targeting specific service pages should be prioritized because they carry the most SEO impact.

Should a new e-commerce site copy product descriptions from the manufacturer?

No. The hosts explain that copying manufacturer content is duplicate content and will rarely rank in organic search results. The original manufacturer is seen as the source, so the copy will not outrank them. Rewriting descriptions is necessary for any SEO benefit, though PPC can still be used without rewritten content.

How does being logged into a Google account affect SEO ranking reports?

If a user is logged into their Google account, their personal search history affects the results they see, which can make their own pages appear higher than they actually rank for everyone else. The hosts note that adding the parameter PWS equals zero to the URL removes this personalized effect.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Plus Local
The name Google used for its combined local business listing and review platform after merging what had been called Google Places with Google Plus profiles.
Link sculpting
The practice of organizing a page's navigation links so that the most keyword-rich and important links appear first, causing search engine spiders to index those pages with higher priority.
Negative SEO
An attempt by a competitor to harm a site's rankings by pointing low-quality or unrelated links at it. The hosts note Google essentially assigns zero value rather than negative value to such links.
Quality score
A Google AdWords metric that rises when a landing page is well optimized for the keywords in its ad group, resulting in better ad placement and lower cost per click.
