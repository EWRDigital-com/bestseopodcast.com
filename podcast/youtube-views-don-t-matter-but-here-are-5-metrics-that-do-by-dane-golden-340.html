Key takeaways
  • Paid pre-roll ad views count toward a video's official view count, making raw view totals misleading.
  • YouTube ranks videos by watch time, meaning a video with 4,000 views can outrank one with 40,000 views.
  • Subscribers gained per video is a key health signal; if people are not subscribing, question the video's purpose.
  • Most YouTube views come from suggested videos in the right-hand column, not from search, making thumbnails and playlists critical.
  • Always place a call-to-action link in the first three lines of the YouTube description, above the see more button.

Questions this episode answers

Why don't YouTube views matter as a metric?

Views do not distinguish paid from organic traffic, do not show how much of the video was watched, do not directly drive sales, no longer determine search ranking, and can be purchased cheaply, for around $10,000 per million views according to the episode.

Terms defined in this episode
Organic Audience Retention
A YouTube analytics heat map showing how much of a video viewers watch on average, which YouTube uses as a core ranking signal under the broader concept of watch time.
Organic Subscriber Velocity
How quickly a channel's existing subscribers watch a newly posted video; getting subscribers to watch within 24 to 48 hours is described as a positive ranking signal.
Suggested Videos
The right-hand column of video thumbnails YouTube displays to users after or during a video; the episode states most YouTube views come from this placement rather than from search.
Discoverability
Dane Golden's preferred term over video SEO, because YouTube videos are found through multiple paths including search, suggested videos, and other factors, not only through search queries.
