A strong website must come before SEO investment; driving traffic to a poor site wastes budget because visitors will bounce and convert with competitors who have better pages.

Key takeaways
  • Fix your website before starting SEO, or first-page rankings will not produce sales.
  • Search rankings fluctuate constantly due to algorithm changes and competitor activity, so one bad report is not a crisis.
  • A business owner's time is better spent closing deals than spending two to three hours daily on meta tags.
  • Website visitors decide within about ten seconds whether to stay or bounce to a competitor's site.

About this episode

We talk about the specific things on your website that are more important then great search engine placement. If you only have 10 seconds to win your visitors over then what should you be working one. We also talk about how every time you look at your search engine placement you might find your website in a different position.

Questions this episode answers

Should I invest in SEO if my website is poor quality?

No. Getting to the first page of Google with a low-quality website means visitors will compare your site to competitors, find it less appealing, and bounce. The hosts say the right first step is to fix the website before spending on SEO, because traffic without conversion produces no ROI.

Why do search rankings drop even when an SEO campaign is working?

Rankings are in constant flux because competitors change their sites, new websites appear, and Google regularly updates its algorithm. The hosts note that 24 of 30 keywords staying on the first page is still a strong result even when a monthly report shows many position drops highlighted in red.

How do E-WebStyle's monthly SEO reports work?

E-WebStyle sends full-color printed reports with each invoice. Position increases are shown in green and drops in red. The hosts review reports manually rather than sending automated emails, so they can spot issues, alert the SEO team, and prepare clients for questions about fluctuations.

When should a small business owner stop doing their own SEO?

When the business is generating enough revenue from SEO traffic, the owner's time is better spent closing sales and serving customers. The hosts argue that an SEO expert handles optimization more efficiently, freeing the owner to focus on the most profitable activities in their own business.

Terms defined in this episode
keyword campaign
A set of 15 keywords that E-WebStyle tracks and optimizes for a client as a single campaign unit.
position fluctuation
The regular up-and-down movement of a website's search ranking caused by competitor activity, website changes, and Google algorithm updates.
bounce
When a visitor immediately leaves a website by hitting the back button and returns to search results, often because the site fails to capture attention within the first few seconds.
inbound links
Links from other websites pointing to your site, described in the episode as one of the fundamental elements to maintain for consistent search rankings.
