More on Google Local Places - Internet Marketing Podcast - Number 94

Episode39 min2011-03-02

Google Places optimization requires consistent NAP data, one video, five photos, a matching Google account, and city-first title tags to improve local search rankings, while avoiding duplicate listings and the areas-served feature.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Upload at least one video to YouTube and five photos to Picasa to complete your Google Places listing.
  • Avoid using the areas-served feature in Google Places, as experts including Dave Basu reported ranking drops when selecting it.
  • Multiple Places pages at the same address can suck listing strength, trigger merges, or lead to a penalty.
  • Put the keyword first in your title tag, then city and state, to boost organic ranking for local searches.
  • If you did not build your own website, check your title tags because they are often wrong or missing keywords.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search Engine Visitor Optimization, a term the hosts say they coined, focused on what to do with traffic once it arrives at your website after you have optimized for Google.
Google Places
Google's local business listing platform that pulls data from multiple directories and links to a business website, used to rank businesses in local search results.
White Spark Local Citation Finder
A tool mentioned by listener James Slattery that helps find local citation opportunities for businesses.
CTA
Call to action, a prompt that encourages a user to take a specific step, such as using a coupon, which the hosts say can also increase click-through ratio on a Places page.
