4 Most Important Ranking Factors According to SEO Industry Studies by Aleh Barysevich #383

Ep. 38337 min2017-07-25

The four most important SEO ranking factors, drawn from studies by Search Metrics, Backlinko, and SEO Power Suite, are comprehensive content, high-quality backlinks, mobile-first user experience, and other technical factors like HTTPS and H1 tags.

Key takeaways
  • Comprehensive content that fully covers a subject outperforms short, keyword-stuffed pages, with top-ranking articles often reaching 3,500 words or more.
  • Backlink quality matters far more than quantity now, and auditing competitor link profiles can reveal easy high-value link opportunities.
  • Google indexes mobile versions first, so launching an unfinished mobile site can hurt your desktop rankings.
  • HTTPS encryption, proper H1 and H2 tags, and diverse anchor text all show strong correlation with higher Google rankings.
  • Thin content should be found and fixed by consolidating related pages with 301 redirects to create longer, more authoritative content.

Questions this episode answers

What are the four most important SEO ranking factors according to industry studies?

According to studies by Search Metrics, Backlinko, and SEO Power Suite, as compiled by Aleh Barysevich, the four most important ranking factors are comprehensive content written in natural language, high-quality backlinks, mobile-first user experience, and other technical factors such as HTTPS, H1 tags, and anchor text diversity.

How long should SEO content be to rank well?

The episode cites a 3,500-word article ranking second only to Wikipedia for a golden retriever search as an example. Hosts suggest 2,000 words as a good target for blogs, and note that 5,000-word or longer high-quality articles could rank even better.

How should site owners handle irrelevant traffic-driving content?

The hosts suggest two options: remove pages with irrelevant content since they may hurt overall SEO due to context issues with RankBrain, or find a way to blend the content toward your core business, such as linking it to a related service you offer.

What technical SEO factors correlate most strongly with higher Google rankings?

The episode highlights HTTPS encryption, presence of H1 and H2 heading tags, diverse and semantically relevant anchor text to avoid a Penguin penalty, and avoiding interstitial pop-ups especially on mobile as the key technical factors showing strong correlation with good rankings.

Terms defined in this episode
RankBrain
A Google system that evaluates context and relevance of content, making irrelevant pages potentially damaging to a site's overall SEO standing.
Skyscraping
A content strategy mentioned in the episode as still effective, referenced in the article covered on the podcast.
Penguin penalty
A Google penalty triggered by unnatural anchor text patterns, requiring diverse and semantically relevant anchor text to avoid.
Mobile-first indexing
Google indexes the mobile version of a website first, meaning an incomplete or content-stripped mobile site can negatively impact overall search rankings.
