Getting More Out of your Conversion Confirmation Page - #seopodcast 212

Episode27 min2013-12-27

Conversion confirmation pages can drive repeat purchases, social sharing, and remarketing by adding thanks, related products, supporting content, shipping expectations, orientation steps, and social prompts.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Add a genuine thank-you message to your confirmation page to mirror the in-person experience of gratitude.
  • Feature related or upcoming products on the thank-you page while the customer's wallet is still open.
  • Provide supporting content like how-to videos or third-party reviews to reduce buyer's remorse right after purchase.
  • Under-promise on shipping time and over-deliver so customers share the positive surprise with others.
  • Use the confirmation page to start a remarketing list and retarget buyers based on what they specifically purchased.

Questions this episode answers

What should you put on a conversion confirmation page to increase sales?

According to this episode, you should say thanks, confirm your brand value, show related products for future purchases, deliver supporting content like how-to videos, set shipping expectations, give orientation steps, and use the page to start a remarketing list for future ads.

How can a thank-you page help with social media growth?

The hosts recommend asking buyers to like your Facebook page, share their purchase, or post a photo on Instagram in exchange for a discount or free shipping on the next order, since a customer who just spent money is more likely to act than someone still undecided.

How does remarketing work from a conversion confirmation page?

You can create segmented lists of buyers based on what they purchased on the confirmation page, then use those lists to show targeted ads for related products, for example showing new cologne ads only to people who bought a specific cologne brand.

What tactic is missing from the seven-point article discussed in this episode?

The hosts say the article by Joseph Kirscherbaum on Search Engine Watch omitted social media. They argue the confirmation page is the ideal moment to prompt a Facebook like, a purchase share, or a photo post with an incentive attached.

Terms defined in this episode
Conversion confirmation page
The thank-you page a visitor sees after completing a purchase, download, or quote request, used to confirm the action and continue marketing to the buyer.
Remarketing
Running targeted ads to people who have already visited your site or made a purchase, using lists built from pages like the conversion confirmation page.
CTA in meta description
Placing a call to action such as a phone number or value statement inside the meta description so it appears in organic search results and begins marketing before the click.
