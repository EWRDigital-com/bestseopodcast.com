Videos and how they affect SEO - #seopdocast 177

Episode19 min2013-02-04

Video SEO beginners should focus on quality content, keyword-rich titles and tags, transcripts, short runtimes, video sitemaps, branding, and social sharing to improve search rankings.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Keep videos at one to two minutes when possible, since the average YouTube user stays about one and a half minutes.
  • Include target keywords in the first two or three sentences of your spoken audio, because Google transcribes video.
  • Add a transcript in the video description so Google can index your content as HTML.
  • Watermark or brand your videos so that if a video goes viral, viewers know who created it.
  • Concentrate views on one platform rather than splitting them across many, to build stronger signals in one place.

Questions this episode answers

How long should a video be for SEO purposes?

The article discussed in this episode recommends five minutes or less, but the hosts suggest targeting closer to one and a half to two minutes, since the average time a user spends on YouTube is around one and a half minutes. Keeping videos short and to the point serves users better.

Do video transcripts help with SEO?

Yes. The episode recommends providing a transcript of your video because Google transcribes audio in videos and indexes the text. Adding an accurate transcript in the description as HTML helps the video rank higher. Keyword stuffing a fake transcript will not work because Google compares it to the actual audio.

Should you put your video on multiple platforms or just one?

The hosts lean toward concentrating views on one platform. Spreading the same video across YouTube, Vimeo, and other sites may split traffic so each platform gets only a small number of views, while keeping it in one place can build a stronger signal with more concentrated view counts.

Why is branding important in video SEO?

The episode warns that a video can go viral and generate millions of views, but if you did not brand it with a watermark or logo, viewers will not know who created it. Branding during video production ensures that brand awareness and potential leads are captured even from unexpected viral traffic.

Terms defined in this episode
Video sitemap
A sitemap specifically for video content that is submitted to Google so that search spiders can find and index video files on a site.
Video transcription
A written text version of the spoken audio in a video, placed in the description to give Google readable HTML content and improve video rankings.
SIVO video
A website analysis video produced by eWebStyle where the hosts review a submitted site on screen and point out what is right and wrong with it.
