Questions this episode answers

How should a brand approach starting a podcast?

Build a full deck first: define the target listener persona, research the competition, lay out potential advertisers, and plan segments before naming the show. The concept and audience definition are the most important steps, and the host should go through training before recording the first episode.

What makes podcast advertising more effective than other formats?

Hosts who integrate ads naturally into the content build listener trust and a sense of community support. A study cited in the episode found that 90% of podcast listeners have acted on an ad heard on a podcast, far outperforming traditional radio or interruption-style placements.

What is the most important metric to track for a podcast?

Retention rate is the single most important number. It can be checked on Spotify. A retention rate above 60% signals the content is worth promoting. Most podcasters overlook this metric and jump straight to promotion before confirming the content actually holds the audience.

Terms defined in this episode
Branded podcast
A podcast created on behalf of a company that centers on a topic or value relevant to its target audience rather than directly promoting its products, used to build brand positioning and reach new listeners.
Programmatic podcast ads
Ads inserted automatically into podcast episodes by platforms like Megaphone, where advertisers buy audience numbers in bulk rather than selecting specific shows, which can mean less contextual fit and unverified download counts.
Retention rate
The percentage of a podcast episode that listeners actually finish, checkable on Spotify. Matty Staudt recommends reaching above 60% before investing in promotion.
Seed content
A piece of long-form content, such as a podcast episode, used as the source from which shorter clips, articles, and social media posts are derived across multiple platforms.
