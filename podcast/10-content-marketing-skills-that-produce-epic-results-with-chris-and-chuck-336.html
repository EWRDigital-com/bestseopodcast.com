10 Content Marketing Skills That Produce Epic Results with Chris and Chuck #336

Ep. 33632 min2016-09-01

Effective content marketers must master 10 skills including in-depth research, trend tracking, multi-channel distribution, data analysis, and collaboration to produce content that reaches audiences and drives results.

Key takeaways
  • In-depth research means knowing the exact terminology and search queries your audience uses, not just what you assume they search.
  • Distributing content across every available channel, email, social, video, and podcasts, is required to maximize reach.
  • Reading analytics data like time on page, referral source, and goal completions tells you whether content is actually working.
  • A detailed editorial calendar and monthly content strategy prevents random, unfocused publishing and keeps efforts aligned.
  • Collaborating with non-competing but relevant businesses expands reach into each other's audience without competing for the same clients.
content marketing is not a said it and forget it kind of situation

About this episode

Does your content marketing produce results? Writing content is great, writing relevant content will get you results. You can’t just write an article and forget it. Writing the proper content for SEO takes research, editing, and revisiting, sometimes multiple times. Chris and Chuck will show you how to leverage trends, do the proper research and tell a story to engage your readers in Best SEO Podcast 336.Presented…

Questions this episode answers

How should content marketers distribute content after publishing?

The hosts say to tweet it, post it on Facebook and boost it, do a Facebook Live about it, email it, include it in a newsletter, turn it into slides for SlideShare, make a video summary, and post that on Facebook and Instagram. Using every available channel is the goal.

Why is having a content strategy and editorial calendar important?

Without a clear strategy you end up throwing content against the wall to see if it sticks. A detailed editorial calendar lets you plan what to write, when to post it, and how to promote it across multiple channels, then rinse and repeat consistently each month.

How can small local businesses approach long-form content without wasting resources?

The hosts say five to six hundred words of original, relevant content is often better for small service providers like roofers or plumbers than investing in 1,500 words. Long-form investment makes more sense for big brands with multiple locations presenting themselves as industry experts.

Terms defined in this episode
interstitial
Anything that prevents your original content from being seen once a page loads, such as a pop-up or splash page, which Google announced a penalty for on mobile web pages.
editorial calendar
A detailed plan that defines what content will be produced, when it will be posted, and how it will be promoted, described as a sign of an all-star content marketer.
shikos
A term used by the hosts to mean shares, likes, and follows on any social media profile.
