#SEOpodcast 274 - Investing in your Content

Episode32 min2015-05-13

Small businesses should invest in content marketing because consistent, quality content builds brand authority, increases web traffic, and makes customers more likely to buy and refer others.

Key takeaways
  • Consistent content publishing builds brand confidence and keeps your audience returning for more.
  • Plan content types that fit your industry, such as videos, blogs, newsletters, or podcasts, and schedule distribution.
  • Content marketing forces you to stay current in your field, which prevents losing credibility with clients.
  • 60% of consumers feel more positive about a company after reading content it created.
  • Engaging with followers through comments, retweets, and responses drives willingness to share your content.

Questions this episode answers

Why should small businesses invest more in content marketing?

According to the episode, consistent quality content increases web traffic, solidifies your position as an expert, builds community loyalty, and makes customers more likely to buy. The episode also notes that 67% of Twitter users are likely to purchase from a brand they follow on Twitter.

What are the top three most effective forms of content marketing according to a 2014 study?

According to a 2014 study by the Content Marketing Institute cited in the episode, the top three effective forms of content are case studies, in-person events, and blogs. The hosts add that the best types depend on your industry and recommend finding the top three that work for your specific business.

How does content marketing help a business owner stay competitive?

The episode explains that producing regular content forces business owners and marketers to constantly research and study their industry. This keeps them up to date and ensures they can answer client questions confidently, avoiding the loss of credibility that comes from not knowing what you should know.

How can a business make content creation easier for a busy owner?

The hosts suggest that the business owner provide bullet points and key concepts, then let a content team draft the article. They note it is significantly easier to edit something than it is to write it, which saves the owner time while keeping the content accurate and relevant.

Terms defined in this episode
Content Marketing
A strategy of consistently creating and distributing valuable, relevant content to build brand awareness, engage audiences, and drive purchases. The episode notes it has been in practice since at least 1895.
NAP
Name, Address, and Phone number. The episode recommends keeping NAP consistent across your site, Google Plus page, Yelp, and any other directory to help rank in Google Maps local listings.
Mopocalypse
A nickname used in the episode for Google's April 21st mobile algorithm update, which was expected to impact sites not optimized for mobile search.
Content Schedule
A planned calendar for when specific pieces of content, such as blog posts, newsletters, and social media posts, will be published and distributed throughout the month.
