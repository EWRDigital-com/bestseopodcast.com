Questions this episode answers

What is entity SEO and why does it matter for AI search?

Entity SEO organizes information about organizations, places, and authors and expresses their relationships through structured data. This helps search engines and generative AI engines understand connections between data points without processing entire pages, making it critical for visibility in AI-driven results.

Is structured data still worth implementing now that AI overviews exist?

Yes. Both Bing and Google have stated that structured data remains valuable and that AI crawlers and engines rely on it extensively. Zach Chahalis argues that FAQ schema and other structured data still help generative engines find and surface your content correctly.

How should SEOs prioritize content when covering a large site?

Zach recommends focusing deeply on one topic area at a time, becoming authoritative there before moving on. Trying to cover everything at once dilutes effort. He also advises pruning outdated content, such as pages about laws that no longer apply, to sharpen topical focus.

How fast is AI mode being adopted and should SEOs panic now?

At the time of recording, AI mode adoption was hovering around 1% to 2% even with Google actively pushing it. Zach believes there is still time before AI mode becomes the default experience, though he expects it will eventually become more standard.

Terms defined in this episode
Entity SEO
The practice of defining and connecting entities such as organizations, places, and authors through structured data and contextual linking so search engines understand the relationships between them.
Structured Data / JSON-LD Schema
Machine-readable markup added to pages that distills page content into a digestible format for crawlers and generative engines, reducing the need to process the full page to understand its meaning and relationships.
Vector Embeddings
A way of representing content so that search and generative engines understand how pieces of content relate to one another mathematically, moving beyond keyword matching toward semantic relationships.
Crawl Budget
The amount of a website that search engines and generative engines actually crawl and process; wasted crawl budget on low-value pages reduces how much of a site is seen and indexed.
