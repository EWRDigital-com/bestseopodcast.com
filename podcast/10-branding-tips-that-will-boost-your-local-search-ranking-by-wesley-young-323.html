10 Branding Tips That Will Boost Your Local Search Ranking by Wesley Young #323

Ep. 32335 min2016-06-09

Local businesses can boost search rankings by targeting local publications, earning reviews across multiple directories, using community lingo, engaging offline and online, and building brand recognition that drives branded searches.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Get listed and mentioned in local and hyper-local publications, including neighborhood and niche circulars, to earn relevant inbound links.
  • Ask for reviews on Google, Yelp, TripAdvisor, Yellow Pages, and other directory sites to build positive brand signals.
  • Use local lingo and neighborhood nicknames in web content so local readers instantly recognize you as one of them.
  • Sponsor community events, local teams, and organizations to generate offline word-of-mouth that converts to online engagement.
  • Offline branding like vehicle wraps drives people to search your brand name, creating a measurable local search signal for Google.

About this episode

Branding is often viewed as the domain of large national businesses. Yet small businesses will benefit from branding strategically on a local level. This week Chris and Charles discuss "10 tips for building a local brand that will boost your local search ranking", an article from Wesley Young over at Search Engine Land.

Questions this episode answers

How does wrapping a vehicle help local SEO rankings?

When people see a branded vehicle, they may search for that business by name on mobile. Google registers that branded search as a signal that the brand has value in that geographic area. This drives a transition from offline activity to online engagement, which can improve local search visibility.

What types of local publications should a business target for local SEO?

The episode recommends targeting large local newspapers, county publications, and hyper-local niche publications such as neighborhood circulars, publications targeting specific cultural groups, and church circulars. Most of these have corresponding websites in 2016, making it possible to earn inbound links alongside print mentions.

Why should a local business borrow branding from national manufacturers?

National manufacturers invest heavily in their own branding. If a local business is a certified installer or preferred dealer, getting listed on the manufacturer's website earns a high-quality, relevant inbound link. That link can improve the local business's search rankings significantly.

When does it make sense to run a branded paid ad without a call to action?

According to the episode, a branded ad campaign without a direct call to action only makes sense when a business already has both a map listing and an organic ranking. In that case, the paid impression reinforces brand familiarity and can highlight the business's unique selling proposition rather than push for a conversion.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP
Name, Address, and Phone number. The episode references maintaining a consistent NAP across all major directories as a foundational step before pursuing other local branding tactics.
Hyper-local publication
A smaller, narrowly targeted print or online publication serving a specific neighborhood, cultural group, or community niche, used to reach highly local audiences and earn relevant links.
Brand signal
Online activity such as fans, followers, likes, and check-ins that tells Google a business is liked and recognized, contributing to local search ranking.
Co-op branding
A local business affiliating itself with a national brand, such as being listed as a preferred dealer on a manufacturer's website, to borrow brand recognition and earn authoritative inbound links.
