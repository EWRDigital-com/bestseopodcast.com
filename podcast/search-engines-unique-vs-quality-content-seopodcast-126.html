Search Engines Unique Vs Quality Content - #seopodcast 126

Episode35 min2011-12-06

Search engines reward content that is both unique and quality; keyword stuffing hurts rankings, link quality beats quantity, and each page should target a single focused keyword phrase for best SEO results.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Keyword density should stay around 3%; exceeding that signals stuffing and search engines penalize it.
  • Link quality matters more than quantity; a link from a high-PR trusted site beats many links from obscure blogs.
  • Each page should focus on one keyword phrase, or closely related phrases, not several unrelated targets at once.
  • Unique content and relevant content together create quality content; copying others can get a site banned, especially after Panda.
  • Google encrypting referral data for logged-in users means SEOs may lose keyword-level traffic data for a growing share of visitors.

Questions this episode answers

What is the recommended keyword density to avoid being penalized by search engines?

The hosts recommend keeping keyword density at around 3%. If a term appears abnormally often in your content, search engines will count that against you rather than for you. Tools like SEO Quake, a Firefox plugin, can show you your keyword density after a page is live.

How should you think about unique content versus quality content for SEO?

According to this episode, unique content and relevant content are two separate circles, and where they overlap you get quality. Content must be original, meaning you create it yourself, and it must be relevant to the subject. Copying others, called site jacking in the episode, can get a site banned, especially after Google Panda.

Why does Google encrypting referral data matter for SEO keyword tracking?

When users are logged into a Google account, Google encrypts the keyword referral data sent to analytics platforms. Site owners can no longer see which specific keywords drove those visitors. The hosts note this affects less than 10% currently, but as Gmail, Android, and Google Plus users grow, the impact will increase.

What is the right approach to link building according to this episode?

Focus on quality over quantity. A link from a high-PR, trusted site like MSN carries more value than many links from obscure blogs. The type of link also matters; a link from a relevant industry source, like a flooring manufacturer linking to a flooring installer, is especially valuable.

Terms defined in this episode
Keyword density
The ratio of how many times a keyword appears in your content relative to total text. The episode recommends staying around 3% to avoid search engine penalties for keyword stuffing.
Referral data
Information passed to a landing page identifying which keywords a visitor used to find it. Google began encrypting this for logged-in users, making keyword-level traffic tracking impossible for that segment.
Link anchor text
The clickable text of a hyperlink. The episode notes the text surrounding a link should also relate to the target keywords, not just the anchor text itself.
EDU links
Links from domains with a .edu extension, which search engines give strong weight. The episode suggests searching for nonprofit EDU sites looking for sponsors as a way to earn them.
