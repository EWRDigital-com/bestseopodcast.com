5 Things You Can Do Right Now to Improve Your Google E-A-T RatingWant to improve your chances of ranking well in Google's search results? Here are five ways to show your expertise, authority, and trustworthiness.

Questions this episode answers

What is a Google Quality Rater and why do they matter for SEO?

Quality Raters are humans who assign quality scores to search results for specific queries. Those scores do not directly impact rankings but are used to develop and refine the algorithm. The Quality Rater Guidelines, about 175 pages, outline what good, medium, and low quality content looks like.

What does YMYL mean and why does it affect E-A-T?

YMYL stands for Your Money or Your Life. These pages cover topics like finance or health where bad information causes real harm. Google holds YMYL pages to the highest E-A-T standards, requiring trusted and authoritative expert authors and heavy moderation of any user-generated content.

How can a site owner build author authority without a formal credential?

According to the episode, creating consistent authorship across platforms such as Medium and Alignable, using a Gravatar, and producing content that demonstrates real experience, for example showing you own and clean a boat when writing about boat cleaning, builds legitimate trustworthiness and authority over time.

Terms defined in this episode
E-A-T
Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google uses E-A-T as a quality signal, especially for YMYL pages, evaluated partly through the reputation of content creators.
YMYL
Your Money or Your Life. Pages covering topics like finance or health that can significantly impact a person's wellbeing, held to stricter E-A-T standards by Google.
Google Quality Rater Guidelines
A roughly 175-page document used by human raters to assign quality scores to search results, serving as a road map for what Google algorithms look for in content.
Same As schema
A schema.org property used to link together an author's various online profiles, helping Google recognize authorship as a single connected entity.
