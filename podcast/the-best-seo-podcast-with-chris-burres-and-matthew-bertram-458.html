Questions this episode answers

Does SEO still matter in a world dominated by paid ads?

Yes, according to this episode. Claims that SEO is dead are described as link bait meant to attract attention. SEO is called supremely important and growing more so, though success now requires a broader strategy and not just a single tactic.

What is the difference between SEO and SEM?

SEO refers to organic placement, including Google Maps and natural search results, and excludes paid ads. SEM is the broader category that includes pay-per-click advertising. On mobile, the first screen is now dominated by ads, making the organic and paid distinction practically significant.

How do you find a good SEO company?

The episode recommends spending time with the company before hiring, checking whether they rank for their own target keywords, reviewing case studies, and evaluating whether their sales process feels structured. A company without a clear process in sales likely lacks one throughout the entire campaign.

Is SEO cost effective compared to PPC?

The episode says SEO can deliver 2x, 3x, 5x, or 10x the return of PPC spending. However, cost-effectiveness depends on who does the work, product margins, keyword search volume, and how long it takes to reach a positive ROI threshold.

Terms defined in this episode
Double-siloing
A strategy where an SEO agency ranks both a homepage and an internal page for the same keyword, taking two of the top ten positions on a single Google results page.
SEM
Search engine marketing, the broader category that includes both organic SEO and pay-per-click advertising.
Gulf Stream effect
A term used in this episode to describe how websites in the top three organic positions tend to stay there due to accumulated ranking momentum.
Link bait
Content designed primarily to attract clicks and backlinks through a provocative claim, such as articles asserting that SEO no longer matters.
