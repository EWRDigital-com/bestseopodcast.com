Questions this episode answers

What is Google Intelligence in Google Analytics and what does it do?

Google Intelligence is a feature inside Google Analytics, listed as the second link on the left margin, that automatically applies statistical analysis to your site data. It flags anomalies, meaning metrics that fall outside the standard deviation, across daily, weekly, and monthly time periods so you can investigate unusual changes.

Can I set up my own custom alerts in Google Intelligence?

Yes. Beyond the automatic alerts Google creates, you can configure custom alert types by visitor type, city, region, country, campaign, keyword, traffic source, medium, referral path, landing page, or exit page, and set thresholds such as when traffic increases or decreases by a certain percentage.

How does adding a city name to a keyword affect SEO difficulty?

Adding a geographic term like a city name to a keyword localizes the phrase and reduces competition. For example, routers is harder to rank for nationally than Houston routers, so targeting city-specific key phrases gives a better chance of reaching the first page of search results.

Why does Google personalization matter for checking your own keyword rankings?

Google personalizes results based on your web history even when you are not signed in. If you have visited a page before, it may appear higher in your results than it does for others, which can give a false read on actual rankings and requires using separate browser sessions to verify.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Intelligence
A feature inside Google Analytics that applies statistical analysis to site metrics and automatically flags anomalies, things outside the standard deviation, in daily, weekly, and monthly traffic data.
Standard Deviation
A statistics concept used by Google Intelligence to identify data points that are unusually high or low compared to normal patterns, triggering an automatic alert.
Google Personalization
A Google feature that uses a visitor's web history, even without sign-in, to return search results tailored to that individual's past behavior and interests.
NFL City Campaign
An SEO campaign strategy targeting the 33 cities with National Football League teams, used as a proxy for markets large enough to have sufficient disposable income to pursue.
