Questions this episode answers

Why does reputation management matter more than traditional SEO for LLM visibility?

LLMs evaluate a brand's overall reputation across reviews, social mentions, website content, and third-party platforms when deciding what to surface. Ross Barefoot argues SEO is now a subset of reputation management because the models ask what your reputation is, not just where you rank on Google.

How are LLMs deciding which sources to cite when answering search queries?

When a query requires fresh or local information, LLMs conduct retrieval augmented generation, essentially reading top search results. That means Google rankings still matter as a gatekeeper. The episode also notes content from position 21 or beyond is being surfaced if it genuinely answers a question.

What is website pruning and why does it help with AI search visibility?

Pruning means removing or de-indexing thin, off-topic, or templated content so a site sends cleaner relevance signals. Ross and Matt both describe using this strategy to help LLMs and Google identify a site as a true authority in its core subject area, comparing it to making a website more aerodynamic.

How can businesses use NotebookLM to keep up with AI search changes?

Ross Barefoot describes adding bulk newsletter links into NotebookLM using a browser extension, then querying across all those sources once a week to get a summarized list of top updates. This lets a team stay current without reading every individual article or newsletter.

Terms defined in this episode
Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG)
A process where LLMs, instead of relying solely on training data, go out and conduct a live search to pull fresh or local information before generating an answer, meaning Google rankings still influence what gets cited.
EEAT
A Google quality framework standing for Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness, used to evaluate content quality, with the Experience component added to distinguish human firsthand knowledge from AI-generated content.
Search Everywhere Optimization
The idea that brands must maintain visibility across multiple platforms including social media, review sites, forums, and LLMs rather than focusing only on traditional search engine rankings.
NotebookLM
A Google tool that lets users upload multiple sources such as newsletters and YouTube videos, then query across all of them at once, used in this episode as a way to synthesize fast-moving SEO and AI search news.
