Episode28 min2014-04-23

Key takeaways
  • Place the AdWords remarketing script on your site at launch so your audience list starts building immediately.
  • Set cookie duration to match your sales cycle: 30 days for fast purchases, 90 days or more for longer decisions.
  • Segment your remarketing lists by page visited, abandoned cart, or previous customers to serve more relevant ads.
  • Top-bidding on remarketing placements raises ad visibility while still keeping click costs far below search network rates.
  • Placing display ads on specific sites like Discovery Channel that match your TV buy amplifies commercial reach at low cost.

Questions this episode answers

What is remarketing and how does it work?

Remarketing shows ads to people who previously visited your site. A script from AdWords drops a cookie on their browser, and Google's display network then serves your ads to those visitors as they browse other sites. The cookie window can be set from 30 to 90 days or more depending on your sales cycle.

What are the main types of remarketing?

The episode covers four types: site remarketing, which targets past visitors; search remarketing, which targets people searching relevant terms; social media remarketing, demonstrated by visiting Overstock and then seeing product ads on Facebook; and email remarketing, which places ads inside Gmail inboxes based on email content.

How should you measure remarketing success?

The hosts recommend comparing money spent on remarketing to revenue generated, then comparing that return against other marketing channels such as TV, radio, and print. Accurate tracking through different phone numbers or landing pages is essential to make a fair comparison.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing
A tactic where an AdWords script drops a cookie on a site visitor's browser, then serves that visitor your ads across Google's display network for up to 90 days or more after the visit.
Display Network
Google's network of sites that show banner ads, including leaderboard, vertical, and square formats, where advertisers can place ads broadly or restrict them to specific site placements.
Abandoned Cart Visitors
A remarketing audience segment made up of users who started a purchase or sign-up process on your site but did not complete it.
Search Remarketing
Targeting ads to users based on search terms they have used, even if they have not yet visited your site, to reach people browsing content relevant to your offer.
