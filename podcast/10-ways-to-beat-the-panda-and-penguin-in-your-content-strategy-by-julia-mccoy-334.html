10 Ways to Beat The Panda and Penguin In Your Content Strategy by Julia McCoy #334

Ep. 33432 min2016-08-19

To avoid Google Panda and Penguin penalties, content must be high quality, properly formatted, keyword-density-controlled, evergreen where possible, long-form when appropriate, on-topic, and designed to attract natural links.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Keep keyword density below 3% and use synonyms to avoid over-optimization penalties.
  • Update old blog posts with new information rather than deleting them to recapture search traffic.
  • Evergreen content such as glossaries and FAQs stays relevant long-term and attracts consistent organic traffic.
  • Long-form content works best for complex sales processes but is not always necessary for simple service pages.
  • Link-worthy content including infographics, polls, and educational posts attracts natural backlinks without spam risk.

About this episode

Are Panda and Penguin bringing you down? Chris and Chuck review "10 Ways to Beat The Panda and Penguin In Your Content Strategy", an article by VIP Contributor Julia McCoy over at Search Engine Journal.

Questions this episode answers

How does Google Panda choose which websites to penalize?

According to the episode, Panda targets sites with bad user experience, thin content, duplicate content, and keyword stuffing. Slow speed and lack of mobile friendliness are also cited as Panda triggers that can put a site in the path of a penalty.

When should you use long-form content for SEO?

The episode says long-form content performs best but is not always necessary. It suits complex sales processes with multiple touch points, like lending. For simpler services like pool cleaning, concise quality content is enough and forcing long-form can cause rambling and errors.

Terms defined in this episode
Panda
A Google algorithm that penalizes sites with thin content, duplicate content, keyword stuffing, bad user experience, and slow or non-mobile-friendly pages. It has become part of Google's core algorithm and refreshes continuously.
Penguin
A Google algorithm geared towards eliminating spam, especially in the form of spammy links. Links coming from Panda-penalized content are treated as Penguin bait.
Evergreen content
Content that remains relevant over time regardless of current events, such as step-by-step instructions, reference material, FAQs, and glossaries relevant to an industry.
Keyword density
A ratio based on the number of times a keyword is used versus the total amount of text in a piece of content. The hosts recommend keeping it below 3% to avoid over-optimization penalties.
