Questions this episode answers

When did eWeb Results start and how did it evolve?

The company started as eWebStyle in 1999, originally delivering websites. Customer demand for search engine rankings pushed it into SEO. A business coach later advised renaming it eWeb Results to reflect its focus on delivering measurable outcomes rather than just design.

How did the eWeb Results SEO podcast grow and why does it matter?

The podcast launched in 2009 and has reached 428 episodes with over 3.7 million downloads across all platforms. It is described as the most popular internet marketing podcast. It now drives roughly 40% of the company's business and keeps the team current on industry changes.

What was the biggest operational failure at eWeb Results and how was it fixed?

The company was not tracking employee time, which led to continual over-delivery that hurt profitability. The fix was adopting Time Doctor software to monitor where hours were going per client, making it possible to manage costs while still delivering strong results.

Can a podcast listener realistically grow traffic by following SEO podcast advice?

According to a testimonial from a listener named Manny, who later became a customer, he grew his traffic from around 200 visits per month to 100,000 visits per month by listening to the podcast and implementing the strategies discussed on it.

Terms defined in this episode
SEO (Search Engine Optimization)
The process of getting a website to appear on the first page of Google; described in the episode as increasingly time-consuming, more involved, and more competitive than in the early days of the web.
Profit Plan
An initial one to two hour consulting session offered by eWeb Results for $4.99, fully refundable, designed to build the right internet marketing strategy and identify pitfalls before a client commits to a full engagement.
Traceable ROI
The ability to connect marketing spend directly to specific customers and revenue, which internet marketing enables and which traditional channels like radio, billboards, or magazine ads do not easily provide.
30-Mile March
A concept from Jim Collins used in the episode to describe consistent, steady output over time, cited as the philosophy behind publishing the SEO podcast week after week regardless of conditions.
