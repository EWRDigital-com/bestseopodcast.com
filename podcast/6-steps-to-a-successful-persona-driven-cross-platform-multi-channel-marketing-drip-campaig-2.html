Questions this episode answers

What is a persona-driven cross-platform multi-channel marketing drip campaign?

It is a campaign that starts by building a detailed avatar of your target customer, then reaches them across traditional and digital platforms through a repeating sequence of emails, phone calls, and physical mail pieces, all supported by ongoing brand awareness advertising.

What is brand mania marketing and when should you use it?

Brand mania marketing is pre-marketing that places ads, including inexpensive banner and YouTube video ads, in front of your target list before the first email is sent. The hosts say you never stop brand mania marketing and recommend five dollars a day to build ongoing brand awareness.

What does the actual drip sequence look like step by step?

The sequence is email or text, then a phone call to verify the contact and gauge interest, then a physical mail piece such as a postcard or 3D mail like a message in a bottle, then another phone call, and then the pattern repeats. Educational content comes first and sales messaging is introduced gradually.

Terms defined in this episode
Brand Mania Marketing
Pre-marketing that runs ads, such as inexpensive banner or YouTube ads, in front of a target list before any direct outreach begins, so contacts already recognize the brand when the first email arrives.
3D Mail
A physical mail piece that includes a three-dimensional object, such as a Rubik's Cube, puzzle piece, or message in a bottle, designed to stand out and ensure it gets opened rather than thrown away.
Remarketing
Showing targeted ads to people who have already engaged with your website or content online, triggered by tracking pixels such as those from Google Analytics.
Shiko
A term used on this podcast meaning a share, a like, and a follow on social media, combined into one action.
