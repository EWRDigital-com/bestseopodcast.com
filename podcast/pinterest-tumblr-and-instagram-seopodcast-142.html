Questions this episode answers

Should I set up social media accounts even if I am not very active on social media?

Yes. Setting up accounts on platforms like Facebook gives your business another place to be found online. However, once you set one up, you should commit to being social or outsource the work, because inactive accounts do not serve your audience or your SEO goals.

How did Google's Panda update affect websites using scraped or spun content?

Panda targeted sites with non-unique content, scraped material, and spun articles. Sites using those tactics likely dropped from page one to page ten or were removed from the index entirely. The fix is to rewrite content and publish original, useful material.

What is the Bermuda Triangle of rel-author tags?

It is a three-part linking structure where the authored page links to a bio page on your domain, that bio page links to your Google Plus profile, and your Google Plus profile links back to the bio page. Completing this loop lets Google pull your profile avatar and display it in search results.

Does site speed have a big impact on SEO rankings?

Matt Cutts stated site speed affected about 1% of search results. The effect is mainly positional, swapping two closely matched pages rather than removing a page from the first page entirely. Very slow pages were likely already being ignored by Google before the update.

Terms defined in this episode
Pinterestification of the Internet
A trend where websites adopt a block-based grid layout similar to Pinterest, replacing traditional banner and sidebar designs with multiple columns of image and text blocks.
Rel-author Bermuda Triangle
A linking structure connecting an authored page to a bio page on the same domain, the bio page to a Google Plus profile, and the Google Plus profile back to the bio page, allowing Google to show an author avatar in search results.
Social signals
Retweets, likes, shares, plus ones, and similar social interactions that Google uses as ranking factors to determine content relevance and importance.
Mayday update
A Google algorithm change focused on long-tail key phrases, rewarding sites with strong content by improving their rankings for less competitive terms that relate to highly competitive phrases.
