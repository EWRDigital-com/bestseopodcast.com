SEO and SEVO for your E-Commerce Site - #seopodcast 185

Episode32 min2013-04-23

E-commerce SEO improves when sites show top-selling products on the homepage, use clear category structures, display persistent shopping carts, and send personalized follow-up emails to encourage reviews and repeat purchases.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Show top-selling products and highest-margin products on your e-commerce homepage.
  • Avoid vague category names; use specific terms like Android tablets instead of just tablets.
  • Make guest checkout more prominent than account registration to reduce cart abandonment.
  • Encourage product reviews on the product page, checkout page, and in follow-up shipping emails.
  • Personalize autoresponder emails with the customer name and purchased product to increase action.
make account registration an optional step of the checkout process

Terms defined in this episode
Panda Everflux
A term used in the episode for Google making Panda a continuous automatic part of its algorithm rather than a manual monthly data refresh, as announced by Matt Cutts at SMX.
Dynamic Keyword Insertion
An AdWords feature where the keyword a user searched for is automatically inserted into the ad title or text, discussed in the episode as the cause of problematic eBay ads on broad match.
Branded Authorship
A Google authorship feature, not yet available at the time of the episode, that would allow a company brand or logo to serve as the author avatar in search results instead of a personal Google+ profile.
Persistent Shopping Cart
A cart that remains visible and consistent across all pages of an e-commerce site throughout the shopping process, as recommended in the episode to reduce bounce and abandonment.
