Search Engine Watch - Three ways to up your video marketing game by Ann Smarty #497

Ep. 49731 min2020-09-09

Video marketing works best when goals are defined first, content is planned as a course, and distribution is spread across owned and third-party platforms rather than relying on YouTube alone.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Define your video marketing goal before spending budget, asking what you are trying to achieve.
  • Plan video content with a course structure in mind so it can later be packaged and monetized.
  • YouTube videos rank well in Google search because Google owns YouTube and treats it as a second-order search engine.
  • Never rely solely on a third-party platform; host video on your own website so you control the content.
  • Diversify video formats across platforms such as Instagram, Facebook, and YouTube to reach different audiences.

Questions this episode answers

What are the three ways to up your video marketing game according to Ann Smarty?

First, define your goals before investing, such as reputation management, building loyalty, or creating monetization. Second, plan your video strategy and each campaign properly, including a roadmap of regular videos. Third, diversify by using multiple formats and platforms rather than depending on one channel like YouTube.

How can video content be monetized beyond ad revenue?

The episode describes several options drawn from Ann Smarty's article: sponsorship opportunities, video creation services, paid content upgrades, video courses, and premium video add-on services. Mapping blog or educational content into a structured sequence can turn it into a sellable video course.

Should you post your videos only on YouTube or also on your own website?

The hosts say you should host video on your own website first so you own and control the content. You can then distribute to YouTube and other platforms, but relying solely on a third-party platform is risky because that platform is ultimately outside your control.
