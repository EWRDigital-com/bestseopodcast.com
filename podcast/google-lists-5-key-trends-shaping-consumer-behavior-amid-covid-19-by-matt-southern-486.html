Google Lists 5 Key Trends Shaping Consumer Behavior Amid COVID-19 by Matt Southern #486

Ep. 48634 min2020-05-27

Google identified five COVID-19 consumer behavior trends including multi-device use, increased Google reliance, virtual socializing, internet-first routines, and more self care, with telemedicine searches up 150% week over week.

Key takeaways
  • Telemedicine search interest rose 150% week over week in the US during COVID-19.
  • Google Meet was hosting 3 billion minutes of video meetings and adding nearly 3 million new users every day.
  • Online grocery delivery search interest grew 23% year over year in the US.
  • Americans were watching roughly 12 hours of media content a day during the pandemic.
  • People are adjusting routines to be internet-first, with telecommuting search interest reaching an all-time high.

Terms defined in this episode
internet-first routines
A behavior shift during COVID-19 where people adapted regular activities such as telecommuting, grocery shopping, and doctor visits to be conducted online rather than in person.
telemedicine
Virtual doctor visits conducted online, with search interest up 150% week over week in the US during COVID-19 as people could not access in-person medical care.
with me videos
A YouTube video trend during COVID-19 where people film themselves going about ordinary tasks like cleaning, shopping, or cooking for others to watch alongside them.
