Key takeaways
  • Use storytelling in content to turn off the sales alarm and keep readers engaged.
  • Keep paragraphs to a maximum of three to five sentences and break sections with headlines.
  • Bullet points attract attention, stand out visually, and carry SEO value as list items.
  • Make content accessible and shareable on all devices, and provide social sharing tools on the page.
  • Use Google Alerts for brand management by setting up alerts for your company name, top products, and target keywords.

Questions this episode answers

Why is my website content not converting visitors?

According to this episode, content fails to convert when it is dry and boring, offers nothing new compared to competitors, is poorly formatted or hard to read, does not solve the specific problem the visitor came to resolve, or cannot be accessed and shared easily across phones, tablets, and desktops.

How should I structure heading tags like H1 and H2 on a content page?

The hosts suggest using one H1 that covers a broad topic, supported by focused H2s beneath it. If you have a large amount of content with multiple distinct sections, it is fine to use more than one H1, keeping each H1 within its own section. Very large content may be better split across separate pages.

How can storytelling improve my content marketing?

The hosts explain that storytelling turns off the sales alarm in readers, making them feel they are not being sold to. Stories are inherently more interesting than dry facts, and people remember stories more than they remember plain information, which helps keep visitors engaged and more likely to take action.

What is Google Alerts and how can it help with SEO?

Google Alerts notifies you by email whenever new content matching a chosen phrase is indexed on the web. The hosts recommend setting alerts for your company name, your name, top products, and target keywords. It is useful for brand management and for finding potential link partners writing about your target topics.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Alerts
A Google tool that sends email notifications whenever new web content matching a specified phrase or subject is indexed, useful for brand management and keyword monitoring.
Bounce rate
A measure of visitors who leave a site without taking further action. The hosts cite a low bounce rate as evidence that content is solving visitor problems.
H1 and H2 tags
HTML heading tags used to structure page content. H1 marks a primary section topic and H2 marks subsections beneath it, both carrying SEO and readability value.
Guest checkout
An e-commerce option allowing customers to complete a purchase without creating or logging into an account, reducing friction and potential lost sales.
