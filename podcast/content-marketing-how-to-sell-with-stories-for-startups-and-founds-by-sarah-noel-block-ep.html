Key takeaways
  • Focus on one channel, one core content format, and one lead generator instead of spreading across every platform.
  • Do stakeholder interviews and customer interviews before building any content strategy or execution plan.
  • Borrow other audiences early by guesting on podcasts, doing webinar swaps, and hosting panels to build your email list.
  • Build community and listen first, then create or refine the product around what that audience actually needs.
  • If sales flatten out, go back to talking to ideal customers because the market or their needs may have shifted.

Questions this episode answers

How should a startup with no marketing budget begin building a content strategy?

Sarah Noel Block recommends picking one channel where your ideal client spends time, committing to one core content format and one lead generator, doing stakeholder and customer interviews first, and starting with collaborative content like podcast guesting or webinar swaps to borrow existing audiences before building your own.

What kind of content should go at each stage of the funnel for a startup?

Top of funnel content should be pain-point focused and centered on the customer's daily experiences. Middle of funnel content includes case studies, webinars, and workshops for problem-aware prospects. Bottom of funnel content is more personal, involving one-to-one emails and direct messages leading to calls.

Why does LinkedIn penalize reposting other people's content?

Sarah Noel Block says LinkedIn has disliked reposting for years and that any reposted content gets next to no impressions. She believes LinkedIn wants to prioritize original content to build its own content machine, though she notes she will not guess exactly what LinkedIn is thinking.

Terms defined in this episode
Lean marketing engine
A streamlined marketing approach using one core channel, one core content format, and one core lead generator, designed for founders or one-person marketing departments with little budget or resources.
Collab content
Content created by borrowing other people's audiences through podcast guesting, webinar swaps, or hosting panels, recommended as a starting point before building your own audience.
Stakeholder interview
A structured conversation with all department heads conducted before building a marketing strategy, used to gather unique customer insights and secure cross-department buy-in.
Middle of funnel
The stage where prospects are problem aware and exploring solutions, best served with case studies, sales pages, webinars, and workshops that teach the solution and lead toward a one-to-one call.
