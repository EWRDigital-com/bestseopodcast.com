Episode35 min2014-09-19

To improve SEO beyond SSL, focus on consistent URLs, short non-parameter URLs, real relevant content, live text high on the page, CTA-friendly title tags, and fast page load times.

Key takeaways
  • Write content that guides visitors toward a specific conversion action, using words like download, subscribe, or buy.
  • Use absolute links consistently across your site for images, text, and documents to keep URLs uniform.
  • Real relevant content mixes what you want to say, what readers seek, and supporting media rather than keyword-stuffed SEO copy.
  • Include action words like shop, buy, or apply in title tags to set user expectations and improve conversions.
  • Page load speed is critical: use Fast CGI, minify style sheets, consolidate JavaScript, optimize images, and enable caching.

Questions this episode answers

What SEO improvements matter more than switching to SSL?

According to Erin Everhart of Search Engine Watch, the seven things that improve SEO more than SSL are: consistent URLs everywhere, short non-parameter URLs, real relevant content, live text high on the page, strong CTA-friendly title tags, and faster page load times. These address indexing, user experience, and conversion.

How should title tags be written for better SEO and conversions?

Include action words like shop, buy, or apply in the title tag so users know what to expect before clicking. Pair the action word with your main key phrase, end with a pipe and your brand name, and keep the full title under 55 characters per Google Webmaster Guidelines.

Why did Google's close variant matching become a problem for PPC campaigns?

Starting in September, pure exact match was removed from AdWords, meaning close variants apply to all exact and phrase match campaigns with no opt-out. The hosts gave an example where a pool maintenance client began receiving impressions for pool maintenance supplies, a retail search unrelated to their service business.

Terms defined in this episode
Like-gating
The practice of incentivizing users to like a Facebook business page by preventing access to certain content unless they like the page first. Facebook announced steps to stop this practice.
Close variants
An AdWords matching option that expands exact and phrase match keywords to include similar searches like misspellings, plurals, and related words. Starting in September it became mandatory with no opt-out.
Absolute links
Links that include the entire URL rather than just the path after the domain. Recommended for consistent internal linking across images, text, and documents.
Minifying
Consolidating and compressing style sheets and JavaScript files to reduce page load time, also referred to as consolidating style sheets in the episode.
