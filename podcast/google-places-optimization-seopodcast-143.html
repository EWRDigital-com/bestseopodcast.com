Questions this episode answers

How do you get a Google Places page to rank for specific keywords?

Complete the profile fully, use the category options Google Places already lists for your industry, and add custom categories that are keyword-friendly and relevant to what you offer. Also make sure your main website is well optimized, since the Places page can display an internal link to your site.

How do you handle negative reviews on a Google Places page?

You cannot simply remove a bad review. The approach is to respond publicly to the negative review so visitors see your reply, then actively pursue more positive reviews. Getting authoritative industry contacts to post positive reviews helps those overshadow the negative ones over time.

What is the relative author tag and why does it matter for SEO?

The relative author tag links your content to your Google Plus profile. When set up correctly, your avatar appears next to your listing in search engine results pages, making that listing stand out visually compared to others and improving click visibility.

What should a client do to maintain a good working relationship with an SEO firm?

Clients should participate actively by providing content, pictures, target audience details, and business goals. They should treat the firm as a vendor rather than an employee, avoid condescending communication, and stay open to expert recommendations rather than dictating tactics based on preconceived notions.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Places page
A business listing on Google that can appear in search results and link to the business website, requiring a fully completed profile and keyword-relevant categories to rank well.
Custom categories
Additional categories a business can add to its Google Places profile beyond Google's preset list; these should be keyword-friendly and relevant to services offered.
Relative author tag
A tag that connects authored content to a Google Plus profile, causing the author's avatar to appear alongside search engine result listings for those pages.
Reputation protection
The practice of publicly responding to negative reviews and building up positive reviews so that good publicity overshadows bad publicity in search results and on review platforms.
