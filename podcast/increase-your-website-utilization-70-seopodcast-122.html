Questions this episode answers

Should I bid on my own brand name or domain in Google AdWords?

No. The hosts say there is no reason to bid on your own domain name or business name in PPC because there is a 99% chance you will rank first organically anyway. Paying for clicks from people already searching for you directly wastes ad budget.

When should I use broad match versus exact match in PPC?

The hosts recommend exact or phrase match for most campaigns to avoid irrelevant traffic. Broad match costs more per click due to higher volume and should only be used for extremely niche products where you want any related search to find you.

How do long-tail keywords help with PPC budget management?

Long-tail keywords have lower competition and lower cost per click. The hosts give an example where a broad Houston AC repair term costs $18 per click, while a more specific long-tail variation drops to around $5, allowing more clicks and more qualified visitors within the same budget.

What keyword variations should I include in a PPC campaign?

The hosts recommend including misspellings, acronyms, abbreviations, verb variations, singular and plural forms, and hyphenated versus merged versions of the same word. For example, email, e-mail, and e mail should all be included to capture every way a user might type the term.

Terms defined in this episode
Match Types
Google AdWords settings including broad match, exact match, and phrase match that control which search queries trigger your ad and affect cost per click.
Long-Tail Keywords
More specific, multi-word search phrases with lower competition and lower cost per click that tend to attract users who are closer to a purchasing decision.
Geographic Targeting
A PPC setting that limits ad delivery by location, including targeting by zip code in AdWords, so ads only show to users in relevant service areas.
Seasonality in PPC
Adjusting which keywords you bid on based on the time of year, such as targeting AC repair in summer and heating in winter for an HVAC client.
