Questions this episode answers

What changed about Facebook video ranking in April 2016?

Facebook began using data on how long users actually spend viewing content to predict engagement and prioritize videos in the feed. Videos that get watched thoroughly and generate the most engagement are shown more often, meaning creators need to focus on quality, calls to action, graphics, and music.

What is Facebook Instant Articles and who can use it now?

Facebook Instant Articles is Facebook's own publishing platform for content created specifically for Facebook rather than an external blog. As of April 2016, it became available to anyone who publishes content on Facebook, not just businesses or accounts with a certain level of clout, with a focus on reaching mobile audiences.

What are YouTube Bumper Ads and how do they work?

Bumper Ads are six-second video ads on YouTube that viewers cannot skip, bypass, or block. They run at the beginning of videos, are managed through AdWords, and are expected to have a lower cost per click than standard ads. Because viewers cannot skip, advertisers are guaranteed the view.

How did Twitter expand its platform in April 2016?

Twitter signed a partnership with the NFL, gaining exclusive streaming rights to Thursday Night Football. The deal allows the NFL to push notifications to individual viewers, run device-specific ads, and interact directly with fans, signaling continued growth for the Twitter platform.
