Questions this episode answers

What are the best remarketing tools for small businesses?

The episode highlights Google Ads remarketing, AdRoll, and MailChimp as accessible options. MailChimp is free for businesses with fewer than 2,000 contacts and includes remarketing ad capabilities. Fixel starts at $69 per month and uses AI to target users with higher purchasing intent.

How does AdRoll compare to running ads directly on Google or Facebook?

AdRoll aggregates remarketing across more than 500 networks in one interface, charging a markup on top of ad spend for that convenience. The hosts note it is a quick way to run remarketing across many platforms without manually setting each one up, and call it a good service for the right situations.

How will remarketing work as third-party cookies disappear?

The hosts expect cookieless retargeting to rely more on PVV networks, permission-based marketing, and data people share through apps, QR codes, and other touchpoints. They believe AI will be part of that future and that remarketing will remain powerful even as the tools and approaches change.

Terms defined in this episode
Remarketing
Showing ads or sending messages to users who have already interacted with your brand, website, or content, to bring them back and win the conversion.
Blind voicemail drop
A tactic used by LeadStacker and GoHighLevel that places a voicemail directly onto a recipient's cell phone without the phone ever ringing.
PVV networks
Networks described in the episode as a cookieless retargeting approach where a pixel or tag on a website identifies visitors who are already in the network, allowing triggered emails or ads.
Custom Lookalike Audience
A Facebook feature where you upload an existing customer list and Facebook finds users with similar profiles, used to reach new prospects likely to become customers.
