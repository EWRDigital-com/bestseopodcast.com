Website Launch Tips - #seopodcast 215

Episode28 min2014-01-15

To launch a website successfully, benchmark current stats, set up Google Analytics and Webmaster Tools, map old URLs to new ones with 301 redirects, check for broken links, and test mobile and site speed before going live.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Benchmark current site stats and install Google Analytics before launching so you have data to compare against.
  • Map all old URLs to new URLs in a spreadsheet before launch so 301 redirects are ready.
  • Check for broken links after launch because they hurt both user experience and SEO rankings.
  • A coming soon page should capture leads with a form and include a countdown to the launch date.
  • Register the new site in both Google Webmaster Tools and Bing Webmaster Tools as early as possible.
Submit your site with a whole bunch of broken links, see what happens to your ranking.

Questions this episode answers

What should I do before launching a redesigned website for SEO?

Benchmark current site stats, install Google Analytics on the old site during development, map old URLs to new ones for 301 redirects, export your link report from Webmaster Tools and disavow harmful links, and register the new site in Google and Bing Webmaster Tools before the launch date.

Why do bounce rates sometimes drop to 2 or 3 percent in Google Analytics?

An artificially low bounce rate, like 2 to 4 percent, usually means a tracking error. In the example discussed, a pop-up on the page contained a second Google Analytics code, so the pop-up load was counted as a second page view, eliminating the bounce and inflating total page views.

Should I use a personal or business Google Plus profile for authorship?

Use both. Set up authorship with your personal profile using rel equals author to claim your content in search results. Use your business page with rel equals publisher so it gets credit as the publisher of that content. Also list yourself as an employee of the business to strengthen the connection.

What should a coming soon page include before a website launches?

A coming soon page should include a lead capture form for name, email, and phone, links to active social media profiles like Facebook, and a countdown timer to the target launch date so visitors know the site is actively being built and can be notified when it goes live.

Terms defined in this episode
301 redirect
A permanent redirect that maps an old URL to a new one, preserving link equity and ensuring visitors and search engines reach the correct page after a site redesign.
canonicalization
The process of choosing one preferred version of a URL, for example deciding whether to use www or non-www, to avoid duplicate content issues before or during a site launch.
rel equals author
A markup attribute placed on a personal Google Plus profile to claim authorship of published content so the author image can appear in search engine result pages.
disavow
The process of telling Google to ignore certain inbound links that may be harmful to a site's link profile, which the hosts recommend doing during a redesign before resubmitting the site.
