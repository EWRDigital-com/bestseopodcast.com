We cut through AI hype to show what actually scales: disciplined ops, brand-led content, and a practical framework for awareness, nurturing, and trust. Eric Huberman shares Hawk Media’s scaling lessons, where AI helps, where it hurts, and how to build a moat in a noisy market.

Key takeaways
  • Six percent of organic traffic now comes from LLMs, making AI optimization useful but not a standalone business strategy.
  • Scale teams and software only when there is a clear business case, pushing to a breaking point before adding headcount or tools.
  • Brand and distribution will become the main moat for software companies as AI makes replication faster and easier.
  • Authentic content builds trust; AI-generated ads that try to look real risk damaging brand credibility with audiences.

Questions this episode answers

How much of organic traffic actually comes from LLMs like ChatGPT right now?

According to Erik Huberman, six percent of organic traffic now comes from LLMs. He says it is happening and is a real channel, but it is not 50 percent and is not yet going to make or break a business on its own.

How is LLM optimization different from traditional SEO?

Erik Huberman explains that LLMs try to cater results to the individual user rather than showing everyone the same result, so marketers must layer persona targeting on top of traditional SEO. LLMs are also more question-and-answer driven and look for high-authority question-and-answer content to replicate.

How did Hawk Media scale its agency without over-hiring or over-spending on software?

Erik Huberman says he pushes teams to a breaking point before adding people or software, requires a solid business case for every new hire or tool, and avoids bloated platforms. He moved from NetSuite and Salesforce back to QuickBooks and HubSpot, cutting his finance team from seven people back to three.

Terms defined in this episode
GEO / AEO / AIEO
Terms Erik Huberman groups together to describe optimization for LLMs, which he says is where clients are most intrigued but is still only one of many marketing tactics to pursue.
Hawk AI
An in-house tool built by Hawk Media that digests about 6,000 companies marketing, media, and revenue data in real time to benchmark performance marketing results instantly.
Branded entertainment
A strategy Erik Huberman promotes where a brand owns a content or entertainment destination relevant to its audience, building loyalty that goes beyond the purchase decision.
Hawk Method
A marketing framework built around three pillars, awareness, nurturing, and trust, described in a book by Erik Huberman that is now taught at universities.
