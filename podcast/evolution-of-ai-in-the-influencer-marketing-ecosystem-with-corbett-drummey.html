Evolution of AI in the Influencer Marketing Ecosystem with Corbett Drummey

AI is reshaping influencer marketing by scaling content creation, automating brand safety vetting, and making marketers more technical, but real human creators and curators remain durable and trusted channels.

Key takeaways
  • 90% of marketers are already using AI weekly, and executives are adopting it faster than entry-level employees.
  • AI can vet creators for brand safety in minutes versus the four to six hours it previously took manually.
  • Creator campaigns follow a power law: 20% of creators drive 80% of results, so test broadly then double down on winners.
  • Influencers have proven surprisingly durable as a channel, and their provenance as real people remains a strong trust asset.
  • Non-technical marketers can now use ChatGPT to do spreadsheet manipulation, data analysis, and even basic coding with plain English prompts.

Questions this episode answers

Will AI replace human influencers in marketing?

According to Corbett Drummey, influencers have shown oddly high durability over more than a decade and that will likely continue. The provenance of content coming from a real, trusted person creates an emotional connection that AI-generated accounts cannot replicate, giving human creators a lasting advantage.

How can small businesses use influencer marketing effectively?

Small and Shopify-type brands can use creators to generate content, seed a community, and drive conversion. Because results follow a power law where 20% of creators drive 80% of outcomes, the recommended approach is to test with many creators and then keep doubling down on the ones that perform best.

Is it safe to upload business data to ChatGPT?

Corbett Drummey says fears are generally overblown for startups, whose biggest risk is not growing. Enterprises can use a business tier that contractually guarantees data is not used in training. Individual users can also use the temporary chat option, which ChatGPT says explicitly deletes that data.

How is AI changing the brand safety process for influencer marketing?

Previously, manually vetting a creator for brand safety took four to six hours per creator. AI tools can now do the same review in minutes and look back for years of content. Corbett Drummey says this is one area where AI is conclusively better, though keeping a human in the review loop is still recommended.

Terms defined in this episode
Power law in creator marketing
A distribution where 20% of creators drive 80% of conversion results, meaning a small subset of partners produces the vast majority of sales outcomes.
Brand safety vetting
The process of reviewing a creator's past content to confirm it is safe for a brand to associate with, which previously took four to six hours per creator manually but can now be done by AI in minutes.
EEAT experience component
Google added an experience element to its quality criteria, requiring content to reflect first-hand experience and a human perspective, not just expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness.
Provenance of content
The known origin or source of a piece of content, such as a real human creator, which shifts audience perception and trust even when AI-generated alternatives are visually indistinguishable.
