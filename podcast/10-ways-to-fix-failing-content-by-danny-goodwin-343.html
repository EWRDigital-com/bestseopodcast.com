10 Ways to Fix Failing Content by Danny Goodwin #343

Ep. 34337 min2016-10-21

Failing content usually suffers from boring headlines, vanilla writing, poor promotion, or no strategy; fixing each of these ten specific problems can rescue underperforming content.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Boring headlines kill clicks; include the target keyword, an action item, and a clear value add.
  • Vanilla content is forgettable; add personal opinion, stories, or humor to make it shareable.
  • One tweet is not enough promotion; repurpose content across platforms multiple times daily.
  • Ugly content with long text blocks drives readers away; use short paragraphs and quality images.
  • Know your target reader and what you want them to do after reading before you write a single word.
headlines are the most important element. It's the first thing people see. You need to hook them instantly or risk losing them permanently.

About this episode

Has your content taken a long walk off a short pier? Chris and Chuck can help! This week they cover "10 reasons your content will fail and what you can do about it" by Danny Goodwin, posted at Search Engine Watch.

Questions this episode answers

What are the most common reasons content fails according to Danny Goodwin?

Danny Goodwin lists ten reasons including a boring headline, vanilla writing, making content hard to share, failing to promote it, nobody knowing the brand, ugly formatting, being too promotional, lacking emotional response, poor grammar and spelling, and having no content strategy.

How often should you promote a piece of content on social media?

The episode suggests tweeting the same content every 20 minutes with different copy to reach people in different time zones, posting on Facebook three or four times a day, and also repurposing it on Instagram, LinkedIn, SlideShare, Reddit, and similar platforms.

How can a new brand grow its audience through content?

The episode says new brands should focus content on high-level information about their product or service rather than their brand name, and can use display ads for remarketing, paid social ads like boosted posts, and guest content on slightly larger brands' sites to build recognition.
