Local SEO success in Google Places depends on claiming your listing, matching your business address to the search city, using proper categories, and keeping your listing 100% complete.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Claim your Google Places listing, find the most complete version, and fill it to 100% completion.
  • Your business address should be in the city you are targeting, not just a nearby suburb.
  • Use Google's predefined categories first, and add custom categories only if the keyword also appears in your listing text.
  • Put product or service keywords in your Places page description, but avoid keyword spamming.
  • Use a local area code as your primary phone number on your Places page to signal geographic location.

Questions this episode answers

How important is your business address location for Google local search rankings?

The survey rated having your business address in the city of the search at 4.16 out of 5 with high agreement. If you are in a suburb, you may still rank for a nearby city, but being inside the target city is a strong ranking factor according to local SEO experts polled in the article.

What is the city centroid and why does it matter for local SEO?

The city centroid is the geographic center of a city, such as downtown Houston. Businesses closer to that center tend to rank better in Google local search results for that city. Being farther away, like in a suburb, makes it harder to rank, though a complete and accurate listing can help offset the distance.

Should you use Google's predefined categories or create your own for a Places listing?

The hosts recommend using Google's predefined categories as much as possible. Custom categories may not be used by Google to determine keywords for features like Google Boost. If you add a custom category keyword, also include that keyword in the text of your Places page description.

Does adding keywords to your Google Places business title help rankings?

The survey showed some disagreement on this point. Experts noted that businesses with keywords in their name do rank well, but Google may penalize listings that change their business name just to manipulate rankings. If keywords are legitimately part of your business name or a registered DBA, it can help.

Terms defined in this episode
City Centroid
The geographic center of a city, such as downtown Houston. Businesses closer to the centroid tend to rank higher in Google local search results for that city.
Google Places Seven Pack
The group of local business listings labeled A through G that Google displays in local search results, considered a high-value placement for local businesses.
Google Boost
A paid local listing product from Google tied to Google Places that uses your Places categories to determine which keywords trigger your ad.
City of the Search
The city a user is searching within or for, used by Google to match local business listings whose addresses fall inside that city boundary.
