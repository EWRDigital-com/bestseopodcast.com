Questions this episode answers

How do you send link juice to pages stuck on page two or three of Google?

Use Google Search Console to find keywords ranking in positions 11 through 30, then use a tool like Ahrefs to identify your most authoritative pages, and add internal links from those pages pointing to the weaker ones you want to boost.

Why does the word 'because' improve outreach email response rates?

The word 'because' makes a request seem more legitimate, regardless of the reason given. Research cited in the episode showed adding a reason with 'because' lifted positive responses from 61 percent to 89 percent in a copy machine line experiment from the book Influence.

Terms defined in this episode
Semantic SEO
After Google's Hummingbird update, Google understands the overall topic of a page rather than just individual keywords, so optimizing for related subtopics helps signal what a page is truly about.
LSI Graph
A tool that shows subtopics related to a keyword you type in, helping content creators cover a topic thoroughly for semantic search.
Link juice
The ranking authority passed from one page to another through internal or external links, which can be directed to boost weaker pages sitting on page two or three of Google.
Long tail keyword
A longer, more specific keyword phrase that can be embedded into title tags alongside a primary keyword to capture additional search traffic.
