Questions this episode answers

What does the Microsoft Yahoo search deal mean for SEO and pay-per-click advertisers?

Under the deal, Microsoft runs Yahoo search results so both engines show identical results, effectively creating one combined search platform. Yahoo keeps 88% of ad revenue. Advertisers will likely manage a single combined pay-per-click account, giving them a bigger audience without the cost of running two separate campaigns.

What is Google Caffeine and how does it affect search results?

Google Caffeine is an unreleased update focused on improving indexing speed, accuracy, and comprehensiveness, the size of pages indexed. According to the episode, results mainly differ on page three and beyond, but sites updating content frequently may see benefits since Google is indexing content from as recently as 10 to 15 minutes ago.

Does posting fresh content more often help SEO under Google Caffeine?

The episode notes that one observed effect of Caffeine is that sites updating their blogs more frequently are getting better search results. Because Google is now indexing content posted just minutes ago, consistently adding new pages over time may outperform a site that published a large volume of content all at once.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Caffeine
An unreleased Google update focused on improving indexing speed, accuracy, and comprehensiveness, with a public sandbox server where users can compare its results to the current Google index.
Indexing
The process where a search engine visits web pages, compiles information from them, and stores that data in a database so it can be returned as search results.
Portal
A gateway web page, like Yahoo's front page, that users set as a starting point for browsing and searching the web.
Caffeine sandbox
A separate Google server made publicly available so users can test and compare Caffeine search results against the standard Google search results before the update is fully released.
