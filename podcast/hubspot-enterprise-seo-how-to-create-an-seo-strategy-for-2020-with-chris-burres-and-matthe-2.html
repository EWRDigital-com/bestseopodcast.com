Key takeaways
  • Start keyword research by identifying 10 to 15 short-tail pillar topics, then build clusters of 5 to 10 long-tail phrases around each.
  • Create a dedicated web page for each pillar topic, outlining it at a high level using the cluster keywords as the structure.
  • Post blog content consistently on a regular schedule, because Google notices cadence and velocity of publishing over time.
  • Interlink pillar pages and cluster blog posts to each other, signaling to Google the relationship between subtopics and the main topic.
  • Long-tail keywords carry stronger buyer intent, so ranking for them often produces more qualified traffic and measurable business results.

Questions this episode answers

What is a topic cluster in SEO and how does it work?

A topic cluster starts with a short-tail pillar keyword and a high-level page built around it. You then create 5 to 10 blog posts targeting long-tail phrases that dig deeper into that topic. The pillar page and cluster posts link to each other, signaling to Google the relationship between the subtopics and the overarching subject.

Why should you target long-tail keywords in an SEO content strategy?

Long-tail keywords represent more specific, targeted searches. As phrases get longer, buyer intent tends to increase. A searcher looking for a very specific phrase is closer to making a purchase decision than someone searching a broad one-word term, making long-tail traffic more qualified.

How often should you publish blog content for SEO?

The hosts recommend posting consistently on a regular schedule rather than in bulk. Google notices publishing cadence, and a steady velocity signals ongoing authority. Dropping to zero content during disruptions like COVID-19 can negatively affect how Google perceives a site's thought leadership.

What are the three types of SEO covered in this episode?

On-page SEO focuses on content. Off-page SEO centers on backlinks, including the number and quality of sites linking to you. Technical SEO covers elements like schema markup, server speed, server location, and the reputation of neighboring sites on the same server.

Terms defined in this episode
Pillar page
A high-level web page built around a short-tail keyword that serves as the primary support for a larger cluster of related long-tail keyword pages.
Topic cluster
A content model where a pillar page on a broad topic is supported by multiple blog posts targeting long-tail subtopics, all interlinked to signal topical relevance to Google.
Long-tail keywords
Keyword phrases that are longer, get less search volume, but reflect more specific searcher intent and tend to indicate higher purchase readiness.
LSI (Latent Semantic Indexing)
Words semantically related to a target keyword that help search engines understand the topic and context of a page.
