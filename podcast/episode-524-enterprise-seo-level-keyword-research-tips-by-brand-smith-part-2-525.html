Key takeaways
  • Use your site's internal search data in Google Analytics to find keywords your customers are already using.
  • Branded phrases you invent, like HubSpot's 'inbound marketing,' can dominate search results because you own the demand.
  • Always pair keyword relevance and volume with competition analysis before committing to a target phrase.
  • Adding a location modifier to a product or service keyword reveals realistic local search demand and ranking opportunity.
  • Competitive backlink analysis shows the strategies rivals use to rank, letting you reverse engineer or improve on them.

Enterprise SEO Level Keyword Research TipsThese 10 keyword research tips will help you focus on what your customers want and need, and figure out how to do a better job giving it to them and helping them find your product, service, or brand!

Questions this episode answers

How do I use onsite search for keyword research?

Enable site search inside Google Analytics, then go into the behavior section to see what visitors are already searching for on your site. These searches reveal the exact words your audience uses and can point directly to content gaps or high-value keyword opportunities.

What is a branded key phrase and why does it matter for SEO?

A branded key phrase is a term you invent and associate with your product or service, the way HubSpot created 'inbound marketing.' If you generate demand around your own phrase, you capture the lion's share of search results for it and avoid sending customers to competitors.

Why is keyword difficulty score not enough on its own?

Keyword difficulty can be misleading because it does not show the full picture. You also need to examine how many social shares and links competing pages have, how strong those links are, and whether the sites ranking for that term are actually authoritative, before deciding to pursue a phrase.

How does location affect keyword research for local businesses?

Adding a location modifier to a product or service keyword gives a realistic estimate of local search demand. It also helps in tools like Ahrefs to compare volumes across areas. Google now localizes results automatically, so targeting location plus service phrases is essential for local visibility.

Terms defined in this episode
Onsite Search
A Google Analytics feature found under Behavior that shows what words visitors type into your website's internal search bar, revealing real customer keyword intent.
Keyword Difficulty
A score indicating how hard it is to rank for a given keyword, based on the strength and link profiles of pages already ranking for it. The episode notes this score alone is not sufficient for keyword decisions.
Affinity Categories
An audience segmentation option inside Google Analytics under Audience and Interest that shows the interest profiles of your site visitors and can inform keyword and content targeting.
AWT (Ahrefs Webmaster Tools)
A free Ahrefs tool that scans a site for 100 plus SEO issues, shows backlink data, and reveals what keywords the site ranks for compared to competitors in the SERPs.
