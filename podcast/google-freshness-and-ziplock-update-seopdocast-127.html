Questions this episode answers

What is the Google Ziploc update and how does it affect SEO?

The Ziploc update is an informal name for a Google algorithm change focused on freshness. It is expected to impact 35% of searches, up from the prior freshness influence of about 17%. It gives more ranking credibility to recently updated, current content, especially for queries about events, products, or rapidly changing topics.

What should I do to my website in response to the Google freshness update?

Update your content regularly, especially if your site covers current events or fast-moving industries. Even for evergreen sites, rewrite service pages, About Us pages, and homepages. The hosts recommend doing this monthly or at least quarterly. Changing headers and titles may also signal fresh content to Google.

What is freshness spam and why does it matter?

Freshness spam refers to manipulating Google's freshness signals by automatically flipping page content each time Google crawls it. The concern, raised in a Search Engine Land article, is that this could become a new form of content farming, similar to tactics that preceded the Panda update.

How do deep links from high PageRank sites help with link building?

According to the episode, deep links from high PR sites carry more weight because high PR signals high trust. The hosts also note that link building efforts should target specific inner pages of a site, not just the homepage, to pass authority to deeper content.
