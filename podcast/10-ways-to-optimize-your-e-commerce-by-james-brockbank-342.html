10 Ways to Optimize Your E-Commerce by James Brockbank #342

Ecommerce conversion rate optimization requires ten focused changes including free shipping, independent reviews, live chat, clear progress indicators, and multiple payment gateways to reduce cart abandonment and increase sales.

Key takeaways
  • Offer universal free shipping because charging for it when competitors do not is a direct conversion killer.
  • Link to third-party reviews from product pages since on-site testimonials carry less trust with shoppers.
  • Add live chat so buyers can get instant answers instead of abandoning their cart late at night.
  • Use clear progress indicators at checkout, since average basket abandonment was 68% in 2015.
  • Replace stock photos and manufacturer descriptions with original images and unique copy to stand out from competitors.

Questions this episode answers

Why should ecommerce sites use independent reviews instead of on-site testimonials?

Shoppers trust third-party review platforms like Yelp, Google, or Angie's List more than reviews posted directly on a merchant's site, because on-site reviews could be influenced by gifts or financial incentives. Linking from a product page to relevant third-party reviews helps convert visitors who are already considering a purchase.

How important is live chat for ecommerce conversion?

Live chat lets shoppers get quick answers without calling or waiting for email, which matters especially for late-night browsing. Even an after-hours response saying the question will be answered in the morning can keep a buyer engaged and prevent them from searching a competitor's site.

What should ecommerce stores do about product descriptions when drop shipping?

Using the manufacturer's or distributor's default descriptions means your site looks identical to every competitor sourcing from the same supplier, which hurts search rankings and gives shoppers no unique reason to buy from you. Writing original descriptions, ideally woven with your value proposition, differentiates your listing.

How can product videos improve ecommerce sales?

Video is the closest a consumer can get to a product without holding it, and retailers investing in video are seeing significant growth. Videos should be under 90 seconds and focus on the product's key selling point, such as ease of use or real-world application, rather than acting as a full infomercial.

Terms defined in this episode
Basket abandonment
When a shopper adds items to an online cart but leaves without completing the purchase. The episode cites a 2015 average rate of 68%.
Value proposition
The reason a buyer should choose your store over competitors, such as price, speed of delivery, or exclusive products. It should be visible and reinforced throughout the checkout process.
Progress indicator
A visual element during checkout that shows shoppers how far along they are and how close they are to finishing, used to reduce cart abandonment.
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows.
