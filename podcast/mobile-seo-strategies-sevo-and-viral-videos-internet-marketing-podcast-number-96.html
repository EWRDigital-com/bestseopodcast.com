Questions this episode answers

What is SEVO and how does it apply to meta descriptions?

SEVO stands for search engine visitor optimization. The hosts describe the meta description tag as the first SEVO opportunity because it appears in search results before a visitor clicks. They recommend writing it as an abbreviated call to action combined with relevant keywords so visitors are engaged before they even reach the site.

What kinds of businesses benefit most from mobile marketing?

According to the hosts, mobile marketing tends to work best for brick-and-mortar retail businesses. They note that service-based internet marketing agencies have less use for mobile apps or campaigns, while businesses like Starbucks gain clear benefits from tools like Foursquare check-ins and SMS campaigns.

How can a business make a mobile marketing campaign effective?

The hosts say campaigns should be fun, personal, short, and integrated with the overall business strategy. They recommend rewarding customers for signing up, keeping a consistent call to action across all channels, limiting how often offers are sent, and helping customers share the message to gain viral or exponential reach.

What happened to the hosts' podcast page in Google, and how did they fix it?

Google made an algorithm change that removed the podcast page entirely from search results, which the hosts attributed to duplicate content. They fixed it by removing the duplicate content, which restored the listing, and then added relevant content to work back toward the first page.

Terms defined in this episode
SEVO
Search engine visitor optimization. The hosts define it as the practice of optimizing the experience for a visitor who is about to arrive from search, starting with the meta description in the search results page.
Meta description
An HTML tag that Google pulls to display as the snippet under a search result. The hosts say it should include target keywords early and an abbreviated call to action to improve click-through and conversion.
QR code
A black and white graphic box, similar to a barcode, that a mobile device can scan to navigate to a web page, app, or video.
Google Local Places
A Google listing tool the hosts describe as something that needs to be a part of a focused internet marketing plan, highlighted as the tip from their previous episode.
