Questions this episode answers

How should businesses prepare for AI changing the marketing landscape?

According to Matthew Bertram, businesses should start digital transformation now, before large companies finish implementing AI governance. Solidify fundamentals like target persona and customer journey, then use AI to speed up the flywheel. Waiting even a few months puts companies at risk of being passed by competitors who have already made those enhancements.

Why does LLM and Bing visibility matter for SEO today?

Matthew Bertram explains that search has expanded beyond Google. Businesses need to ensure their content is indexed and optimized for Bing and large language models like ChatGPT, because the weighting and metrics for those platforms work differently from Google and require separate attention.

What are AI agents and why do they matter for marketers?

Bertram describes AI agents as AI systems with a decision-making process that can take actions independently, such as finding products, bidding, buying, and negotiating on a user's behalf. He cites ChatGPT's $200 a month tier as a current example, and says this capability will significantly affect how consumers and businesses interact online.

Terms defined in this episode
AI agents
AI systems that have a decision-making process and can take independent actions such as finding products, bidding, buying, and negotiating on a user's behalf, as described by Matthew Bertram.
LLM visibility
The goal of having a business or brand appear in responses generated by large language models like ChatGPT, treated by EWR as a ranking target separate from traditional search engines.
7-11-4 rule
A marketing rule referenced by Matthew Bertram relating to touch points needed to engage a client, which he has been incorporating more into his strategy.
hub and spoke model
A marketing structure used by larger companies where a central team coordinates multiple specialized agencies handling different marketing functions, which Bertram notes can lead to siloing.
