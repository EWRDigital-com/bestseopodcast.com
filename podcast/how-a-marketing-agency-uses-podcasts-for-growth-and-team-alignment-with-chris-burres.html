Key takeaways
  • It took 12 months of weekly podcasting before the first inbound call arrived, so long-term consistency is essential.
  • Podcasts originally served as a training and team alignment tool before becoming an external marketing strategy.
  • A ratio of three solo expert episodes to one interview episode is recommended for building audience and authority.
  • Being a guest on high-audience podcasts can cost up to $30,000 but deliver strong ROI when the host has real influence.
  • AI governance, data permissions, and internal AI policies are being overlooked while most businesses focus only on AI output and performance.

Questions this episode answers

How can a podcast help with internal team alignment?

Chris Burres explains that the podcast was originally created to train a new sales manager and keep the whole team on the same page. Everyone listening to the same podcast develops shared enthusiasm, consistent knowledge, and a unified understanding of company direction.

What is AI governance and why does it matter for businesses?

AI governance covers internal AI policies, data permissions, who has access to data, and where it is being sent. Matt Bertram notes that regulations and laws are moving ahead of what most businesses are discussing, and fines are starting to emerge for companies that have not addressed these questions.

Terms defined in this episode
Longevity Escape Velocity
A concept where in any given year the medical community extends life by slightly more than a year, raising the theoretical possibility that someone could be the last person to die of natural causes.
AI Governance
The set of internal policies, data permissions, and frameworks that determine who has access to data, where it is sent, and how AI tools are used responsibly inside an organization.
ESS60
A spherical molecule of 60 carbon atoms described in the episode as a carbon nanomaterial that in a peer-reviewed study caused test subjects to live 90% longer than the control group.
BOSS Theory
Stands for Buffering Oxidative Stress System, the proposed mechanism by which ESS60 molecules enter mitochondria and hold negatively charged reactive oxygen species to reduce cellular damage.
