Questions this episode answers

How does Google AdWords quality score affect cost per click?

A low quality score always equals a higher cost per click. Google rewards ads whose landing pages are relevant to the search term with better placement and lower costs. Optimizing the landing page with matching content, URLs, images, and H1 tags improves quality score and brings cost per click down.

Should you bid on paid search keywords you already rank for organically?

The hosts debate this and lean toward keeping bids if the budget allows, reasoning that two positions on a page are better than one. If budget is tight, shift spend toward keywords where organic rankings are weak and pause bids on terms already ranking well organically.

How can site search data improve SEO and PPC keyword strategy?

E-commerce and other sites with internal site search can query that data from the back end to see exactly what visitors are searching for after landing on the site. Those real-user queries can then be used as keyword targets for both SEO optimization and paid search bidding.

How should a multilingual website decide which language versions to maintain?

Pull Google Analytics reports filtered by language folder, such as the AR folder for Arabic pages, to see page views and the countries generating that traffic. That data lets site owners make cost-based decisions about whether to continue paying for professional translation of each language.

Terms defined in this episode
Quality Score
A Google AdWords metric that reflects the relevance of an ad, keyword, and landing page. A low quality score raises cost per click and can cause ads to rarely be shown.
Landing Page Quality
A measure Google uses in AdWords to assess whether a landing page is relevant to the keyword and ad. Higher quality earns a boost in the ad auction and lower cost per click.
Cannibalization
In paid search, bidding on keywords for which you already rank organically on the first page, raising the question of whether paid spend duplicates free organic traffic.
Content Gap Analysis
Matching PPC keyword data against targeted SEO keywords to find phrases that rank poorly or not at all organically, then focusing resources on closing that gap.
