Penguin, Panda and SEO - #SEOpodcast 263

Episode32 min2015-02-23

To beat Google Panda and Penguin, create high-quality, original, long-form content with proper formatting, consistent topic focus, and clean link practices that prioritize user experience.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Research before writing: gather data, charts, and links before drafting any content.
  • Long-form content of 1,000 to 2,000 words tends to rank better in search results.
  • Duplicate content, even from a company in another state, triggers Panda penalties.
  • Keep keyword density conversational and avoid stuffing keywords that do not fit naturally.
  • Evergreen content like how-tos, FAQs, and step-by-step guides stays useful and engaging over time.
If you provide a good experience to the Google user, Google will place you well.

Questions this episode answers

What is Google Panda and what kind of content does it penalize?

Google Panda targets sites with bad, thin, non-relevant, or duplicate content. It also penalizes content that causes high bounce rates or a poor user experience. Keyword-stuffed content that ranks without providing value was a primary target of the Panda update.

How long should blog posts or web content be to rank well today?

The hosts note that long-form content of 1,000, 1,500, or 2,000 words is the current trend and that pages with long content tend to rank well. They advise using short paragraphs, list items, and images so it does not read like a novel.

Long form content performs best these days and has a direct correlation with performance in the search engine results page.

Terms defined in this episode
Panda
A Google algorithm update targeting sites with thin, duplicate, outdated, or low-quality content that gives users a bad experience, pushing them down in search rankings.
Penguin
A Google algorithm update targeting spammy links, link farms, and bad linking practices such as buying links or using non-randomized anchor text.
Evergreen content
Content like how-tos, FAQs, and step-by-step guides that remains relevant and engaging over time without needing frequent updates.
Keyword density
A ratio based on the number of times a keyword is used versus the total amount of text in a piece of content; should be kept conversational and not spammy.
