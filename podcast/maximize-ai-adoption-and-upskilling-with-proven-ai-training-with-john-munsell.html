Questions this episode answers

What is the crawl-walk-run framework for AI adoption in business?

Crawl means learning to write structured prompts so AI output reflects your voice and thought leadership. Walk means building custom GPTs or Gemini Gems with uploaded documents for repeatable tasks. Run is the agent stage, where those GPTs are linked and automated, but it should only come after the earlier stages are solid.

How can businesses use AI to improve their sales proposals?

John Munsell describes recording sales calls, running transcripts through a custom GPT that knows all products and services, generating a proposal, then having another GPT build a DISC and enneagram persona of the prospect, assume that persona, read the proposal, surface objections, and rewrite the proposal to address them, cycling three times.

What does an AI-first company culture actually look like?

Every employee asks how AI can help them work faster, from ideation to research to documentation. The organization builds shared custom GPTs, documents AI processes as SOPs, establishes AI governance with an innovator rather than IT in charge, and trains each department through skills tracks suited to their function.

How much is AI adoption really happening at small and medium businesses?

John Munsell says that while headlines report 86 to 87 percent of companies have adopted AI, when he asks an audience of small to medium businesses for a show of hands, that number drops to about 30 percent. Most users who are active are essentially self-taught.
