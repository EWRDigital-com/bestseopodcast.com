Key takeaways
  • Set geographic and language targeting to match your actual service area and audience, not a broad default.
  • Use mobile bid modifiers by tracking analytics to see where conversions actually happen and spend there.
  • Test ad copy constantly, keep a control ad that converts, and create variations to challenge it.
  • Use remarketing audience lists segmented by behavior, such as visitors who almost converted versus those who did.
  • Monitor shared budgets carefully so a low-converting campaign does not absorb funds from a high-converting one.

Questions this episode answers

How should I use audience lists in Google AdWords remarketing?

Create separate lists for visitors who did not convert, visitors who started but did not finish a purchase, and visitors who completed a purchase. Show each group tailored ads, such as a reminder of the item left behind or related products after checkout, because these people have already shown interest and are more likely to convert.

When should I use automated bid rules in AdWords?

The hosts prefer managing bids manually for accounts ranging from one thousand to twenty thousand dollars per month. They suggest automated bids may make more sense when managing very large accounts, but for typical small to mid-sized budgets, manual management gives more control over performance.

What are product listing ads and how do I manage them correctly?

Product listing ads, or PLAs, are managed through AdWords after Google replaced Google Base and Google Merchant. For e-commerce sites, make sure the price submitted to Google matches the price on your site exactly. Start with your most profitable products if you are new to PLAs to reduce risk of errors.

How do ad extensions affect cost per click and quality score?

Adding ad extensions such as phone numbers, site links, newsletter signups, or downloads does increase your cost per click initially. However, if an extension performs well and drives conversions, your quality score improves and the cost per click for that extension can come back down over time.

Terms defined in this episode
Mobile bid modifier
A setting in AdWords that lets you increase or decrease your bid specifically for mobile traffic, useful when analytics show mobile conversions are higher or lower than desktop.
Remarketing
Dropping a cookie on a site visitor's browser so that as they browse other sites using Google's ad network, they continue to see your ads, keeping your brand visible throughout a longer sales process.
Shared negative list
A list of negative keywords applied across multiple campaigns in AdWords to prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches, with additional ad-group-specific negatives layered on top.
Product listing ads (PLAs)
E-commerce ads managed through AdWords that display product details and pricing directly in search results, requiring the submitted price to match the price shown on your site.
