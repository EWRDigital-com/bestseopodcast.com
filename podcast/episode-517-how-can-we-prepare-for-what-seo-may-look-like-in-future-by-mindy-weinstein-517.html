Questions this episode answers

What are the key things to prepare for in the future of SEO?

According to the episode, the five areas to focus on are question answering, local driven results, stronger searcher intent, user experience, and conversion rate optimization. These reflect Google's increasing use of AI and natural language processing to match searchers with the most relevant, trustworthy content.

How is Google handling pages with thin content or bad user experience?

Google is now doing two things: pulling pages out of search results entirely, showing them as discovered but not indexed in Search Console, and also rewriting titles and meta descriptions based on its own understanding of the business. Pages must be resubmitted for indexing after fixes.

Why is local SEO still important for future search rankings?

Customers rely heavily on map results to find local services and products. The episode recommends tying your brand to local events and organizations, using geotagged images, and building a consistent digital identity across platforms to establish the trust and credibility Google looks for in local rankings.

What is BERT and why does it matter for SEO?

BERT stands for Bi-Directional Encoder Representations from Transformers. It is a language model referenced in a Google patent granted in May 2020 centered on natural language processing. It helps Google understand questions being asked and find the best answers, making question-focused content increasingly important.

Terms defined in this episode
BERT
Bi-Directional Encoder Representations from Transformers, a Google language model focused on natural language processing tasks that helps Google understand searcher questions and identify the best answers.
Search Intent
The underlying goal or need behind a search query. The episode describes it as understanding where searchers are in the customer journey and what they want to know, not just what keywords they type.
CRO (Conversion Rate Optimization)
The practice of improving a website so that visitors take desired actions. The episode frames it as essential because driving traffic without conversions means SEO efforts produce no ROI.
EAT
Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trust. The episode notes Google is building on these signals and that upcoming updates will require backing up claims with verifiable authority.
