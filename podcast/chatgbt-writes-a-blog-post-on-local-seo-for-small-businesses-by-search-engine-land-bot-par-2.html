Questions this episode answers

Do local business listings like NAP citations actually help with local SEO?

Yes, but only if you are active on those platforms. Most listings do not get indexed because they contain the same static information with no new content. The host cites data from Whitespark and BrightLocal suggesting around 62% of listings do not even show up because they are not relevant or active.

How important are online reviews for local SEO?

Reviews are described as probably the most important factor for both conversions and search rankings. Search engines evaluate the keywords in a review, whether the reviewer is a real person, how often reviews are received, and how the business responds to them. Responding to reviews is strongly recommended.

Can a small business compete with large corporations in local SEO?

Yes. The host says large companies typically target broad seed words and are unlikely to pursue long tail keyword phrases. Small businesses can find niche keyword opportunities, called honey holes, that convert well and have little competition, giving them a real advantage in local and regional search.

What are the risks of using AI written content for SEO?

The host says the helpful content update took many websites down by 30 to 50 percent, likely because of heavy AI written content. Google allows AI content but requires a human voice driving what is being said. Pure AI to AI content lacks uniqueness and can be filtered out by search algorithms.

Terms defined in this episode
NAP listings
Name, address, and phone number citations on local business directories. They function as links but must be actively maintained with fresh content to get indexed by search engines.
Honey holes
The host's term for long tail keyword phrases that convert well and have little to no competition, often invisible in Google Keyword Planner because no one is bidding on them.
Helpful content update
A Google algorithm update referenced in this episode that penalized sites with heavy AI written content, causing some sites to lose 30 to 50 percent of their rankings.
Schema
Structured data markup that the host calls critical for local SEO but says is almost never mentioned in mainstream local SEO content or articles.
