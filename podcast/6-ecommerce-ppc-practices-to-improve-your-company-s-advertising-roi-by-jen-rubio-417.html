Questions this episode answers

What are the six ecommerce PPC best practices covered in this episode?

The six practices are: consider where to advertise such as Google Merchant and Amazon, keep shopping feeds up to date, set up proper ecommerce tracking, optimize shopping ads with negative keywords and ad extensions, focus on quality content on product pages, and stay competitive by monitoring rivals and using auction insight reports.

Why are negative keywords so important in ecommerce PPC campaigns?

Without negative keywords, ads show for irrelevant searches and waste budget. Examples given include a consulting firm showing up for job-seekers searching who is hiring, and a manufacturing sensor company appearing for health sensor searches. Campaigns must be watched daily, especially when new with no past data.

How does remarketing help ecommerce advertisers recover lost sales?

Since 94% of visitors leave a site without buying and only 3% are ready to purchase on first contact, remarketing follows those users across the internet and gives advertisers another chance to convert them. Dynamic remarketing and abandoned cart remarketing are highlighted as especially effective tactics.

How should ecommerce shopping campaigns be structured for best results?

Target branded keywords at the high-conversion level, mid-funnel keywords for product research, and awareness-phase terms. Use negative keywords to separate each category so campaigns do not compete against each other or double-spend. AB testing different campaign structures helps find the most cost-effective approach.

Terms defined in this episode
Product Listing Ads (PLAs)
Shopping ads that pull product information including brand, price, and availability from a Google Merchant Center feed to show in search results.
Negative Keywords
Keywords added to a PPC campaign to prevent ads from showing for irrelevant searches, avoiding wasted spend on traffic unlikely to convert.
Dynamic Remarketing
A form of remarketing that automatically shows ads featuring the exact products a user previously viewed, used heavily by Amazon.
Auction Insight Report
A Google Ads report that shows how competitors are performing relative to your ads in terms of impression share and rank.
