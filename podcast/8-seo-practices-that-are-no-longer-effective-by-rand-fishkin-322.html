8 SEO Practices That Are No Longer Effective by Rand Fishkin #322

Ep. 32233 min2016-06-07

Eight old-school SEO practices identified by Rand Fishkin are no longer effective, including keyword stuffing in titles, exact match domains, multiple microsites, paid directory links, and non-strategic link bait.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Use conversational page titles that incorporate keywords naturally rather than cramming multiple keyword variants into one title.
  • Consolidate separate pages targeting keyword variants into one longer, well-optimized page to rank better.
  • Avoid exact match domain names as Google devalues them and they appear spammy to users and press.
  • Do not use AdWords competition columns to judge organic SEO difficulty; the two metrics are unrelated.
  • Create link bait content that is relevant to your actual services, not trending topics that drive high bounce rates.
using methods that were once tried and true can be alluring, but it can also prove dangerous to your search strategy

Questions this episode answers

Can I use AdWords cost per click data to gauge how hard a keyword is to rank for organically?

No. The competition column in AdWords reflects paid ad competition, not organic SEO competition. You can use AdWords for keyword research and search volume data, but using it to estimate organic ranking difficulty is, as the hosts put it, utterly ridiculous.

Terms defined in this episode
Exact Match Domain (EMD)
A domain name built entirely from a target keyword, such as poolmaintenancehouston.com. Google has an algorithm update that devalues these domains for appearing spammy.
CVO (Search Engine Visitor Optimization)
What you do with a user after they arrive from a search engine. The hosts say CVO begins at the SERP, meaning the meta description and title are part of the conversion process.
Link Bait
Content created specifically to attract inbound links and traffic. The episode distinguishes strategic link bait, content relevant to your services, from non-strategic link bait, trending content unrelated to your site.
Anchor Text
The clickable text used in a hyperlink. Overuse of repetitive keyword-rich anchor text on internal links was discounted by a Google algorithm update and can appear spammy or manipulative.
