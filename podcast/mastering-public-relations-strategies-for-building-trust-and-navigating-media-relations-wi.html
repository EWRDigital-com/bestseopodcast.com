Mastering Public Relations: Strategies for Building Trust and Navigating Media Relations with Justin Goldstein

Ep. 62536 min2024-12-01Guest: Justin Goldstein

PR builds long-term business credibility by generating media coverage that supports sales funnels, nurtures prospects, and reinforces brand trust, but it works best when paired with infrastructure to actually use the placements.

Key takeaways
  • Media coverage is not a silver bullet for leads; it supports sales efforts rather than replacing them.
  • Before hiring a PR firm, confirm you have budget and internal infrastructure to repurpose what they produce.
  • Proactive reputation management means publishing positive stories early, before a crisis forces your hand.
  • Breaking down internal silos is essential so PR messaging reaches all departments, not just the point person.
  • Media relations requires building rapport over time, not just sending cold pitch press releases.

Questions this episode answers

How can a small business use PR to win new clients?

According to Justin Goldstein, media coverage works as a top and middle of funnel asset. You can send a placement to a cold prospect as social proof, then re-engage a warm prospect with a newer placement to restart the conversation and move toward a close.

What should a company do when facing a PR crisis?

First assess whether the negative press will appear on Google long-term and genuinely damage the brand. If there is any uncertainty, pay for a 30 to 60 minute call with a crisis expert. At minimum, prepare reactive statements for the founder and the broader team so everyone is ready if questions arise.

When does it make sense to hire a PR firm?

Justin Goldstein says two things must be true: the company needs adequate budget so the campaign does not feel forced, and it needs internal infrastructure to repurpose placements in newsletters, social posts, and outbound sales messages. Without both, the PR work is unlikely to produce visible results.

Are press releases an effective SEO tool?

Justin Goldstein says press releases distributed on wire services are hit or miss and should be treated as a supporting proof document rather than an inbound lead generator. They confirm that an announcement is official and can be shared with reporters and prospects, but should not be the sole PR strategy.

Terms defined in this episode
Social proof flywheel
A term used by Justin Goldstein to describe how media coverage circulates through outbound DM campaigns, newsletters, and paid marketing to build long-term business credibility.
EAT (Expertise, Authority, Trust)
Google's framework for evaluating content quality, referenced in the episode as the reason third-party media coverage and awards help a brand rank and convert online.
Crisis PR
A specialized, resource-intensive form of public relations that activates when negative press threatens long-term brand damage, involving rapid messaging development and coordinated response across internal and external stakeholders.
Reactive vs. proactive PR
Proactive PR means going to the press to get ahead of a story; reactive PR means preparing statements in advance and responding only if questions arise, to avoid shining a light on an issue unnecessarily.
