Questions this episode answers

Why did E-Web Results stop offering a standard three-posts-per-week social media package?

The hosts explained that organic reach dropped dramatically as algorithms changed, making three weekly posts largely ineffective. They cut it down to one post per week just to show activity, and eventually dropped the full package because it was not adding measurable bottom-line value for about 30 percent of clients on it.

What social media tactics is E-Web Results recommending now instead of regular posting?

They recommend guerrilla marketing tactics such as participating thoughtfully in LinkedIn groups, Nextdoor, and Facebook groups, tagging relevant people to expand reach, running Facebook remarketing ads, producing video content, and using an internal marketing admin coached on best practices to execute these strategies.

Why is LinkedIn highlighted as a priority social media platform right now?

Matt says he is seeing strong organic engagement on LinkedIn currently, plans to write a full article on his LinkedIn strategy, and describes it as the platform where the game is right now. He views it as a coming topic worthy of a full podcast series.

Terms defined in this episode
Guerrilla Marketing
A strategy referenced from the book Guerrilla Marketing where businesses use their own voice and time to drive leads through low-cost, high-effort tactics like group participation and forum posts rather than paid ads.
Remarketing
Showing ads to people who have already visited your website, described in the episode as a bare minimum Facebook ad strategy that businesses should activate once they reach about 5,000 monthly visitors.
Pay to Play
The condition on platforms like Facebook where businesses receive almost no organic viewership without spending on ads to reach their target audience.
Social Signals
Activity on social media platforms that the hosts reference as having some SEO value, described as psychosocial signals from an SEO standpoint.
