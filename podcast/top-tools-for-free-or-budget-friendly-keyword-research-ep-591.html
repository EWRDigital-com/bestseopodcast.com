Questions this episode answers

What free keyword research tools should I use after Ahrefs changed its pricing?

Matt Bertram recommends Google Search Console as the top free option because its data is factual. He also highlights SEMrush's free plan with 10 analytic reports per day, Google Keyword Planner, Ahrefs' free keyword generator, and tools like Ubersuggest, KW Finder, and Also Asked as strong free or low-cost alternatives.

Why is Google Keyword Planner not enough on its own for keyword research?

Google Keyword Planner only shows keywords active in the paid search world. It misses keywords that nobody is bidding on but that can still be high converting. Matt Bertram calls these opportunities little honey holes and recommends supplementing with other tools to find them.

What is the difference between Google Search Console and Google Analytics data?

Search console data is factual and direct. Google Analytics relies on a pixel that may not fire if a page is slow or if users leave immediately. More data appearing in Search Console than in Analytics often signals slow page speed or poor core web vitals, according to Matt Bertram.

What are topic clusters and why do they matter for keyword research?

Topic clusters are groups of multiple pages that each discuss a different angle of one subject, linked together to build topical authority. Matt Bertram explains that tools like Also Asked help map these clusters and visualize how people ask questions, making it easier to rank for head terms.

Terms defined in this episode
Long tail key phrases
Longer, more specific search queries with lower search volume but higher intent that typically convert better and face less competition than short core keywords.
Topic cluster
Multiple pages that talk about a topic in length from different perspectives, linked together to signal topical authority and help rank for head or core terms.
Oscillation theory
The pattern where keyword rankings go up and down as Google tests a page with different keywords and measures user interaction, with the general trend expected to move upward over time.
QSR (Quoted Search Results)
A metric cited in the Jaaxy tool description, noted as one of Brian Dean's favorite search features for evaluating keyword competition.
