Episode 521: The 4 Pillars of Enterprise SEO Success by Jenn Mathews #521

Ep. 52125 min2021-09-262,691 plays

Enterprise SEO success depends on four pillars: technical error mitigation, analytics and reporting, project management, and relationship building across engineering, creative, legal, and PR teams.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Technical SEO fixes, like resolving duplicate or thin content on dynamically built sites, can produce significant ranking gains quickly.
  • Calculating organic traffic as a percentage of revenue using Google Search Console data helps justify SEO investment to executives.
  • Internal linking architecture and using headers for on-page SEO rather than just style are common fixable issues at enterprise level.
  • Project management that coordinates dependencies across multiple teams is critical because delays, like taking a quarter to add Tag Manager, are common.
  • Building relationships with engineering, creative, legal, and PR teams before you need them is the best predictor of enterprise SEO success.
Pulling clicks from Google Search Console from specific keywords and then calculating the percentage of all clicks to get the estimated revenue for that keyword will allow you to make better decisions that allow you to understand how your SEO is performing.

About this episode

The 4 Pillars of Enterprise SEO SuccessMitigation, reporting, project management, and relationship building are all important to the success of enterprise SEO. Here's why.

Questions this episode answers

Why is relationship building considered so important in enterprise SEO?

Jenn Mathews spends the most time on this pillar because enterprise SEO requires working across engineering, creative, legal, and PR teams. Building those relationships before you even need them is described as the best predictor of success or failure in an enterprise SEO project.

How can SEO teams prove the value of organic search to executives?

The episode recommends calculating organic traffic from Google as a percentage of all search traffic, then pulling clicks from Google Search Console by keyword and calculating their estimated revenue contribution. This gives stakeholders a clear picture of how SEO performance ties to revenue.

What are common technical SEO problems on enterprise dynamically built sites?

The episode highlights duplicate content, thin content, products or content appearing and disappearing, and user generated content at massive scale as key issues. These problems require ongoing monitoring because multiple teams editing the site simultaneously can cause gradual SEO creep out of conformity.

The higher up you can go at organization where people understand SEO, the better, because then those people can help smooth out whatever needs to happen or get approvals or expedite things.

Terms defined in this episode
SEO creep
The gradual drift out of SEO conformity that happens when multiple teams make independent changes to a large site without coordinating under a unified SEO strategy.
PIM and DAM
Product Information Management and Digital Asset Management, systems that help enterprise organizations manage product data and digital files across large websites.
Google Search Console
A tool referenced in the episode for pulling click data by keyword so SEO teams can calculate estimated revenue per keyword and measure SEO performance.
SME roles
Subject matter expert roles, a structure some enterprise companies use where SEO responsibilities are split among individual contributors who come together periodically to communicate rather than sitting in a single centralized team.
