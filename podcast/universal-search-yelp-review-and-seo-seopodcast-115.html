Key takeaways
  • Google removed Yelp and CitySearch review snippets from Places pages, replacing them with a prompt for Google user reviews.
  • In Universal Search, Google or Google products took four of the top five category slots, missing only in news results.
  • Using YouTube for video and Google Maps for embeds on your site may help rankings because Google favors its own products.
  • Google Plus One signals on specific search result pages may act similarly to reviews for local ranking purposes.
  • WP e-commerce caused major client problems with Authorize.net integration; Cart 66 is recommended as a working alternative.

Questions this episode answers

Why did Google remove Yelp reviews from Google Places pages?

Google's Places product manager stated that after careful thought and user feedback, review snippets from third-party sites were removed so that ratings and review counts reflect only Google user reviews. Google still provides links to external review sites like Yelp so users can find comprehensive information.

What types of content win in Google Universal Search?

According to a Search Engine Land article by Barry Schwartz discussed in this episode, Google or Google products took four of the top five Universal Search category slots, covering video, maps, shopping, and images. News was the one category where Google did not come first, with the Wall Street Journal appearing instead.

Is WP e-commerce a reliable WordPress shopping cart plugin for Authorize.net?

Based on this episode, WP e-commerce had serious compatibility problems with Authorize.net through their Gold Cart premium upgrade. The hosts experienced unresolved support issues and a broken ticket system, and instead recommend Cart 66 as a WordPress shopping cart that worked correctly on first install.

Terms defined in this episode
Universal Search
Google search results that blend multiple content types such as video, images, maps, news, and shopping into a single results page, with Google favoring its own products in most categories.
Google Local Places
Google's local business listing pages, which previously showed third-party review snippets from sites like Yelp but now show only reviews written by Google users.
Plus One
A Google social signal where users endorse a search result or page, which the hosts suggest may carry ranking value similar to a review, especially for local listings.
Gold Cart
A paid premium upgrade for the WP e-commerce WordPress plugin, costing forty dollars, intended to enable integration with payment processors like Authorize.net.
