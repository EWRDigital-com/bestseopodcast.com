Questions this episode answers

What are the top growth hacks to drive new search traffic from content?

Simon Pinson's article on Search Engine Watch lists 10 hacks: target long tail phrases, reverse engineer competitors, amplify with paid search, pursue digital PR, share original business data, rotate evergreen and trending content, build referral relationships, reference and link to authoritative sources, optimize for social sharing, and use rich snippets and microdata.

Why should you target long tail keywords in your content strategy?

Long tail phrases require longer pages with more words, around 500 to 700, which create more link opportunities. More importantly, searchers using long tail phrases are likely in a buying mood, closer to converting, and have a clearer understanding of what they are looking for.

How does paid search amplification help content get more links?

Boosting a well-researched post on Facebook, Twitter, or LinkedIn exposes it to more people, which drives more shares and likes. Statistics cited in the episode show that promoted posts and tweets also tend to build more inbound links because more people see and engage with the content.

What is a link profile and why should you monitor it?

A link profile covers how many inbound and outbound links a site has, where they come from, how often they appear, and whether they are spammy or relevant. Monitoring it proactively helps ensure links are legitimate, prevents association with blacklisted or hacked sites, and protects against algorithmic penalties like Google Penguin.

Terms defined in this episode
Link Profile
The full picture of a site's inbound and outbound links, including their quantity, source, relevance, and whether they come from spammy or blacklisted sites.
Long Tail Key Phrase
A longer, more specific search query that attracts users who are further along in the buying process and more likely to convert.
Rich Snippets
Structured markup using schema data applied to content such as events, locations, video, and images to increase visibility in search results and enable discovery beyond text searches.
Evergreen Content
Content that remains relevant over time, such as best practice guides and how-to articles, as opposed to trending or seasonal content.
