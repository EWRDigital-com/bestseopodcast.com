10 Best Practices To Winning In Digital Retail by Benjamin Spiegel #300

Ep. 30033 min2015-12-09

E-commerce and e-retail sites win by optimizing product titles, adding FAQ content inside product descriptions, collecting reviews, offering same-day shipping, and continuously testing and measuring results.

Key takeaways
  • Optimize product titles like SEO titles: target phrase first, brief description, then SKU number within 55 characters.
  • Place FAQs inside product descriptions, not on a separate FAQ page, because buyers read product descriptions.
  • Replace blank star ratings showing 'no reviews' with 'be the first to review' to avoid signaling a poor product.
  • Offering same-day shipping has often doubled conversion rates, meeting on-demand consumer expectations.
  • Always test, measure, and adjust your e-commerce content to avoid unknowingly wasting money.

Questions this episode answers

What is the difference between e-retail and e-commerce?

E-retail refers to online retail establishments like Amazon or Google Shopping that carry bulk products from multiple manufacturers. E-commerce typically refers to a single brand selling only its own products online. The article's principles apply to both, but the distinction matters for scale and strategy.

Where should FAQ content go on an e-commerce product page?

According to the hosts, FAQs should go inside the product description itself, not on a separate FAQ page. Shoppers read product descriptions closely before buying but rarely visit standalone FAQ pages, so embedding questions and answers there increases the chance they are seen.

How do ratings and reviews help e-commerce sites?

Ratings and reviews influence actual rankings in search results, increase overall conversion rates, and act as digital word of mouth. Third-party reviews from sources like Yelp or Google Plus, as well as on-site user-generated reviews, can all help improve search rankings and buyer confidence.

What does 'test and measure' mean for e-commerce site owners?

Test and measure means regularly reviewing what is and is not working, making adjustments, and repeating the cycle. The hosts warn that business owners who skip this step are likely throwing away money without realizing it, and that knowing the cost of inaction motivates action.

Terms defined in this episode
Live blog carousel
A Google search carousel for live blog posts. If a site publishes a live blog post and marks it up with the live blog schema, the site can appear in the carousel at the top of search results for related real-time events.
SKU
A product identifier code used in e-commerce. The hosts recommend including the SKU at the end of an optimized product title, after the target phrase and brief product description.
Multiple SKUs
A product page setup that lets consumers configure attributes such as color and size on one screen without changing pages or reloading the browser, improving shopping experience and conversion likelihood.
E-retail
Online retail operations that carry bulk products from multiple manufacturers, such as Amazon or Google Shopping, as distinct from a single-brand e-commerce site.
