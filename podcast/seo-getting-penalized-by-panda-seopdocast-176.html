SEO - Getting Penalized by Panda - #seopdocast 176

Episode26 min2013-01-28

To recover from a Google Panda penalty, audit your content quality, stop over-using exact match anchor text, and treat algorithm updates as data refreshes that reward sites that already fixed their problems.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Before writing any page, answer planning questions about topic, purpose, content timing, keywords, links, titles, and headings.
  • For blog post link exchanges, research the destination site's IP, link neighborhood, and geographic relevance before agreeing.
  • Google Local Plus setup and linking to your site carries more SEO impact than the geographic target setting in Webmaster Tools.
  • SEMrush covers keyword suggestions, keyword traffic and competition, and keyword tracking, addressing all three needs in one tool.
If you were affected when Panda first happened, like last year sometime, you should have come back from that by now. If you haven't, then change your strategy.

Questions this episode answers

How do I recover from a Google Panda penalty?

Check your content quality first. According to this episode, Panda updates after the initial wave are data refreshes, so if you already corrected your content and moved forward, they should help you by weeding out competition. If you are still affected, change your strategy and update your content.

Is swapping blog posts and backlinks with overseas sites worth it for SEO?

The hosts advise caution. Verify which site will host your link, research its IP address and link neighborhood, and consider geographic relevance. A link from a U.S. site to an Australian site may have little value because Google knows your location, making local link partners more effective.

What questions should I ask before optimizing a content page?

The episode lists questions from a Search Engine Land article: what is the page about, what is its purpose, how long will the content stay relevant, what keywords are relevant, should you include links, and what should the title, descriptions, and headings say. Answering these before writing creates a complete outline.
