Yandex Data Leak: The Ranking Factors & The Myths We Found Part 2 Ep. 564

Ep. 56331 min2023-02-203,585 plays

Analysis of the Yandex source code leak confirms that dwell time, backlink quality, anchor text diversity, and on-page ad ratios are real ranking factors, offering lessons applicable to Google SEO strategy.

Key takeaways
  • Dwell time appears in 102 Yandex ranking factors, contradicting Google's public claim that it does not use such signals.
  • Backlinks from home pages carry more weight than internal pages, and the link spam calculator uses around 80 active factors.
  • Anchor text diversity matters: mix branded, commercial, unbranded, and naked URL anchors for a natural link profile.
  • YMYL content requires clear authorship, publication dates, and trust signals, or rankings can suffer in both Yandex and Google.
  • A high ratio of advertisements to visible content is a negative ranking factor in Yandex, mirroring Google's approach.

Questions this episode answers

What does the Yandex data leak reveal about dwell time as a ranking factor?

The leak shows 102 ranking factors contain a dwell time tag referencing user duration or average page dwell time. This conflicts with Google's public stance that it does not use dwell time or similar user interaction signals, but the hosts argue internal data and SEO experience suggest dwell time does matter.

What are the best practices for building backlinks according to the Yandex leak?

Build links with a natural frequency and varying amounts, use branded anchor text alongside commercial keywords, avoid buying links from websites with mixed topics, and prioritize quality over quantity. The age of a backlink, link relevance by topic, and whether a link comes from a home page versus an internal page are all factors.

How does Yandex use Metrica analytics data for ranking?

Six ranking factors relate to Metrica data, including average time spent on URLs by similar visitors, average time a user spends on a host when accessed from an external non-search site, average depth of pages visited, and whether the domain has Metrica installed. One of the six factors is tagged as deprecated.

Does the Yandex leak apply to Google SEO?

The hosts and the analysis by Dan Taylor suggest yes. Tens of engineers have worked at both companies, meaning similar approaches may exist across both search engines. Factors like dwell time, YMYL content treatment, backlink quality, and ad-to-content ratios appear in both systems.

Terms defined in this episode
YMYL (Your Money Your Life)
A category of content covering medical, legal, and financial topics that Yandex and Google treat with stricter ranking requirements around authoritativeness, trustworthiness, and accuracy.
Pessimization
A Yandex penalty that reduces a page rank to zero, representing one of the most severe punishments available in the Yandex ranking system.
Spam Karma
A Yandex ranking concept that penalizes hosts and individual domains associated with spammy neighborhoods or low-quality link environments.
Metrica
Yandex's analytics platform, equivalent to Google Analytics, which comes with built-in heat map tools and user session recording and whose data feeds into several Yandex ranking factors.
