About this episode

Local search results - and their impact to your business - are constantly in flux. These 10 stats can help you understand the state of local SEO today.

Questions this episode answers

How often does the Google local three pack show up in search results?

According to the stats discussed in this episode, the Google three pack appears in the top spot in 93% of searches. That means nearly nine out of ten searches display the local three pack, so having an optimized and claimed Google My Business page is critical for visibility.

How important are online reviews for local businesses?

The episode cites that 88% of consumers have read reviews to determine the quality of a business, and 72% say positive reviews make them trust a local business. The hosts note this means roughly three out of four people rely on what previous customers posted before making a decision.

What percentage of local mobile searches lead to an actual purchase?

The episode states that 78% of local mobile searches result in an offline purchase. The hosts use examples like searching for a restaurant or shoe store nearby as cases where the searcher is already intent on buying, and only bad reviews or unavailability would stop them.

Why should local businesses optimize for near me searches?

The episode notes that near me search interest grew 34 times since 2011 and that 90% of near me searches are voice searches. Businesses need schema markup, a properly set up Google My Business page, and accurate hours of operation to appear in these results.

Terms defined in this episode
Google three pack
A local search feature that shows three local business listings at the top of search results, which the episode notes appears in 93% of searches after Google reduced local pack results from seven to three.
NAP
Stands for name, address, and phone number. The episode describes keeping this information consistent across all local citations as improving SEO performance and helping prospects call, visit, or make a purchase.
Near me search
A voice-driven local query such as Italian restaurant near me. The episode states 90% of near me searches are voice searches and that interest in them grew 34 times since 2011.
Schema markup
Structured data added to a website that helps search engines identify specific information like business address, phone number, and hours, which the episode says is especially important for near me and local voice searches.
