Key takeaways
  • Direct paid ads to a dedicated landing page, not your homepage, so visitors see the specific offer.
  • Change the offer first if a campaign is underperforming; it produces the biggest shift in results.
  • Copywriters should spend roughly 80 percent of their time on the headline because it determines whether visitors read further.
  • Every form field added reduces completions by about 10 percent, so keep forms as short as the goal allows.
  • Social proof including reviews, video testimonials, names, photos, and locations is essential, not just helpful, for conversions.

Questions this episode answers

What are the key elements every landing page must have?

According to this episode, a high-converting landing page needs consistent branding and color, a clear and compelling headline, a persuasive sub-headline, supporting copy, a clean layout with no navigation distractions, a strong offer, simple forms, a direct call to action, rich media, reviews and social proof, and emotional appeal.

How should I write a call to action for a landing page?

The episode advises against vague CTAs like learn more or get started. Instead, spend significant time crafting a specific CTA tied to the offer, such as secure your supply. The hosts also note that different CTAs perform better in different industries and geographic regions.

Why should landing pages avoid navigation links and headers?

Landing pages are meant to give visitors a single path forward. Including navigation links, headers, footers, or extra calls to action creates distractions that pull visitors away from the one action you want them to take, reducing the chance of conversion.

Terms defined in this episode
Landing page
A page specifically designed for a prospect to land on after clicking a call to action in an email, ad, or other channel, with a single focus on driving one conversion action such as a lead, sale, download, or signup.
Search intent
The underlying goal a user has when typing a keyword, revealed by how visitors interact with pages that rank for that keyword and by the cost-per-click data visible in tools like SE Ranking.
Social proof
Reviews, testimonials, video testimonials, and user-generated content that reassure potential customers by showing other people have had success with a product or service. The episode cites data suggesting roughly 12 reviews are needed for social proof to meaningfully influence decisions.
CTA (Call to Action)
A button or prompt that directs a visitor to take a specific next step on a landing page. Effective CTAs are specific to the offer rather than generic phrases like learn more.
