Tips to Avoiding High Bounce Rates - #SEOpodcast 252

Episode36 min2014-10-30

To avoid high bounce rates on landing pages, match ad copy to landing page content, test headlines with AdWords, highlight benefits clearly, and use urgency tactics like FOMO to drive conversions.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • A high bounce rate on a landing page can signal success if visitors are converting via phone calls or form submissions.
  • Ad continuity matters: the landing page must reflect exactly what the ad promised, from headline to offer.
  • Use AdWords to test headlines before committing to them organically, then optimize around the ones that convert.
  • For long sales cycles, highlight lead capture steps rather than pushing for an immediate close.
  • Incorporate FOMO, fear of missing out, especially for limited products, to create urgency and drive faster action.
A high bounce rate could be a good thing.

Questions this episode answers

When is a high bounce rate actually good for a landing page?

A high bounce rate can be a positive sign when visitors are converting before leaving. For example, if a user calls after seeing a phone number, or fills out a form and reaches a thank you page, that counts as a conversion even though the session ends quickly.

What is the most important rule for landing page ad continuity?

The landing page must reflect exactly what the ad said. If someone clicks an ad for a specific offer like same-day windshield replacement, they should land on a page dedicated to that offer, not a generic homepage. Mismatched messaging increases bounce rates without conversions.

How should you use urgency on a landing page to reduce bounce rates?

Incorporate FOMO, or fear of missing out, especially for limited products. Communicating scarcity or time limits, such as limited stock or a deadline, compels visitors to act fast. The hosts cite limited Jordan sneaker releases as a real-world example of this working effectively.

What does FAB mean in the context of landing page copywriting?

FAB stands for feature, advantage, and benefit. The hosts recommend addressing all three in landing page copy: describe the product feature, explain its advantage, and most importantly, state the benefit to the user, such as saving money, gaining time, or improving security.

Terms defined in this episode
FOMO
Fear of missing out. An emotional trigger used in marketing copy to create urgency, especially effective for limited or exclusive products where scarcity is real.
Bounce Rate
The percentage of sessions where a visitor leaves a site after viewing only one page. A high bounce rate is not always negative if the visitor converted, such as by calling or submitting a form.
FAB
Feature, advantage, benefit. An old school sales framework the hosts recommend applying to landing page copy to speak to what users gain from a product or service.
Panda 4.1
A Google algorithm update and refresh targeting thin and low-quality content, mentioned in the episode as rolling out on September 26th, which could restore rankings for sites that had improved their content.
