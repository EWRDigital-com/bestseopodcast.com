Key takeaways
  • Post great original content on your own site first, then remix portions for guest posts with a link back.
  • Repurpose proven content into video, infographics, or other formats to get more traction from the same material.
  • Links must come from quality, relevant, credible sites; high PageRank alone no longer guarantees value.
  • Diversify anchor link text to avoid keyword-stuffed, spammy link patterns after algorithm updates addressed exact match anchor text.
  • SEO is now more about overall strategy than individual tactics, including social, blogging, video, and newsletter integration.
content continues to be important, but requires more depth and more detail

Questions this episode answers

Should I post content on my own site or submit it to guest blogs for SEO?

Post original content on your own site first because the content on your site has more value than an inbound link from a guest post. After publishing, you can remix a portion for a guest post with a link back for more information. Save your best link-bait-worthy articles for your own site.

Does keyword-rich URL folder structure still help SEO?

It works, but much less than it used to. Better content and better inbound links now outperform URL keyword tricks. The hosts note that exact match domain names and keyword-rich directories had more impact before 2012 algorithm changes, and content quality matters far more today.

What does it mean that SEO is more strategy than tactics?

Instead of just checking off on-page tasks and building links, you now need to define what purpose your content serves, what audience you want, what you want visitors to do, and how social, blogging, video, and newsletters all connect. The hosts say this strategic thinking applies to PPC as well.

Terms defined in this episode
author authority
The SEO value tied to Google authorship markup, which Google was actively testing and reporting positive results on according to a Matt Cutts video mentioned in the episode.
link text diversification
Varying the anchor text used in inbound links so it is not all keyword-stuffed exact match text, required after an algorithm update targeting exact match anchor text.
exact match domain
A domain name that exactly matches a target keyword phrase, such as SEOHouston.com, which the hosts say only carries value if that phrase is the actual company name.
repurposing content
Taking a proven piece of content, such as a blog post, and reformatting it into video, infographics, or other media to reach more audiences and gain additional traction.
