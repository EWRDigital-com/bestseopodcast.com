5 ways to rethink your paid strategy by Todd Saunders #384

Ep. 38424 min2017-08-03

Great content alone does not generate leads; pairing it with paid strategies like Gmail ads, remarketing, and PPC alongside organic SEO drives real results, according to Todd Saunders.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Document your content strategy and perform keyword research before publishing any content.
  • Combine paid and organic search so you get double listings on the same results page.
  • Build audiences using Gmail ads and target competitor URL domains for granular reach.
  • Use remarketing with custom messaging to pull visitors back into the buying funnel.
  • Place a LinkedIn pixel and a Facebook pixel on every website immediately, even before running ads.
Remember the number one visited spot on the internet is still the inbox.

About this episode

Does your content attract clicks? Before creating your PPC ads, ensure your content has the right CTA and uses the best keywords to attract your target audience. Check out Chris and Matt as they give you some quick tips on PPC strategies!

Questions this episode answers

Why does great content fail to generate leads?

The episode compares launching content to a ribbon cutting where only friends and family show up. Without a documented content strategy, keyword research, and a paid distribution plan, even strong content reaches almost no one because 2.7 million WordPress posts are added to the internet every day.

How does running PPC ads help organic rankings?

Running paid ads increases click-through rate, and Google sees the site getting more traffic. If visitors also spend more time on the site due to good content, the hosts say they have observed organic rankings rise as a result, though they note they do not have hard facts to confirm this directly.

What is remarketing and how should it be structured?

Remarketing drops a cookie or pixel on a visitor's browser so they see targeted ads as they browse the web or Facebook. Matt Bertram recommends a seven-day strategy and a 30-day strategy, with custom messages tailored to where the visitor dropped off, such as an unfilled form or a landing page visit.

Why are Gmail ads worth using in a paid strategy?

Gmail has one billion active monthly users according to the article discussed. Advertisers can go broad by targeting keywords associated with competitors or get granular by targeting competitor URL domains, making it a channel many PPC managers overlook in favor of standard search ads.
