Questions this episode answers

How can ecommerce sites use social media to improve conversions?

The episode recommends displaying social sharing options during the checkout or sign-up process, when users are actively engaged. For example, prompting users to like a Facebook page or follow on Twitter right after completing a purchase or while their account is being created makes it easy and timely.

What is the Google Filmstrip SERP feature discussed in this episode?

The hosts describe a Google search results layout they call the Google Filmstrip, where a horizontal strip appears at the top of the page showing hotel images, names, average room costs, and star ratings. It appeared above paid ads and made finding hotel information faster and more visual.

What are the top Foursquare marketing tips for local businesses from this episode?

The episode outlines ten tips from an article by Aaron Strout: set clear goals, claim your business, reach out to influencers, create a special strategy, test different specials, measure and refine, promote the campaign with signage, train staff, experiment with paid ads, and download the Foursquare business app to track check-ins.

Why should brick-and-mortar businesses use Foursquare for marketing?

The episode says Foursquare works best for businesses with a physical location, especially restaurants, because it drives foot traffic. Check-in specials like percentage discounts can bring customers in, and the business app sends push notifications when someone checks in so staff can respond with personalized service.

Terms defined in this episode
Google Filmstrip
A name coined by the hosts for a Google SERP layout showing a horizontal strip of hotel images, names, prices, and star ratings that appears above paid ads.
Foursquare Mayor
The Foursquare user who has checked in to a specific location the most, which businesses can use to offer exclusive rewards and encourage repeat visits.
Check-in Special
A discount or reward offered to customers who check in to a business on Foursquare, used to drive foot traffic and encourage social sharing.
