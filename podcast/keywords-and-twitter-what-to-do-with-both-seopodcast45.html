Episode27 min2010-03-02

Long tail keywords outperform short tail for faster SEO results because they are more specific, face less competition, and attract buyers who know exactly what they want.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Target long tail keywords first for quicker results and more relevant traffic before pursuing high-competition short tail terms.
  • Before choosing a keyword, search it in Google and count competing results to gauge whether you can realistically rank.
  • Anchor text in external links should use target keywords, not generic words like home, to signal relevance to Google.
  • Short tail keywords bring more traffic but less relevant visitors; long tail brings fewer visitors who are closer to buying.
  • Adding city or product specifics to keywords, like quit smoking hypnosis Houston, makes ranking faster within limited budgets.

Questions this episode answers

What is a long tail keyword and why does it matter for SEO?

A long tail keyword is a longer, more specific search phrase that falls outside the high-volume bell curve of popular terms. It gets fewer searches but those searchers know exactly what they want, making it easier to rank for and more likely to result in a sale.

How do you balance short tail and long tail keywords in an SEO strategy?

Use both but approach them differently. Long tail keywords deliver faster, more relevant results and should be pursued first to show early ROI. Short tail keywords are harder to rank for and require more budget and time but can deliver broader traffic long term.

Terms defined in this episode
Long tail keyword
A longer, more specific search phrase that falls in the outer tail of the search-volume bell curve, gets fewer searches than popular short terms, but attracts more targeted visitors who are closer to making a purchase.
Short tail keyword
A shorter, broad search term that sits inside the high-volume bell curve, gets many searches, faces heavy competition, and tends to bring less relevant traffic.
Keyword-focused anchor text
The clickable link text on a page that uses target keywords rather than generic words, signaling to Google what the linked page is about and influencing search placement.
Page rank
Google's iterative, algorithm-based measure of a page's global link popularity, influenced by the quantity and quality of external sites linking to it.
