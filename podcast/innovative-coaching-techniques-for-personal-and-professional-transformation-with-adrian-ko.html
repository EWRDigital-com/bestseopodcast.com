Questions this episode answers

What makes Adrian Koehler's coaching approach different from typical executive coaching?

Koehler focuses on reaching self-revelation in clients rather than delivering content or a fixed formula. He listens for language that reveals a client's hidden patterns and omissions, then confronts those directly. He will not work with clients who are unwilling to have courageous conversations, and he says most coaching fails because it never gets to causal issues.

What are the four survival needs that block honest business conversations?

Koehler identifies four survival needs that act as gravity for every human being: looking good, feeling good, being right, and being in control. These needs cause leaders to avoid difficult conversations, carry underperforming staff too long, and resist hearing honest assessments of current reality.

How does Koehler describe the P plus E plus O over C framework?

P stands for patterns, the habitual ways people think and behave. E stands for emphasis, what people spotlight in communication. O stands for omissions, what people consciously or unconsciously leave out. All of this sits over C, which is context or the stated aim, allowing Koehler to map the gap between vision and current reality.

What is the Revenant Process and the Intrepid that Adrian Koehler offers?

The Revenant Process consists of four-day intensive training events listed under public trainings on the Take New Ground website. The Intrepid is a more specific business leadership training that runs across three weekends. Both are available for individuals or leaders who want structured development outside of one-on-one coaching.

Terms defined in this episode
Fierce Advocacy
Koehler's term for getting crystal clear on a client's committed future, then delivering a sober and honest picture of current reality, even when the gap between the two is uncomfortable or alarming.
Recidivism Rate
The percentage of people released from prison who return. Koehler's partner Dan Tacchini developed work in the criminal justice system that reduced that rate from 98 percent down to 12 percent.
Future-Based Language Creates Present State
A principle Koehler uses: the future a person anticipates, spoken or unspoken, shapes how their brain filters present experience, causing them to find evidence that confirms their prediction.
Racket
Koehler's term for an under-the-table transaction a person has with themselves, such as imposter syndrome, where the complaint is stated openly but hidden payoffs keep the person from changing.
