Questions this episode answers

How much should a small business spend on marketing during a crisis like COVID-19?

According to the U.S. Small Business Administration, businesses doing one to five million in sales per year should spend 7 to 8% of gross revenue on marketing. The article cited in this episode calls 7% a bare minimum, with high-growth companies budgeting 10 to 12%. That means about $7,000 per $100,000 in gross revenue.

What specific SEO tactics can businesses use during the coronavirus slowdown?

The episode recommends auditing all content assets, creating and embedding videos that target long-tail keywords, planning webinars to answer customer questions, cleaning up administrative content backlogs, and targeting position zero features like featured snippets and video carousels in search results.

Why is it a bad idea to pause SEO during an economic downturn?

Because organic search is a zero-sum game, any ground you give up is picked up by competitors. Google does not react well to stopping and starting activity, and businesses that stay the course are positioned to come out on top when the economy recovers.

What should businesses do with video content they create during downtime?

The episode advises embedding videos in blog posts, including transcripts, taking screenshots for social media images, pulling five to ten quotes for Instagram, and reusing quotes in upcoming blog posts and media releases to maximize the reach of each video produced.

Terms defined in this episode
Position Zero
The result that appears above standard organic listings in Google, including video carousels, paragraph snippets, and other featured formats.
Zero Sum Game (organic search)
The idea that gains or losses in organic search rankings are exactly balanced by the gains or losses of competing sites, making it a live competitive environment.
Digital Assets
Online content a business owns and can use, including webpages, blog posts, emails, e-books, videos, infographics, and presentations.
SERPs
Search Engine Result Pages, the pages displayed by a search engine in response to a query, which can include various formats like featured snippets and video carousels.
