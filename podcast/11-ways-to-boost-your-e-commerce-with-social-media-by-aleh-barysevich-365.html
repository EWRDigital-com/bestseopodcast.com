11 Ways to Boost Your E-Commerce with Social Media by Aleh Barysevich #365

Ep. 36540 min2017-03-22

E-commerce brands can boost social media sales by focusing on audience-favorite platforms, creating exceptional content, retargeting satisfied customers, using messaging apps, and staying agile as platforms change.

Apple PodcastsSpotifyYouTubeiHeartRadioRSS
Key takeaways
  • Focus social spending on platforms where your audience is most active and engaged, not just where they exist.
  • Exceptional content must solve a problem, answer a question, or entertain, giving users a reason to share and return.
  • Retarget existing customers differently than new visitors, using what you know about buyer personas and purchase cycles.
  • Marketing budgets for social media are expected to rise from 10.7% to 23.8%, signaling where competitors are investing.
  • Stay agile because one app update can change your entire social strategy, so test, measure, and adjust continuously.
social media should be not only something you do externally to kind of promote content and promote your business. Social media really should be integrated throughout your entire company.

About this episode

Can social media improve your online sales? Only if you use it correctly! Tune in to SEO Podcast 365 and learn how to use the right platform, publish amazing content, and use live video to your advantage.

Questions this episode answers

Why is retargeting existing customers valuable for online stores?

Existing customers have already had a positive experience, making them the best leads. Retarget them with relevant offers based on buyer persona data, such as reminding them of a product refill cycle, and treat them differently from visitors who never purchased.

What role do messaging apps play in e-commerce customer support?

The top four messaging apps have over 2 billion monthly active users. Being available on Facebook Messenger, Instagram DMs, Twitter DMs, and website chat lets businesses serve customers in the way they prefer to communicate, capturing leads even during off hours.

Terms defined in this episode
Shiko
An eWebResults branded term for social engagement, standing for shares, likes, and follows.
Buyer persona
A profile of your ideal customer used to improve remarketing, such as targeting related products or predicting repurchase timing.
Retargeting
Showing ads on social platforms like Instagram, Facebook, Google, and Twitter to users who previously visited your site or purchased from you.
Exceptional content
Content that solves a problem, answers a question, or entertains, earning bookmarks, shares, and return visits rather than just immediate clicks.
