Key takeaways
  • Understand which of the five stages of awareness your prospect is in before writing any copy or building any funnel.
  • A funnel is just every touch point someone has with your business, and each touch point is a potential revenue leak.
  • Use a qualification form of three to seven questions after your landing page to filter for buyers, not just leads.
  • Track ROI and lead-to-sales time, not just CPL or ROAS, to see the full picture of funnel performance.
  • Technology is a multiplier: psychology plus economics times technology, and one thousand times zero is still zero.

Questions this episode answers

What are the five stages of awareness used in funnel building?

The episode references five stages from the book Breakthrough Advertising, focusing on three: problem aware, where someone knows they have a problem but not the solution; solution aware, where they are comparing options; and product aware, where they are choosing between specific brands or providers.

Why are Facebook lead form ads bad for lead quality?

Because there is no friction, lead form ads produce a high volume of leads who have no idea who you are, do not show up to calls, and do not convert into buyers. The guest says she hates them and prefers long-form landing pages with a qualification system to filter for serious prospects.

How does a thank you page function as a funnel asset?

Instead of just saying thank you, the page should congratulate the prospect, tell them the next steps, and immediately get them to take another action while momentum is high, such as watching testimonial videos, to build authority and increase show-up rates for booked calls.

What made the NDIS client case study successful despite a higher cost per lead?

By switching from Facebook lead form ads to a long-form landing page with a qualification system, the agency dramatically increased ROI and cut the lead-to-sales time from 30 days down to two days, even though the cost per lead slightly increased.

Terms defined in this episode
Self-liquidating funnel
A funnel where the front-end offer revenue covers the cost to acquire a customer, with profit coming from back-end upsells or high-ticket ascension products.
Stages of awareness
A framework from Breakthrough Advertising describing how educated a prospect is, ranging from problem aware to solution aware to product aware, used to match funnel messaging to the prospect's mindset.
Micro yeses
A series of small, sequential commitments a prospect makes as they move through a funnel, rather than one large ask, each one being the right next step toward a sale.
Post-click experience
Everything that happens after a prospect clicks an ad, including the landing page, qualification form, thank you page, and automations, which the guest says is grossly neglected by most businesses.
