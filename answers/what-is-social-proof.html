What is Social Proof?

Answered across 5 episodes of The Best SEO Podcast

Edited and curated by Matthew Bertram, host of The Best SEO Podcast (produced by EWR Digital).

The short answer

Reviews, testimonials, video testimonials, and user-generated content that reassure potential customers by showing other people have had success with a product or service. The episode cites data suggesting roughly 12 reviews are needed for social proof to meaningfully influence decisions.

How experts on the show define it

Reviews, testimonials, video testimonials, and user-generated content that reassure potential customers by showing other people have had success with a product or service. The episode cites data suggesting roughly 12 reviews are needed for social proof to meaningfully influence decisions.

From How to Create Landing Pages That Convert Sponsored by SE Ranking Ep. 539

Third-party validation, such as Amazon reviews or podcast appearances, that builds consumer trust more effectively than self-promotion because it comes from outside the brand.

From Power of Storytelling: Innovative Digital Marketing and Podcasting Strategies with Shaahin Cheyene

Reviews, likes, shares, video testimonials, and other signals from real users that prove to visitors a business can do what it claims, used on landing pages to increase conversions.

From The Perfect Local SEO Landing Page by Marcus Miller #329

Any social media activity about your event, product, or service that came from other people, such as tweets, check-ins, or hashtag feeds, functioning as digital word of mouth.

From Landing Pages You Need for Event Marketing by Mark John Hiemstra #299

Evidence such as Facebook likes, follower counts, reviews, and testimonials displayed on a page to show that other people trust and engage with a brand, increasing conversion likelihood.

From 6 Quick Tips to Improve Landing Page Conversion Rates by The AdVenture Media Group #311
