What is E-A-T?

Answered across 4 episodes of The Best SEO Podcast

Edited and curated by Matthew Bertram, host of The Best SEO Podcast (produced by EWR Digital).

The short answer

Stands for expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. A scoring factor that subject matter experts help improve, especially important for your money and your life content.

How experts on the show define it

Stands for expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. A scoring factor that subject matter experts help improve, especially important for your money and your life content.

From New SEO Strategies: 3 Steps To Perfect SEO Content Creation Ep. 552

Expertise, authoritativeness, and trustworthiness. A set of conceptual signals Google quality raters use to evaluate content quality, not a direct ranking factor or a single algorithm.

From Google’s E-A-T: Busting 10 of the Biggest Misconceptions by Lily Ray #503

Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness. Google uses E-A-T as a quality signal, especially for YMYL pages, evaluated partly through the reputation of content creators.

From 5 Things You Can Do Right Now to Improve Your Google E-A-T Rating by Manish Dudharejia #504

Google's framework of Expertise, Authority, and Trustworthiness used to rate page quality within its ranking algorithm, confirmed by Google in February and August of the prior year.

From Paving the Path Forward with SEO: Preparing for New Normalities by Lemuel Park #487
